# Chapter XIII: Trouble Brewing

> *"Pirates? Smugglers? We don't have any of them on Formos."* — Lieutenant Pavan, Formos Garrison

**Source:** Star Wars: Edge of the Empire Core Rulebook, Chapter XIII (pp. 417–437)
**Type:** Introductory Adventure
**XP Award:** 20 XP per session + 5 XP bonus for finding glitterstim or capturing Dobah

---

## Setting

**Planet Formos** — a forbidding, arid Outer Rim backwater world with a rocky desert surface shrouded by dusty, dark clouds. Breathable atmosphere, standard gravity, but inhospitable. Its sole value lies at the junction of two busy hyperspace routes: the Pabol Sleheyron (running Coreward into Hutt Space) and the Triellus Trade Route (spinward and trailing Outer Rim). Formos is also linked directly to the prison world of Kessel via the Kessel Run. This makes it a notorious haven for smugglers, spacers, and scum.

**Formos Spaceport** — the only settlement. A hardscrabble spaceport with a reputation for shady deals. A Limited Services Port consisting of a large duracrete landing area, a small control tower, and various sheds. Surrounded by a ramshackle settlement of ugly, utilitarian buildings. Mostly human population, though cosmopolitan. Two sides to life here: violent and lawless on one hand, an icon of freedom-fighting defiance on the other.

---

## The Situation

**Bandin Dobah**, a vicious Aqualish pirate, has seized control of contraband movement through the Formos Spaceport. He operates a franchise-style smuggling ring from a hideout on a nearby asteroid within the Kessel Run. Two bounties sit on his head:

1. **Imperial Bounty (10,000 credits):** Offered by Moruth Doole, administrator of the Kessel spice mines, for killing or capturing Dobah. Collected on Kessel.
2. **Hutt Bounty (5,000 credits):** Offered by Thakba Besadii Diori, a Hutt kingpin on Sleheyron. Full reward only if Dobah is delivered alive.

Dobah has abducted an R4 astromech droid (R4-W9) prized for its comprehensive Kessel Run navigational data. He has also recruited Godon Netakka, a Rodian bounty hunter, though Godon remains conflicted about his role.

**The PCs** arrive on Formos and head to a local cantina. En route they discover a wrecked protocol droid (J9-B8) who tells them about the droid abduction. At the cantina they learn about Dobah's bounty, encounter his local gang led by Daro Blunt, and ultimately track Dobah to his asteroid hideout for a final confrontation.

## Key Locations

### Formos Spaceport — Landing Area
- Limited Services Port; large duracrete pad, small control tower, maintenance sheds
- Landing fees are small; a smuggler could arrange a discount with an appropriate **Charm check**
- Stale, parched atmosphere; most species feel thirsty shortly after landing
- Aquatic species (Aqualish, Mon Calamari, Herglic) notably absent

### Imperial Army Barracks
- 18 Imperial Army Troopers (2 squad sergeants); a token garrison
- Fortified building with armoured clothing, breastplates, blaster rifles, frag grenades
- Heavy weapons: pair of E-Web heavy repeating blasters, antiquated Atgar 1.4 FD P-Tower on roof
- Troopers and locals maintain an uneasy mutual tolerance; troopers ignore PCs unless provoked
- Occasionally patrol the docks but rarely interfere with smuggling

### The Rii Jenks Cantina
- Run by Rii Jenks, a canny old Bith; the social hub of Formos
- Welcomes droids (oil bath prices are extortionate)
- Live music: Fia Mund (Sullustan Kloo Horn player), a Xexto Troiken percussion group
- Two levels: main floor with bar, circular balcony above with semi-private booths
- Holoscreen near door displays rotating bounty notice for Bandin Dobah
- Cosmopolitan clientele: humans (three-quarters), Devaronians, Duros, droids
- Patrons are paranoid; do not discuss smuggling openly

### Daro's Gang Safe House
- An old warehouse, refurbished; single large room
- Relaxation area with salvaged couches and hammocks
- Old-fashioned stove, larder, chemical toilet
- Reinforced steel cabinet (opened with Daro's key): assorted tools, 235 credits, gang weapons
- Centre piled with empty crates and boxes (cover in a firefight)
- **R4-W9** stands in one corner with a restraining bolt and wired-in portable computer

### The Asteroid Hideout (Kessel Run)
- Large, slowly drifting asteroid riddled with caverns left by massive space slugs
- Smooth cavern walls coated in slick, stringy ichor and black excreta
- Corrosive gunk; pilots must pass a **Hard (ddd) Piloting (Space) check** to avoid daubing their vessel (2 hull trauma if not removed within a few days)
- Navigational scan: **Easy (d) Computers check** reveals life forms, metallic objects, sentry droid in orbit

**Point A — Godon Netakka's Ship:** Small abandoned one-person vessel in poor shape. Hold contains five helium grenades, a plastic box, glow rods, and six Thalassian Security binders.

**Point B — Mynock Cavern:** Large cavern; flock of mynocks nesting in rocky crevices. Five mynocks attack any ship that enters, latching onto power cables. Act as a single minion group.

**Point C — Bandin Dobah's Vessel (*The Vagrant*):** A YV-666 Medium Freighter; Dobah's ship and hideout. Pressure curtain at cave entrance. Power cables run from cargo bay to pressure curtain generator and arc lights. Main hyperdrive broken (backup only).

#### The Vagrant — Internal Layout
- **Cockpit:** Pilot and co-pilot seating; ventral twin laser cannon operable from co-pilot seat
- **Cargo Bay:** Piled with empty boxes. Hidden floor compartment (**Hard (ddd) Perception check**): 100 doses of glitterstim (5,000 credits black market). Loading ramps on either side. Three cargo pods with two containers of glitterstim each (rigged to destroy contents if opened incorrectly; **Easy (d) Perception** to notice, **Average (dd) Mechanics** to disarm)
- **Galley and Refresher:** Basic kitchen and unhygienic shower; decent caf machine
- **Cabins:** Five small cabins (bunk, chest, dresser). First mate's cabin occupied by an Aqualish. Captain's cabin: 530 credits in desk
- **Common Room:** Long table, dejarik table (virtually unplayable)
- **Boarding Ramps and Machine Shop:** Armoury area; three blaster carbines, spare parts, shock gloves, hefty iron bludgeon (Gaffi Stick)
- **Escape Pods:** Room for four each. Godon Netakka uses the starboard pod as a makeshift cabin
- **Engineering Section:** Access to engines and laser cannon turret via ladder

## Encounter Flow

### Act I: Arrival and Discovery

#### Encounter 1: Landing on Formos
- **Situation:** PCs disembark at the spaceport and make their way towards the Rii Jenks Cantina
- **Read-aloud:** Description of the stale, parched atmosphere; immediate thirst; absence of aquatic species; water sellers everywhere at extortionate prices
- **PC Options:** Explore the spaceport, interact with locals, head to cantina
- **Skill Checks:** None mandatory; **Charm** to negotiate cheaper docking fees

#### Encounter 2 (Optional): Smuggling Operation
- **Situation:** PCs walk down an alleyway and witness a smuggling operation
- **Skill Checks:** **Easy (d) Perception** — notice earth bulging beside a warehouse; a hidden trapdoor opens and men move crates to a speeder
- **PC Options:** Observe, intervene, or ask questions (smugglers suggest talking to cantina patrons about Bandin Dobah)

#### Encounter 3 (Optional): Tritonite Missionary
- **Situation:** A lone insectile Tritonite preacher, inspired by Holy Gactimus, offers PCs a flexiplast tract about abstinence from vice
- **PC Options:** Listen politely, ignore, engage in conversation
- **Notes:** Purely flavour; no mechanical impact

#### Encounter 4 (Optional): The Cheapest Water Seller on Formos
- **Situation:** Mama Nobah, a seemingly charming old human woman, sells water at genuinely low prices and gossips with PCs. Claims to be from Alderaan
- **Skill Checks:** **Perception check opposed by Jok Nobah's Cunning 3** — while Mama Nobah distracts PCs, her son Jok pickpockets them
- **PC Options:** Catch the thief, buy water, chat. If caught, Mama Nobah begs forgiveness and offers free water
- **NPCs:** Mama Nobah and Jok Nobah (use Spaceport Urchin profile; minions)

#### Encounter 5 (Optional): Vreebo Gango — Rodian Wallflower
- **Situation:** A painfully shy young Rodian smuggler mistakes the PCs for his contacts and follows them at a distance
- **Skill Checks:** **Average (dd) Perception** to notice Vreebo tailing the party
- **PC Options:** Confront him (he becomes flustered, stammers about "business contacts"), ignore him. He is harmless and carries only a hidden holdout blaster

#### Encounter 6 (Optional): Lei Muoroy — Young Bandit on the Make
- **Situation:** A young human male pretending to be part of Dobah's network, running a petty extortion racket on locals
- **Dialogue:** *"I'm here to collect a cut for the Big Man around these parts, know what I mean? That'll be twenty credits for you, plus five extra for the trouble."*
- **PC Options:** Intimidate him (he tries to flee), pay him, interrogate him. If questioned, Lei can reveal the location of Daro Blunt's safe house (he has no actual connection to Dobah)
- **NPC:** Use Street Tough profile

### Act II: Becoming Embroiled

#### Encounter 7: The Sad Fate of J9-B8
- **Situation:** En route to the cantina, PCs hear a buzzing voice calling from a large trash compactor: *"Hello! Bzzzt! Help pleazzzle! I need help!"*
- **The Droid:** J9-B8, a 3PO-variant protocol droid by Cybot Galactica, badly damaged (dented head, smashed eye, pulled-apart body). Has a monotone voice with a buzzing speech impediment
- **J9-B8's Information:**
  - He and R4-W9 were free droids, waylaid by thugs ~20 hours ago
  - Attackers were mostly human, all male, wearing hoods
  - They fitted a restraining bolt to R4-W9 and dragged him away
  - Suspects connection to Bandin Dobah, an Aqualish who has been muscling in on Kessel Run smuggling
  - R4-W9 is prized for comprehensive Kessel Run navigational data
- **Skill Checks:** **Mechanics** — J9-B8 is beyond repair regardless of the check; can only offer comfort
- **PC Options:** Listen to J9-B8, ask questions, investigate the area. Nearby locals recall fighting near the warehouses but offer no details
- **Outcome:** J9-B8 shuts down permanently after sharing his information

#### Encounter 8: At the Cantina — Snoo the Information Broker
- **Situation:** PCs enter the cantina. A bounty holoscreen displays Dobah's notice (10,000 credits Imperial bounty). Patrons are paranoid and rebuff questions about Dobah
- **Key NPC:** Snoo, a Devaronian information broker, approaches the PCs and offers to "explain some matters" — privately, no recording equipment
- **Skill Checks:** **Average (dd) Charm, Coercion, or Deception** to haggle his fee down from 50 credits to 20
- **Snoo's Information:**
  - Formos is great for bounty hunting and contraband work, but you must earn respect first
  - Bandin Dobah heads a franchise-style smuggling ring of many cells (4–10 smugglers each)
  - The Empire's bounty is not the only one; Thakba Besadii Diori (Hutt kingpin) offers 5,000 credits for live capture
  - A Rodian bounty hunter (Godon Netakka) appeared weeks ago hunting Dobah and has not been heard from since — may be dead, or may be "padding" (deliberately delaying capture to let the bounty increase)

#### Encounter 9: Zukata's Woes
- **Situation:** An elegant Rodian woman (Zukata) enters the cantina, followed shortly by three smugglers from Dobah's network: Daro Blunt, Spir Fraxis, and Gut-Guro
- **Zukata's Role:** She is verifying an electronic galactic atlas for a Rodian publisher. She knows about R4-W9 and offers 2,000 credits for its navigational data. She also knows Godon Netakka is her brother (she reveals this if pressed; she is searching for him)
- **Zukata's Topics of Conversation:**
  - R4-W9's comprehensive Kessel Run data — she would pay well for it
  - Godon Netakka — *"a reckless young man... his clanspeople are very worried"*
  - She suspects Godon might be "padding" — delaying Dobah's capture for a bigger bounty
- **The Smugglers' Entrance:** Spir Fraxis harasses Zukata with lecherous comments. PCs can intervene or watch

- **PC Options and Outcomes:**
  - **Keep cool / polite to Spir:** He insults PCs and the three smugglers leave
  - **Verbal sparring:** **Coercion check opposed by Discipline** — if the PC succeeds, Spir backs down. If failed, Spir escalates to a fist fight
  - **Fist fight:** Gut backs Spir. Daro leaves the cantina. Spir and Gut fight until one is incapacitated, then break off and follow Daro
  - **Weapons drawn:** Daro and Gut back Spir. Daro stays near the door and flees if either ally is incapacitated/killed

- **Outcome:** Zukata thanks the PCs. She offers to repay the favour — food, drink, money, or information. Daro escapes with no more than minor injuries; Zukata suffers no harm.
- **Perception:** **Average (dd) Perception** as conversation with Zukata ends — PCs may notice a Toydarian named Zalg eavesdropping. He grins and heads for the exit, intending to sell information about the PCs to Daro Blunt

#### Encounter 10: Shadowing the Spy (Zalg the Toydarian)
- **Situation:** If PCs follow Zalg, they can learn the location of Daro's safe house
- **Skill Checks:**
  - **Average (dd) Stealth** to follow Zalg without being spotted
  - **Average (dd) Perception** to keep him in sight if he spots them and flees down an alley
  - **Average (dd) Stealth** to eavesdrop at the warehouse (Daro's safe house)
- **If Stealth succeeds (eavesdropping):** PCs overhear Daro and Zalg discussing the PCs — Daro plans to "pay them back for Spir" and is concerned the PCs are interested in Dobah's bounty
- **If Stealth fails:** A gang member spots the PCs and raises the alarm — proceed to Confronting Daro's Gang
- **If PCs take no action:** Zalg is paid 20 credits and goes home. Daro musters his gang and comes looking for the PCs

### Act III: Confronting Daro's Gang

#### Encounter 11: The Street Fight
- **Situation:** PCs must confront Daro and his gang, either at the safe house (advantage to PCs — gang is unprepared) or on the street (gang ambushes PCs outside the cantina ~30 minutes after Zalg's report)

- **Daro's Gang Composition:**
  - Daro Blunt (Rival — leader)
  - Spir Fraxis (Minion) and Gut-Guro (Minion) — if still alive
  - One additional Smuggler (Minion)
  - Three Street Toughs armed with slugthrower rifles (fight as a minion group)
  - If Spir and Gut are dead: add one additional Smuggler

- **Gang Armament (if forewarned):** Heavy blaster rifle with Galactic Arms XA Classic Bipod (Cumbersome reduced by 2 when braced), six stun grenades. Street Toughs take stun grenades; the Smuggler takes the heavy blaster rifle

- **Street Fighting Rules:**
  - Characters are never further than medium range from a pile of crates, and no more than long range from a rooftop, building corner, or trash compactor
  - Crates: +1 defence (basic cover)
  - Trash compactor / building corner: +2 defence (better cover)
  - Rooftop / trash compactor top: add a Boost die to shooting attacks made from above

- **The Warning Round:** Gang spends one round firing warning shots. Daro says: *"Look, you idiots clearly have no idea who you're dealing with. Drop your weapons and we'll go easy on you."*
  - **If PCs capitulate:** They receive a severe beating (strain to incapacitate) and are warned to keep their noses out of Daro's business
  - **If PCs fight:** Gang concentrates fire on biggest threats. Fights ferociously until Daro and Gut are killed/incapacitated, then the rest flee

- **Aftermath:**
  - Daro's body yields: medpac, two stimpacks, 50 credits, a restraining bolt tool (for droids), keys to the safe house
  - Local fringers approach and treat PCs as heroes, revealing the gang is linked to Bandin Dobah and providing the safe house address if not already known

#### Encounter 12: The Gang's Safe House — Recovering R4-W9
- **Situation:** PCs enter the safe house (opened with Daro's key) and find R4-W9 in one corner with a restraining bolt and wired-in computer
- **The Computer:** **Average (dd) Computers check** to access. Contains coordinates uploaded to R4-W9 (accounts of glitterstim transactions). With 3+ Advantage: access an additional file named "Pass" — a short-wave radio signal pattern for a ship's navigational computer (the sentry droid passcode)
- **Freeing R4-W9:** Restraining bolt keeps him inactive. Remove with the tool from Daro's backpack, or **Average (dd) Mechanics check** to remove/force it off. Failure: the droid takes 3 wounds
- **R4-W9's Information:**
  - Loaded with coordinates for a large asteroid field not far from Formos — Bandin Dobah's hideout
  - Reachable quickly by hyperspace-capable craft
  - Immediate concern: mourns J9-B8's destruction
  - Offers to join the PCs and help take down Dobah

### Act IV: Tracking Down Dobah

#### R4-W9's Motivations for the PCs to Attack Dobah
- Word will get around; Dobah will seek reprisal regardless
- Best to strike now while Dobah's location is known and before reinforcement
- Dobah's goons have murdered droids and intimidated the smuggling community — a moral imperative
- 10,000 credits bounty plus potential contraband prizes aboard the ship

#### Encounter 13: The Sentry Droid
- **Situation:** A small drone spacecraft orbits the asteroid, scanning for incoming vessels every 20 minutes
- **Skill Checks:** **Average (dd) Computers check** each turn to determine scan results when within medium range
- **PC Options:**
  - **Sneak in:** Wait for the droid to pass to the far side; fly into the tunnels. Requires **Hard (ddd) Piloting (Space) check**. Failure: droid detects PCs and alerts Dobah and Alger
  - **Destroy the droid:** If destroyed in a single round, no warning sent. If it takes more than one round, the droid sends an alert before being destroyed
  - **Broadcast the "Pass" file:** If PCs found the passcode on Daro's computer, broadcasting it causes the sentry droid to stand down and inform Dobah that a friendly vessel is arriving (Daro's crew expected)
  - **Jam the droid:** **Average (dd) Computers check** (one action). Jammed droid cannot send alerts but will fight back. Any PC with Piloting (Space) or Computers knows this is an option

#### Encounter 14: Dealing with the Z-TIE
- **Situation:** Alger Sodoth, a smuggler, guards Dobah's hideout from a pressure-shed hangar in a small cave. He mans the Z-TIE (a salvaged hybrid starfighter)
- **Trigger:** If the sentry droid alerts Dobah, Alger scrambles to engage. If PCs broadcast the passcode, Alger remains grounded. If PCs attack/jam the droid, Alger launches
- **Combat:** Alger closes to short range, fires his concussion missile, then engages with lasers
- **If PCs sneak past both droid and Alger:** They must deal with Alger when leaving the asteroid (unless they've dealt with Dobah, in which case Alger — stranded without hyperdrive — may bargain for a ride with his 500 credits, or attack out of desperation)

#### Encounter 15: Inside the Asteroid — Dobah's Lair
- **Dobah's Response depends on the sentry droid situation:**

**Full Alert (sentry droid warned Dobah):**
- First mate mans the ship's medium laser cannon
- Three human crew grab blaster carbines and take defensive positions behind crates in the cargo bay (+1 ranged defence)
- Godon Netakka remains by Dobah's side, unsure of his loyalties

**Unprepared (sentry droid destroyed/jammed before warning):**
- Crew in default positions: first mate in cockpit, Dobah in cabin, three humans in common room, Godon in starboard escape pod
- PCs who land and board quickly (within 3 rounds) escape laser fire; crew scrambles to respond
- First mate abandons laser cannon and fights personally; Godon attempts to find Dobah

**Expecting Daro (PCs broadcast "Pass" file):**
- Crew expect friendly arrivals; remain in default positions
- Three humans walk to cargo bay to meet "Daro"
- Once shots are fired or alarm raised, crew reacts as per Full Alert

#### Encounter 16: Making Appeals to Godon Netakka
- **Situation:** Godon is conflicted — ashamed of failing to capture Dobah, grateful for Dobah's clemency, uncertain whether he is a criminal or bounty hunter. PCs can try to turn him
- **Timing:** Each appeal takes one action during combat. If PCs fail the check but score 3+ Advantage, the GM may spend the Advantage to have Godon sit out the fighting
- **Options:**
  - **Threaten Godon:** **Hard (ddd) Coercion check** — least effective option
  - **Shame Godon:** **Average (dd) Charm check** — appeal to his wounded pride. Add a Boost die if the PC making the check is Rodian. Add a Boost die if PCs mention his sister Zukata
  - **Offer a Reward:** Godon considers the 10,000 Imperial bounty decent but not overwhelming. PCs must mention a figure of 12,000+ credits to sway him, or bluff with a **Hard (ddd) Deception check**
- **If no effort is made:** Godon fights alongside Dobah to the death out of desperation

### Act V: Collecting the Reward

#### Option A: Visiting Sleheyron (Hutt Bounty — 5,000 credits)
- Heavily industrialised planet in Hutt Space via the Pabol Sleheyron hyperlane
- **Thakba Besadii Diori** — a bulbous, slimy green Hutt in a palatial mansion guarded by Gamorreans
- **Dobah alive:** 5,000 credits paid immediately, plus an invitation to watch Dobah fight as a gladiator
- **Dobah dead:** 3,000 credits, non-negotiable
- Thakba buys any recovered glitterstim at half going rate; **Average (dd) Negotiation check** increases price by 100 credits per container per Success and 100 credits per Advantage for the whole deal

#### Option B: Visiting Kessel (Imperial Bounty — 10,000 credits)
- Prison planet; contacted by orbiting Imperial satellites and TIE fighter squadrons
- Stormtroopers meet PCs at landing pad, search for weapons, escort to Moruth Doole's office
- **Dobah alive:** Officials confirm identity and process the reward — but bureaucracy ensues
- **Dobah dead:** Officials dispute that the corpse is the right Aqualish; refuse to pay unless a PC passes a **Daunting (dddd) Charm check**
- **The Catch:** Only 2,300 credits can be found immediately. PCs are given a metal cylinder containing an encrypted code claiming they are owed 7,700 more. The cylinder contains an in-built tracking beacon (**Daunting (dddd) Computers check** to discover). It takes at least a full Coruscant year of repeat visits to collect the remaining 7,700
- **Additional complication:** While PCs are inside, stormtroopers search their ship for glitterstim. If found, officials claim it as lawful Kessel spice property and "generously" offer to buy it back, deducting 4,000 credits per bale from the reward

## Solo Play Adaptation Notes

### General Approach
- The adventure is designed for a party of 3–5 PCs. For solo play, the single PC should be accompanied by one or two NPC companions to provide combat support and skill coverage
- **Recommended companions:** R4-W9 (once freed) provides Astrogation, Computers, and Mechanics. Zukata or Godon Netakka (if recruited) can provide combat support

### Encounter Adjustments

**Optional Encounters (Encounters 2–6):** Run one or two for flavour and world-building. The Mama Nobah pickpocket scene and Vreebo Gango work well for solo play as they require no combat.

**J9-B8 (Encounter 7):** Works as-is. A solo PC benefits from the clear plot direction this provides.

**Cantina — Snoo (Encounter 8):** Works as-is. The information broker scene is a natural one-on-one interaction.

**Zukata and the Smugglers (Encounter 9):** Reduce to Spir Fraxis only (remove Gut-Guro from the cantina scene). Daro Blunt still leaves before any fight. A solo PC engaging in a fist fight with Spir should be manageable. Zukata's conversation works well as a one-on-one exchange.

**Shadowing Zalg (Encounter 10):** Works well for solo play — stealth and investigation suit a single character.

**Confronting Daro's Gang (Encounter 11):**
- Reduce the gang: Daro Blunt + Spir (or replacement Smuggler) + two Street Toughs (minion group of 2 instead of 3)
- Remove the heavy blaster rifle from the gang's arsenal
- Ensure the solo PC attacks the safe house (advantage of surprise) rather than being ambushed on the street
- Consider giving the PC a local fringer ally (use Street Tough profile) who volunteers to help after hearing about the PCs' confrontation with Spir

**The Safe House (Encounter 12):** Works as-is. R4-W9 becomes the PC's companion from this point forward.

**Sentry Droid and Z-TIE (Encounters 13–14):**
- Broadcasting the "Pass" file is the ideal solo approach — it bypasses both the sentry droid and the Z-TIE entirely
- If combat occurs, the PC's ship should have at least one turret weapon that R4-W9 can operate (Gunnery is not in R4-W9's skill list, so this is a last resort)
- Consider having the sentry droid already damaged (reduce HT to 6) if the PC must fight it

**Dobah's Lair (Encounter 15):**
- Reduce Dobah's crew: remove one of the three human crew members
- If PCs broadcast the "Pass" file, the crew is unprepared — this is the strongly recommended approach for solo play
- Godon Netakka should be easier to recruit: reduce the Charm check to shame him to **Easy (d)** with Boost dice for being Rodian or mentioning Zukata
- Once recruited, Godon provides significant combat support with his disruptor pistol
- Bandin Dobah himself remains at full strength (he is the climactic antagonist) but consider removing his Adversary 2 talent or reducing it to Adversary 1

**Collecting the Reward (Act V):**
- Sleheyron is the simpler and more rewarding option for solo play
- The Kessel route involves significant bureaucratic frustration and the risk of losing glitterstim to stormtroopers — this works well narratively but can feel punishing for a solo player

### Skill Coverage for Solo Play
The adventure requires a broad range of skills. A solo PC should ideally have:
- **Combat:** Ranged (Light) or Ranged (Heavy) for firefights
- **Social:** At least one of Charm, Coercion, or Deception for the cantina scenes
- **Technical:** Computers or Mechanics for accessing R4-W9's data and removing the restraining bolt
- **Awareness:** Perception for spotting Zalg, Vreebo, and Jok Nobah
- **Piloting:** Piloting (Space) for the asteroid approach

Where the solo PC lacks a skill, R4-W9 can cover Computers, Mechanics, Astrogation, and Piloting (Space). For social skills, consider allowing the PC to use Streetwise as an alternative in the cantina (the PC is navigating underworld culture, after all).

### Destiny Pool
With a single PC, the Destiny Pool will be small. Consider starting each session with 2 Light Side points and 1 Dark Side point (rather than rolling) to ensure the PC has narrative flexibility.
