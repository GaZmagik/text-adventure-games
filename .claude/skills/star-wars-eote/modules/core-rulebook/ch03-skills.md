# Chapter III: Skills

> *"Do or do not, there is no try."* -- Master Yoda

## How Skills Work

Skills represent abilities a character has learnt through education or experience. When a character attempts an action with a reasonable chance of success or failure, skills determine the result.

### Skill Ranks and Dice Pools

- **Skill values work in conjunction with characteristics** to form a dice pool. The higher value of the two determines the number of **Ability dice**; the lower value determines how many of those are upgraded to **Proficiency dice**.
- A character with **no ranks** in a skill is unskilled (value of 0) and rolls using only the governing characteristic's Ability dice, with none upgraded.
- Skill ranks range from **0 to 5**:
  - **Rank 0**: Unskilled; relies entirely on the characteristic.
  - **Ranks 1--2**: Thorough grounding but limited practical experience (apprentice/entry-level).
  - **Ranks 3--4**: True professional; capable and well-honed.
  - **Rank 5**: Master-level expertise; exceptional reputation.

### Skill Categories

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| **General** | Non-combat actions; the broadest category covering most situations. |
| **Combat** | Actions during direct physical confrontation. Rarely used outside violence. |
| **Knowledge** | Entirely cerebral; govern understanding rather than action. |

### Custom Skills

The GM may introduce new skills at their discretion. New Knowledge skills are the most common addition. New skills should be carefully considered for campaign tone, frequency of use, and impact on career/specialisation balance.

## Social Skill Interactions

When one character attempts to convince another to act in a specific way, this is an **Influence check**. These are typically **opposed checks** (or set difficulty against groups).

| Acting Skill | Opposing Skill |
|---|---|
| Coercion, Deception, Leadership | Discipline |
| Charm | Cool |
| Negotiation | Negotiation or Cool |

Prior relationships may add Boost or Setback dice to the check.

## Medical Check Difficulty

| State of Health | Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Current wounds equal to half or less of wound threshold | Easy |
| Current wounds equal to more than half of wound threshold | Average |
| Current wounds exceed wound threshold | Hard |
| Recover Critical Injury | Critical Injury Severity Rating |

- Only **one Medicine check per week** per patient for recovering from Critical Injuries.
- Droids may substitute **Mechanics** for Medicine healing checks.
- Characters treating **their own injuries** increase the difficulty by two Difficulty dice.

## Key Mechanics Notes

- **Opposed checks**: Many social and awareness skills use opposed checks (e.g., Stealth vs Perception, Charm vs Cool, Deception vs Discipline).
- **Extra Successes**: On most skill checks, additional Successes beyond those needed can enhance the result (e.g., reduce time, increase distance, improve quality).
- **Advantage/Triumph**: Each skill description provides suggestions for spending Advantage and Triumph for additional narrative effects beyond simple success.
- **Threat/Despair**: The GM may spend Threat and Despair generated on checks to introduce complications, penalties, or narrative setbacks specific to the skill used.
- **Initiative**: Determined by either **Cool** (when the character is prepared and acts calmly) or **Vigilance** (when reacting to unexpected events). All Successes count towards Initiative order.
