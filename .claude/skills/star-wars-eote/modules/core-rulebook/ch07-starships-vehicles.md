# Chapter VII: Starships and Vehicles

Condensed reference for the Edge of the Empire Core Rulebook, Chapter 7.

---

## Vehicle Characteristics

| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| **Handling** | Agility and responsiveness. Baseline 0. Positive values add Boost dice; negative values add Setback dice to Piloting checks. |
| **Speed** | Raw speed and acceleration (0-6). Speed 0 = stationary. Dictates available manoeuvres. |
| **Silhouette** | Abstract size (0-10). 0 = smaller than human. 3-4 = starfighters/light freighters. 10 = Death Star. Used to calculate attack difficulty. |
| **Defence** | First line of protection (shields). Each point adds a Setback die to incoming attacks. Max 4 per zone. Sil 1-4: two zones (fore/aft). Sil 5+: four zones (fore/aft/port/starboard). |
| **Armour** | Soaks damage (like personal soak). 1 point of ship armour = 10 points personal scale. |
| **Hull Trauma Threshold** | Physical durability. Exceeding it disables or destroys the vessel. 1 hull trauma = 10 wounds personal scale. |
| **System Strain Threshold** | Internal systems resilience. Exceeding it shuts down engines, weapons, shields (speed drops to 0, defence to 0). Recovers 1 per full day without further strain. **Cannot** be recovered by spending Advantage. |
| **Customisation Hard Points** | Slots available for attachments and upgrades. |

### Scale

- Planetary scale: 1 point = 10 personal scale points (weapons, armour, hull trauma).
- Personal scale weapons must deal 10+ damage to inflict even 1 point to a ship.
- Planetary Blast weapons hit primary target and every additional target within short range (not engaged).
- Damage must exceed armour to inflict a Critical Hit on a starship.

### Atmospheric and Sublight Speed

- Atmospheric speeds are slower than space equivalents (e.g., speed 4 in atmosphere is slower than speed 4 in space).
- Ships with transatmospheric capability (typically silhouette 5 or smaller) move at drastically lower speeds in atmosphere even if their speed rating stays the same.

- **Range:** Close, Short, Medium, Long (planetary scale).
- **Damage:** Base damage; each additional Success adds +1.
- **Critical Hit Rating:** Advantage cost to trigger. Roll d100 on Table 7-9. Additional Critical Hits on same attack add +10 to the roll each.
- **Fire Arc:** Forward, Aft, Port, Starboard (each 90 degrees). Some have Dorsal (top) and Ventral (bottom). "Fire Arc All" = turret-mounted.

### Weapon Categories

- **Blaster Cannons:** Heavy crew-served blasters. Less powerful than laser cannons. Effective against infantry and light vehicles. Includes Auto-Blasters (rapid-fire variants).
- **Laser Weapons:** Focused light beams with charged particles. Long-range, accurate, high damage. Standard anti-ship armament.
- **Ion Weapons:** Disable rather than destroy. Interfere with electrical systems, short circuits, corrupt computers. Little physical damage.
- **Turbolasers:** Massive, capital-ship-only weapons. Punch through shields, armour, and hull. Slow recharge, poor tracking against small targets.
- **Tractor Beams:** Modified shield generators projecting energy beams to ensnare ships. Used for docking, cargo handling, and capturing enemy vessels.
- **Ordnance (Concussion Missiles):** Kinetic penetrator + shaped explosive. Anti-fighter primary role. Additional missiles cost 500 credits each (Restricted).
- **Ordnance (Proton Torpedoes):** Irradiating, proton-scattering warhead. Heavier punch against large vessels. Additional torpedoes cost 750 credits each (Restricted).

### Limited Ammo and Starfighters

Starfighters with Limited Ammo weapons generally carry only one payload of torpedoes/missiles. They must reload at a base or carrier (not mid-flight). Reloading is normally a manoeuvre but not possible in mid-combat for starfighters.

### Fire Arcs

Four standard arcs: Forward, Aft, Port, Starboard (90 degrees each). Fixed weapons cover one arc. Turret weapons can traverse to cover any arc ("Fire Arc All"). Some ships also have Dorsal (top) and Ventral (bottom) arcs. Dorsal weapons cannot hit ventral targets and vice versa.

## Starship and Vehicle Combat

### Combat Overview

Follows the same structure as personal combat (Chapter VI) with additional starship-specific manoeuvres and actions.

1. **Determine Initiative:** Simple Cool or Vigilance check.
2. **Assign Initiative Slots:** PC results = PC slots; NPC results = NPC slots.
3. **Participants Take Turns:** Fill initiative slots one at a time; any un-acted PC/NPC can fill their side's slot.
4. **Round Ends:** "End of round" effects expire. If combat continues, repeat from step 3.
5. **Encounter Ends:** Once-per-encounter abilities reset. Recover strain.

### Combat and the Piloting Skill

When a Piloting check is called for, use **Piloting (Planetary)** or **Piloting (Space)** as appropriate. The GM decides which is most fitting.

### Small Craft Combat (Sil 1-4)

The pilot has one starship manoeuvre and one starship action (or two starship manoeuvres) per turn. A second Pilot Only manoeuvre costs 2 system strain. Max two Pilot Only manoeuvres per round per pilot.

### Capital Ship Combat (Sil 5+)

One starship manoeuvre and one starship action (or two starship manoeuvres) for the pilot. Each additional crew member aboard can use their own actions and manoeuvres to man weapons, operate sensors, perform damage control, etc. Only track individuals doing pertinent things; ignore the rest.

## Starship Manoeuvres

### Accelerate/Decelerate

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Increase or decrease current speed by 1 (min 0, max = ship's maximum speed).

### Fly/Drive

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Move closer to or further from something at current speed.
- **Speed 0:** Cannot use this manoeuvre.
- **Speed 1:** 1 manoeuvre = move within close range, or 2 manoeuvres = close to short or short to close.
- **Speed 2-4:** 1 manoeuvre = within close range or close-to-short/short-to-close. 2 manoeuvres = close-to-medium or medium-to-close.
- **Speed 5-6:** 1 manoeuvre = within close or close-to-medium/medium-to-close. 2 manoeuvres = close-to-long or long-to-close.
- **Note:** Moving between one range band and the next always takes two manoeuvres regardless of speed, with the exceptions detailed above.

### Evasive Manoeuvres

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** 1-4 | **Speed:** 3+
- Upgrades difficulty of all attacks against the ship once until end of pilot's next turn.
- Also upgrades difficulty of attacks made **by** the ship until end of pilot's next turn.

### Stay on Target

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** 1-4 | **Speed:** 3+
- Upgrades Ability of all combat checks from the ship/vehicle until end of pilot's next round.
- Also upgrades Ability of all attacks **against** the ship until end of pilot's next round (easier to be hit).
- Restricted to silhouette 4 or lower.

### Punch It

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** 1-4 | **Speed:** Any
- Immediately jump to maximum speed from any speed.
- Ship suffers 1 system strain per point of speed difference between current and maximum.

### Angle Deflector Shields

- **Pilot Only:** No | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Reassign up to 1 point of Defence from one zone to another. Only works if the ship has reassignable defence (e.g., deflector shields).

## Starship Actions

### Damage Control

- **Pilot Only:** No | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Successful **Mechanics** check recovers 1 system strain. Can also repair Critical Hits (difficulty per Table 7-9).
- In general, PCs can only attempt one Damage Control check to reduce hull trauma (one per success) per encounter.

#### Table 7-2: Damage Control Difficulty

| Condition | Difficulty |
|---|---|
| System strain < half threshold AND hull trauma < half threshold | Easy |
| System strain >= half threshold OR hull trauma >= half threshold | Average |
| System strain exceeds threshold OR hull trauma exceeds threshold | Hard |

### Gain the Advantage

- **Pilot Only:** Yes | **Silhouette:** 1-4 | **Speed:** 4+
- **Piloting check** with difficulty based on relative speeds (Table 7-3).
- On success: ignore penalties from own and opponent's Evasive Manoeuvres until end of following round; choose which defence zone to hit.
- On failure: no advantage gained, still suffer Evasive Manoeuvre effects.
- Opponent can counter on following turn (difficulty increases by one step per prior successful Gain the Advantage).

#### Table 7-3: Speed Advantage Difficulty

| Speed Difference | Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Same speed | Average |
| Initiator 1+ faster | Easy |
| Initiator 1 slower | Hard |
| Initiator 2+ slower | Daunting |

### Perform a Combat Check with Vehicle Weapons

- **Pilot Only:** No | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Each weapon may fire once per round.
- Uses **Gunnery** skill (or Ranged [Heavy] for some vehicles).
- Difficulty based on silhouette comparison (Table 7-4).

#### Steps:
1. **Declare attack and select targets.**
2. **Assemble dice pool** -- difficulty from Table 7-4.
3. **Roll and deal damage** -- each uncancelled Success adds +1 damage.
4. **Resolve Advantage and Triumph** (Table 7-5).
5. **Resolve Threat and Despair** (Table 7-6).
6. **Reduce damage by armour, apply hull trauma, apply Critical Hits.**

#### Table 7-4: Silhouette Comparison (Attack Difficulty)

| Difference | Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Same silhouette, or firing vessel 1 larger/smaller | Average |
| Firing vessel 2+ smaller than target | Easy |
| Firing vessel 2 points larger than target | Hard |
| Firing vessel 3 points larger than target | Daunting |
| Firing vessel 4+ points larger than target | Formidable |

#### Sil 4 or lower targets: defender chooses which defence zone the attack hits.
#### Sil 5+ targets: defence zone determined by relative position in the narrative.

### Additional Ship and Vehicle Actions

- **Pilot Only:** No | **Silhouette:** Any | **Speed:** Any
- Available to non-pilot crew members aboard.

#### Table 7-7: Additional Starship and Vehicle Actions

| Action | Skill & Difficulty | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Plot Course** | Average Astrogation / Hard Perception | Each Success reduces Setback suffered for difficult terrain by 1. |
| **Copilot** | Average Piloting | Each Success downgrades difficulty of pilot's next Piloting check by 1. |
| **Jamming** | Average Computers | Enemy must make Average Computers check to use comms. Difficulty increases by 1 per additional Success/Advantage spent. |
| **Boost Shields** | Hard Mechanics | +1 Defence to one zone until next turn (1 system strain). Additional Success extends duration by 1 round. |
| **Manual Repairs** | Hard Athletics | Remove 1 hull trauma + 1 per additional Success/Success. Once per encounter (follows Damage Control limits). |

## Planetary Scale Range Bands

| Range Band | Surface | Space |
|---|---|---|
| **Close** | Few dozen metres to several kilometres | Dogfight "knife fight" range; capital ships avoid this |
| **Short** | Up to several dozen kilometres; beyond line-of-sight for most | Just out of dogfighting range; ordnance range |
| **Medium** | Up to ~50 km | Up to a few hundred km; most capital ship engagements |
| **Long** | 100-200 km; engagements never happen here | Up to several thousand km; only largest capital weapons reach |
| **Extreme** | Far edge of scanners; weapons cannot reach | Beyond almost all weapons; jockeying for position |

Close range in planetary scale encompasses all five personal-scale range bands.

## Stellar Phenomena and Terrain

### Navigation Hazards

Piloting difficulty = ship's current speed or half silhouette (round up), whichever is lower. The lower value indicates how many times the difficulty is upgraded.

#### Table 7-8: Navigation Hazard Setback Dice

| Setback Dice | Planetary Description | Space Description |
|---|---|---|
| -- | Flat, clear terrain; roads; clear skies | Easy, unchallenging; broad asteroid field; thin nebula |
| 1 Setback | Scattered trees, rolling hills, sand dunes, windy | Tricky obstacles; flying over high mountains on a moon; thicker asteroid field |
| 2 Setback | Thick forest, steep rock-covered hills, violent storm | Dangerous astronomical feature; fracturing comet; dense asteroid field |
| 3+ Setback | Sheer cliffs, deep swamps, lava, canyon barely wide enough | Extremely dangerous; the Maw; deadly pulsar; asteroid tunnels |

### Celestial Hazards and Difficult Manoeuvres

When already within a hazard and performing an action requiring a Piloting check, use the action's specified difficulty (not speed/silhouette) but add Setback dice per Table 7-8. GM may also upgrade difficulty based on speed (once if speed 1-3, twice if speed 4-6).

### Ground Vehicles and Terrain

#### Table 7-15: Difficult Terrain

| Setback | Terrain Type |
|---|---|
| -- | Light terrain: rough gravel, clear dry fields, low hills, light urban ground or air traffic |
| 1 Setback | Medium terrain: deep mud, standing water <50cm, drifting sand, dense undergrowth, ancient ruins, heavy urban ground or air traffic |
| 2 Setback | Heavy terrain: fast-flowing water >1m, deep rocky terrain, thick forests, narrow winding streets, urban ground or air traffic so heavy it has stopped |

- Wheeled vehicles suffer full terrain effects.
- Tracked vehicles treat terrain as one step lower difficulty.
- Walkers ignore light terrain and ignore penalties from difficult terrain lower than half the vehicle's height.
- Repulsorcraft ignore hazards below their operational altitude.

### The Chase

- Determine starting distance. Both parties make competitive Piloting checks.
- Winner closes/opens distance by one range band. If winner is faster, open/close by additional range bands equal to the speed difference.
- Chase ends when pursuer reaches close/engaged range or chased reaches beyond extreme range.

## Taking Damage

### Hull Trauma

- When damage exceeds armour, the excess becomes hull trauma.
- **Exceeding hull trauma threshold (Sil 1-3 or unimportant):** Vehicle explodes or is disabled (GM choice).
- **Exceeding hull trauma threshold (Sil 4+ or with PC aboard):** Immediate Critical Hit. Systems shut down, emergency power only. Ship is adrift and effectively out of combat. Speed reduced to 1, Handling -3, all weapons inoperable. If it re-enters combat, any hull trauma attack generates a Critical Hit with +30.

### System Strain

- When a ship exceeds its system strain threshold: speed drops to 0 next round, weapons cannot fire, defence drops to 0.
- Crew can recover strain via the **Damage Control** action.
- Recovers 1 per full day spent without taking more system strain.
- **Cannot** be recovered by spending Advantage.

### Collisions

- **Minor collision:** All vehicles suffer a single Critical Hit. Subtract ship's defence x10 from the roll; if result is zero or less, Critical Hit is cancelled.
- **Major collision:** All vehicles suffer a single Critical Hit. Subtract ship's defence x5 from the roll only.
- Large vehicles may ignore collisions with very small vessels (GM discretion). Larger ships have more difficulty avoiding larger asteroids/terrain.

### Repairing Hull Trauma

Requires proper facilities (orbital station, spaceport, well-stocked garage), money, and time. Cost: ~500 credits per hull trauma point (highly variable). Light damage (up to 1/4 threshold): days. Heavy damage: weeks or months. Makeshift repairs using scavenged parts are possible but inferior (Hard or Daunting Mechanics checks).

## Interstellar Travel

### Sublight Travel

#### Table 7-12: Sublight Travel Times

| Trip Time | Example |
|---|---|
| 5-15 minutes | Planet's orbit to safe hyperspace jump distance |
| 30-90 minutes | Planet's surface to its moons |
| 6-12 hours | Between planets in the same system |
| 12-72 hours | Centre of a star system to its furthest limits |

### Hyperspace Travel

An alternate dimension coterminous with realspace. Objects with powerful gravity (stars, planets) cast "shadows" in hyperspace that are real obstacles. Fixed hyperspace routes exist between major population centres but are constantly shifting.

**Astrogation check** required: base **Easy Astrogation check** under ideal conditions. Modified by Table 7-13.

#### Table 7-13: Astrogation Modifiers

| Modifier | Circumstance |
|---|---|
| +3 Difficulty | Damaged or missing astromech droid or navicomputer |
| +1 Difficulty | Quick calculations or entry under duress (escaping pursuit) |
| +1 Setback | Ship is lightly damaged |
| +2 Setback | Ship is heavily damaged |
| +1 Setback | Outdated, corrupt, or counterfeit navigation charts or navicomputer data |

#### Table 7-14: Hyperspace Travel Times (Class 1 Hyperdrive)

| Distance Travelled | Average Duration |
|---|---|
| Within a sector | 10-24 hours |
| Within a region | 10-72 hours |
| Between regions | 3 days to 1 week |
| Across the galaxy | 1-3 weeks |

Multiply travel time by the hyperdrive's class number (e.g., Class 2 = x2 time, Class 3 = x3).

### Fatal Encounters

Collision with a mass shadow is the most feared hyperspace hazard. Failsafes drop the ship to realspace near gravity wells. Accidents are documented: ships colliding with planets or flying into stars. Coming out of hyperspace too close to a planet's surface can cause catastrophic damage or impact a population centre.

## Starship and Vehicle Modifications

### Attachments and Mods

Starships and vehicles follow the same modding rules as personal equipment (Chapter V). The only difference is that each mod costs **ten times** as much to install on a starship or vehicle.

### Attachment Details

#### Advanced Targeting Array
- **Base Modifiers:** Upgrades Ability of Gunnery checks by 1.
- **Mod Options:** 1 Additional upgrade Mod, 1 Innate Talent (Sniper Shot) Mod, 1 Innate Talent (True Aim) Mod.
- Applicable to all starships and vehicles.

#### Enhanced Carbon-Durasteel Armour
- **Base Modifiers:** +1 armour, -1 handling.
- **Mod Options:** None.
- Applicable to all starships and vehicles.

#### Electronic Countermeasures Suite
- **Base Modifiers:** Blocks sensors within range, counting ship's silhouette as one step smaller when fired at. Notifies all ships in star system or within 100 km planetside.
- **Mod Options:** 1 Increase difficulty by 1 when making subsequent attacks due to Guided quality Mod.
- Applicable to all starships and vehicles.

#### High-Output Ion Turbine
- **Base Modifiers:** +1 speed, -1 system strain threshold.
- **Mod Options:** None.
- Applicable to any airspeeder or starship.

#### Hydraulic Control Circuits
- **Base Modifiers:** Half damage from Ion weapons (after armour soak). -1 handling, -1 system strain threshold.
- **Mod Options:** 1 Defender chooses component affected by Ion Weapon quality (instead of attacker) Mod, 1 Increase system strain threshold by 1 Mod.
- Applicable to non-fighter starships.

#### Hyperdrive Generator
- **Base Modifiers:** Reduces Hyperdrive class by 1 (min 1).
- **Mod Options:** 2 Reduce hyperspace class by 1 to a minimum of 0.5 Mods.
- Applicable to any ship with hyperdrive.

#### Reinforced Shield Generator
- **Base Modifiers:** +1 Defence to one zone of choice.
- **Mod Options:** 2 additional +1 Defence to one zone of choice Mods.
- Only works on ships with existing defence rating.

#### Retrofitted Hanger Bay
- **Base Modifiers:** Carry smaller vehicles (max sil 2 smaller than carrier) in a converted hold. Sil 5 = total sil capacity 5; Sil 6 = 20; Sil 7+ = 60.
- **Mod Options:** 5 Additional maximum silhouette capacity Mods.
- Sil 5+ only.

#### Smuggling Compartments
- **Base Modifiers:** Stores up to 25 Encumbrance per compartment (counts toward total). Increases difficulty to find by +2.
- **Mod Options:** 2 Additional storage (25 Encumbrance) Mods.
- Any ship/vehicle with encumbrance capacity 25+.

#### Upgraded Weapons
- **Base Modifiers:** See Table 7-17 for weapon options.
- **Mod Options:** None (inherent to the weapon).
- **HP Required:** 0 if replacing existing weapon system; 1 if adding new. Combining 2+ weapons of same type always costs 1 HP.
- Applicable to any ship or vehicle.
