# Chapter I: Playing the Game — Condensed Reference

> Star Wars: Edge of the Empire Core Rulebook

## The Core Mechanic

The game revolves around the **skill check**, which has two elements:

1. **Roll a pool of dice** — assembled from characteristics, skills, equipment, and situational factors.
2. **Interpret the results** — if at least one Success symbol remains after cancellations, the check succeeds.

Success/failure and side effects (Advantage/Threat) are resolved independently, producing a rich narrative outcome for every roll.

## Dice Symbols

### Positive Symbols

| Symbol | Name | Found On | Effect |
|--------|------|----------|--------|
| Success | **Success** | Ability, Proficiency, Boost | Determines whether the check succeeds. Each uncancelled Success also increases magnitude (e.g. extra damage). |
| Advantage | **Advantage** | Ability, Proficiency, Boost | Positive side effect regardless of success/failure (recover strain, gain positional benefit, trigger weapon quality). Cancelled by Threat. |
| Triumph | **Triumph** | Proficiency only | Counts as **one Success** *plus* triggers a powerful beneficial effect. The Success portion can be cancelled by Failure, but the special effect cannot. |

### Negative Symbols

| Symbol | Name | Found On | Effect |
|--------|------|----------|--------|
| Failure | **Failure** | Difficulty, Challenge, Setback | Cancels one Success. If no net Successes remain, the check fails. |
| Threat | **Threat** | Difficulty, Challenge, Setback | Negative side effect regardless of success/failure (suffer strain, lose cover, give enemy an opening). Cancels one Advantage. |
| Despair | **Despair** | Challenge only | Counts as **one Failure** *plus* triggers a powerful negative effect. The Failure portion can be cancelled by Success, but the special effect cannot. |

### Force Symbols

| Symbol | Name | Found On | Effect |
|--------|------|----------|--------|
| Light Side Point | **Light Side** | Force die | Resource for Force powers; used by PCs |
| Dark Side Point | **Dark Side** | Force die | Resource for Force powers; used by the GM or at cost to PCs |

### Die Face Distributions

**Ability Die (Green d8):** Blank, Success, Success, Success×2, Advantage, Advantage, Success+Advantage, Advantage×2

**Proficiency Die (Yellow d12):** Blank, Success, Success, Success×2, Success×2, Advantage, Success+Advantage, Success+Advantage, Success+Advantage, Advantage×2, Advantage×2, Triumph

**Difficulty Die (Purple d8):** Blank, Failure, Failure×2, Threat, Threat, Threat, Threat×2, Failure+Threat

**Challenge Die (Red d12):** Blank, Failure, Failure, Failure×2, Failure×2, Threat, Threat, Failure+Threat, Failure+Threat, Threat×2, Threat×2, Despair

**Boost Die (Blue d6):** Blank, Blank, Success, Success+Advantage, Advantage×2, Advantage

**Setback Die (Black d6):** Blank, Blank, Failure, Failure, Threat, Threat

**Force Die (White d12):** Light×1, Light×1, Light×1, Light×1, Light×1, Light×1, Light×2, Dark×1, Dark×1, Dark×2, Dark×2, Dark×2

*Force die totals: 8 light pips across 7 faces, 8 dark pips across 5 faces. Light side results appear more often but in smaller amounts; dark side appears less often but in larger bursts.*

### Cancellation Rules

- **Success** and **Failure** cancel one-for-one.
- **Advantage** and **Threat** cancel one-for-one (separate from Success/Failure).
- **Triumph** and **Despair** do **not** cancel each other — both results are interpreted separately even if they appear in the same roll.

## Building a Basic Dice Pool

### Step 1: Determine Characteristic and Skill

Each skill is linked to a characteristic (e.g. Athletics → Brawn, Deception → Cunning). The GM and player agree which skill applies.

### Step 2: Assemble Ability and Proficiency Dice

1. Compare the character's **characteristic rating** and **skill rank**.
2. Start with a number of **Ability dice** equal to the **higher** of the two values.
3. **Upgrade** a number of those Ability dice into **Proficiency dice** equal to the **lower** of the two values.

> *Example: Computers 2, Intellect 3 → start with 3 Ability dice, upgrade 2 into Proficiency = 1 Ability + 2 Proficiency.*

If the character is unskilled (rank 0), no dice are upgraded; the pool is pure Ability dice from the characteristic alone.

### Step 3: Apply Task Difficulty

The GM sets the difficulty level, which determines how many **Difficulty dice** are added.

### Step 4: Modify the Pool

Modifications are applied in order: **add** → **upgrade** → **downgrade** → **remove**.

| Modification | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| **Adding dice** | Boost/Setback dice for situational advantages/disadvantages. They do *not* cancel each other out. |
| **Upgrading dice** | Ability → Proficiency, or Difficulty → Challenge (e.g. spending a Destiny Point). If no die of the base type remains, add one first, then upgrade it. |
| **Downgrading dice** | Proficiency → Ability, or Challenge → Difficulty. |
| **Removing dice** | Certain talents remove Setback or Boost dice from the pool entirely. Applied last. |

## Difficulty Levels

| Level | Difficulty Dice | Description | Examples |
|-------|:-:|---|---|
| Simple | — | Routine; usually no roll needed | Automatic success unless Setback applies |
| Easy | 1 | Little challenge; failure still possible | Picking a primitive lock, first aid for minor cuts |
| Average | 2 | Routine but failure is not surprising | Picking a typical lock, shooting at medium range |
| Hard | 3 | Demanding; failure is common | Setting broken bones, shooting at long range |
| Daunting | 4 | Pushes characters to their limits | Grafting implants, shooting at extreme range |
| Formidable | 5 | Nigh impossible without preparation | Incomprehensible mechanisms, survival without atmosphere |
| Impossible | 5 (special) | Requires spending a Destiny Point to even attempt; no other Destiny Points may be spent on the check |

## Interpreting Results

Resolve in order:

1. **Success vs. Failure** — net Success ≥ 1 means the check succeeds. Triumph adds one Success; Despair adds one Failure.
2. **Advantage vs. Threat** — net result determines positive or negative side effects, independent of success/failure.
3. **Triumph & Despair** — resolve their special effects separately; they do not cancel each other.

### Outcome Matrix

| Net Successes? | Net Advantage? | Narrative Outcome |
|:-:|:-:|---|
| Yes | Yes | Success with bonus side effects |
| Yes | No (Threat) | Success with complications |
| Yes | Neutral | Clean success |
| No | Yes | Failure but with a silver lining |
| No | No (Threat) | Failure with additional complications |
| No | Neutral | Simple failure |

Triumph and Despair layer on top of any of the above, providing powerful boons or dire consequences.

## Types of Checks

### Simple Check

A standard skill check against a fixed difficulty set by the GM. The player assembles Ability/Proficiency dice; the GM sets Difficulty/Challenge dice. Most checks are simple checks.

### Opposed Check

Used when another character is actively resisting. Instead of the GM setting a flat difficulty:

1. The **active character** builds their pool normally (Ability/Proficiency from their characteristic and skill).
2. The **opposing character's** higher value (characteristic or skill) determines the number of **Difficulty dice** added; the lower value determines how many are **upgraded** to Challenge dice.

> *Example: Pash (Agility 4, Stealth 2) sneaks past a patrol (Cunning 2, Perception 1). Pash rolls 2 Ability + 2 Proficiency. The patrol contributes 2 Difficulty, 1 upgraded to Challenge.*

### Competitive Check

Used when two or more characters attempt the same task simultaneously (e.g. a race, a drinking contest). Each character makes a skill check against the **same difficulty**. The character with the most net Successes wins.

**Tiebreakers** (in order):
1. Most Triumph symbols
2. Most net Advantage symbols
3. GM adjudication

### Assisted Check

One character helps another. Two forms:

| Type | Condition | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| **Skilled Assistance** | The assisting character has a higher characteristic or skill rating | The dice pool uses one character's characteristic and the other's skill rating (whichever combination is best) |
| **Unskilled Assistance** | The assisting character has no relevant advantage | The GM adds one **Boost die** to the pool |

- Only one character may provide skilled assistance at a time.
- Additional assistants contribute one Boost die each.
- In structured encounters, assistance requires the **Assist manoeuvre** and lasts until the assisted character's next activation.

## Destiny Points

### Preparing the Pool

At the start of each session, every PC rolls one **Force die**. Light side results become **light side Destiny Points**; dark side results become **dark side Destiny Points**. The pool size is fixed for the session, but composition shifts as points are spent.

### Spending Destiny Points

| Spender | Point Used | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Player | Light side | **Upgrade** one Ability die → Proficiency die on a PC's check |
| Player | Light side | **Upgrade** one Difficulty die → Challenge die on an NPC's check |
| Player | Light side | Trigger certain talents or special abilities |
| Player | Light side | Introduce narrative "facts" (with GM approval) |
| GM | Dark side | **Upgrade** one Ability die → Proficiency die on an NPC's check |
| GM | Dark side | **Upgrade** one Difficulty die → Challenge die on a PC's check |

### Key Rules

- When a light side point is spent, it **flips** to dark side (and vice versa).
- Only **one** Destiny Point may be spent per player per check. The GM may also spend one on the same check.
- The active participant (player or GM) decides first; the opposing side may then respond.
- Conversion occurs at the **end** of the action, preventing immediate re-spending of a just-flipped point.

## Obligation

Obligation represents a PC's debts, duties, blackmail, bounties, or other entanglements that reinforce the "fringe" experience.

### Obligation in Play

1. **Before each session**, the GM rolls a **d100** against the group's total outstanding Obligation.
2. If the roll is **greater** than the total Obligation → no effect this session.
3. If the roll is **equal to or less** than the total Obligation → the Obligation is triggered:
   - The specific PC whose Obligation range was hit feels the pressure.
   - That PC may suffer strain penalties or narrative consequences.
   - The GM weaves the Obligation into the session's story.

Obligation can be increased voluntarily during character creation for extra starting credits or XP, and reduced through play by paying debts, completing favours, etc.

## Experience and Development

### Starting Experience Points

Each species provides a starting XP budget used during character creation. Unspent creation XP carries over into play.

### Improving Characteristics

| When | Cost | Cap |
|---|---|---|
| During character creation | 10 × next rating value | Max 5 |
| During play | Only via specific high-tier talents | Max 6 |

Characteristics **cannot** be purchased with XP after character creation.

### Skill Training

Each skill has a maximum of **5 ranks**.

| Skill Type | Cost per Rank |
|---|---|
| Career skill | 5 × next rank value |
| Non-career skill | 5 × next rank value + 5 |

Skills may not exceed rank 2 during character creation.

### Acquiring Talents

- Talents are purchased from **talent trees** tied to specialisations.
- Top-row (tier 1) talents cost **5 XP** each.
- A talent must be in the top row or connected to an already-purchased talent.
- Cost increases by tier; similar talents may cost differently across specialisations.

### Acquiring New Specialisations

| Type | Cost |
|---|---|
| Within career | 10 × total number of specialisations the character would then possess |
| Non-career | 10 × total number of specialisations + 10 |

There is no limit to the number of specialisations a character may acquire.

## Characteristics

Six characteristics define a character's innate abilities. Ratings typically range from 1 to 6 (average humanoid = 2).

| Characteristic | Governs | Example Skills | Derived Attribute |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Brawn** | Physical power, toughness | Athletics, Brawl | Wound threshold, soak value |
| **Agility** | Dexterity, coordination, body control | Coordination, Ranged (Light), Ranged (Heavy) | — |
| **Intellect** | Intelligence, education, reasoning | Astrogation, Computers, Knowledge, Lore | — |
| **Cunning** | Craftiness, deviousness, creativity | Deception, Perception, Survival | — |
| **Willpower** | Discipline, mental fortitude, faith | Coercion, Vigilance | Strain threshold |
| **Presence** | Charisma, confidence, force of personality | Charm, Leadership | — |

---

## Derived Attributes

| Attribute | Derived From | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Wound Threshold** | Species base + Brawn | Total physical damage a character can absorb before being incapacitated |
| **Strain Threshold** | Species base + Willpower | Total mental/emotional stress a character can endure before being overwhelmed |
| **Soak Value** | Brawn (+ armour) | Amount subtracted from incoming damage before wounds are applied |
| **Defence** (Ranged & Melee) | Equipment/armour/talents (default 0) | Number of Setback dice added to incoming attack pools |

## Talents (Overview)

- **Active talents** require the player to declare their use; some have costs (strain, Destiny Point, action).
- **Passive talents** are always in effect or trigger automatically.
- Talents are organised into **talent trees** within each specialisation, arranged in five tiers of increasing power and cost.
- Some talents can be taken multiple times for cumulative effect (ranked talents).

## Quick Reference: Dice Pool Assembly

```
1. Identify the skill and its linked characteristic.
2. Take Ability dice equal to the HIGHER of (characteristic, skill rank).
3. Upgrade a number equal to the LOWER value into Proficiency dice.
4. GM sets difficulty → add that many Difficulty dice.
5. GM may upgrade Difficulty dice to Challenge dice (opposition, Destiny).
6. Add Boost dice for advantages; add Setback dice for disadvantages.
7. Apply any upgrades or downgrades from talents/abilities.
8. Remove any dice as specified by talents/effects.
9. Roll the pool and interpret results.
```
