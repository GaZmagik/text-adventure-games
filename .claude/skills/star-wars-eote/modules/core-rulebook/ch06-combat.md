# Chapter VI: Conflict and Combat

> Condensed reference for the Edge of the Empire Core Rulebook, Chapter 6.

---

## Narrative vs Structured Gameplay

| Mode | Description |
|---|---|
| **Narrative** | Default mode. GM describes events, players respond. No strict time tracking. Order of actions is unimportant. |
| **Structured** | Used for combat and tense scenes. Breaks time into **rounds** and **turns**. Each character gets one turn per round. Rounds last roughly one minute (deliberately unspecified). |

---

## Structured Gameplay: Encounter Steps

1. **Determine Initiative** -- All participants make a **Simple (-) Cool or Vigilance check**.
2. **Assign Initiative Slots** -- Results are ranked highest to lowest successes (Advantage breaks ties; PCs win ties with NPCs). Slots are marked as PC or NPC slots.
3. **Participants Take Turns** -- Slots are filled from top to bottom. Any PC may fill a PC slot; GM chooses which NPC fills an NPC slot. Characters who have already acted that round may not act again.
4. **Round Ends** -- End-of-round effects resolve. If action continues, repeat from step 3 using the same Initiative order.
5. **Encounter Ends** -- Once-per-encounter abilities reset. PCs may recover strain.

### Cool or Vigilance?

| Skill | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Cool** | Character is aware and ready -- quick-draw gunfight, springing an ambush, prepared situation. |
| **Vigilance** | Situation begins unexpectedly -- being ambushed, stumbling into enemies. Default if unsure. |

---

## The Turn

Each turn comprises up to three activity categories:

| Category | Limit | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Incidentals** | No hard limit (GM discretion) | Trivial efforts: speaking, dropping an item, minor shifts in position. |
| **Manoeuvres** | Max 2 per turn | Activities requiring time/effort but no skill check. One free; second costs 2 strain. |
| **Actions** | Max 1 per turn | Significant activities, usually requiring a skill check. |

---

## Manoeuvres

A character receives **one free manoeuvre** per turn. A **second manoeuvre** may be gained by:
- Voluntarily suffering **2 strain**, OR
- Exchanging the character's **action** for a manoeuvre.

**A character may never perform more than two manoeuvres in a single turn.**

### Types of Manoeuvres

| Manoeuvre | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Aim** | Gain +1 Boost die on next combat check (two consecutive Aim manoeuvres = +2 Boost). Alternatively, target a specific item/body part (inflicts Setback dice on the combat check instead). Lost if the character moves or performs other manoeuvres/actions before attacking. |
| **Assist** | Grant an engaged ally +1 Boost die on their next check. Multiple characters may stack Boost dice. Must be used on the assisted character's next turn or lost. |
| **Guarded Stance** | Add Setback die to all combat checks the character makes until end of next turn. Gain **melee defence 1** until end of next turn. |
| **Interact with the Environment** | Open/close doors, duck behind a wall, flip a switch, grab an item, move a large object. Broad category for environmental interactions. |
| **Manage Gear** | Draw, holster, ready, or load a weapon. Draw/stow items from containers. |
| **Mount or Dismount** | Mount/dismount a creature or vehicle. Mounting an untrained animal requires a **Medium Survival check**. |
| **Move** | Change range increment (one band per manoeuvre), engage/disengage from an opponent, or move within short range to a new position. See Range Bands below. |
| **Drop Prone** | Add Setback die to all incoming ranged attacks. Add Boost die to all incoming melee attacks. |
| **Stand from Prone** | Return to standing (costs one manoeuvre). |
| **Preparation** | Fulfil a prerequisite for a specific action, talent, or ability. Effect defined by the ability that requires it. |
| **Taking Cover** | Gain ranged defence 1 (some cover may grant higher). Retained until circumstances change or character leaves cover. |

### Manoeuvres Outside a Character's Turn

The GM may grant bonus manoeuvres to characters outside their turn (e.g., an enemy's failed Stealth generates enough Threat for the target to react). These do not count towards the two-manoeuvre limit, but the GM should cap out-of-turn manoeuvres at one or two per round.

---

## Actions

A character may perform **one action** per turn. Five major types:

### Types of Actions

| Action | Description |
|---|---|
| **Exchange Action for a Manoeuvre** | Forfeit the action to gain one additional manoeuvre (still capped at two manoeuvres per turn). |
| **Spend Action to Activate an Ability** | Use the action to trigger a talent or ability (even if no check is required). |
| **Activate a Force Power** | Use an action to activate a Force power (see Chapter VIII). |
| **Perform a Skill Check** | Any non-combat skill check that carries meaningful risk of failure (e.g., Coordination to cross a chasm, Computers to slice a terminal). |
| **Perform a Combat Check (Attack)** | Use a combat skill to attack a target. See below. |

## Performing a Combat Check

### Combat Skills

| Skill | Use |
|---|---|
| **Brawl** | Unarmed hand-to-hand combat. |
| **Melee** | Close combat with weapons (vibroknives, vibro-axes, etc.). |
| **Gunnery** | Heavy, crew-served, and vehicle-mounted weapons. |
| **Ranged (Light)** | Pistols and similar one-handed ranged weapons. |
| **Ranged (Heavy)** | Rifles and other two-handed ranged weapons. |

### Combat Check Steps

1. **Declare attack and select target.** Choose weapon, combat skill, and target.
2. **Assemble dice pool.** Skill + characteristic + talents + modifiers (Critical Injuries, environmental effects, etc.).
3. **Pool results and deal damage.** Must generate more Successes than Failures. Base weapon damage + 1 per uncancelled Success. Additional damage applies to each target if multiple are hit.
4. **Resolve Advantage and Triumph.** Player decides how to spend (see Table 6-2).
5. **Resolve Threat and Despair.** GM decides how to spend (see Table 6-3).
6. **Reduce damage, apply to wound threshold, apply Critical Injuries.** Subtract soak from damage; remainder inflicted as wounds. Apply any triggered Critical Injuries.

### Table 6-1: Ranged Attack Difficulties

| Range Band | Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Engaged | Easy + modifiers (see Table 6-4) |
| Short | Easy |
| Medium | Average |
| Long | Hard |
| Extreme | Daunting |

**Melee attack difficulty is always Average, regardless of other factors.**

## Spending Advantage/Triumph and Threat/Despair in Combat

### Table 6-2: Spending Advantage and Triumph

| Cost | Options |
|---|---|
| **1 Advantage or Triumph** | Recover 1 strain (may be selected multiple times). |
| | Add +1 Boost to next allied active character's check. |
| | Notice a single important point in the ongoing conflict. |
| | Inflict a Critical Injury on a successful attack that deals damage past soak (Advantage cost may vary by weapon Critical Rating). |
| | Activate a weapon quality (Advantage cost may vary). |
| **2 Advantage or Triumph** | Perform an immediate free manoeuvre (does not exceed two-manoeuvre limit). |
| | Add Setback die to targeted character's next check. |
| | Add Boost die to any allied character's next check (including self). |
| **3 Advantage or Triumph** | Negate target's defensive bonuses (cover, Guarded Stance, etc.) until end of current round. |
| | Ignore penalising environmental effects until end of active character's next turn. |
| | When dealing damage, disable one piece of the opponent's gear or deal wounds or strain instead of normal damage type. |
| | Gain +1 melee or ranged defence until end of active character's next turn. |
| | Force the target to drop a weapon they are wielding. |
| **Triumph** | Upgrade the difficulty of the targeted character's next check. |
| | Upgrade any allied character's next check (including self). |
| | Do something vital (e.g., shoot blast door controls to seal them). |
| **2 Triumph** | When dealing damage, destroy a piece of the target's equipment. |

### Table 6-3: Spending Threat and Despair

| Cost | Options |
|---|---|
| **1 Threat or Despair** | Active character suffers 1 strain (may be selected multiple times). |
| | Active character loses benefits of a prior manoeuvre (cover, Guarded Stance, etc.) until performed again. |
| **2 Threat or Despair** | An opponent may immediately perform one free manoeuvre in response. |
| | Add Boost die to targeted character's next check. |
| | Active character or allied character suffers Setback die on next action. |
| **3 Threat or Despair** | Active character falls prone. |
| | Active character grants the enemy a significant advantage in the narrative. |
| **Despair** | Ranged weapon runs out of ammunition for the remainder of the encounter. |
| | Upgrade the difficulty of an allied character's next check (including self). |
| | The weapon the character is using becomes damaged. |

## Defence

- **Defence rating** adds **Setback dice** equal to the rating to all combat checks directed against the character.
- Sources: shields, talents, armour, personal shield generators.
- Three types: **general**, **melee**, and **ranged** defence.
  - **General** applies to all attacks.
  - **Melee** applies only to Brawl and Melee checks.
  - **Ranged** applies only to Ranged (Light), Ranged (Heavy), and Gunnery checks.
- Multiple sources **do not stack**; use the **best** defence rating available for the attack type.

---

## Soak

- Default soak = **Brawn rating**.
- Armour and other protection add to soak.
- When taking damage: **total damage - soak = wounds suffered**. If result is zero or negative, no wounds.
- Soak is applied to **each hit separately** (e.g., auto-fire).
- Different soak sources from the **same type** do not stack (cannot wear two suits of heavy armour).
- Soak **does not reduce strain** (except weapons with the Stun Damage quality in specific instances).

## Range Bands

EotE uses abstract range bands rather than precise measurements.

| Range Band | Approximate Distance | Movement Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Engaged** | Within reach (melee distance) | 1 manoeuvre to engage/disengage | Sub-category of short range. Required for melee attacks, interacting with objects. |
| **Short** | Up to several metres | 1 manoeuvre | Conversation voice. Thrown weapons, small firearms. |
| **Medium** | Up to several dozen metres | 1 manoeuvre from short | Reliable pistol range. Must talk loudly to be heard. |
| **Long** | A few dozen metres+ | 2 manoeuvres from medium | Blaster rifles, mounted weapons. Must yell to be heard. Cannot close short-to-long in one round (3 manoeuvres needed). |
| **Extreme** | Farthest interaction range | 2 manoeuvres from long | Sniper weaponry, vehicle-mounted armaments. May not hear each other. |

**Note:** Moving between short and long in a single round requires 3 manoeuvres and is therefore impossible without giving up the action or suffering strain plus giving up the action.

### Planetary Scale Ranges

Planetary scale picks up where personal extreme range ends. Used for starship combat on a planet's surface or in space. The scales are considered separately; personal extreme is roughly planetary close range.

## Additional Combat Modifiers

### Ranged Attacks at Engaged Targets

- If the target is engaged with an **ally**, upgrade the attack's difficulty by one. If the attack generates at least one Despair, that Despair is automatically spent to hit the ally instead.

### Table 6-4: Ranged Modifiers (Attacking Whilst Engaged)

| Condition | Modifier |
|---|---|
| Engaged with Ranged (Light) | +1 difficulty |
| Engaged with Ranged (Heavy) | +2 difficulty |
| Engaged with Gunnery | May not make Gunnery checks whilst engaged |

### Prone Targets and Attacking Whilst Prone

| Situation | Effect |
|---|---|
| Attacking a prone target with Melee or Brawl | Attacker gains +1 Boost |
| Attacking a prone target with Ranged/Gunnery | Attacker suffers +1 Setback |
| Prone character making a Melee or Brawl check | Suffers +1 Setback |

### Two-Weapon Combat

- Wield two one-handed weapons (Ranged (Light) or one-handed melee/brawl weapons).
- Perform a **combined check**: designate one weapon as primary.
- Use the **lower** skill and **lower** characteristic of the two weapons for the dice pool.
- Take the **higher** base difficulty of the two weapons, then **increase difficulty by one** (if same skill) or **two** (if different skills).
- On success, primary weapon hits. Spend **2 Advantage or Triumph** to hit with secondary weapon as well. Each hit deals its own base damage +1 per uncancelled Success.

### Unarmed Combat (Brawl)

- Base damage = **Brawn rating**.
- Range: Engaged.
- Critical Rating: 5.
- Qualities: Disorient 1, Knockdown.
- Attacker may choose to deal damage to strain threshold instead of wound threshold (still reduced by soak).
- Brawl weapons may augment this with improved Critical Rating, additional qualities, and bonus damage.

### Grappling

Handled narratively via Brawl attacks (Disorient, Knockdown, strain damage). No separate grappling rules.

### Improvised Weapons

| Size | Dam | Crit | Range | Encum | Special | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Small | +1 | 5 | Engaged | 1 | -- | Bottle, fist-sized rock, hydrospanner |
| Medium | +2 | 5 | Engaged | 3 | Cumbersome 2 | Two-handed chair, holovid screen |
| Large | +3 | 5 | Engaged | 4 | Cumbersome 4 | Shovel, large tree branch, table, crate |

- Use **Melee** skill; add Brawn to damage.
- Automatic **Inferior** quality (generate one automatic Threat).
- Any Despair or double-Despair result: weapon breaks and is useless.
- Large improvised weapons require two hands (silhouette 1 user).

### Size Differences (Silhouettes)

| Silhouette | Examples |
|---|---|
| 0 | Jawas, Ewoks, astromech droids, small creatures |
| 1 | Humans, Wookiees, Gand, Rodians, Trandoshans, most humanoid species |
| 2 | Dewbacks, wampas, tauntauns, banthas, most riding animals |
| 3 | Rancors, krayt dragons, other large dangerous creatures |
| 4+ | Even larger creatures |

- Attacking a target **2+ silhouettes larger**: decrease difficulty by one.
- Attacking a target **2+ silhouettes smaller**: increase difficulty by one.

## Environmental Effects

### Concealment (Darkness, Smoke, Intervening Terrain)

| Setback Dice Added | Examples |
|---|---|
| +1 | Mist, shadow, waist-high grass |
| +2 | Fog, early morning/late evening darkness, thick shoulder-high grass |
| +3 | Heavy fog, thick choking smoke, darkness of night, dense head-high underbrush |

Concealment adds Setback dice to Ranged (Light), Ranged (Heavy), Gunnery, and Perception checks against targets with concealment. Conversely, it adds Boost dice to Stealth checks made by the concealed character.

### Cover

- Being behind cover (rock, crate, wall, vehicle) increases **ranged defence by 1**.
- GM may add additional Setback dice for particularly well-covered targets (trenches, blockhouses, prepared positions).

### Difficult and Impassable Terrain

- **Difficult terrain**: Costs **twice as many manoeuvres** to move through (tight passages, slippery ice, thick undergrowth, loose rubble, shifting sand, waist-deep water).
- **Impassable terrain**: Cannot be traversed via manoeuvres (sheer cliffs, deep pits). Requires at least one action and possibly manoeuvres, using Athletics or Coordination.

### Gravity

| Gravity | Effect |
|---|---|
| Normal | No effect on checks. |
| Heavy | Add up to 3 Setback dice to Brawn-based checks (except Resilience) and Coordination. |
| Light | Add up to 2 Boost dice to Brawn-based checks (except Resilience) and Coordination. |
| Zero | Add Boost or Setback to Brawn/Agility-based checks. All movement treated as difficult terrain (three-dimensional movement). |

### Water and Swimming

- Water is treated as **difficult terrain** unless otherwise noted.
- Swift currents/storms may require an **Athletics check** to swim; costs at least one action plus manoeuvres.
- Fully submerged: hold breath for rounds equal to **Brawn**, then begin suffocating.

### Falling

| Range Fallen | Damage | Strain |
|---|---|---|
| Short | 10 | 10 |
| Medium | 30 | 20 |
| Long | Incapacitated, Critical Injury at +50 | 30 |
| Extreme | Incapacitated, Critical Injury at +75 (or death at GM discretion) | 40 |

- Damage is reduced by soak; strain is not.
- An **Average Athletics or Coordination check** can reduce damage by 1 per Success and strain by 1 per Advantage. A Triumph may reduce the effective fall distance by one range band.

### Vacuum

- Hold breath for rounds equal to **Brawn**, then begin suffocating.
- Each round of vacuum exposure also inflicts **3 wounds**.
- Exceeding wound threshold: incapacitated + one Critical Injury as normal, plus one additional Critical Injury each subsequent round of exposure.

### Fire, Acid, and Corrosive Atmospheres

| Rating | Examples |
|---|---|
| 1-2 | Campfires, mildly caustic substances, ash-filled air from heavy fire |
| 3-5 | Flammable liquids, flame projectors, industrial acids, chemical leaks |
| 6-9 | Welding torches, weaponised acids, substantial dangerous gases |
| 10+ | Lava, blast furnaces, completely unbreathable/hazardous atmospheres |

- Each round, a character subjected to the hazard suffers **wounds equal to the rating** at the beginning of their turn.
- **Putting out fire/neutralising acid**: Coordination check -- Average on hard surfaces, Easy on grass/soft ground. Jumping into water stops fire damage immediately.

### Suffocation

- Suffer **3 strain** at the beginning of each turn whilst suffocating.
- Exceeding strain threshold: incapacitated. If still suffocating, suffer one **Critical Injury** per subsequent turn until suffocation ends or character dies.

## Wounds, Strain, and States of Health

### Wounds and Wound Threshold

- Each point of damage past soak inflicts **one wound**.
- **Exceeding wound threshold (PCs)**: Knocked out and incapacitated. Immediately suffer **one Critical Injury**. Must heal below wound threshold to act again. Can track wounds up to **twice** the wound threshold.
- **Exceeding wound threshold (NPCs)**: Defeated (dead, unconscious, or otherwise removed -- GM's choice). Exception: nemesis-level NPCs follow PC rules.

### Strain and Strain Threshold

- Strain represents stress, fatigue, fear, and mental/emotional impairment.
- Common sources: voluntary (extra manoeuvre = 2 strain), environmental effects, talents, emotional turmoil.
- **Exceeding strain threshold**: Incapacitated (unconscious/dazed). Must reduce strain below threshold to act.
- **NPCs** (non-nemesis): Strain is applied directly to wound threshold.

### States of Health

| State | Description |
|---|---|
| **Unwounded** | No wounds, no injuries. Operating at peak. |
| **Wounded** | Has wounds below wound threshold. Battered but functional. |
| **Critically Injured** | Suffering from one or more Critical Injuries (regardless of wound count). |
| **Incapacitated** | Wounds exceed wound threshold OR strain exceeds strain threshold. Unconscious and unable to act. |

## Critical Injuries

- Triggered by: spending Advantage/Triumph equal to a weapon's **Critical Rating** on a successful hit that deals damage past soak, OR exceeding wound threshold.
- Roll **d100** on Table 6-10. Each existing Critical Injury adds **+10** to the roll (cumulative).
- A Triumph may also inflict a Critical Injury; a Despair can activate one as well (no matter the weapon's Critical Rating).

|---|---|---|---|

| 11-15 | Easy | **Sudden Jolt** | Drop whatever is in hand. |
| 16-20 | Easy | **Distracted** | Cannot perform a free manoeuvre next turn. |

| 31-35 | Easy | **Stunned** | Staggered until end of next turn. |
| 36-40 | Easy | **Stinger** | Increase difficulty of next check by one. |
| 41-45 | Average | **Bowled Over** | Knocked prone and suffer 1 strain. |
| 46-50 | Average | **Head Ringer** | Increase difficulty of all Intellect and Cunning checks by one until end of encounter. |
| 51-55 | Average | **Fearsome Wound** | Increase difficulty of all Presence and Willpower checks by one until end of encounter. |
| 56-60 | Average | **Agonising Wound** | Increase difficulty of all Brawn and Agility checks by one until end of encounter. |

| 151+ | -- | **Dead** | Complete, obliterated death. |

### Ongoing Status Effects

| Status | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Staggered** | Cannot perform actions (including downgrading actions to manoeuvres). Default duration: until end of next turn. Multiple instances extend duration but not beyond the current encounter. |
| **Immobilised** | Cannot perform manoeuvres (including those gained via strain or Advantage). Default duration: until end of next turn. Multiple instances extend duration but not beyond the current encounter. |
| **Disoriented** | Add Setback die to all checks. Default duration: until end of next turn. Multiple instances extend duration but not beyond the current encounter. |

### Strain Damage and Critical Hits

Attacks that deal strain damage (notably stun weapons) can also trigger Critical Injuries. When a PC inflicts a critical hit on a minion, the minion is simply incapacitated. PCs and nemesis NPCs who suffer a stun-weapon critical hit may experience tangential injuries narratively (knocked off balance, hit the ground, etc.).

### Death

- A character dies if they roll **141+** on the Critical Injury table (including the +10 per existing Critical Injury modifier).
- A dead character cannot be brought back to life. A destroyed droid may not be restored.

## Recovery and Healing

### Recovering from Wounds

#### Natural Rest

- **One wound recovered** per full night's rest (regardless of health state).
- At the end of each full **week** of rest, attempt a **Resilience check** to recover from one Critical Injury. Difficulty = the Critical Injury's severity rating. Success removes the injury; failure retains it but still recovers one wound. A Triumph heals one additional Critical Injury.
- Droids benefit from natural rest (subroutines and automated self-repairs).

#### Medical Care (Medicine Check)

- One **Medicine check** per character per encounter.
- Difficulty based on the target's state of health (see Table 6-11).
- On success: recover wounds equal to **Successes** generated, and strain equal to **Advantage** generated.
- Droids use **Mechanics** checks instead of Medicine (same rules/difficulties, except self-healing only increases difficulty by one rather than two).

| State of Health | Medicine Check Difficulty |
|---|---|
| Current wounds <= half wound threshold | Easy |
| Current wounds > half wound threshold | Average |
| Current wounds exceed wound threshold | Hard |
| Recover Critical Injury | Critical Injury's Severity Rating |

- **Self-treatment**: Increase difficulty by **two**.
- **Without proper medical equipment**: Increase difficulty by **one**.
- **Critical Injury recovery via Medicine**: One check per week per Critical Injury; difficulty equals the injury's severity.

#### Bacta Tanks

| State | Recovery Rate |
|---|---|
| Wounded | 1 wound per 2 hours |
| Incapacitated | 1 wound per 6 hours |

- Every 24-hour period in a bacta tank: attempt one **Resilience check** to remove one Critical Injury (difficulty = severity rating). Success removes it; failure retains it but still recovers one wound.

#### Oil Baths (Droids Only)

- **1 wound per hour** in an oil bath.
- Do **not** restore Critical Injuries (use Mechanics checks for those).

#### Stimpacks

- First stimpack of the day heals **5 wounds** (no roll required).
- Each subsequent stimpack in the same day heals **one fewer wound** (4, 3, 2, 1, 0). A sixth stimpack has no effect.
- Must spend a full night's rest or wait 24 hours to reset stimpack effectiveness.
- Requires **one manoeuvre** to inject. Must be engaged with the target (or apply to self with a free appendage as one manoeuvre).
- **No effect on Critical Injuries.**

#### Emergency Repair Patches (Droids Only)

- Function like stimpacks for droids.
- Limit of **five per day**; heal **three wounds** per use (viability does not reduce over time).
- Do not fix Critical Injuries (use Mechanics checks; difficulty = Critical Injury severity).

### Recovering from Strain

- At the **end of an encounter**: each player makes a **Simple (-) Discipline or Cool check**. Each Success recovers 1 strain.
- A **good night's rest** generally removes **all strain**.

### Recovering from Critical Injuries

- Critical Injuries persist even after their short-term effect expires. They must be **treated** (Medicine or Mechanics check) to be removed.
- Untreated Critical Injuries remain and continue to add **+10** to subsequent Critical Injury rolls.
- Difficulty of the healing check = the Critical Injury's **severity rating**.
