# Chapter 2c: Character Creation (Steps 6-10) & Chapter 4: Talents

## Step 6: Invest Experience Points

Each player starts with a species-dependent pool of starting XP. Unspent XP carries over into play.

### Spending Starting Experience (Table 2-4)

| Option | Cost | Creation Limits |
|---|---|---|
| Increase characteristics | 10 x the purchased rating (sequential) | Max 5 during creation |
| Purchase skill ranks | 5 x the purchased rank (sequential); non-career skills cost +5 per rank | Max rank 2 during creation |
| Purchase talents in specialisation trees | Depends on row position in tree | Standard tree limits only |
| Purchase new specialisations | 10 x total number of specialisations owned; non-career costs +10 | Standard limits only |

**Important:** Characteristics may _only_ be raised with XP during character creation (never afterwards, except via Dedication). No characteristic may exceed 5 at creation (max 6 during play).

### Skill Training

- Five ranks available per skill.
- Career skill: cost = 5 x rank being purchased (e.g. rank 0 to 1 = 5 XP; rank 1 to 2 = 10 XP).
- Non-career skill: add 5 XP to each rank's cost (e.g. rank 0 to 1 = 10 XP).
- No skill above rank 2 during creation.

### Acquiring Talents

- Talents are purchased from specialisation talent trees (4 columns, 5 rows).
- Row costs: Row 1 = 5 XP, Row 2 = 10 XP, Row 3 = 15 XP, Row 4 = 20 XP, Row 5 = 25 XP.
- Must follow connecting lines from already-purchased talents (or start in row 1).
- Each tree slot may only be purchased once; ranked talents stack only if multiple slots exist on available trees.

### Acquiring New Specialisations

- Each character begins with one specialisation.
- Additional career specialisation cost: 10 x total number of specialisations the character would possess.
- Non-career specialisation cost: as above + 10 XP.
- New specialisation career skills become career skills for the character (no free ranks).

### Additional XP via Obligation

Players may take on additional starting Obligation to gain bonus XP (see Starting Obligation rules).

## Step 7: Determining Derived Attributes

### Wound Threshold

Species wound threshold + Brawn rating (after spending starting XP). Later Brawn increases do **not** raise wound threshold; purchase Toughened talent instead.

### Strain Threshold

Species strain threshold + Willpower rating (after spending starting XP). Later Willpower increases do **not** raise strain threshold; purchase Grit talent instead.

### Defence

- Ranged defence and melee defence; default is 0 in each.
- Gained primarily through armour, cover, or certain talents.

### Soak Value

- Default soak = Brawn rating.
- After initial determination, further Brawn increases also raise soak.
- Additional soak from armour and talents (e.g. Enduring).

### Motivation Types

- **Ambition:** Internal, often selfish drive -- a personal goal the character pursues (love, status, power, spiritual knowledge, etc.).
- **Cause:** An external group or concept important enough that the character takes risks for it. Need not be altruistic.
- **Relationship:** A bond with a specific person, creature, or place. May be loving, antagonistic, or one-sided.

### Motivations in Play

- Motivations are guides, not restrictions.
- The GM may provide "bait" matching a character's Motivation to encourage roleplay.
- Motivations may change over the course of a campaign (with GM collaboration).
- Secret motivations and "false front" motivations are permitted with GM approval.

## Step 9: Gear and Appearance

### Starting Gear

- Budget: **500 credits** (may be increased by taking additional Obligation).
- Spend credits on items from Chapter V before play begins.
- Restricted (R) items require GM dispensation.
- Unspent credits are kept; after finishing purchases, roll **1d100** for additional "pocket money."

### Physical Description & Personality

Consider species traits when determining: height/weight/build, skin/scale/fur colour, hair and eye colour, scars/tattoos/identifying marks. Personality may be developed from Motivation and Obligation or allowed to evolve during play.

| Ship | Role | Notes |

## Chapter 4: Talent Descriptions

### Talent Mechanics Overview

- **Activation:** Passive (always on) or Active (requires action type -- Action, Manoeuvre, Incidental, or Out of Turn).
- **Ranked:** Yes (may be purchased multiple times; effects stack) or No (purchased once only).
- **Trees:** Each talent is available on specific specialisation trees.
- **Talent Tree Structure:** 4 columns x 5 rows; must follow connecting lines from purchased talents.
- **Dedication** appears on every tree (usually row 5) and is the only way to raise characteristics after creation.
