## Step 1: Character Backgrounds

Backgrounds are narrative, not mechanical. They inform Obligation and Motivation choices.

### The Down and Out

Humble or hardscrabble origins -- moisture farmers, indentured servants, street urchins. The character knows life is nasty, brutish and short. Driven to escape their plight through training, resourcefulness or sheer will.

### Middle Class Struggles

Modest but comfortable prior life -- skilled technicians, minor officials, professional soldiers. Not shielded from crime, corruption or slavery, but may not have experienced them first-hand. Obligation often ties to the circumstances that made ordinary life untenable.

### The High and Mighty

Fallen from wealth, power or social standing -- landed gentry, business owners, politicians. The fall may stem from scandal, poor decisions, war or revenge. Obligation holders tend to be equally wealthy and influential.

### The Outsider

From a society outside the galactic norm -- isolated worlds, prison colonies, wilderness upbringing. Likely unfamiliar with galactic politics, customs or technology. Works well for species from primitive homeworlds.

### Entering Life as a Miscreant (Narrative Hooks)

| Hook | Summary |
|------|---------|
| **Opportunity Knocks** | Seized a chance to better oneself or escape boredom. |
| **A Higher Calling** | Vowed to change the galaxy; may tie to Oath Obligation. |
| **Enemies and Antagonists** | Rubbed someone the wrong way; fled former life. |
| **Wrong Place, Wrong Time** | Embroiled in adventure through no fault of one's own. |
| **A Failure of Character** | An inherent flaw (Addiction, Obsession, Betrayal) forced departure. |

## Step 2: Obligation

Obligation represents debts, duties and entanglements a PC carries. It has both narrative and mechanical weight.

### Table 2-1: Obligation Types

| d100 | Obligation | Summary |
|------|-----------|---------|
| 01--08 | **Addiction** | Must keep feeding a physical or mental addiction. Withdrawal causes Setback dice on skill checks. |
| 09--16 | **Betrayal** | Either the target or perpetrator of a deep betrayal that haunts daily life. |
| 17--24 | **Blackmail** | Someone holds damaging evidence and demands money or favours for silence. |
| 25--32 | **Bounty** | A price on the character's head -- legal warrant, criminal contract or personal vendetta. |
| 33--40 | **Criminal** | Has a criminal record (deserved or not) and is entangled in the legal system. |
| 41--48 | **Debt** | Owes a significant financial debt -- gambling, patronage, corporate. Paying it off may not end the trouble. |
| 49--56 | **Dutybound** | Compelled to fulfil a duty -- military service, contract, thieves' code. Legal or ritualistic bind. |
| 57--64 | **Family** | Deep family ties requiring time and attention -- care, inheritance, mediation. |
| 65--72 | **Favour** | Owes a big favour that will be called in piece by piece. |
| 73--80 | **Oath** | A sworn oath shaping thoughts and actions -- to a deity, code, cause or group. Personal and deep. |
| 81--88 | **Obsession** | An unhealthy obsession (celebrity, movement, cultural icon) pursued to the detriment of health or finances. |
| 89--96 | **Responsibility** | Strong accountability to a person, place, thing or minority group. |
| 97--00 | **Roll Twice** | Split starting Obligation into two equal parts of different types. |

### Table 2-2: Starting Obligation Values

| # of PCs | Default Starting Obligation per Player |
|----------|---------------------------------------|
| 2 | 20 |
| 3 | 15 |
| 4 | 10 |
| 5 | 10 |
| 6+ | 5 |

### Table 2-3: Additional Obligation (Optional)

Each option may only be chosen once. Total additional Obligation cannot exceed the character's original starting value.

| Bonus | Cost |
|-------|------|
| +5 starting XP | +5 Obligation |
| +10 starting XP | +10 Obligation |
| +1,000 starting credits | +5 Obligation |
| +2,500 starting credits | +10 Obligation |

### Obligation in Play

- At session start, the GM rolls percentile dice against the group's assembled Obligation chart.
- **Roll > total Obligation:** No effect this session.
- **Roll <= total Obligation:** All PCs reduce strain threshold by 1 for the session. The PC whose Obligation was triggered reduces strain threshold by an additional 1 (total -2).
- **Doubles rolled (e.g. 11, 44):** Effects doubled -- all PCs reduce strain threshold by 2; triggered PC reduces by 4.

### Key Obligation Rules

- **Range:** Practically 5--100. Cannot be reduced below 5.
- **Exceeding 100:** Obligation triggers every session. No PC may spend XP to improve abilities, train skills or acquire talents until total Obligation is brought back under 100.
- **As a Threshold:** The GM may set Obligation thresholds for access to contacts, jobs or locations.
- **As a Resource:** PCs may voluntarily take on Obligation to acquire items or accomplish goals during play.

## Step 3: Selecting a Species

Eight playable species are provided. Species determines starting characteristic ratings, wound/strain thresholds, special abilities and starting XP.

---

### Bothan

Information brokers of the galaxy. Fur-rippling communication, elaborate spy network ("spynet"), and a society built on knowledge and prestige.

**Homeworld:** Bothawui (Mid Rim)
**Language:** Bothese (spoken), Botha (written)

#### Characteristics

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|:-----:|:-------:|:---------:|:-------:|:---------:|:--------:|
| 1 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 2 | 2 |

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Wound Threshold | 10 + Brawn |
| Strain Threshold | 11 + Willpower |
| Starting XP | 100 |

**Special Abilities:**
- Begin with one rank in **Streetwise** (max rank 2 during character creation).
- Begin with one rank in the **Convincing Demeanour** talent.

---

### Droid

Self-aware mechanical beings. Treated as a species with bonuses reinforcing their robotic nature. Cybernetic implant cap of 6 (instead of Brawn rating).

**Models include:** 2-1B Surgical, 3PO Protocol, IG Assassin, LE-VO Law Enforcement, LOM Protocol, R-Series Astromech.

#### Characteristics

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|:-----:|:-------:|:---------:|:-------:|:---------:|:--------:|
| 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 |

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Wound Threshold | 10 + Brawn |
| Strain Threshold | 10 + Willpower |
| Starting XP | 175 |

**Special Abilities:**
- Do not need to eat, sleep or breathe. Unaffected by toxins or poisons.
- After selecting career: may train one rank in **6 of 8** career skills (not the usual 4). After selecting first specialisation: may train one rank in **3 of 4** specialisation skills (not the usual 2).
- **Inorganic:** Cannot benefit from bacta, stimpacks or Medicine checks. Require **Mechanics** checks instead (same difficulties). Emergency repair patches and stimpacks work for repair. Begin with one rank in the **Enduring** talent.
- **Mechanical Being:** Cannot become Force sensitive, acquire a Force Rating, use Force powers, or be affected by mind-altering Force powers.

### Gand

Mysterious insectoid species. "Findsmen" track quarry as religious duty using ritualistic divination methods.

**Homeworld:** Gand (Outer Rim, ammonia/methane atmosphere)
**Language:** Gand (clicks and chirrups; most lack vocal cords for Basic)

#### Characteristics

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|:-----:|:-------:|:---------:|:-------:|:---------:|:--------:|
| 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 3 | 1 |

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Wound Threshold | 10 + Brawn |
| Strain Threshold | 10 + Willpower |
| Starting XP | 100 |

**Special Abilities:**
- Begin with one rank in **Discipline** (max rank 2 during character creation).
- **Ammonia Breathers:** Player chooses one of two sub-species:
  - **Lungless Gand:** Immune to suffocation (but not vacuum wounds). No additional XP.
  - **Lunged Gand:** Starts with an ammonia respirator; treats oxygen as a dangerous atmosphere (Rating 8). Gains **+10 starting XP** (total 110 XP).

### Trandoshan

Belligerent, lizard-like humanoids with supersensitive infrared vision, regenerative powers and an avowed hatred of Wookiees. Society revolves around the hunt and the Scorekeeper goddess.

**Homeworld:** Trandosha/Dosha (Kashyyyk system)
**Language:** Dosh (can learn Basic, though with a heavy sibilance)

#### Characteristics

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|:-----:|:-------:|:---------:|:-------:|:---------:|:--------:|
| 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 |

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Wound Threshold | 12 + Brawn |
| Strain Threshold | 9 + Willpower |
| Starting XP | 90 |

**Special Abilities:**
- Begin with one rank in **Perception** (max rank 2 during character creation).
- **Regeneration:** Whenever recovering one or more wounds from natural rest or bacta tank recuperation, recover one additional wound. Does *not* apply to first aid, Medicine checks from another character, or stimpacks. Can regrow lost limbs (usually takes at least a month).
- **Claws:** Brawl checks deal +1 damage with a Critical Rating of 3.

Strong, intelligent and fierce. Loyal friends, fearsome enemies. Live for centuries in the treetop cities of Kashyyyk. Enslaved under the Empire.

**Homeworld:** Kashyyyk (forest world, Mid Rim)
