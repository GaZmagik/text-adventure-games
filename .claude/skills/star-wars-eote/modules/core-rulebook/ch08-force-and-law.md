# Chapter VIII: The Force & Chapter XI: Law and Society — Condensed Reference

## The Force

### Overview

The Force is a unifying energy that reaches through every being, planet, and star system. It binds, entwines, and penetrates all life. At its most basic level, the Force is balance — light and darkness are not in conflict but form facets of a unified whole. Those who use the Force for knowledge and defence are bolstered by the light side; those who tap into hatred, anger, and fear find their destructive desires amplified by the dark side. The dark side is not stronger, but it is easier to use and constantly tempts Force-sensitive individuals.

Belief in the Force is nearly extinct under the Empire. The Jedi have been destroyed, and Force users are hunted by Darth Vader and the Emperor. A Force-sensitive being must learn to use the Force on their own, relying on will and instinct, and must keep their abilities secret.

### The Force in Edge of the Empire

- Any character (except droids and certain species) can theoretically be Force sensitive.
- Being Force sensitive is not the same as being a Jedi — many are simply more in touch with the Force than average.
- A character's ability to use the Force is represented by their **Force rating**. Most characters have a Force rating of zero. Those who are Force sensitive have a rating of 1 or higher.
- A Force rating of 1+ makes a character **Force sensitive**, granting access to Force talents and Force powers.
- Certain talents and specialisation trees are only available to Force-sensitive characters.

### Force Dice

- The Force die is a twelve-sided white die, the seventh die type in EotE.
- It bears white circular symbols (light side) and black circular symbols (dark side).
- Unlike other dice, Force dice do not generate successes or failures. They generate **Force points** — resources the player spends to fuel Force power effects.
- Light and dark side results do **not** cancel each other out; both are available simultaneously.

### Becoming Force Sensitive

- The primary route is choosing the **Force Sensitive Exile** specialisation.
- This is a **universal specialisation** — available to any career but costs 10 XP multiplied by the total number of specialisations the character would then possess.
- Universal specialisations are not career specialisations.
- Droids and certain other species cannot become Force sensitive.
- The specialisation can be taken at character creation or at any later point.

### Force Sensitive Exile Specialisation

**Concept:** The Exile is not merely Force sensitive — they are a survivor of the purges. They may have been a young Padawan, a minor Jedi who fled and hid, or a child hidden by friends or family. Their formal training is limited or non-existent; their powers focus on concealment and control rather than flashy displays.

**Key features:**
- No career skills associated with this specialisation.
- Automatically grants Force rating 1 (if the character does not already have it; does not stack).
- Grants the specialisation's unique talent tree (see below).

**Talent Tree (5 rows, 4 columns):**

| Row | Cost | Talents |
|-----|------|---------|
| 1 | 5 | Uncanny Senses, Insight, Forager, Uncanny Reactions |
| 2 | 10 | Convincing Demeanour, Overwhelm Emotions, Intense Focus, Quick Draw |
| 3 | 15 | Sense Danger, Sense Emotions, Balance, Touch of Fate |
| 4 | 20 | Street Smarts, Uncanny Senses, Uncanny Reactions, Street Smarts |
| 5 | 25 | Sixth Sense, Force Rating, Dedication, Superior Reflexes |

**Notable talents:**
- **Uncanny Senses / Uncanny Reactions:** Add Boost dice to Perception / Vigilance checks.
- **Insight:** Perception and Discipline become career skills.
- **Overwhelm Emotions:** May add Force dice to Charm, Coercion, or Deception checks (light/dark results add Boost/Setback).
- **Sense Danger:** Once per session, remove two Setback from any one check.
- **Sense Emotions:** Add Boost to Charm, Coercion, and Deception checks (unless target is immune to Force powers).
- **Balance:** When healing strain at end of encounter, add light side results per Force Rating; recover additional strain equal to dark side results generated.
- **Touch of Fate:** Once per session, add two Boost to any one check.
- **Sixth Sense:** Gain +1 ranged defence.
- **Superior Reflexes:** Gain +1 melee defence.
- **Force Rating:** Gain +1 Force Rating (bottom row, cost 25).
- **Dedication:** +1 to a single characteristic (cannot exceed 6).

### Force Talents (General)

- Unique talents available only to characters with a Force rating of 1+.
- Represent the Force's instinctive ability to aid in minor ways — not complex enough to be full Force powers.
- They follow all normal talent rules with the additional caveat of requiring a Force rating.

### Force Powers

Force powers are the primary means by which a Force user deliberately manipulates the Force. Each power focuses on a particular task and costs **10 XP** to initially purchase, with additional XP spent on upgrades.

**Activating Powers:**
- Activating a Force power is an **action** (unless stated otherwise).
- The character creates a dice pool of Force dice equal to their Force rating and rolls.
- Every light side result generates one **Force point**; every dark side result generates none (and is disregarded) for a light side user.
- Force power checks do not generate successes/failures — they generate resources to spend.

**Dark Side Force Use:**
- A character may choose to use dark side results to generate additional Force points.
- To do so, the player must **flip one Destiny Point** from light to dark per dark side result used, and suffer **strain** equal to the number of dark side results used.
- The long-term consequences of tapping the dark side are left to roleplay — fear, anger, and hatred should grow in the character.
- For NPC dark side users, the GM flips Destiny Points from dark to light instead.

**Ongoing Effects:**
- Some powers create **ongoing effects** that last until the user deliberately ends them.
- To activate an ongoing effect, the character must **commit** a number of Force dice (not spend Force points). Each committed die is unavailable for other checks.
- This temporarily reduces the character's effective Force rating.
- An ongoing effect may be ended at the end of any subsequent turn as an incidental.

### Force Power Upgrade Categories

Each Force power has a basic form (first row) and upgrades purchased with XP, connected to the basic form or a previously purchased upgrade:

- **Strength:** Amplifies the basic effect, making it more potent.
- **Magnitude:** Increases the number of targets affected.
- **Duration:** Increases the length of the power's effects.
- **Range:** Increases the distance from which the power can affect targets.
- **Control:** Adds new effects or modifies existing ones, changing how the power spends Force points.

### Force Power: Sense

**Prerequisite:** Force Rating 1+

**Basic Power:** The Force user can sense the Force interacting with the world around him.
- The user may spend 1 Force point to sense all living things within short range (including sentient and non-sentient beings).
- The user may spend 1 Force point to sense the current emotional state of one living target with whom he is engaged.

**Upgrade Tree:**

The Sense tree is laid out in a 2-column structure with 5 rows. The left column focuses on defensive/offensive ongoing effects; the right column focuses on reading thoughts at greater range and on more targets. Connections flow downward within each column; the two columns are independent paths from the Basic Power.

| Row | Col | Type | Cost | Connects From | Description |
|-----|-----|------|------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | — | **Basic Power** | 10 | — | See above. |
| 2 | L | **Control** (Defence) | 10 | Basic Power | Ongoing effect: Commit Force die. Once per round, when an attack targets the Force user, he upgrades the difficulty of the pool once. |
| 2 | R | **Control** (Thoughts) | 10 | Basic Power | Effect: Spend Force point. The Force user senses the current thoughts of one living target with whom he is engaged. |
| 3 | L | **Duration** | 10 | Control (Defence) | Sense's ongoing effects may be triggered one additional time per round. |
| 3 | R-1 | **Range** | 5 | Control (Thoughts) | Spend Force point to increase power's range by a number of range bands equal to Range upgrades purchased. |
| 3 | R-2 | **Magnitude** | 5 | Control (Thoughts) | Spend Force point to increase number of targets affected by power equal to Magnitude upgrades purchased. |
| 4 | L | **Strength** | 10 | Duration | When using Sense's ongoing effects, upgrade the pool twice, instead of once. |
| 4 | R-1 | **Range** | 10 | Range (Row 3) | Spend Force point to increase power's range by a number of range bands equal to Range upgrades purchased. |

**Total upgrade cost (all nodes):** 100 XP (plus 10 XP for the Basic Power).

**GM Guidance for Sense:**
- Some species (and some individuals with particularly strong minds) are more resistant to powers such as Sense than others.
- Some species are completely immune.
- Sense does not work on communications over long distances.
- Sense only allows a Player Character to read the emotional state and surface thoughts of a target. This means that Player Characters can only ever get an imperfect snapshot of another's mind, and not simply call up information on demand.
- Overall, the GM should endeavour to allow Player Characters to use Sense to learn valuable information they would not normally know. However, this information should not allow them to automatically succeed in their goals, but instead provide a valuable foundation as they proceed. It should help them with their work, but not do their work for them.

### Force Power: Influence

**Prerequisite:** Force Rating 1+

**Basic Power:** The character may attempt to guide, shape, and even twist the thoughts and feelings of others.
- **Special Rule (Light/Dark Side use):** When guiding and shaping thoughts, only dark side results may be used to generate negative emotions such as rage, fear, and hatred. Only light side results may be used to generate positive emotions such as peace, tranquillity, and friendliness. Other emotions such as confusion can be generated with either light or dark side results. This rule is in addition to the rule about using dark or light side results. This means that to generate negative emotions, the average player must first roll dark side results, then flip a Destiny Point and suffer strain to use them.
- The character may spend 1 Force point to stress the mind of one living target he is engaged with, inflicting 1 strain.

**Upgrade Tree:**

The Influence tree is laid out in a 4-column structure with 5 rows. The left two columns focus on affecting larger groups at greater range; the right two columns focus on altering emotions/beliefs and extending their duration. A central Control (Skill Enhancement) node bridges the left and right halves.

### Force Power: Move

**Prerequisite:** Force Rating 1+

**Basic Power:** The Force user can move small objects via the power of the Force.
- The user may spend 1 Force point to move one object of silhouette 0 that is within short range up to his maximum range. The default maximum range is short range.
- Default movement speed is considered relatively slow and deliberate, not fast enough to cause damage.

**Upgrade Tree:**

The Move tree is laid out in a 4-column structure with 5 rows. The left column is Magnitude (number of targets); the second column is Strength (silhouette limit); the third column contains the three Control upgrades (Hurl, Pull, Fine Manipulation); the right column extends Range.

| Row | Col | Type | Cost | Connects From | Description |
|-----|-----|------|------|---------------|-------------|
| 1 | — | **Basic Power** | 10 | — | See above. |
| 2 | 1 | **Magnitude** | 5 | Basic Power | Spend Force point to increase targets affected equal to Magnitude upgrades purchased. |
| 2 | 2 | **Strength** | 10 | Basic Power | Spend Force point to increase silhouette able to be targeted equal to Strength upgrades purchased. |
| 2 | 3 | **Range** | 5 | Basic Power | Spend Force point to increase power's range by a number of range bands equal to Range upgrades purchased. |
| 2 | 4 | **Range** | 5 | Basic Power | Spend Force point to increase power's range by a number of range bands equal to Range upgrades purchased. |
| 3 | 1 | **Magnitude** | 5 | Magnitude (Row 2) | Spend Force point to increase targets affected equal to Magnitude upgrades purchased. |
| 3 | 2 | **Strength** | 10 | Strength (Row 2) | Spend Force point to increase silhouette able to be targeted equal to Strength upgrades purchased. |
| 3 | 3 | **Control** (Hurl) | 10 | Range (Row 2, Col 3) | The Force user can hurl objects to damage targets, by making a **Discipline check** combined with a **Move power check**, dealing damage equal to 10 times silhouette. Silhouette 0 objects deal 5 damage. This attack follows all the rules for ranged attacks, such as ranged defence and aiming. |
| 4 | 1 | **Magnitude** | 10 | Magnitude (Row 3) | Spend Force point to increase targets affected equal to Magnitude upgrades purchased. |

| 4 | 4 | **Range** | 15 | Control (Hurl) | Spend Force point to increase power's range by a number of range bands equal to Range upgrades purchased. |
| 5 | 1 | **Magnitude** | 10 | Magnitude (Row 4) | Spend Force point to increase targets affected equal to Magnitude upgrades purchased. |
| 5 | 2 | **Strength** | 20 | Strength (Row 4) | Spend Force point to increase silhouette able to be targeted equal to Strength upgrades purchased. |

**Note on Hurl damage:** The Force user makes a Discipline check with a difficulty equal to the silhouette of the object being thrown, making a Move power check as part of the pool. To successfully hit the target, he must gain enough Force points to move the object from its starting location to the target *and* he must succeed on the check. The damage is resolved following all the rules for ranged attacks. Silhouette 0 objects deal 5 damage, while other objects deal damage equal to 10 times their silhouette. If the player wants to use Move to throw multiple objects at multiple targets, he may do so using the rules for hitting multiple targets with the Auto-fire quality.

**Total upgrade cost (all nodes):** 140 XP (plus 10 XP for the Basic Power).

**Moving and Duration:** As written, the Move power does not have a set duration. This is intentional. During narrative gameplay, the duration can be as long as it needs to be; in general, users will be moving items from one point to another. If they do need to keep an item suspended for a short period of time, that is perfectly acceptable and does not require a second check. If they need to keep an item suspended for a long period (more than several minutes), the GM can decide to require a second check, or start inflicting strain on the Force user as they attempt to maintain the power. The same holds true for durations during encounters. Generally, Force users suspend items for a single round when they use the Move power. If they wish to lift an item for multiple rounds, the GM could treat that as an ongoing effect, requiring the Force user to commit Force dice to maintain the power. For particularly large items (silhouette 2 or larger, for example), the GM can also inflict strain equal to the silhouette each round the Force user maintains the power.

### Disturbances in the Force

Any form of rapid or violent change in the galaxy creates a disturbance in the Force. Death causes the greatest ripples, but anything from a birth to a galactic cataclysm can trigger tremors. Force users feel these as a sense of uneasiness or discomfort. No disturbance should be dismissed lightly — each signals a transformation occurring in the galaxy. Sensing disturbances also allows a Force user to detect Force-sensitive objects and beings.

## Law and Society

### The Galactic Empire

The Empire shapes daily life on every planet through military might and bureaucratic control. Its structure is pyramidal with the Emperor at the pinnacle.

**Tiers of Order:**
- **Grand Moffs:** Supreme political and military leaders over priority sectors (not necessarily aligned with traditional sector boundaries). Given total autonomy; report only to the Emperor. Appointed personally by Palpatine.
- **Moffs:** Command traditional sectors (collections of systems united by proximity, economy, and tradition). Each commands at least a Sector Group of military resources. Responsible for sector productivity, security, and related issues.
- **Planetary Governors:** Imperial agents appointed by Palpatine or advisors. Rule extends to all planets within a single star system. Nominally command local Imperial military forces, though the Navy often acts independently. Tend towards observe-and-advise unless the planet conflicts with Imperial wishes.

### COMPNOR (Commission for the Preservation of the New Order)

The Emperor's personal machine of political power. Key elements relevant to fringers:

- **CompForce:** Military arm — frontline Assault troops and Observation volunteers. Assault troops are fanatically loyal but poorly trained; Observ operatives monitor military loyalty.
- **Imperial Security Bureau (ISB):** The Emperor's secret police. Reports to the Select Committee and the Emperor. Tasked with gathering intelligence on all potential threats. Branches include Surveillance (35% of personnel), Investigations, Internal Affairs, Re-Education, and Enforcement. Has its own cadre of stormtroopers — even more fanatically loyal. The most feared and hated organisation in the galaxy.
- **Coalition for Progress — Justice Division:** Contains the **Imperial Office of Criminal Investigations (IOCI)** and the **Commerce** division. IOCI is the primary law enforcement concern for criminals. Commerce focuses on economic warfare and shutting down Rebellion support.

### Imperial Intelligence

Formed in the waning days of the Old Republic. Combines four older agencies under the **Ubiqtorate**. Focuses on military and external threats (as opposed to ISB's internal/political focus). Structure includes system cells (small independent units galaxy-wide), the Bureau of Operations (Infiltration and Assassination branches), and the elite **Adjustments** branch for the most dangerous targets.

### Imperial Military

- **Navy:** Backbone of Imperial military structure. Commands the systems and sectors. Officers rarely bother with local disputes unless Imperial assets or installations are involved.
- **Army:** Rapid ground operations capability. Increasingly integrated with the Navy post-Yavin.
- **Stormtrooper Corps:** Separate branch answering directly to the Emperor via Stormtrooper Command. Ultimate shock troops — faceless, fanatically loyal, rapidly deployable.
- **Imperial Customs:** Separate agency with its own fleet of corvettes and space stations. Monitors imports/exports, enforces taxes and tariffs, interdicts smugglers and pirates. The Commerce branch of COMPNOR has begun requisitioning Customs assets.

### Dealing with the Empire

Keeping out of the Empire's way is the highest priority for most fringers. Ways a group might cross paths:
- Hired by a governor or Moff for a clandestine operation.
- Political past attracting COMPNOR attention.
- Encountering a CompForce attack on a helpless community.
- Targeted by an ISB investigation.
- Pursued by IOCI detectives or Sector Rangers.
- Suspected of ties to the Rebellion, drawing Imperial Intelligence.
- Stumbling across secret Naval operations.
- Constant risk from Imperial Customs when flying through Imperial space.

## Criminal Organisations

### Black Sun

A galaxy-spanning criminal confederation founded over three thousand years ago on Coruscant. Possibly as powerful as the Alliance and a real threat to Imperial power.

**Organisation:**
- Divided into nine territories, each overseen by a **Vigo** (old Tionese for "nephew").
- Above all Vigos is the **Underlord** — currently **Prince Xizor**, a Falleen with a mysterious past.
- Xizor's most prominent associate is **Guri**, his lieutenant, major-domo, and bodyguard (widely believed to be more than human — a human replica droid).
- Each Vigo oversees a vast network of sub-lieutenants. Most lower-level operatives have no idea they work for Black Sun.

**Known Vigos and their domains:**
- **Clezo:** Conservative and business-minded; controls criminal activity in a few systems. Holds bounty hunting guilds including House Tresario.
- **Durga the Hutt:** Exploits transport and smuggling around Nar Shaddaa; connection to Hutt affairs and the Klatooinian Trade Guild. Gun-running and slavery.
- **Green (Human):** Aspires to become the next Underlord. Racketeering, extortion, blackmail in the Core Worlds. Expanding spice smuggling efforts.
- **Kreet'ah:** Inherited position from mother. Corporate espionage Vigo — stealing secrets, revenue slicing, spy networks, market manipulation.
- **Lonay (Twi'lek):** Outer Rim focus — slave trading, spice smuggling, gun-running, vice crimes. Highly flexible organisation.
- **Perit (Mon Calamari):** Ex-slave, master slicer. Technological and computerised crime; manipulates entire markets. Controls assassin droids and impenetrable electronic security.
- **Sprax:** Master of shipping and transport. Widest galactic range of any Vigo (Core Worlds to Outer Rim). Runs syndicates of pirates, smugglers, shipjackers, slavers, and black market traders — most unaware they serve Black Sun. Apparently supplies ships and weapons to the Rebellion with Xizor's blessing.
- **Vekker:** Patient and loyal; oversees casinos and entertainment businesses. Vice-related criminal activity. Keeps paying customers happy.
- **Wumdi (Etti Vigo):** Corporate Sector specialist. Bribed officials, spies, droids, and specialists. Vice crimes, smuggling, corporate espionage, revenue slicing.

**Activities:** Smuggling, slavery, racketeering, kidnapping, extortion, spice, illegal weapons — and legitimate businesses and even planetary ownership.

**Xizor's Agenda:** Evidence suggests he plays the Alliance and Empire against each other. Some rumours indicate a powerful personal enmity with Darth Vader.

**Dealing with Black Sun:**
- Extremely dangerous — once involved, there are virtually no options to get back out.
- Obligations to Black Sun are common and binding.
- Fringers may be drawn in through smuggling and transport work, ship repairs (Perit's people are always looking), bypassing bounty notices, power-plays between Vigos, debts of friends or loved ones, or being offered clean records in exchange for infiltrating a Black Sun operation.

### The Hutt Cartels

The Hutts are both a species and an organisation of immense power and influence. Their sense of collective superiority over all other species and their immense wealth (accumulated over long lifespans and passed through elaborate inheritance protocols via their clans, called **kajidics**) form the foundation of their power.

**Society and Organisation:**
- Crime activities are a family affair within each kajidic.
- Each Hutt in a kajidic takes up residence where they can directly oversee a criminal operation, surrounded by soldiers, servants, and specialists.
- Non-Hutts virtually never achieve any rank of distinction; such roles are reserved for Hutts of the particular clan.
- Their collective ego and arrogance stem from surviving the destruction of their homeworld.
- Overall strategy originates on their new homeworld, **Nal Hutta**.
- **Jabba Desilijic Tiure** convinced the Hutts to work together against the "lesser races," making himself a major player in Outer Rim criminal enterprises. He avoids costly conflicts, preferring a live-and-let-live philosophy (though he maintains a delicate balance with Black Sun).

**Hutt Space:** A large swath of Outer Rim territories where the Hutts maintain absolute control. Black Sun respects this territorial claim.

**Activities:**
- Controlling product flow and taking percentages of every transaction.
- Smuggling anything and everything, especially illegal arms and substances.
- **Spice trade** (particularly ryll) — the biggest and most widespread Hutt endeavour.
- Brisk slave trade — their disdain for other races makes this easy.
- Revenge as cultural pastime — no being is allowed to wrong a Hutt without extreme consequences (torture, humiliation, not merely murder). Hutts will spend exorbitant amounts tracking down and punishing those who cross them.

**Dealing with the Hutts:**
- Any fringer in the Outer Rim will likely end up working for a Hutt eventually.
- Unlike Black Sun, a Hutt will not stay hidden — anyone useful and resourceful will eventually be called before the Hutt for evaluation.
- Meetings are a test of savvy or bravery. Hutts respond well to bravado and confidence but despise excessive cravenness or dismissiveness.
- Subservience and respect are greatly appreciated.
- Fringers may be brought in for questioning about operations gone wrong, recruited for their skills, asked to supply swoop racers or combat arena fighters, or asked to arrange smuggling through back channels.

### Other Notable Criminal Organisations

- **The Zann Consortium:** Led by Tyber Zann (former protege of Jabba). Militaristic criminal empire built through rapid expansion and raiding Imperial caches. Growing in power; Black Sun has made cooperative overtures though Xizor likely plans betrayal.
- **The Tenloss Syndicate:** Straddles the line between criminal syndicate and legitimate corporate entity (Tenloss Corporation front). Produces illicit weapons, armour, and even starships (the Hornet Interceptor). Close ties with Black Sun. Professional and business-focused — writes off minor losses, but brings impressive force against serious threats.
- **The Mandalorians:** Not a criminal organisation per se, but their clans produce many of the galaxy's most dangerous bounty hunters, mercenaries, and warriors. Known for their unmistakable armour with T-shaped visor. Culture has shifted from militarism to peace, though some remain true to the warrior legacy.

### The Bounty Hunters Guild

The vast majority of professional bounty hunting operates through the massive Bounty Hunters Guild — a collection of houses and syndicates:

- **House Benelex:** Kidnapping retrievals; tied to the Empire and Drearian Defence Conglomerate.
- **House Neuvalis:** Exclusively Imperial/corporate contracts; high-credit bounties only; guarantees fulfilment within a year or returns double the fee.
- **House Paramexor:** Contracts for hunting murderers; elite hunters known for never taking targets alive.
- **House Renliss:** Female-only "huntresses"; reputed ties to Imperial Intelligence; only takes bounties against male acquisitions.
- **House Salaktori:** Most prestigious and respected house; hires only the very best.
- **House Tresario:** Founded by retired Imperial Navy officers; specialises in dealing with pirates. Growing favour and lucrative Imperial contracts.
- **The Mantis Syndicate:** More mercenary army than bounty hunting guild; takes on large groups like swoop gangs and criminal guilds.
- **The GenoHaradan:** Ancient secret guild evolved from Xim the Despot's secret police. Elite assassination-focused guild that manipulates galactic events. May still exist during the Imperial era.
- **The Ragnar Syndicate:** Breaks most Guild rules; extensive freelance work including mercenary activities, counter-terrorism, assassination, sabotage.
- **Skine Bounty Hunter College:** Network of the very best in catching the uncatchable. Avoids Imperial contracts; prefers corporate and individual clients.
- **The Slaver Syndicate:** Collects bounties on anyone who can be sold into slavery. Low-paying, high-volume work that other groups disdain.

## Imperial Law

### Principles of Imperial Law

The Imperial Charter (based on the Galactic Constitution of the Republic) places supreme executive power in Palpatine's hands — head of state, head of government, commander-in-chief, and final authority on all economic matters. The Senate was dissolved in 0 BBY.

**Core tenets (from the Declaration of a New Order):**
- The Jedi are dangerous elitists who must be exterminated.
- Security and stability are primary in the wake of the Separatist crisis.
- A new constitution requires a ruling body serving the majority, with a sovereign ruler who remains in power for life.
- All beings ruled by one law, one language.
- A strong military protects citizens' rights and security.
- All enemies of the Empire will be crushed for the greater good.

**Personal freedom:** Citizens are free to travel, eat, and do as they wish — provided it does not hinder the Empire's goals. Speaking out or organising against Imperial policies is illegal. Non-humans are treated as second-class citizens under the policy of **Humanocentrism** (Human High Culture).

### Criminal Justice

- The philosophy of **"Imperial Infallibility"** is being incorporated into the legal code — the ISB is working to install more pliable legal minds.
- The **Procurator of Justice** sits atop the criminal justice system. A highly protected, anonymous post reporting only to the Emperor; can be overruled only by the Emperor himself (unlikely).

**IOCI (Imperial Office of Criminal Investigations):**
- The Empire's central law enforcement agency under the Justice group of COMPNOR.
- Investigates multi-system criminal syndicates, nefarious organisations, and individuals who have committed crimes on multiple planets.
- Works with Imperial Intelligence, Military branches, ISB, and local agencies.
- The ISB focuses on political threats; the IOCI focuses on criminal law.

**Sector Rangers:**
- Independent law enforcement officers with centuries of tradition.
- Apolitical; the only real source of law and order in distant regions.
- Operate in pairs or small teams, commandeering local police or militia as needed.
- Remain mainly independent, though COMPNOR keeps close watch.

**Special Enforcement Officers (SEOs):**
- Elite agents recruited from the Sector Rangers.
- Broad authority to deal with the most dangerous criminals.
- Work undercover in highly dangerous circumstances.
- Overlook minor offences in pursuit of greater threats — popular with citizens, disliked by ISB and COMPNOR loyalists.
- Can recruit and temporarily deputise civilians, including fringers.

### Imperial Penal References (ImpPeRe)

Five classes of criminal offence:

| Class | Severity | Examples | Typical Penalties |
|-------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Five ("Quint")** | Minor | Local import/export violations, missing emergency equipment | Fines up to 1,000 credits; Alliance connections escalate to imprisonment |
| **Four ("Quad")** | Low | Common spice transport, expired licences, low-grade contraband without permits | Fines up to 5,000 credits |
| **Three ("Trip")** | Moderate | Bribery of Imperial officials, high-end contraband, regulated technology, upper-level narcotics | Ship impounded, goods confiscated, fines up to 10,000 credits; prison possible |
| **Two ("Deuce")** | Serious | Restricted technology, armaments, supplies of war; illegal ship armaments | Immediate arrest, ship confiscated, 10,000+ credit fine, 5-30 years prison; Rebellion/piracy connections bring harsher results |
| **One ("Ace")** | Capital | Overt piracy, firing on spacers/Imperial assets, restricted technology of highest order, substantiated Alliance connection, conspiracy against the Empire | Life in a penal colony, Kessel spice mines, or execution |

### Doing Business in the Empire

- Ship ownership and operation are regulated; vessels must be registered.
- **Imperial Revenue Codes** regulate transport and interstellar trade with extensive taxes and tariffs. The burden of proof falls on the citizen.
- Failure to maintain proper documentation risks confiscation, impounding, and labour camps.

### Bureau of Ships and Services (BoSS)

- Possibly the most independently powerful civil agency in the Empire (over 18,000 years old).
- Maintains records on every ship and captain — registrations, certifications, transponder codes, weapons, shields, and overhaul history.
- Captains must maintain a BoSS datapad at all times.
- Even the Empire does not dare interfere directly with BoSS.
- Coordinates with the **Imperial Space Ministry**, which publishes the Spacer's Information Manual containing the ImpPeRe.

### Sector, Zone, and Planetary Law

- Laws vary enormously by sector and planet.
- Proximity to Core Worlds generally means stricter Imperial enforcement.
- Outer Rim sectors tend to have smaller, less capable Imperial presence — a savvy traveller can avoid officials and bribe their way out of trouble.
- Some sectors have unique laws based on stellar phenomena, cultural traditions, or economic concerns (e.g., asteroid belt mining regulations).
- The **Corporate Sector Authority (CSA)** has its own legal system entirely, replacing Imperial oversight. Enforced by the feared **Security Police ("Espos")** with their own Picket Fleet.

### The Black Market

- Not a single entity but myriad networks and dealers scattered across civilised worlds.
- Vague, loosely connected networks dealing in regulated or illegal goods and services.
- Success depends on contacts — more contacts means better redundancy against instability.
- Key skills: street instincts, smooth talking, mercantile sense, and having someone dangerous for backup.
- Dealing with the Alliance through black market channels is the worst offence in Imperial eyes but can be lucrative — the Rebellion has the greatest need for smuggled goods but limited funds, often paying in barter (ship overhauls, military hardware, record-cleaning).

## Obligations to Criminal Organisations

Fringers on the Edge of the Empire frequently find themselves bound to criminal organisations through Obligation — the game's core mechanic for debts, duties, and entanglements. Key sources of criminal Obligation include:

- **Hutt Cartels:** Working in the Outer Rim almost inevitably leads to Hutt employment. Once a Hutt identifies a useful operative, they are called in and evaluated. Refusal is not advisable. Obligations to Hutts are personal and vindictive — crossing a Hutt results in relentless, disproportionate retribution.
- **Black Sun:** Involvement gets hooks deep into everyone who becomes part of it, with virtually no way out. Obligations may arise through transport work, ship repairs, being caught between rival Vigos, or having debts assumed by the organisation.
- **Bounty Hunters Guild:** Various houses may hold obligations over fringers — from House Tresario's Imperial connections to the Slaver Syndicate's willingness to collect bounties on anyone.
- **The Zann Consortium / Tenloss Syndicate:** Professional criminal enterprises that may draw fringers in through legitimate-seeming corporate dealings before revealing their true nature.
- **Pirates:** Involvement with pirate bands can create obligations through shared loot, protection arrangements, or being pressed into service.
- **Black Market Networks:** Obligations arise through debts to contacts, failed transactions, or being compromised by authorities — forcing the fringer deeper into illegal networks for protection.
