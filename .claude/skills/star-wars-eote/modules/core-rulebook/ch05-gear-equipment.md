# Chapter V: Gear and Equipment - Condensed Reference

> *"Hokey religions and ancient weapons are no match for a good blaster at your side, kid."* -- Han Solo

## Galactic Economics

### Currency

- The universal currency is **credits**, backed by the InterGalactic Banking Clan (IGBC).
- Credits can be carried as physical bills/coins or tracked via digital **cred sticks** (also called "chips").
- Cred sticks are encoded with values up to the currency limit of the issuing planet.
- Physical currency is favoured by criminals (no electronic trail) and those on distant worlds who distrust institutions.

### Keeping the Crew Hungry

- Limit cash rewards; award 10,000 credits or fewer per job.
- Award commodities, Obligation reduction, or non-cash items instead of large sums.
- **Carousing:** Spending credits at a spaceport requires a Charm check; costs 50-100 credits. Advantage/Triumph yield useful information; Despair doubles costs and causes trouble.
- **Obligation:** Credits can be spent to temporarily offset Obligation triggers.
- **Ship Maintenance:** Battle damage and upkeep drain funds (see p.243).

### Selling and Trading

- **Selling legal items:** Base price is 25% of listed cost. Successful Negotiation check increases to 50%; with 3 Advantage or more, 75%.
- **Selling illegal items:** Same base (25%) with a Streetwise check instead. 50% with Advantage, 75% with 3+ Advantage.
- **Buying illegal items:** Requires a Streetwise check at difficulty based on rarity. 3+ Threat or Despair means trouble (robbery, police, etc.).

## The Black Market

- Not all goods are legal everywhere. Finding illegal goods requires a **Streetwise check** at the item's rarity difficulty, modified by local law enforcement.
- Selling illegal goods also requires a Streetwise check at the same difficulty.
- Items marked with **(R)** in their price are restricted and always count as black market items.
- Black market items cost far more than list price depending on scarcity; the GM determines the markup.
- If 3+ Threat or Despair are rolled when buying/selling illegally, something goes wrong (robbery, arrest, etc.).

## Encumbrance

An abstract measure of weight, bulk, and awkwardness.

### Encumbrance Values

| Item Size | Typical Encumbrance |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Tiny (datapads, pistols, knives) | 1-2 |
| Medium (blaster rifles, grenades, grappling hook) | 3-4 |
| Large/Heavy (cargo crates, repeating blasters, heavy toolbox) | 5-6 |
| 10 loosely carried incidental items | 1 |
| 20 efficiently stored incidental items | 1 |

### Encumbrance Threshold

- **Threshold = 5 + Brawn** (for living beings).
- Exceeding threshold: suffer 1 Setback die to all Agility and Brawn rolls per point over.
- At Brawn or more points over threshold: lose free manoeuvre each turn; each manoeuvre costs 2 strain.
- Armour encumbrance is reduced by 3 when worn.

### Lifting Excessive Encumbrance

- Lifting an object above threshold requires an **Athletics check**.
- Difficulty is Simple if the object's encumbrance is at or below the character's threshold.
- Add 1 difficulty per point above threshold, up to a maximum additional encumbrance of 4 (Daunting).
- Additional characters add their raw Brawn to the total threshold.

### Concealing Gear

- Items with encumbrance 1 or less can be hidden without a roll unless physically searched.
- If searched: **Opposed Perception vs Stealth check**, adding Setback for each encumbrance point over 1.

## Weapon Characteristics

- **Skill Used:** The combat skill for attack rolls.
- **Base Damage (Dam):** Minimum damage on a hit. Each net Success adds +1.
- **Critical Rating (Crit):** Number of Advantage needed to trigger a Critical Injury (on a successful hit exceeding soak).
- **Range:** Engaged, Short, Medium, Long, Extreme.
- **Encumbrance (Encum):** Weight/bulk value.
- **Hard Points (HP):** Attachment slots for customisation.
- **Price:** Open market cost. (R) = restricted/black market.
- **Rarity:** Difficulty to locate (before modifiers).
- **Special:** Weapon qualities.
- **Brawl/Melee damage:** "+X" means add Brawn to the listed number. A flat number (e.g. 10) means do not add Brawn.

## Item Qualities

Some equipment features special qualities that add variety and depth to the vast array of armaments in the Star Wars universe. Some special qualities are inherent to certain items, while others are general qualities that can be applied by the Game Master to specifically tailor an item to the story.

Special qualities are generally either passive or active. Passive qualities are always on and require no activation on the part of the user. Active qualities must be triggered by the user, often by spending one or more Advantage to activate the effect.

Item qualities often have a number associated with them. This is their rating. Ratings affect qualities in different ways, depending on the quality in question.

Active qualities require 2 Advantage to activate unless otherwise stated in their description. Weapon qualities can only trigger on a successful attack, unless specified otherwise.

### Passive Qualities (Always Active)

| Quality | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Accurate X** | Add X Boost dice to attack pools |
| **Breach X** | Ignore X points of armour (also ignores 10 soak per rank) |
| **Cortosis** | Weapons: immune to Sunder. Armour: soak immune to Pierce and Breach |
| **Cumbersome X** | Requires Brawn >= X to wield properly; each point deficient adds 1 difficulty |
| **Deflection X** | Increases wearer's ranged defence by X |
| **Defensive X** | Increases wielder's melee defence by X |
| **Inaccurate X** | Add X Setback dice to attack pools |
| **Inferior** | Generates automatic Threat; damage -1, armour encumbrance +1 and defence -1, soak -1 (min 0) |
| **Ion** | Damage dealt as system strain (vehicles) or strain (droids). Reduced by armour/soak |
| **Limited Ammo X** | May fire X times before reloading (manoeuvre). Each shot uses 1 ammo. Includes grenades |
| **Pierce X** | Ignore X points of target's soak |
| **Prepare X** | Requires X preparation manoeuvres before firing |
| **Slow-Firing X** | Must wait X rounds between shots |
| **Stun Damage** | Deals strain instead of wounds (still reduced by soak). Stun setting: range becomes Short only |
| **Superior** | Generates automatic Advantage; damage +1 (weapons) or soak +1 and encumbrance -1 (armour) |
| **Tractor X** | Target cannot move unless successful Piloting check at difficulty based on rating |
| **Vicious X** | Add +(X * 10) to Critical Injury rolls |

#### Accurate (Passive)

Accurate weapons are easier to aim or wield, whether through design or technology. For each level of this trait, the attacker adds one Boost die to his attack dice pools while using this weapon.

#### Breach (Passive)

Weapons with Breach burn through the toughest armour, and are often heavy weapons or starship weapons. Breach weapons ignore one point of armour for every rating of Breach (meaning they also ignore 10 points of soak for every rating of Breach).

#### Cortosis (Passive)

Cortosis is an ore found primarily in the Outer Rim. It is extremely rare and valuable, and was used primarily during the Clone Wars against the Jedi knights. There are two varieties of Cortosis ore. The rarest versions can actually short out a lightsaber's blade, causing it to fail temporarily. The far more common Cortosis ore is still a miraculous substance, because when moulded into armour, it forms an interlocking molecular bond that is extremely resistant to energy weapons.

Weapons with the Cortosis quality are immune to the Sunder quality. Armour with the Cortosis quality makes the wearer's soak immune to the Pierce and Breach qualities.

#### Cumbersome (Passive)

A Cumbersome weapon is large, unwieldy, awkward, or heavy. To wield a Cumbersome weapon properly, the character needs a Brawn characteristic equal to or greater than the weapon's Cumbersome rating. For each point of Brawn the character is deficient, he must increase the difficulty of all checks made while using the weapon by one.

#### Defensive (Passive)

Defensive weapons are particularly good at fending off incoming melee attacks. A character wielding a weapon with the Defensive quality increases his melee defence by the weapon's Defensive rating.

#### Deflection (Passive)

An item with the Deflection quality increases the wearer's ranged defence equal to its Deflection rating.

#### Inaccurate (Passive)

Inaccurate weapons are weapons of a shoddy or inferior construction which means they are less likely to be accurate or precise. Alternatively, the weapon's nature may inhibit accuracy. Inaccurate weapons add Setback dice to the attacker's dice pool equal to their Inaccurate rating.

#### Inferior (Passive)

An Inferior item is a lackluster example of its kind, representing shoddy and poor craftsmanship. An Inferior weapon generates automatic Threat on all checks related to its use, and has its base damage decreased by one. Inferior armour has its encumbrance increased by one and its defence decreased by one. If it does not have defence, decrease its soak value by one to a minimum of zero.

#### Ion (Passive)

Ion weapons are designed to affect electrical systems as opposed to dealing raw damage. They are fitted to ships to knock out opponents' shields, sensors, and engines. They are shorter range than laser weapons, and deal larger amounts of damage, but their damage is dealt to the target as system strain (usually on vehicles). It is still reduced by armour and soak. Droids are affected by ion weapons, taking damage to their strain threshold.

#### Limited Ammo (Passive)

Some weapons fire particularly large or complex projectiles that cost significant amounts of credits, or are themselves a complete weapon that once launched is expended. A weapon with the Limited Ammo quality may be used to make a number of attacks equal to its Limited Ammo rating before it must be reloaded with a manoeuvre. In addition, each shot expends one of a limited number of rounds of ammo, which must be purchased or otherwise obtained before the weapon can be used. This also applies to grenades and other "one use" weapons which have the Limited Ammo 1 quality (here, the user is not "reloading" the grenade, but drawing another to use — but mechanically they are equivalent).

#### Pierce (Passive)

An attack made with this weapon ignores one point of soak for each rank of Pierce. If the weapon has more ranks of Pierce than the target's total soak, it completely ignores the target's soak. For example, Pierce 3 against a soak of 2 ignores 2 points of soak, but the extra "point" of Pierce has no further effect.

#### Prepare (Passive)

Weapons with this quality require time to set up before being used. The user must perform a number of preparation manoeuvres equal to the weapon's Prepare rating before making attacks with that weapon.

#### Slow-Firing (Passive)

Slow-Firing weapons tend to be large, heavy, emplacement-mounted weapons like the turbolasers found on capital ships, or planetary defence ion cannons. While they deal incredible damage, they need time to recharge or cool down between shots. A weapon's Slow-Firing rating dictates how quickly the weapon can be fired after an attack. For example, a heavy turbolaser with Slow-Firing 2 must wait two rounds after being fired before it can be fired again.

#### Stun Damage (Passive)

Some weapons deal Stun damage instead of regular damage. In this case, the weapon deals damage as strain instead of wounds. This damage is still reduced by a target's soak.

A variant of this is a Stun setting. As an incidental, the wielder can choose to switch the setting of his weapon to "Stun." In this case, it does stun damage as described above. When weapons with a stun setting are used to deal stun damage, their range changes to short and cannot be increased.

#### Superior (Passive)

A Superior item is a sterling example of its kind, representing masterful craftsmanship. A Superior weapon generates automatic Advantage on all checks related to its use, and has its base damage increased by one. Superior armour has its encumbrance reduced by one and its soak value increased by one.

#### Tractor (Passive)

Instead of firing searing beams of laser fire or crackling ion discharges, this weapon fires relatively harmless electromagnetic beams that ensnare ships and hold them fast in space. Tractor beams, like all weapons, are fired at their target using the appropriate skill check (generally Gunnery) with all suitable modifiers. Once the weapon hits its target, the target may not move unless its pilot makes a successful Piloting check with a difficulty based on the tractor beam's rating. The tractor beam's rating is always noted in parentheses in the weapon's stats next to the quality.

#### Vicious (Passive)

When this weapon scores a critical injury or hit, the character adds ten times the Vicious rating to the critical roll. With Vicious 3, for example, the victim adds +30 to his critical hit result.

### Active Qualities (Require Advantage/Triumph to Activate)

Unless stated otherwise, active qualities cost 2 Advantage to activate.

| Quality | Activation | Effect |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| **Auto-fire** | 2 Advantage | Increases attack difficulty by 1. Each activation = additional hit (base damage + uncancelled Success). May hit multiple targets in range. Also activates Crits per hit |
| **Blast X** | 2 Advantage (or 3 Advantage on miss) | On hit: each engaged character suffers X wounds (plus normal damage to target). On miss (3 Adv): original target and all engaged suffer X wounds |
| **Burn X** | 2 Advantage | Target suffers base damage for X rounds. Extinguish: Average Coordination (hard surface) or Easy Coordination (soft ground). Water stops immediately |
| **Concussive X** | 2 Advantage | Target is staggered for X rounds (cannot perform actions) |
| **Disorient X** | 2 Advantage | Target is disoriented for X rounds (add Setback to all skill checks) |
| **Ensnare X** | 2 Advantage | Target is immobilised for X rounds (cannot perform manoeuvres). Break free: Hard Athletics check |
| **Guided X** | 3 Advantage | On miss: make attack check at end of round (silhouette 0 vs target silhouette, Ability dice = Guided rating). Success = hit |
| **Knockdown** | Advantage + 1 Advantage per silhouette above 1 | Target is knocked prone |
| **Linked X** | 2 Advantage per additional hit | Successful attack = 1 hit. May spend 2 Adv up to X times for additional hits (each = base damage + Success). Same target only |
| **Stun X** | 2 Advantage | Target suffers X strain |
| **Sunder** | 1 Advantage | Damage one item wielded/worn by target one step (Undamaged > Minor > Moderate > Major > Destroyed). May activate multiple times per attack |

#### Auto-fire (Active)

A weapon with Auto-fire can be set to shoot in rapid succession and potentially spray an area with bolts, flechettes, slugs, or other types of projectiles. The advantage in using Auto-fire is that it has the chance to hit multiple targets or hit a single target multiple times.

Attacking with a weapon on Auto-fire is generally less accurate and the attacker must increase the difficulty of the attack check by one. The user may choose to not use the Auto-fire quality on a weapon; in this case, he cannot trigger the quality but also does not suffer the aforementioned penalty.

If the attack hits, the attacker can trigger Auto-fire by spending 2 Advantage. Auto-fire can be triggered multiple times. Each time the attacker triggers Auto-fire, it deals an additional hit to the target. Each of these counts as an additional hit from that weapon, and each hit deals base damage plus the number of uncancelled Success on the check.

These additional hits can be allocated to the target, or to other targets within range of the weapon. If the attacker wishes to hit multiple targets, he must decide to do so before making the check. Furthermore, if he wishes to hit multiple targets, his initial target must always be the target with the highest difficulty and highest defence (if this is two separate targets, the GM chooses which target is his initial target). The initial hit must always be against the initial target; subsequent hits generated can be allocated to any of the other designated targets.

Auto-fire weapons can also activate one Critical Injury for each hit generated on the attack per the normal cost; the Critical must target the target of the specific hit.

#### Blast (Active)

The weapon has a large spread, an explosive blast, or similar area of effect, like the detonation of a grenade or a warhead fired from a missile launcher. If the attack is successful and Blast activates, each character (friend or foe) engaged with the original target suffers wounds equal to the weapon's Blast rating (plus an additional wound per Success as usual).

In a relatively small and enclosed area, the Game Master might decide that everyone in the room suffers damage.

If the Blast quality does not activate, the ordnance still detonates, but bad luck or poor aim on the part of the firer (or quick reactions on the part of the victims) means the explosion may not catch anyone else in its radius. However, the user may also trigger Blast if the attack misses, by spending 3 Advantage. In this case, the original target and every target engaged with the original target suffers damage equal to the Blast rating of the weapon.

#### Burn (Active)

Weapons with Burn inflict damage over time. If the attack is successful, the target continues to suffer the weapon's base damage for a number of rounds equal to the weapon's Burn rating. Damage is applied at the start of each of the target's actions.

A victim might be able to stop the damage by rolling around on the ground and making an Agility check as an action. This is an Average Coordination check on hard surfaces such as the hall of a spaceship, or an Easy Coordination check on grass or soft ground. Jumping into a body of water stops the damage immediately. Both situations assume the flame is from actual combustion rather than a chemical reaction. With the latter, there is usually little the victim can do.

#### Concussive (Active)

The weapon's attack can leave the target shell-shocked from mighty blows or punishing shockwaves, unable to perform any but the most basic actions. The target is staggered (see page 218) for a number of rounds equal to the weapon's Concussive rating. A staggered target cannot perform actions.

#### Disorient (Active)

A weapon with Disorient can daze an opponent. When Disorient is triggered, the target is disoriented (see page 218) for a number of rounds equal to the weapon's Disorient rating. A disoriented target adds one Setback die to all skill checks he performs.

#### Ensnare (Active)

A weapon with Ensnare binds a foe and restricts his movements. When Ensnare is triggered, the target is immobilised (see page 218) for a number of rounds equal to the weapon's Ensnare rating. An ensnared target may attempt a Hard Athletics check as his action on his turn to break free from the effect. An immobilised target cannot perform manoeuvres.

#### Guided (Active)

Certain types of projectiles may benefit from course alterations after being fired — such as guided missiles. If a character misses while firing a Guided weapon and if Guided activates, he may make an attack check at the end of the round. The difficulty of the check is calculated by comparing the weapon's silhouette of 0 to the silhouette of the target (see page 235), and the check's Ability dice equal the weapon's Guided rating. If the test is successful, the weapon strikes the target and damage is dealt normally.

Guided requires 3 Advantage to activate, unless otherwise specified in the weapon's description. Remember, the Guided effect can activate on its subsequent attacks, representing the projectile continuing to track the target.

"Spoofing" is a countermeasure designed to work against the particular projectile — such as flares designed to draw off an infrared missile. Spoofing directly increases the defence of the target against attacks with the Guided quality.

#### Knockdown (Active)

When Knockdown triggers, the target is knocked prone. Unless specified otherwise, Knockdown requires 2 Advantage to trigger, plus one additional Advantage per silhouette beyond one.

#### Linked (Active)

Some weapons, like the laser cannons fitted to the Z-95 Headhunter, are designed to fire together at the same target. This increases the possibility of a hit as well as the damage dealt. When firing a linked weapon, on a successful attack, the weapon deals one hit. The wielder may spend 2 Advantage to gain an additional hit, and may do so a number of times equal to the weapon's Linked rating. Additional hits from linked weapons may only be applied against the original target. Each hit deals the weapon's base damage plus total Success scored on the check.

#### Stun (Active)

A weapon with Stun causes strain to the target. When the Stun quality is activated, it inflicts strain equal to the weapon's Stun rating.

#### Sunder (Active)

When activated, the attacker chooses one item openly wielded by the target (such as a weapon, shield, or item on a belt). That item is damaged one step: to Minor if undamaged, from Minor to Moderate, or from Moderate to Major. If a weapon already suffering Major damage is the target of a successful Sunder, it is destroyed.

Sunder requires 1 Advantage to activate. If activated multiple times in the same attack, each can be applied against the same weapon, potentially taking a weapon from undamaged to destroyed in a single attack.

## Weapon Maintenance and Repairs

### Table 5-4: Repairing Gear

| Repair Level | Difficulty | Penalty for Use | Cost |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|------|
| Minor | D | +1 Setback die | 25% of base cost |
| Moderate | DD | +1 Difficulty die | 50% of base cost |
| Major | DDD | Unusable | 100% of base cost |

- Repairs take 1-2 hours per difficulty level.
- Reduce cost by 10% per Advantage on the Repair roll.
- Lacking proper tools: +1 difficulty. Less time: +1 difficulty. Both cumulative.
- Low-tech weapons: repaired with Mechanics or the weapon's combat skill.
- High-tech weapons: repaired with Computers or the weapon's skill (Mechanics always works).

## Customisation and Modifications

### Attachments Overview

- Attachments slot into an item's **Hard Points**.
- Each attachment has **Base Modifiers** (automatic bonuses) and optional **Modification Options** (mods).
- Only certain attachments fit certain weapon/armour types. Use common sense and follow descriptions.
- Under-barrel attachments: rifles and slugthrower rifles only. One under-barrel per weapon.

### Installing Mods

1. Select an uninstalled mod from the attachment's modification options.
2. Spend **100 credits** on components and supplies.
3. Requires a toolkit and workbench.
4. Make a **Hard (DDD) Mechanics check**.
5. **Success:** Mod is installed and functional.
6. **Failure:** Mod is not installed; may not attempt that mod again.
7. **Failure + Despair:** Mod is not installed AND the attachment is rendered useless.
8. Each additional mod beyond the first on the same attachment increases the Mechanics difficulty by 1.
9. Each additional mod costs +100 credits beyond the base cost.

### Types of Mods

| Mod Type | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Damage** | Increases weapon damage |
| **Weapon Quality** | Adds or increases a weapon quality (e.g. "Pierce +1") |
| **Innate Talent** | Grants a talent only when wielding/using the modded gear |
| **Skill/Characteristic** | Grants +1 rank in a skill or characteristic when using the item |
| **Additional** | Unique effects described in the specific entry |
