# Under a Black Sun -- EotE Adventure

> Adventure module for the star-wars-eote skill. A Free RPG Day adventure for Star Wars: Edge of the Empire, set in the criminal underworld of Coruscant. The PCs must track down a double-crossing bounty hunter named Kaa'to Leeachos on behalf of the Pyke Syndicate.

---

## Setting

**Planet:** Coruscant (Imperial Centre)
- **System:** Coruscant system, Corusca Sector, Core Worlds
- **Orbital Metrics:** 368 days per year / 24 hours per day
- **Government:** Galactic Empire
- **Population:** ~1 trillion (humans 68%, other 32%)
- **Languages:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Ecumenopolis (planet-wide city)
- **Major Cities:** Imperial Centre/Coruscant
- **Areas of Interest:** Senate District, Imperial Palace, ruined Jedi Temple, Underworld, Manarai Mountains, Invisec
- **Major Exports:** Culture, luxury items, hyperdrive systems, governance
- **Major Imports:** Foodstuffs, raw materials, water
- **Trade Routes:** Martial Cross, Shawken Spur, Koros Trunk Line, Metellos Trade Route, Perlemian Trade Route, Corellian Run
- **Special Conditions:** Internally regulated environments; periodic superstorms due to climate instability

**Era:** During the Rebellion, after the Battle of Yavin but before the Battle of Hoth.

**Atmosphere:** The adventure takes place in the lower levels of Coruscant -- a crime-ridden underworld of smugglers, death stick dealers, bounty hunters, and petty thieves. Even under Imperial control, the sheer scale of Coruscant makes it impossible to police entirely. Criminals range from feral wretches in the lowermost levels to powerful rogues overseeing galaxy-spanning syndicates.

**Native Vermin:** Hawkbats, granite slugs, duracrete worms, and other creatures adapted to the artificial environment.

## The Situation

**Opening Crawl:**

> *It is a dark time for the galaxy. The city of Coruscant, once the glittering pearl of the Republic, now languishes under the shadow of the Galactic Empire. A bustling black market flourishes in the underworld, controlled by the notorious crime syndicate BLACK SUN.*
>
> *Hired to infiltrate a Black Sun outpost and retrieve vital data on a bounty hunter who betrayed the PYKE crime family, a small group of enterprising individuals works rapidly to access the heavily encrypted Black Sun network as things take a turn for the worse...*

**Background:** The PCs were hired by the Pyke Syndicate (a preeminent spice-dealing cartel based on Oba Diah in the Outer Rim) to infiltrate a Black Sun facility and steal data. A bounty hunter working for the Pykes betrayed them -- he was sent to dispatch a rival smuggler and intercept a large spice shipment, but instead delivered the shipment to Black Sun for a bigger bounty. This cost the Pykes hundreds of thousands of credits and dried up their jobs. The stolen file should reveal the hunter's real name so the PCs can bring him to justice.

**The Target:** The bounty hunter is **Kaa'to Leeachos**, a Kadas'sa'Nikto formerly bound in servitude to the Hutts. Since gaining his freedom, Kaa'to has built a reputation through high-risk, unauthorised bounties and is considered a formidable operative. He is holed up in the abandoned section of a kelerium processing plant in Sector 943 on Coruscant.

**The Employers:** The Pyke Syndicate. Their fortunes have dwindled under the Empire. After the Shadow Collective fell apart, the Pykes branched out by incorporating smaller groups into their cartel. They operate from Oba Diah, maintaining operations throughout the Core Worlds. Contact is through **Cho'pa'ailor ("Chopper")**, a Besalisk who runs a speeder repair shop in Quadrant L-42 and is a Pyke confidant.

**Reward:** 1,000 credits per PC upon successful completion. The Pykes also reduce each PC's Obligation by 5.

**The Adventure Structure:**
1. **Episode I: No Easy Jobs** -- Escape from the Black Sun facility and meet the Pyke contact.
2. **Episode II: City of Spires and Strife** -- Investigate three locations in the Coruscant underworld to find Kaa'to.
3. **Episode III: Endgame** -- Confront Kaa'to at the NovaCORE kelerium processing plant.

**Destiny Points:** Generate a pool by adding one light and one dark side Destiny Point per Player Character.

## Encounter Flow

### Episode I: No Easy Jobs

#### Encounter 1: Hot Pursuit

**Situation:** The adventure begins in medias res. The PCs have tripped an alarm inside the Black Sun facility whilst stealing an encrypted data file. They must flee to a landing platform where an automated droid-driven airspeeder awaits. Four Black Sun thugs pursue on swoop bikes.

**Read Aloud:**
> *Klaxons blare overhead as you flee from the facility with the stolen data. You hear Black Sun agents scrambling to investigate on the floors above. You exit the skyscraper onto a bustling landing platform where a droid-driven airspeeder sits nearby, awaiting a fare. A blaster bolt whizzes past your heads, and the speeder seems like the best (and only) option for escape! As you jump inside, the droid quickly zooms away into the streams of traffic. Unfortunately, the Black Sun thugs are not that easy to lose, and within seconds they are in hot pursuit on swoop bikes of their own. More blaster fire screams past your speeder, when suddenly a lucky shot takes out the piloting droid with a bang!*

**Options & Skill Checks:**
- **Regain control of speeder:** Average Piloting (Planetary) check. If failed within two rounds, the speeder crashes -- each PC suffers 2 strain and is disoriented (add a Setback die to all skill checks) until end of next turn.
- **Find escape routes:** Average Perception check. Success reveals open air vent tunnels, sewers, alleyways between buildings, and other paths of escape.
- **Hide from pursuers:** Easy or Average Stealth check (depending on how busy or dark the escape route is). Vanishing into Coruscant's underlevels may trigger an optional encounter with a duracrete slug.
- **Note:** Whilst on the busy thoroughfare, all characters suffer a Setback die from traffic, speed, and movement of the speeder.
- Stray blaster fire injuring innocents or killing bystanders attracts attention from local police droids or Coruscant Underworld Police.

**Conclusion:** Once the PCs escape, their comlink lights up with an encrypted transmission from the Pykes, directing them to bring the data chip to Chopper's garage in Quadrant L-42. A patched-together speeder (piloted by a spare pre-generated character or an Ugnaught) arrives to collect them.

---

#### Encounter 2: Chop Talk

**Situation:** The PCs arrive at Chopper's speeder repair shop to deliver the stolen data and receive their mission briefing.

**Read Aloud:**
> *Sparks fly from numerous locations throughout the large, cluttered repair shop. Everywhere you look, piles of speeder parts, half-built droids, and stranger contraptions are stacked to the ceiling. Several groups of Ugnaughts are welding and working away on a disassembled airspeeder, paying you no heed.*
>
> *A booming voice rings out from behind one of the junk piles: "My friends, it is so good to see you again!" A large, green-mottled Besalisk emerges, his four arms opening to hug the nearest member of the party.*

**Key Information from Chopper:**
- The bounty hunter was operating under an alias.
- He betrayed the Pykes by delivering a stolen spice shipment to Black Sun for a bigger bounty.
- This cost the Pykes hundreds of thousands of credits.
- The Pykes want him brought in dead or alive (preferably captured, but dead is acceptable).

**Pyke Message (Read Aloud):**
> *"Once we have this bounty hunter's identity secured, the Pyke family wants you to bring him down for his betrayal. He cost the bosses a fortune by stealing a shipment of their spice and bringing it to Black Sun instead. Unsurprisingly, the Pykes aren't happy about it, and they'll pay well if you can take care of this problem for them. They may even forget about a couple of favours you owe them, you know?"*

**Decryption Skill Check:**
- Hard Computers check with a Setback die.
- If Jovel (pre-gen slicer) assists, any Advantage generated grants bonus information.

**Bonus Information (from Advantages on decryption):**
- 1 Advantage: Kaa'to delivered only part of the stolen spice shipment. One message suspects the Nikto is holding out on the rest.
- 2 Advantage: The file lists pickups from Zelcomm Tower referencing the 4th-floor terrace and a side street underground entrance.
- 3+ Advantage or Triumph: A seemingly misplaced list of names, ranks, and associated figures -- possibly Imperial security forces Black Sun paid off.

**Chopper reveals:**
- The bounty hunter is **Kaa'to Leeachos**, a Nikto formerly bound to the Hutts.
- He frequents or has been seen at: **The Umbra Club**, **The Spyder**, and **Zelcomm Tower**.
- PCs should consult contacts before confronting him.
- If PCs aren't using pre-gens, they can attempt an Average Charm or Streetwise check to learn the location names.
- Chopper is willing to buy speeders or swoop bikes for 250-500 credits depending on condition.
- The Pykes will pay 1,000 credits per PC upon completion.

**Payment Questions:**
- Chopper cannot give an advance -- it isn't his money.
- He offers to buy any speeders or swoop bikes for 250-500 credits depending on condition.

### Interlude: Contacts and Connections

**Situation:** Before visiting the three locations, the PCs may rest, recuperate from wounds, and consult their underworld contacts.

**Gathering Information:** Average Charm or Streetwise check to learn about each location:
- **The Umbra Club:** A seedy den of death stick dealers in Sector 1459, catering to criminals. Speng, a male human death stick dealer, might have heard about a certain bounty hunter trying to offload spice.
- **The Spyder:** A sabacc parlour frequented by many gamblers in Black Sun's employ, known to all criminals of Coruscant. Certain patrons wager information at their tables.
- **Zelcomm Tower:** Home of Zelcomm Industries, a shipping company specialising in cargo transportation between the Core Worlds and the Outer Rim. Known front for weapons smuggling. Coruum Sa'dia (Ishi Tib) runs the operation.

---

### Episode II: City of Spires and Strife

The three locations can be visited in any order. Actions at one venue can drastically change what happens at the next. PCs should visit at least one or two locations (all three is a significant time investment).

---

#### Location 1: The Umbra Club

**Read Aloud:**
> *As soon as you enter the cantina you notice the smell: the combined odour of stale liquor, burning death sticks, and other rank substances washes over you. Inside, a collection of sparse tables, half-broken chairs, and tattered couches adorns the main room. A series of private booths line the right wall, each with a ragged curtain pulled shut. In the centre of the room, a large, brightly lit dance floor pulses with coloured lights in an almost hypnotic pattern. A handful of patrons relax at various tables and couches, all lost in their own reveries. Behind the bar, a gruff Gotal slings drinks to a group of humans clustered at one end.*

**Key NPC -- Omacala (Gotal Bartender):**
- Cares little about what goes on so long as he is paid for the drinks.
- Willing to share information for the right price.
- Average Negotiation check to reach a price.
- Success: shares knowledge about Black Sun and its minions for 30 credits.
- Failure: refuses to divulge anything for less than 50 credits.

**Omacala's Information:**
- *"What do you know about Black Sun?"* -- In addition to its stake in the spice trade, Black Sun is expanding into illicit arms, especially now that the Rebellion is chipping away at the Empire's supply. He refuses to discuss Black Sun further.
- *"What do you know about Kaa'to Leeachos?"* -- He has seen the Nikto bounty hunter recently, meeting with a skittish death stick dealer named Speng in a private booth.
- *"Who is Coruum Sa'dia?"* -- The Ishi Tib works at Zelcomm Tower as owner of a somewhat shady shipping company. Likely some smuggling on the side.

**Key NPC -- Speng (Human Death Stick Dealer):**
- If the PCs barge in on him, he is found mid-deal with a hollow-eyed human female handing over credits for a black pouch.
- Average Deception check to pose as prospective buyers.
- Failure: Speng realises he is in trouble and summons his guards via comlink.
- The PCs might bribe him with 200 credits for information, or attempt a Negotiation check to bargain him down.
- Any threats cause him to summon two Klatooinian hired guards from outside.

**Speng's Information (from successful approach or Coercion after his guards are dealt with):**

> *"Kaa'to? Yeah, I know Kaa'to. Comes around here sometimes to move merchandise he acquires on his jobs. He likes the sabacc over at the Spyder, and I hear he owes them a lot of money. That's why he always brings me valuable objects he finds on his jobs, since I like the finer things, you know?"*

**Additional Info (Average Coercion check, if PCs already know about gambling debts):**

> *"Fine! I know where he stays. He's been known to hole up somewhere around the kelerium processing plant in Sector 943. It's an out-of-the-way place that no one is supposed to know about. He stays there when he's on Coruscant so he can work without prying eyes on him."*

**Tracking Speng (if he flees):**
- Hard Perception checks or Average Survival checks, each with a Setback die, to track him through Sector 1459.
- Speng attempts to double back to the club.
- If caught, a Hard Coercion check (or if PCs have inflicted wounds) extracts the same information.

**Combat Notes:**
- If a firefight erupts, patrons scatter and head for the street.
- Omacala dives behind the bar; afterwards he demands the PCs pay for damages.

---

#### Location 2: The Spyder

**Conditional Modifiers:**
- **Visited after the Umbra Club:** Depending on what happened with Speng, patrons may be wary. If the Umbra Club visit ended in violence, add a Setback die to all skill checks whilst speaking with gamblers.
- **Visited after Zelcomm Tower:** The Falleen underboss catches wind of the Zelcomm raid and tips off the Coruscant Underworld Police; two officers try to arrest the PCs.

**Read Aloud:**
> *The sights and sounds of the Spyder are similar to those of many sabacc parlours, with a central pit of card tables surrounded by various electronic games. The establishment is crowded and noisy; the cheers that accompany winning hands and lucky rolls ring out across the room. Behind the bar, a two-headed Troig deftly passes out drinks using all four arms, chatting with down-on-their-luck gamblers or himself intermittently. A quick scan of the room shows that the Nikto bounty hunter is not here, so you must rely on the various patrons and employees for information.*

**Key NPC -- Pillaat & Nerro (Troig Bartender):**
- Have worked at the Spyder for many years.
- Quick to share gossip after a few credits pass their way.
- Average Charm check to get them to share what they know.
- Pillaat reveals: Kaa'to is very quick to anger and has injured customers over games. He avenges losses outside the establishment. He owes the Spyder's owner a great deal of money and has only recently begun paying it back.

**Key NPC -- Korsin Fenn (Bothan, Spyder Manager):**
- Male Bothan, clansman of Jovel's. Runs the Spyder most nights.
- Reserved but warms quickly to fellow Bothans and those who prove trustworthy.
- Very reluctant to divulge client information (strict confidence policy).
- If PCs emphasise Kaa'to has already forfeited his privacy by betraying employers, Average Negotiation check to get Fenn to cooperate.
- He offers the PCs 1,000 credits to split if they lay a trap for Kaa'to.
- Hard Negotiation check for more -- maximum 1,500 credits.

**Fenn's Information:**
- Kaa'to's debts have increased greatly due to a bout of bad luck at sabacc. He now owes the Spyder close to 20,000 credits and has become desperate.
- Fenn banned Kaa'to until the debts are paid.
- The spice shipment Kaa'to offered was bound for Black Sun -- too hot for Fenn to handle.
- Kaa'to is extremely dangerous and known for using any means necessary.
- Fenn speculates it won't be long before Kaa'to betrays Black Sun too.
- Fenn points out a group of Black Sun members, including an influential underboss, playing sabacc.

**The Sabacc Game:**
- 25-credit entry fee (starting ante; goes straight to pot). 10 credits for additional hands.
- Two spots open at the Black Sun operatives' table.
- The Falleen underboss (Verannis) is dismissive of newcomers.
- **Sabacc Rules:** Use Cool to establish a base dice pool. GM adds Difficulty dice based on the game's difficulty and opponent skill. Player rolls, then rolls a single Force die to illustrate the shifting card values.
- After several hands at Average and Hard difficulty, the Falleen makes motions to leave.
- PCs can mention Kaa'to and try to convince the underboss that the bounty hunter will turn against Black Sun.
- Hard Streetwise check or beating the Falleen at sabacc gets him to listen.
- Throughout conversation, Hard Charm or Deception checks determine Verannis's willingness to listen. Initial skill checks have a Setback die (Verannis is dubious).
- **If Successful:** Verannis dispatches his guards to seek out Kaa'to.
- **If Unsuccessful:** Verannis sends his two lackeys (and possibly a Trandoshan, Lar'omx) to shadow the PCs.

**Sabacc Results Table:**

| Result | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 1 Advantage | Recover 1 strain as a card unexpectedly flips in your favour |
| 2 Advantage | Add a Boost die to your next roll as you successfully bluff |
| 3 Advantage | Figure out an opponent's tells; downgrade the difficulty of your next roll once |
| Triumph | Reveal a positive or negative 23 and win the entire pot (determined by GM) |
| 1 Threat | Suffer 1 strain as a card unexpectedly flips, forcing a strategy change |
| 2 Threat | Add a Setback die to your next roll as you fall for an opponent's bluff |
| 3 Threat | Lose concentration; upgrade the difficulty of your next roll once |
| Despair | Caught cheating, bomb out, run out of chips, or get ejected from the game |

#### Location 3: Zelcomm Tower

**Conditional Modifiers:**
- **Visited after the Umbra Club:** If PCs learnt from Omacala or Kaa'to's files that Zelcomm is a weapons smuggling front, they should be prepared for a fight. Finding additional entrances requires only Easy Perception checks (instead of Average).
- **Visited after the Spyder:** If PCs engaged in a firefight with Verannis's men, security at the tower is heightened. Guards posted outside the main entrance (use Zelcomm Enforcer profile). Internal security system activated (add 2 Setback dice to any slicing attempts).

**Read Aloud:**
> *Looming over its surroundings, the Zelcomm Tower is home to a shipping company that delivers goods between the Core Worlds and the Outer Rim territories. Its headquarters in Sector 1265 is bustling with visiting clients and traders during business hours, but now the place is dark and apparently closed for the day. Nevertheless, there appears to be some activity going on around the building under the cover of darkness, and a small shuttle departs from an upper docking pad as you approach.*

**Entry Options:**

| Entry Point | Skill Check | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Front Door | Average Computers check with Setback die | If Threat generated, alarm sounds. Leads to main lobby with two elevators. Highly visible. |
| Maintenance Bay | Average Computers check with Setback die (same as above) | Side of building; blast door to dark hangar with two speeders. Out of the way. |
| Ventilation Grates | Average Perception check to find; Average Resilience check to resist claustrophobia (or suffer 2 strain); Easy Coordination check to manoeuvre safely | Back of building. Leads to sublevel 1A. Cramped corridors. |
| Elevated Terrace | Hard Athletics check to climb (4 storeys); then Hard Skulduggery or Average Computers check to open door | 4th floor. Can be reached by airspeeder or climbing. Appropriate climbing gear adds a Boost die. |

**Inside the Lobby:**
- A Zelcomm Enforcer sits behind vid screens at a security desk.
- Easy Computers check on the database reveals Coruum Sa'dia's private offices are on the 19th floor.
- Another security patrol visible on screens, checking various floors.
- Security guards become aware of PCs if they generate 2+ Threat on any skill checks.

**Elevator Access:**
- Elevators go to basement sublevel 1A, but that level requires an access card.
- Daunting Computers check to slice the panel.
- Alternatively: Easy Mechanics check or Average Athletics check to breach the elevator cabin and climb into the shaft, then two Average to Hard Athletics or Coordination checks to descend, plus a Hard Mechanics check to force open the elevator doors.

**Coruum's Office (19th Floor):**
- Door locked: Average Skulduggery check to pick (or shoot it open).
- If Threat generated, a silent alarm is tripped (not detectable by PCs).
- Inside: neat, orderly desk, file cabinets lining the east wall, computer terminal on desk.

**Computer Terminal:**
- Hard Computers check to bypass password.
- Three attempts before lockout and alarm.
- **Success results:**
  - The files include shipment records, invoices, and balance sheets. A search for Kaa'to reveals an invoice for a partial weapons delivery one day ago, plus an address: **the kelerium processing plant in Sector 943**. Remaining items scheduled for delivery later that evening.
  - 1 Advantage: One item marked "shipped" is a miniature thermal detonator.
  - 2 Advantage: A recall notice from MerrSonn Munitions -- latest batch of mini thermal detonators is defective (they rarely detonate properly).
  - 3+ Threat or Despair: Security system activates; Coruum is alerted; guards assemble.

**File Cabinets:** Stacks of datapads detailing clients. Searching reveals many Black Sun shipments located in different bays of sublevel 1A -- Coruum is a primary supplier to Black Sun.

**Locked Drawer:** Easy Skulduggery check. Contains a holdout blaster, a spare security card for sublevel 1A, and a hand scanner.

**If Coruum is Alerted:** He contacts his men in the storage room (sublevel 1A) and dispatches enforcers. A group of three arrives on the office floor. PCs have ample cover (add a Setback die to enemy shots). When two guards fall, the remaining enforcer flees to the storage area.

**Storage Facility (Sublevel 1A):**

**Read Aloud:**
> *As you wind down the stairs, you reach a catwalk that spans a large open area. This subterranean room is at least thirty metres wide by forty metres long and filled with a number of crates, loaders, and other shipping facility equipment. At the far end of the room is a large set of blast doors, easily large enough to allow access by cargo speeders and shuttles. The door must lead to an underground tunnel system capable of moving the illicit wares unseen.*

- Nine workers and enforcers initially present (fewer if PCs tripped alarms).
- They are packing a shipment onto a cargo speeder on repulsorlift sleds.
- PCs can survey from the catwalk and choose their vantage point.
- If a fight breaks out, smugglers run for the nearest crate and open it, replacing blaster pistols with blaster rifles (Ranged [Heavy]; Damage 9; Critical 3; Range [Long]; Stun setting).
- NPCs focus on escaping with the shipment rather than fighting the PCs.
- **Weapons Shipment Contents:** Blaster rifles, stun grenades, frag grenades (Melee; Damage 8; Critical 4; Range [Short]; Blast 6), and mini thermal detonators.
- Taking a large quantity of weapons gives PCs 5 additional Obligation points each but brings down the wrath of Coruum Sa'dia and Black Sun.

#### Optional Encounters (During Travel Between Locations)

**Coruscant Underworld Police:**
- A recent speeder chase with blaster fire has put them on heightened alert.
- Local holovids circulate the PCs' descriptions.
- A small squad of police droids stops and questions PCs.
- How PCs handle this is crucial -- a shootout brings backup.
- To keep police off the trail, erase police droid memories with a Hard Computers check.

**Black Sun Minions:**
- While travelling, PCs may encounter Black Sun operatives going about their business.
- At this stage, PCs are still unknown to the greater organisation, but there is a chance of running afoul of these thugs.

**Optional Encounter: First Impressions:**
- As PCs ask around about Kaa'to, word spreads back to the bounty hunter.
- GM should use Threat or Despair rolled when questioning individuals to represent Kaa'to becoming aware.
- Kaa'to can confront the PCs between locations -- a fast, furious hit-and-run attack (not a final confrontation). He uses his jet pack to strike from the shadows and flee.

### Episode III: Endgame

**Situation:** After gathering information, the PCs must decide how to confront Kaa'to. Options include:
1. **Direct Assault:** Travel to the NovaCORE kelerium processing plant in Sector 943 and explore the factory to find his hideout.
2. **Setting a Trap:** Lure Kaa'to using the weapons shipment (posing as buyers) or the spice shipment. The GM determines how and where PCs want to challenge him.
3. **No Leads:** If the PCs still have no leads, Kaa'to finds them -- he strikes after they leave their final location, picking them off in the back alleys using his jet pack.

**Travel:** PCs approach the Works via speeder (either the one acquired earlier or hired transport for ~10 credits). They dock on the landing pad.

#### Sector 943: The Works

A once-thriving manufacturing area now a run-down sprawl. Home to smugglers, death stick addicts, malfunctioning droids, gangsters, bounty hunters, and vermin. Duracrete slugs, stone mites, conduit worms, and worse can be found throughout. The Empire uses some facilities here for TIE fighter production under constant Imperial stormtrooper guard.

The NovaCORE kelerium processing plant sits on the western fringe. The mineral is used in many manufacturing practices, and the plant has been operating for centuries. It is massive -- stretching across a third of the sector, nearly 100% automated with droids carrying out the majority of work. A small contingent of NovaCORE employees maintains an office in the north tower.

Kaa'to stays in an annex of the plant that is no longer in use. Old exhaust vents are large enough to safely house his ship, *Blade's Edge*, within.

---

#### NovaCORE Factory Locations

**Location A: Landing Pad**

> *The barely lit landing pad on the southern corner of the complex is perpetually covered in waves of steam emanating from nearby exhaust ports. Small tracks of lights in the centre are the only markers to assist in navigating through the hazards. A number of crates, boxes, and other neglected shipments sit on the outskirts of the landing pad, still waiting for attention from the factory workers.*

- Two paths lead off: one to the NovaCORE offices (B) and the other towards kelerium storage tanks and the refinery.
- Crates on the pad contain tools, machine parts, and other basic materials.

**Location B: Offices**

> *The austere offices of NovaCORE are run by a skeleton crew. As you enter the building, you see an elevated walkway that overlooks the main floor filled with shambling worker droids that monitor various screens and equipment for the main factory. Large banks of security screens show most of the main refining plant and the processes taking place inside. Across the building from your vantage point, a small office sits above the activity, overseeing it all. Behind the large windows, three Mustafarians run the operation.*

- Most droid workers ignore the PCs. One droid notices them on a security screen and sends an alert after a few minutes.
- A Mustafarian comes to investigate, barking at the PCs demanding to know why they have entered a restricted area.
- Average Charm check to bluff past the worker.
- Failure: he summons a group of four security droids (use Police Droid profile) to escort the PCs out. The Mustafarians are unaware of Kaa'to's hideout.
- A firefight with the droids sends NovaCORE workers scurrying to summon reinforcements (this takes some time and should not impact the rest of the adventure).

**Location C: Kelerium Processing Plant**

> *The dominant structure of the complex, the kelerium processing plant is a massive facility sprawling across nearly half a kilometre of land. Repulsorlift sleds come and go from large hangar doors as crates of processed ore move out of the facility and empty crates return to be refilled.*
>
> *Inside, rivers of molten kelerium run through artificial canyons as gigantic crucibles dip down to extract the material for the next stage of processing. Catwalks crisscross the entire building, many filled with droids tirelessly checking gauges, readouts, and screens. Intense heat permeates the area, making it obvious why only droids are assigned to this gruelling work.*

- **Environmental Hazard:** Due to oppressive heat, all characters must pass an Average Resilience check or reduce their strain threshold by 2 for the duration (including the showdown with Kaa'to).
- **C1 -- Catwalks:** Series of catwalks running above the kelerium. Various large platforms with terminals. Below, automated crucibles and grasping claws slide along rails. Jumping onto a moving claw-arm or crucible requires an Average Athletics or Coordination check to land safely, and a second to jump back. Anyone who falls can attempt a Hard Coordination check or spend a light side Destiny Point to grab on before falling into the molten kelerium. **Falling into the molten kelerium means instant death.**
- **C2 -- Command Tower:** Large central tower, four storeys above ground floor. Manned by a dozen droids. Reachable by elevator or ground floor. If PCs interfere, droids try to stop them (use Police Droid profile). Average Computers check to access terminals.
- **C3 -- Kelerium Rivers:** Raw kelerium enters from holding towers, is dumped into molten material below. Crucibles dip and scoop fiery liquid, dumping it into cooling tanks (C4). Material heats to thousands of degrees and can melt nearly anything in seconds.
- **C4 -- Cooling Tanks:** Superheated kelerium is deposited and cooled, then extracted through larger tubes for final processing into refined powder.

**Location D: Rubble**

> *Like the other rubble scattered about, these piles are comprised of old droid parts, unprocessed ore, and by-products. The stacked debris here is arranged to make a tight path to a large blast door, partially obscured by the rubble. The door leads to an unused, forgotten section of the factory, that appears not to have been opened in decades.*

- The blast door is purely for show -- Kaa'to sealed it from the inside.
- Behind rubble (Easy Perception check): a hidden door with booby-trapped charges along the lower edge.
- Average Perception check to detect charges; Hard Cool check to disarm them.
- Detonation: 5 damage to all within short range, knocked prone.

**Location E: Abandoned Factory Section**

> *As you enter the darkened mineral processing area, old conveyer belts, centrifuges, and compactors lie forgotten under a thick layer of dust. Smashed crates; an ancient, inoperable speeder; and other forgotten remnants litter the floor. Leading out from the cavernous area are a few ladders for climbing massive exhaust pipes on one side, and a staircase to an abandoned office on the other. A dim light glows from the room at the top of the stairs.*

- Archaic machinery is still operational. A grime-covered control panel can be switched on with a Hard Mechanics check, bringing the equipment to life.
- The stairs are booby-trapped. Hard Perception checks to spot the explosives. Average Cool check to diffuse them.
- Detonation: 5 damage to all in short range, cuts off access to Kaa'to's hideout.
- Door at top of stairs: Average Skulduggery check (or blow it open with a blaster).
- **If PCs convinced Black Sun to send thugs (from the Spyder):** Two bodies found near the stairs -- Black Sun thugs were unsuccessful in their attempt to rein in the Nikto. Kaa'to has suffered one Average Critical Injury.

**E1 -- Conveyer Belts, Centrifuges, and Compactors (if activated):**
- Conveyer belts: Easy Coordination check to stay balanced. Moving same direction = incidental; opposite direction = two manoeuvres.
- Centrifuges: open to the side and spin at high speeds. Anyone who falls in must escape within one round using an Average Athletics check with a Setback die or suffer five wounds.
- Compactors: even more lethal, instantly crushing anyone who does not jump out of the way in time.

**Location F: Kaa'to's Hideout**

> *The old foreman's office serves as Kaa'to's home while he is on Coruscant. A simple bed sits against the north wall, and eight crates are stacked along the west wall. These crates resemble those housed in the Zelcomm Tower, which were remnants of the bounty hunter's last weapons shipment.*

- Three blaster rifles, two frag grenades, four stun grenades, and a mini thermal detonator remain in the crates.
- Kaa'to uses the bed sporadically; he is usually out tracking his prey.

**F1 -- Windows:** Two sets of windows look out over the loading bay, grimed over with years of soot. Poor quality; shatter with a few blaster shots.

**Location G: Kaa'to's Ship "Hangar" (Exhaust Pipes)**

> *These large exhaust pipes vent directly to the outside and haven't been used in decades. Taking advantage of this fact, the bounty hunter used one of the pipes for housing his heavily modified Z-95-AF4 Headhunter, Blade's Edge. The ramp to the ship is down, and the area seems deserted. Cargo sits at the base of the ramp, including the stolen spice shipment!*

- If PCs return here after the battle, they find a small crate of spice in the cargo hold.
- The starfighter (*Blade's Edge*) is worth 55,000 credits.
- The spice can be returned to the Pykes (reducing each PC's Obligation by 5) or sold independently (10,000 credits with a Hard Streetwise check, but adds 5 Obligation each).

---

#### The Showdown

**Where's Kaa'to? (Starting Position):**
- **PCs failed or botched a majority of locations/encounters:** Kaa'to is on high alert, patrolling the catwalks (C1). PCs can only surprise him with a Hard Stealth check.
- **PCs were predominantly successful:** Kaa'to is unaware in his hideout (F). He attempts to pin down the PCs with blaster fire and escape into the main plant (C) through the windows (F1).
- **PCs are setting a trap:** Kaa'to has taken a vantage point at his hideout's (F) stairs, looking down to investigate the visitors. Disguises may be necessary if PCs have already had a run-in with him.

**Read Aloud (Kaa'to's Taunt):**
> *"Ha, so you're proud of yourselves for tracking me down so you can put an end to my traitorous ways? Don't kid yourselves -- you'd have done the same had Black Sun paid better than the Pykes. Now, let's see how long it takes me to add you to my kill list."*

**Combat Notes:**
- Kaa'to uses his jet pack to move around vertically and avoid hazards in the plant.
- The jet pack follows standard manoeuvre rules for moving in and out of range bands. It can hover for up to two consecutive rounds before overheating and shutting off.
- PCs may try shooting his jet pack to disable it (use the aim manoeuvre).
- All ranged attacks in the processing plant have a Setback die added (machinery whirring, overhead steam rising).
- Kaa'to fires at the PCs from his elevated vantage point; combat checks from this position gain a Boost die (does not apply if PCs are on the same level).
- Kaa'to flies towards the catwalks crisscrossing the molten kelerium, heading towards the Command Tower (C2).

**Kaa'to's Tactics:**
1. Opens fire from elevated position.
2. Uses jet pack to maintain distance and height advantage.
3. At three-quarters wound threshold: attempts to threaten PCs with a mini thermal detonator. **The detonator is defective** (from the faulty MerrSonn batch).
4. Continues fighting in the kelerium processing plant using environmental hazards.

**Mini Thermal Detonator (Defective):**
- Only detonates if 3 Advantage or a Triumph are spent during the Ranged (Light) check.
- On a Despair: it explodes prematurely in the wielder's hand.
- Stats: Ranged (Light); Damage 12; Critical 2; Range (Short); Blast 10.
- Blast 10 means the attacker can spend 2 Advantage to ensure the blast hits everyone within short range of the original target, dealing 10 damage to each.

**Defeating Kaa'to:**
- Three options: inflicting enough wounds, dealing enough strain, or utilising the environment.
- Death by crushing, piercing, or incineration are all possible amid the machinery, presses, and fusioncutters.
- PCs might dump a crucible of molten kelerium on him, push him into the fiery rivers, or have him fall from the catwalks.
- A crafty GM could even have the Nikto survive to become a recurring nemesis.

## Solo Play Notes

This adventure was designed for 3-4 players using the four pre-generated characters. For solo play, consider the following adaptations:

### Pre-Generated Characters Available
1. **Matwe** -- Human Smuggler (Scoundrel). Brawn 3, Agility 3. Key skills: Deception 2, Ranged (Light) 2. Talents: Quick Draw, Plausible Deniability. Knows Speng at the Umbra Club.
2. **Jovel Nial** -- Bothan Technician (Slicer). Brawn 1, Intellect 3. Key skills: Computers 2, Mechanics 2. Talents: Technical Aptitude, Bypass Security. Clansman of Korsin Fenn at the Spyder.
3. **Sinoca "Sin" Meeku** -- Rodian Explorer (Trader). Brawn 2, Agility 3, Presence 3. Key skills: Negotiation 2, Piloting (Planetary) 2, Streetwise 2. Talents: Convincing Demeanour, Expert Tracker, Wheel and Deal. Has limited experience with Zelcomm Industries.
4. **Tray'Essek** -- Trandoshan Hired Gun (Marauder). Brawn 4, Willpower 3. Key skills: Brawl 2, Coercion 2, Ranged (Heavy) 1. Talents: Intimidating, Toughened. Has a vendetta against Lar'omx.

### Solo Adaptations

**General:**
- Run one PC with one or two of the remaining pre-gens as companion NPCs (simplified decision-making by the solo player).
- The solo player makes all skill checks; companion NPCs provide Boost dice through assistance rather than rolling independently.

**Episode I (Hot Pursuit):**
- Reduce pursuers from four Black Sun thugs to two.
- The Piloting (Planetary) check to regain control of the speeder should be the primary challenge; allow companions to provide assistance (Boost die).

**Episode II (Investigation):**
- Visit only one or two of the three locations rather than all three. The Umbra Club is the most straightforward for a solo experience. The Spyder offers the richest roleplaying.
- Reduce the number of guards at each location (e.g., one hired guard instead of two at the Umbra Club).
- Consider providing information more freely -- reduce some check difficulties by one step (e.g., Hard to Average) since a solo player has fewer skill specialisations to draw upon.
- The sabacc game at the Spyder can be played out as a series of opposed Cool checks rather than the full mini-game.

**Episode III (Endgame):**
- **Kaa'to's wound threshold** should be reduced to 12-14 for a single PC.
- Remove the Enhanced Nemesis Combat ability (no extra Initiative slot).
- Reduce the number of environmental hazards the solo player must navigate simultaneously.
- If the PC has companions, Kaa'to's full stats can be used but consider removing Lethal Blows.
- The defective mini thermal detonator is an excellent dramatic moment for solo play -- let the player discover its flaw through the Zelcomm Tower investigation beforehand.

**Obligation Sidebars:**
- The four Obligation-triggered sidebars (Blood Runs Thicker, Old Wounds, A Signature Spike, Smile You're on Camera) are designed for specific pre-generated characters. In solo play:
  - If using that character, apply the sidebar as written.
  - If not using that character, ignore the sidebar entirely.

**Destiny Points:**
- Generate 1 light and 1 dark side Destiny Point per PC (including companions). For a solo PC with two companions, this means 3 light and 3 dark side points.

**Rewards:**
- Scale rewards to the single PC: 1,000 credits plus the potential ship and spice are already generous for a solo character.
- Obligation reduction of 5 applies to the solo PC only.
