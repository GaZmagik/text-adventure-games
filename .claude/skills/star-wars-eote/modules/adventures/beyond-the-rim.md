# Beyond the Rim — EotE Adventure
> Adventure module for the star-wars-eote skill.
> Published by Fantasy Flight Games (SWE05, 2013). Designed for starting Player Characters.

---

## Setting

The adventure takes place shortly after the Battle of Yavin, during the early days of the Galactic Civil War. Three primary locations feature across three episodes:

- **The Wheel** — A massive hub-and-spoke space station on the Perlemian Trade Route near the outer edge of the Mid Rim. It operates within an "immunity sphere" that nominally bars Imperial ships, making it a haven for smugglers, merchants, and those avoiding the Empire. Population ~300,000 (70% Human). Governed by a committee of station section chiefs following the death of Senator Greyshade. Master-Com, the station's central AI, runs virtually everything on board.
- **Cholganna** — A remote, isolated jungle world at the outer edge of the Outer Rim (Chol system). Homeworld of the nexu. Temperate forests in the north, dense rainforests in the south. No known sapient species. Partially engulfed by the Chol Nebula for approximately two-thirds of its orbit. Population: unknown/transient. 323 local days per year, 20 standard hours per day.
- **Raxus Prime** — A toxic junkyard planet in the Raxus system (Tion Hegemony, Outer Rim). Under Imperial control with an orbital shipyard. Population 1.3 million (37% Human, 63% Jawa). Covered in mountains of industrial waste and starship wreckage. 388 local days per year, 22 standard hours per day.

## The Situation

At the end of the Clone Wars, Captain Rel Harsol of the CIS Banking Clan frigate *Sa Nalaor* fled the newly declared Empire. He was protecting an Arkanian cybertech researcher named Cratala and her staff. Pursued by Imperial cruisers, the *Sa Nalaor* made a desperate hyperspace jump toward the remote world of Cholganna. The ship dropped out of hyperspace too close to the planet, its engines failed, and it crash-landed in a deep jungle valley. Before impact, Harsol launched a jury-rigged hyperspace message pod to his contacts at the Wheel.

The pod drifted for twenty years before being struck by a luxury liner near the Wheel. It was delivered to IsoTech, an illicit tech company run by a Twi'lek named Reom. Reom's father, Ropok, had originally partnered with Harsol to distribute Cratala's advanced cybernetics on the black market, but Ropok was arrested and died in Imperial custody.

Now Reom hires the Player Characters to travel to Cholganna, find the *Sa Nalaor*, assess its condition, locate any survivors, and report back. The PCs are not alone — the Yiyar clan (Rodian salvagers led by Yav Yiyar) and the Imperial Security Bureau (led by Supervisor Cal Alsen at the Wheel, then Supervisor Liers Ossnan on Cholganna) are also pursuing the wreck. The adventure is a race across three worlds against rival factions, hostile wildlife, and the ever-tightening grip of the Empire.

## Encounter Flow

### Episode I: The Tale of the *Sa Nalaor*

#### 1. A Business Proposition

**Situation:** The PCs receive an invitation (personal, direct order, or through ulterior motives) to meet Reom at IsoTech's offices on the Wheel. Reom explains the discovery of the *Sa Nalaor*'s hyperspace message pod and offers them a job: travel to Cholganna, find the ship, assess its condition, locate survivors, and report back.

**Options:**
- Negotiate payment (base 10,000 credits, up to 15,000 with Negotiation; Reom adds 1,000 per Advantage on a successful Negotiation check)
- Agree to 10% up front, or 25% if they generate a Triumph
- Ask questions about the *Sa Nalaor*, Cholganna, the pod, Captain Harsol, Eyetee-Three
- Accept the deal or walk away

**Skill Checks:**
- **Negotiation check** to improve payment terms
- **Opposed Stealth check** (Yiyar snitch makes Stealth with 3 Proficiency dice vs. best PC Perception) — to detect the Rodian spy outside Reom's office

**The Hyperspace Message Pod:**
- **Easy Computers check** — reveals pod needs mechanical repairs
- **Hard Computers check** (with additional Setback if no proper tools) — on success, recovers the *Sa Nalaor*'s vector, reducing Setback on future Astrogation checks to Cholganna; each Advantage adds a Boost or Triumph adds two Boosts

#### 2. Preparations and Rumours

**Situation:** The PCs have approximately 8 hours to prepare before departure. They should purchase gear, gather information, and watch for rivals. A timeline of NPC events runs in the background.

**Options:**
- Explore hangar deck locations (cantina, shops, kiosks, etc.)
- Gather information via skill checks (see tables below)
- Guard their ship against Imperial tracking device
- Purchase equipment and supplies

**Skill Checks (Information Gathering):**
- **Average Computers, Streetwise, or Knowledge (Underworld) checks** — consult Tables 2-1 through 2-3 for results on topics: Cholganna, IsoTech/Reom, Raxus Prime, the *Sa Nalaor*
- **Knowledge (Lore), Knowledge (Outer Rim), or Knowledge (Xenology) checks** — planetary information
- **Average Perception checks** — notice being watched/followed
- **Waroon Dak (infochant):** Average Charm or Coercion check to approach; Hard Negotiation check to set per-question fee (success = 10 credits/question, failure = 15); then Charm or Coercion checks per question with Boost/Setback dice as indicated by topic tables
- **Sonou (clothing store):** PCs gain Boost to Streetwise and Knowledge (Outer Rim) checks
- **Nols (gear store):** PCs gain Boost to Knowledge (Lore) or Knowledge (Xenology) checks
- **Jokol (bookmaker):** yields Boost on astrogation/hyperlane checks if PCs share destination
- **Hal Lars (parts store):** PCs gain Boost to Knowledge (Underworld) or Streetwise checks

**Timeline to Departure:**
- **T+0:** Meeting with Reom concludes
- **T+1 hour:** Yiyar Clan starts threatening anyone pursuing the *Sa Nalaor*
- **T+5 hours:** Reom hides pod; Imperial maintenance droid observes
- **T+6 hours:** Apoto and Lasan break into IsoTech offices (forced to flee)
- **T+6.5 hours:** Yiyar relocate *Nightflyer* closer to PCs' hangar
- **T+7.5 hours:** Shira arrives with IT-3PO; messages PCs about break-in
- **T+8 hours:** Yiyar ambush IT-3PO delivery

#### 3. Imperial Interests

**Situation:** ISB agents led by Supervisor Cal Alsen are undercover on the Wheel, watching IsoTech. They use commandeered WED Treadwell repair droids to spy and attempt to place a tracking device on the PCs' ship.

**Options:**
- Detect the tracking device attempt
- Capture or confront the maintenance droid
- Ignore it (ISB obtains pod data from a Wheel informant regardless)

**Skill Checks:**
- **Opposed Stealth check** (droid's Stealth vs. PC/guard Perception) — to detect the droid placing the tracker
- If tracker is placed, ISB requisitions scout troopers to follow PCs to Cholganna

#### 4. Picking Up the Droid / Kidnapping IT-3PO

**Situation:** Shira contacts the PCs to say IT-3PO will arrive in 5 minutes. Ten minutes pass with no droid. The Yiyar clan has ambushed the delivery and kidnapped Eyetee-Three in the hangar area.

**Options:**
- Call Reom to check on the droid's status
- Return to IsoTech offices (find them ransacked)
- Chase the Yiyar through the hangar decks
- Commandeer a V5 medium cargo floater for pursuit

**Skill Checks:**
- **Average Athletics or Coordination checks** — manoeuvring during the chase
- **Average Coordination check** — avoid knocking over an R2 unit
- **Hard Coordination check** — avoid colliding with a crowd outside Tasia's Tapcafe (Despair = PC falls entangled in crowd)
- **Hard Athletics check** — stop a cargo container using brute force
- **Average Coordination check** — dodge cargo containers
- **Hard Charm or Leadership check** — recruit bystander help
- **Easy Perception check** — spot a V5 cargo floater to commandeer
- Ranged combat adds at least one Setback for congested conditions

**Chase Obstacles (in order):**
1. R2 unit emerges from Dented Droid — Average Coordination to avoid
2. Crowd outside Tasia's Tapcafe — Hard Coordination to avoid collision
3. Rodian fires from information kiosk cover — combat/chase continues
4. Massive cargo containers on repulsor sleds — Hard Athletics to stop or Average Coordination to dodge

#### 5. Aftermath and Departure

**Situation:** The chase may end several ways. The PCs either recover IT-3PO or the Yiyar escape with the droid. Security may or may not arrive.

**Outcomes:**
- **Yiyar escapes with IT-3PO:** They depart immediately on *Nightflyer*. PCs can try to engage them in the hangar first. Yiyar escape into hyperspace; they arrive at Cholganna before the PCs.
- **PCs recapture IT-3PO and blast off before security/Yiyar can stop them:** Hard Piloting (Space) check to avoid an incoming freighter. Yiyar delayed a day.
- **Security arrives before PCs escape:** Security detains everyone. Working with security delays departure by several days. Making a break for it harms IsoTech's reputation and PCs are pursued by Wheel Security fighters and turbolaser fire.

**Episode I XP Rewards:**
- Investigating the *Sa Nalaor* and Cholganna clues: 5-10 XP
- Saving IT-3PO from the Yiyar clan: 10 XP
- Disabling or removing the homing beacon: 5 XP
- Additional encounters: 1-5 XP
- Plus Motivation and roleplaying bonuses

### Episode II: Welcome to the Jungle

#### 1. Journey Beyond the Rim

**Situation:** The PCs must plot a hyperspace course to Cholganna. Two options exist.

**Option 1 — Following the *Sa Nalaor*'s Flight Plan (two jumps):**
- First leg along Perlemian Trade Route: **Average Astrogation check**. ~48 hours with Class 1 hyperdrive. Each Threat adds 4 hours.
- Second leg to Cholganna: **Hard Astrogation check with Setback Setback** (decades-old data). ~24 hours. PCs may have reduced Setback or added Boost from pod data restoration.

**Option 2 — Direct Jump:**
- **Daunting Astrogation check**. Pod data neither helps nor hinders. ~55 hours with Class 1 hyperdrive.

**Astrogation Results (negative symbols):**
- *Option 1, 1st Leg:* Threat Threat or more = arrive far out of position, upgrade next Astrogation difficulty once. Despair = emerge in asteroid field (Hard Piloting (Space) checks to avoid collisions; 1 system strain per subsequent failed check).
- *Option 1, 2nd Leg or Option 2:* Threat Threat or more = 5 system strain, sensors damaged by Chol Nebula (offline until Hard Mechanics check). Despair = ship brought too close to atmosphere (Hard Piloting (Space) check to avoid damage; ignore comet encounter on p.40).

**A Few Days in Hyperspace:**
- Prepare gear, repair damage, get acquainted
- Quiz Eyetee-Three about the *Sa Nalaor* crew (it shares information but conceals its true ownership by Captain Harsol)
- **Daunting Computers check** to force truth from IT-3PO's programming directly

#### 2. Arrival at Cholganna

**Situation:** The Cholganna system lies at the edge of the Chol Nebula. The nebula interferes with sensors and visibility.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Hard Computers check** — adjust sensors after nebula gas interference (5 minutes; each additional Advantage reduces time by 1 minute, minimum 3). Threat Threat = partial success with Setback to subsequent sensor/combat checks. Advantage Advantage or Triumph compensates for nebula effects and adds Boost to sensor use.
- Dust cloud adds Setback Setback to all Perception checks and visual sightings until clear.
- **Hard Piloting (Space) check** (add Setback for dust) — navigate meteoroid/asteroid field on approach. Smaller hits deflect off shields (3 points each), each Threat adds one point of damage. Despair = Critical Hit.
- Three additional **Piloting (Space) checks** to clear the meteoroid cluster.
- **Average Computers checks** — scan the area for other ships once clear.

#### 3. Searching Cholganna

**Situation:** The PCs must locate the crash site from orbit and then descend to the surface.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Average Computers check** (from orbit) — identify correct hemisphere. Despair = false reading (wrong hemisphere). Advantage/Threat add Boost/Setback to subsequent checks. ~25 minutes per check (reduced by 5 min per uncancelled Advantage after the first, minimum 10 min).
- **Average Computers check** (quarter scan, from low orbit) — ~5 hours, subject to nebula effects. Results:
  - Success = half a dozen areas within a 100km range (Major Debris Field)
  - Multiple successes narrow range by 10km per Advantage
  - Advantage or Advantage Advantage = possible escape pod location (Escape Pod 34 Besh)
  - Triumph or Advantage Advantage Advantage Advantage = possible hit for main crash site (Engine Section)
  - Despair = tempting false positive
- **Average Survival check** — analyse atmospheric scan data, removing Setback from subsequent Computers checks
- **Average Computers check** (atmospheric scan) — with Setback Setback for terrain modifications and additional Setback if scanning beyond sensor limits

**Fearsome Cholganna (Fear Checks):**
- First entering wilderness: **Average Discipline check**
- PC isolated or alone: add one Setback or upgrade one Difficulty die
- Night or dark: upgrade difficulty twice
- Suspicious wild animal hunting/stalking: **Hard Discipline check**
- First attacked by dangerous creature: **Hard Discipline check**
- Subsequent attacks or if PC was previously wounded: upgrade one or more Difficulty dice

#### 4. Escape Pod 34 Besh

**Situation:** The PCs detect a half-buried hulk at the bottom of a steep, overgrown crevasse. It is an escape pod, home to hundreds of venomous green bugs.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Average Perception check** — detect wild animals and aggressive creatures in area (Triumph = creatures scared off by ship landing may return)
- **Hard Athletics check** — climb into and along the crevasse
- **Average Knowledge (Lore) or Perception check** — identify hulk as escape pod
- **Average Knowledge (Xenology) or Survival check** — identify insects as dangerous
- **Hard Perception check** — notice skeletal limbs, damaged blasters, CIS Navy spacesuit shreds, and a picked-clean quadruped skeleton with blaster damage
- **Hard Mechanics check** — physically remove flight logs (Despair destroys fragile system; Threat degrades recorded files)
- **Average Computers check** — copy and view retrieved memory (files contain *Sa Nalaor* position at pod launch, descent recording, voice traffic revealing Captain Harsol's condemnation of those who abandoned ship, and a holographic recording of three humans and two Neimoidians)

#### 5. Deadly Cholganna (Wildlife Encounters)

These encounters may be dropped into any scene on Cholganna.

**Arboreal Octopus Ambush:**
- **Opposed Perception vs. Stealth check** (add Setback Setback to account for unfavourable terrain) — or opposed Stealth vs. Perception check (adding Boost Boost) using best PC Perception
- Octopi ambush from tree limbs above; grab and try to haul prey upward
- Flee if they sustain a Critical Hit or damage exceeding half wound threshold

**Bark Rat Burst:**
- PCs inadvertently collide with or blast a bark rat tree-nest
- Two to three minion groups of five rats swarm perceived attackers
- They combine speed and numbers, whipping tails and lacerating legs

**Nexu Stalking:**
- Solitary nexu stalks weakest/smallest PC
- **Opposed Perception vs. Stealth check** (if PCs keeping watch) or **opposed Stealth vs. Perception check** (if PCs distracted, add Setback Setback if watchful PCs might notice)
- Nexu springs from trees/ground, latches on, tries to drag prey into forest
- Flees if it sustains wounds exceeding half its wound threshold

#### 6. Convergence on the Crash Site

**Situation:** The *Sa Nalaor* crash site lies at the bottom of a long, narrow valley. The ship came to rest in two main sections: the aft engine section (wedged as a dam) and the forward hull/bridge section (several km north). Survivors have camouflaged both sites and maintain a hidden sentry post.

**Engine Section:**
- **Average Perception check** or **Easy Computers check** (add Setback for deteriorated condition) — identify hull as Banking Clan frigate
- **Hard or Daunting Athletics checks** (with Setback for wet/crumbling conditions) — climb onto the ship
- **Hard Perception check** — find rotting wooden ladders on east side (Advantage reveals they appear usable; Threat reveals they are rotten and useless)
- **Average Coordination check** — tumble to safety if ladder collapses (failure = 3 wounds)
- **Hard Athletics check** (add Setback near hull, Setback Setback within hull) — recover from a fall into treacherous water
- Setback Setback to all Perception checks within the hull (noise and darkness)
- **Hard Computers check** — scan southern end of lake (~15 minutes)

**Main Hull:**
- **Hard Perception check** (with Boost Boost) or **Average Computers check** — detect main hull on approach
- **Hard Perception check** — discern details of the wreck (Advantage = camouflage netting detected; additional Advantage = stripped for salvage; Advantage = old encampment visible; Triumph = something alive hiding in darkened wreckage)
- **Hard Survival check** — analyse old encampment (Advantage = held 30-50 people; Advantage Advantage = nearby burial site with ~20 graves)

**Main Hull Interior Locations:**

*T-Junction and Hatch to Lower Levels:* Starboard corridor (long, blackened by fire) and port corridor (daylight at end). Aft corridor buckled. Hatch in floor leads deeper (ladder rungs with moss and slime).

*Sensor Array Control Room:* Burnt-out computers, corroded, large section of decking missing from explosion. Tarp covers a tool kit in the corner — someone was recently working on a security scanner.

*Bridge Security:* Rows of lockers (stripped bare). Hard Perception check reveals a vibroknife with magnetic hilt concealed inside a locker. Blast door leads to turbolift shafts and Bridge Command Tower.

*Bridge Command Tower:* Overgrown with vines, moss, and plants. Consoles stripped. Hidden compartment contains a still-operational holoprojector with a recording of the ship's final moments (Captain Harsol barking orders, a white-haired Arkanian woman at his side, the crash).

*Damaged Decking and Hallway:* Average Coordination check to cross safely without falling through or causing collapse.

*Sunken Cavern:* Accessible via hallway from the hatch.

#### 7. Cyber-Nexu Ambush

**Situation:** After exploring the main hull, cybernetically enhanced nexu ambush the PCs. These are Cratala's creations — territorial cyborgs meant to destroy anyone discovering the wreck.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Stealth check** (cyber-nexu) opposed by best PCs' Vigilance (add Setback Setback for cover; add Boost if PCs were tipped off earlier)
- **Hard Fear check** (upgrade difficulty once) — upon first encountering a cyber-nexu

**The Unexpected IT-3PO:** During the battle, IT-3PO recognises Cratala's signature cybernetic style and deduces survivors must be nearby. It activates an internal homing beacon installed by Captain Harsol. From this point, IT-3PO does everything it can to keep the PCs in the area (stalling, negotiation, encouraging a lengthy search).

#### 8. A Night in the Wilderness

**Situation:** The jungle is much more dangerous at night. Nexu wander close. Creatures splash in the lake. Fire attracts attention.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Upgraded Easy Discipline check** — to sleep through alien creature sounds (failure = kept awake, reduce strain thresholds by 2 until rest)
- Fear checks as appropriate for night encounters

#### 9. Rival Arrival

**Situation:** If they haven't already, the Yiyar clan catches up to the PCs, arriving the morning after the PCs discover the *Sa Nalaor*. Yav, Lasan, Eshon, and two Trandoshan crewmen disembark with weapons drawn.

**Options:**
- Negotiate with the Yiyar clan to share the discovery
- Intimidate them
- Fight — Yiyar flee if half their number are killed/seriously injured; if more than half are killed, they give up on Cholganna and settle for revenge at Raxus Prime
- If Yiyar outnumber PCs, they try to isolate and capture individuals

#### 10. Trek to the Retreat

**Situation:** About mid-morning the day after IT-3PO activates the beacon, Captain Harsol tracks it down. He arrives with two survivors and six B1 battle droids, travelling on reevos (large amphibious creatures) via the lake.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Average Computers or Perception check** — detect Harsol's group approaching via ship sensors or visual watch
- **Charm checks** opposed by Harsol's Cool — to establish trust with the captain (PCs get Boost from association with Reom once IT-3PO explains Ropok's death)

**Jungle Cruise:** Travel upriver by reevo to the Retreat. ~30 minutes. Survivors prefer to stick to the banks under jungle canopy cover. Generally uneventful unless PCs try something.

**Survivors' Settlement Arrival:** The Retreat is built into a deep depression in the rock face of a bluff. PCs are guided through a large gate into a crowd of ~30 survivors dressed in patchwork clothing.

#### 11. Touring the Settlement

**Situation:** Harsol assembles everyone in HQ. He introduces IT-3PO and the PCs. Cratala arrives with her staff. The emotional state of the survivors is fragile — Harsol is paranoid and defensive, Cratala fears the Empire, and the survivors are divided.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Opposed Charm, Deception, or Leadership checks** vs. Harsol, Cratala, or the crowd (Hard difficulty) — to gauge and influence mood
- **Hard Knowledge (Lore) and Medicine checks** — when asked about cybernetics by Cratala (showing aptitude grants Boost when dealing with Cratala)
- **Negotiation checks** with Cratala — to work out a deal
- **Charm or Deception checks** against Harsol — to gain his approval (negative results increase paranoia and make subsequent checks harder)

#### 12. Imperial Contingencies

### Episode III: Toxic Motherlode

#### 1. Arrival at Raxus Prime

**Situation:** The PCs arrive near Raxus Prime. An Imperial base and shipyard orbit the planet. The Imperials' disposition depends on whether ISB has alerted them.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Hard Astrogation check** — trip from Cholganna (~36 hours, Class 1 hyperdrive). Threat = closer to Imperial shipyard; Advantage = closer to planet; Despair = dangerously close to shipyard with TIE fighter diversion.
- **Hard Deception, Charm, or Negotiation checks** — stall Raxus Control's demands for permits and transmission clearance. Failure/poor results escalate to demands to board (which means arrest).

#### 2. Junkyard Juke

**Situation:** As the PCs head toward Scrapheap Point in atmosphere, two pairs of Imperial TIE fighters pick up the pursuit. The PCs must lose them in the junkyard canyons without leading them to the hidden base.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Piloting (Space) checks** — navigate unstable junk heaps and hazardous environment. Multiple Advantage = Boost on next manoeuvre; Advantage = Boost to gunner; Despair = minor collision; Despair = major collision; Advantage = pursuing ships get Setback on next check.
- **Average Computers check** (manning sensors) — analyse enemy trajectory, granting Boost to pilot or upgrading pilot's next check on Triumph
- **Average Coordination checks** — for anyone not strapped in during minor collisions

**Canyon Hazard Table (d100):**
- 1-10: Canyon Entry — canyon/junk rises on either side. Add Setback.
- 11-20: Capacitor Jolt — old capacitor discharges energy. Apply Setback Setback. If unsuccessful, 5 system strain + Setback to piloting/attacks until Hard Computers check removes it.
- 21-30: Debris Avalanche — junk wall collapses. Add Setback Setback to avoid 9 damage + 3 system strain (successful = physical damage only).
- 31-40: Extremely Narrow Canyon — fly in unusual dimension. Add Setback Setback to initial manoeuvre; all subsequent Piloting checks and attacks add Setback.
- 41-50: Jawa Scavenging Party — dozens of Jawas on junk walls. Add Setback to Piloting (Space) to avoid minor collision.
- 51-60: Junk Arch — precariously balanced archway. Setback Setback. Failure = major collision + Critical Hit.
- 61-70: Noxious Fog — chemical fog, add Setback Setback unless Hard Perception or Computers check guides the way.
- 71-80: Sharp Turn — sudden sharp turn. Add Setback.
- 81-90: Sludge Waterfall — chemical-laden sludge waterfall. Banking to avoid adds Setback Setback (Advantage adds only Setback). 2 system strain. Piloting checks obscured for two rounds. Threat applies Setback to gunners for two rounds.
- 91-100: Tunnel Entry — horizontal or vertical tunnel. Piloting (Space) check with Setback to avoid major collision. Terrifying Tunnel: multiple Hard Piloting (Space) checks with Setback Setback. Triumph collapses tunnel behind PCs. Despair brings tunnel down on ship (10 damage + 3 system strain; trapped ship needs full-power engines to break free: 5 additional damage + 2 system strain).

#### 3. Landing at the Drop-Off Point

**Situation:** The PCs outrun or outgun the TIEs and arrive at Scrapheap Point. They are greeted by Norta, IsoTech's Raxus Prime manager. He briefs them, gives 2,000 credits down payment, and arranges meetings with IT-3PO and any survivors.

**Skill Checks:**
- **Deception check** (Norta) vs. PCs' best Vigilance — Norta lies about Reom being expected in a day (Reom is already here, recovering from a blaster wound on the corvette's upper level)

#### 4. Exploring the Scrapheap Point Environs

**Situation:** PCs have downtime to explore, trade, and run errands for Norta (locating a multi-phase transformer, power line splicers, labour droid servos, and a 2-metre-wide sensor dish — items that help with the corvette escape later).

**Skill Checks:**
- **Survival and Perception checks** — dig through junk to find items (4-6 hours excursion)
- **Stealth checks** — avoid Imperial probe droid patrols

**Encounters:**
- **Jawa Scavengers:** Trading encounter; may lead to a massive walking recycling droid (Jawa sandcrawler equivalent) for a larger selection
- **Imperial Probe Droid Patrol:** Stealth checks or deal with the droid
- **The Yiyar Clan (foreshadowing):** Hard Perception check to detect disguised attackers (Jawas that look too tall, use only hand signals)

#### 5. It's Not Over Yet

**Situation:** The Yiyar clan — what remains of them plus "Too Low" Talo and Trandoshan mercenaries — launches a surprise attack on Scrapheap Point disguised as Jawas. They converge from multiple directions.

**Skill Checks:**

**Battle Locations:**

- **PCs' Ship:** Five Rodians attempt to board. Four Sullustans run for the corvette. If unable to board, they throw a grenade into an engine nozzle.

- **Bantha Can:** Fierce fighting; four Trandoshans preparing to throw a thermal detonator. PCs can stop them just in time.
- **Corvette:** Fighting throughout lower level. Attackers don't know about upper levels. Turbolifts: an apparently disabled lift suddenly opens — Reom and three heavily armed mercenaries step out, routing the invaders.

Three additional skiffs at each alcove, plus a pair at each entrance to the *Crushed Courier*. A dozen Trandoshan mercenaries on the ridge working down the passageways.

**Situation:** The Imperials catch up. TIE fighters detected by IsoTech sensors. ISB has known about IsoTech's existence but not its location until now. A troop shuttle is on standby.

**Imperial Deployment Timeline:**

## Solo Play Notes

Beyond the Rim is designed for a group of starting PCs, but can be adapted for solo play with the following considerations:

### Encounter Scaling
- **Yiyar Clan encounters:** Reduce the number of Yiyar clan members in each encounter. In the hangar chase (Episode I), use two Rodians instead of five. In the Cholganna rival arrival, Yav and one companion instead of five. At Scrapheap Point, scale Trandoshan mercenaries from twelve to four.
- **Cyber-nexu:** Use one cyber-nexu for a solo PC (the book recommends one for groups of three or fewer).
- **Imperial scout troopers:** Use one lance (five troopers) instead of two. Keep Agent Ossnan as a single Rival adversary.
- **Scrapheap Point battle:** Reduce Yiyar attackers to two per skiff and six Trandoshans total.
- **TIE fighters:** Use one pair instead of two pairs during the Junkyard Juke.

### Ally Management
- **IT-3PO** serves as a natural companion throughout the adventure. Use the droid as an NPC ally for social checks (Negotiation, Charm) and to provide exposition and plot direction.
- **Reom** may assign a guard or assistant to accompany the solo PC from the Wheel onwards.
- **Captain Harsol** and the survivors can serve as allies during Episode II's Imperial Contingencies encounter, handling some of the combat whilst the PC focuses on key objectives.
- **Norta** and IsoTech technicians handle most of the *Blockade Bandit* repairs in Episode III; the solo PC should focus on one or two critical repair tasks and any combat encounters.

### Investigation Phase (Episode I)
- The free-form investigation on the Wheel works well for solo play. The various shop owners and infochants provide structured social encounters.
- Consider allowing the solo PC to detect the Yiyar snitch automatically (or with a reduced difficulty) to maintain narrative momentum.
- The IT-3PO kidnapping chase can be run as a series of skill checks rather than a combat encounter; the solo PC pursues whilst bystanders and obstacles create the tension.

### Exploration Phase (Episode II)
- Cholganna's exploration is well-suited to solo play as it is largely atmospheric and skill-check-driven.
- Fear checks are important — a solo PC isolated on a hostile world should feel the weight of the wilderness. Do not reduce these.
- The wildlife encounters (arboreal octopi, nexu, bark rats) should be used sparingly — one type per scene rather than layering multiple threats.
- The Trek to the Retreat and Touring the Settlement sections are primarily social/narrative and translate well to solo play without modification.

### Negotiation and Diplomacy
- The multi-faction negotiations at the Retreat (Harsol, Cratala, Yiyar clan, and eventually Imperials) are the heart of Episode II. For solo play, focus on one-on-one conversations with each faction leader rather than a crowded assembly.
- Use IT-3PO as a mediator NPC who can make supporting Negotiation checks.
- The "Meeting Reom's Terms of the Deal" point system works well for solo play — it gives clear, measurable objectives.

### Combat Pacing
- Episode III's overlapping battles (Yiyar attack + Imperial deployment + corvette repairs) are the most challenging section for solo play. Break them into sequential scenes rather than simultaneous events:
  1. First: Yiyar attack springs (handle combat at one location)

### Difficulty Adjustments
- Reduce minion group sizes by half throughout.
- Rivals encountered alone (Yav Yiyar, Talo, Trandoshan mercenaries) should have their wound thresholds reduced by 25% for solo play.
- Nemesis characters (Reom, Harsol, Cratala) are primarily social opponents and should retain their full statistics.
