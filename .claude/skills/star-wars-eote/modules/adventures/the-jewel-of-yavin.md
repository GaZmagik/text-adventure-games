# The Jewel of Yavin — EotE Adventure

> Adventure module for the star-wars-eote skill. A multi-part heist set on Cloud City, Bespin, in which the Player Characters scheme to steal the "unstealable" Jewel of Yavin — not once, but twice.

---

## Setting

**Planet:** Bespin (Outer Rim)
**Location:** Cloud City — a floating tibanna gas mining colony and luxury tourist destination, sky-high above the gas giant.
**Era:** Galactic Empire (post-Clone Wars). Baron Administrator Lando Calrissian has recently taken charge and is working to clean up corruption left by his predecessor, Dominic Raynor.
**Tone:** Heist caper with social intrigue, cloud car racing, slicing, and a dramatic multi-faction chase finale. Less linear than typical published adventures — the PCs roam Cloud City freely completing tasks in the order of their choosing.

Cloud City is roughly 16 kilometres in diameter and 17 kilometres tall. It comprises 392 internal levels:
- **Levels 0-50 (Tourist District):** Resorts, hotels, casinos, museums, restaurants. Well-patrolled by the Wing Guard and observation droids. Elegant Alderaanian architecture.
- **Levels 51-100:** High-income housing; guest accommodation for the Baron Administrator.
- **Levels 101-120:** Administrative offices, Wing Guard headquarters, Baron Administrator's offices.
- **Levels 121-160 (Port Town):** Landing bays, warehouses, starship repair, seedy cantinas, gambling dens, flophouses, black markets. Less Wing Guard presence.
- **Levels 161-220:** Mid-low income housing, service sector employees.
- **Levels 221-280 (Industrial Levels):** Bespin Motors, life support, water treatment, tibanna processing. Kept by Ugnaughts; Wing Guard largely absent.
- **Levels 281-370:** Tibanna gas mining and processing.
- **Levels 371-392:** Tractor beams and repulsorlift generators keeping the city aloft.

**Economy:** Driven by tibanna gas mining and tourism. Net +1 modifier to rarity of all goods. Spin-sealed tibanna gas (military grade, technically contraband) is Cloud City's primary black-market export.

**Culture:** Cloud City's citizens are famously neutral and disinterested in galactic politics. A person's past does not matter here. The Empire and the Rebel Alliance are treated with equal suspicion.

## The Situation

### Adventure Background

**Arend Shen** is a middle-aged Pantoran financier who has worked as a tax administrator on Cloud City for roughly ten years, skimming from the top and using underworld connections to keep things quiet. With Lando Calrissian cleaning up corruption, Arend has decided to cut and run — but not before seizing a perfect opportunity for revenge and profit.

Ten years ago, Arend and his family were wealthy investors in a subsidiary branch of the Cloud City Central Bank specialising in venture capital. One of their clients, **Marus Grayson**, a commissioner in the Imperial Trade and Commerce Authority, headed a business enterprise that went bankrupt. Rather than accept the loss, Grayson used his Imperial connections and COMPNOR to freeze and seize the Shen family's assets on allegations of treason, ruining them utterly.

Now Grayson has arrived at Cloud City to auction off a precious corusca gem — the **Jewel of Yavin**, one of the largest and most beautiful specimens ever discovered. The gem's legal value ranges from 500,000 to 1,000,000 credits. Bespin is the perfect location to duck Imperial taxes on such a sale, and the gem has recently been branded contraband since it can be repurposed as a lightsaber crystal.

Arend plans to exact his revenge twice over:
1. **Divert the winning auction bid** by slicing the Cloud City Central Bank network, redirecting the funds to his own account.
2. **Steal the gem itself** from the Figg & Associates Art Museum during the gala.

He has sent his adult daughter, **Aris Shen**, to recruit the PCs in Port Town. However, Arend has no intention of sharing the profits — the getaway speeder he provides is rigged with explosives to kill the PCs after the heist. He plans to recover the gem from the wreckage.

### Three Main Plotlines

The adventure has three interlocking jobs, bookended by the Grand Prix and a holo-park chase:

1. **The Bidding War** — Investigate the seven bidders, gather leverage, and manipulate them at the gala to maximise the auction price (and thus the stolen funds).
2. **The Slicing Job** — Prepare a computer spike, sample government encryption, deal with Lobot's counter-slicing, and kidnap a remote banking droid to divert the winning bid.
3. **The Gem Heist** — Case the Figg & Associates Art Museum, chart entry and escape routes, determine security, and steal the Jewel of Yavin on the night of the gala.

These are preceded by winning the **Cloud City Grand Prix** to secure an invitation to the gala, and followed by the revelation of Arend's betrayal and a desperate **escape from Cloud City**.

## Encounter Flow

### Episode I: Odds and Opportunity

#### 1. Shadowport Blues (Opening Encounter)

**Situation:** The PCs rendezvous with Aris Shen on Market Row in Port Town. Thugs in the employ of Aris's husband, Vorse Tabarith, have been assigned to tail her. There is one human thug per PC, plus Bhurra the Lutrillian lieutenant. The thugs are at medium range from Aris and short range from the PCs.

**Running the Encounter:**
- Roll initiative: PCs use Cool; thugs use Vigilance (roll once for human thugs, once for Bhurra).
- Characters act in initiative order even if combat does not break out.
- If thugs win initiative, they spend turns manoeuvring toward Aris. They only engage in violence if someone accosts Aris, tries to spirit her away, or insults Vorse Tabarith.
- Aris acts last each round, spending two manoeuvres moving away from the thugs (downgrading her action).
- Ranged combat difficulty is upgraded once for targets farther than engaged (Market Row crowds). Any Despair on a combat check may strike an innocent bystander.

**Options for the PCs:**
- **Disguise Aris** with clothing from a stall — opposed Deception check.
- **Distract the thugs** and let Aris use Stealth — opposed Charm, Coercion, or Deception check.
- **Physically block** the thugs (bodies, tipping over goods, moving carts) — Average Athletics check.
- **Find Aris a shortcut** — Hard Knowledge (Underworld) or Streetwise check.
- **Direct combat** — standard combat rules apply.

**Success:** If Aris moves to extreme range from the thugs without them actively fighting the PCs, the encounter is over. If she cannot escape, she presses a datachip with a time and address on Level 50 into a PC's hand.

**Moving On:** Aris leads the PCs (or they follow the datachip) to Arend Shen's apartment on Level 50. Arend lays out the plan: infiltrate the auction, manipulate the bidders, slice the banking network to redirect the winning bid, and steal the gem from the museum. The PCs' cut is an equal share split with Arend and Aris.

**Key Questions the PCs May Ask:**
- *"How much is our cut?"* — Equal split. Arend expects the bidding to reach hundreds of thousands of credits.
- *"Who are the bidders?"* — Seven: Kaltho the Hutt, Shreya Ordassa, Mil Mikkir, Pos Podura, Zekra Fol, Elaiza, and Vorse Tabarith.
- *"How do we infiltrate the auction?"* — Win the Cloud City Grand Prix to secure a gala invitation. Arend can lend a cloud car.
- *"What about the heist?"* — PCs must develop their own plan. Arend will provide a getaway speeder.
- *"What about the current owner?"* — Marus Grayson, a corrupt Imperial bureaucrat who ruined the Shen family.

#### 2. Heist Homework (Free-form Investigation)

**Three preparation objectives:**
1. Chart entrance and escape routes for the museum.
2. Determine security in place and its strength.
3. Figure out how to blind or bypass security.

**Casing the Joint:**
- Visit the museum during open hours (10am-6pm). 30-60 visitors at any time. Ticket: 12 credits.
- Security cameras: opposed Stealth vs. Nallith's Vigilance. Hiding behind exhibits adds a Boost die.
- Auction Room (7): locked before the gala. Hard Skulduggery check to pick. Contains corusca gem displays.
- Schematics: Hard Computers check at public information consoles yields general layout (excludes storeroom, tapcafe kitchen, cleaning room, garbage disposal). Construction Guild at Level 154 has detailed plans — speak to Xoff Ix (Ugnaught chief foreman). Charm or Deception check opposed by Xoff's Discipline to persuade him; 500 credits for copies.

**Grayson's Crew:**
- Two squads of six private security guards, rotating 10-hour shifts.
- Squad leaders: **Burr Naxis** (Corellian human, gambles at the Trest casino on Level 44, can be plied with drinks — Hard Charm check) and **Gantel Dro** (Defel thief blackmailed into service, frequents the Four-and-a-Half, sympathetic to fellow rogues — Hard Streetwise or Stealth check to approach).
- Gantel shares: garbage disposal is the obvious break-in point; museum cameras have blind spots (storeroom, cleaner's room, tapcafe kitchen); guards are limited to riot guns, no disruptors.

**Kitting Out:**
- Restricted weapons/explosives available only in Port Town.
- Finding a black market arms dealer: Hard Charm, Coercion, or Streetwise check. Smuggler characters add a Boost die. Offering a 50-credit bribe reduces to Average check. Three NPCs can be approached; if all three checks fail, word spreads and other dealers refuse.
- Stealth suits: 250 credits each (R); +1 soak, adds a Boost die to all Stealth checks. Masks: 25 credits each.

#### 4. Setting Up the Slicing Shop

**The Legwork (Three Preparation Steps):**

**a) Creating a Computer Spike (Hard Computers check):**
- Materials cost ~300 credits (half at specialist retailers).
- Requires at least 10 hours of work (can be rushed, adding a Setback die).
- Results determine bonuses to the final slicing check (Table 1-2: each Success adds a Boost die; Advantage/Triumph remove Setback dice; Threat/Despair add complications).

**b) Sampling the Encryption:**
- **Direct approach:** Get an access card from Arend's secretary (full sample removes all encryption penalties, but secretary may leak information).
- **Sneaking in:** Break into government offices after hours, avoiding Government Security Droids. Successfully sneaking past droids grants a full or basic sample at GM's discretion.
- **No sample:** Computers check to slice the bank suffers two Setback dice penalty. Basic sample reduces to one Setback die.

**c) Dealing with Lobot:**
- Lobot monitors Cloud City's networks via cybernetic implant. Three approaches:
  1. **Learn his schedule** and slice when he sleeps (only a few hours/day). Daunting Stealth check to track personally, Hard Streetwise check to hire a tail, or find an informant (extensive roleplaying).
  2. **Divert his attention** by creating a crisis (e.g. false weather emergency at Kerros Tower). Limits his defences rather than removing them.
  3. **Incapacitate him by force** — renders all protection moot but Lobot is protected by Cloud City's best law enforcement. Flagged as a top security threat.
  4. **Ignore Lobot** — proceed anyway. Risks are much greater; the Computers check becomes a Daunting opposed check instead of Hard.

**Expert Help (Hired Slicers):**
- **R3-8C:** Astromech droid with Intellect 3 and Computers 3. Charges 500-1,000 credits (Hard opposed Negotiation check).
- **Naboka:** Ugnaught technician with Intellect 4 and Computers 5. Starts at 3,000 credits (minimum 2,000). Will not risk his life; rats out PCs if things go badly.

#### 5. The Cloud City Grand Prix

**Getting Started:** Arend lends the PCs a Storm IV cloud car from a Wing Guard vehicle berth (secretly appropriated). If the PCs do not have a pilot, Merl Terll or Thera Morrow can be hired.

**The Competition:**

| Team | Pilot | Co-Pilot/Gunner | Strategy |
|------|-------|-----------------|----------|
| **Stormhawks** (MandalMotors, favourites) | Gerrol Hunn | Chara Tull | Push hard early, increase difficulty of their own checks for style once in the lead |
| **Hutt Cartel** (anonymous, linked to Kaltho) | Relgar "The Carbine" Carrae | Garulo (Xexto) | Punch It immediately, maintain speed, gunner fires on opponents freely |
| **Cloud City's Own** (local underdog) | Tarryn Rayzer | K4-76A (security droid) | Accelerate to full speed ASAP, Evasive Manoeuvres if confronted |
| **Player Characters** | PC Pilot | PC Gunner | Player's choice |

**Conducting the Race (7 Legs):**
- Each leg = one competitive Piloting (Planetary) check (difficulty per Stellar Phenomena or Terrain rules, EotE Core Rulebook p.240).
- Each pilot's speed determines relative positioning. Same speed = same range band; each speed difference = one range band apart.
- Pilot with most total Successes leads; Advantages break ties (PCs break further ties). Lead pilot adds a Boost die to next check.
- Place Value: sum of each round's speed. Highest Place Value at the end wins.
- Table 1-4 details spending Advantage, Triumph, Threat, and Despair in racing.

**Race Legs:**

| Leg | Buoys | Hazard | Special Rules |
|-----|-------|--------|---------------|
| 1-3 | 1-3: The Open Stretch | None — standard Piloting checks | Proving grounds. Best time to build a lead. |
| 4 | 3-4: The First Feeding Ground | **Tibannuck** (airborne creatures) | Add two Setback dice to Piloting check. Impacting tibannuck may damage vehicle (1 system strain per Threat; Despair = minor collision, roll Vehicle Critical Hit -20). |
| 5 | 4-5: The Second Feeding Ground | **Beldons** (enormous jellyfish-like creatures) | Add a Setback die to Piloting check. Threat/Threat/Threat or Despair on Piloting = collision with beldon (roll Vehicle Critical Hit). Weapon fire near beldons: roll a Force die; on dark side pips, beldon detonates (Average Piloting to ride turbulence or lose 1 speed). |
| 6 | 5-6: The Doldrums | **Carbine attacks** other racers | Dead zone initially; Carbine fires blaster cannons at leaders in second portion. |
| 7 | 6-7: The Gas Fields | **Volatile gas pockets** | Roll a Force die per racer; on dark side pips, vehicle ignites a gas pocket (Average Piloting to regain control; upgrade once on two dark pips). Trailing vehicles: Hard Piloting to avoid explosion effects. |
| 8 | 7-8: The Gauntlet | **Velkers** (massive ray-like predators) | Gunner must fend off velkers before Piloting check. Two Threat on Gunnery = velker attacks with disruption field. Despair = velker bites hull. |
| — | 8 to City: The Home Stretch | None | Final sprint. No obstacles. |

**The Winnings:** 1st place receives 30,000 credits, a holo-plaque, and invitations to the gala. (Arend Shen's administration skims 10,000 in paperwork, leaving PCs with 20,000.) 2nd and 3rd place also customarily attend gala functions.

**Vehicle Upgrades Available:**
- **Augmented Buffer** (750 credits, 0 HP): +1 system strain threshold.
- **Maneuvering Thrusters** (500 x silhouette credits, 1 HP): +1 handling.
- **Repulsorlift Enhancement** (2,500 credits, 1 HP): As a manoeuvre, suffer 1 system strain for +1 max speed for one round.
- **Upgraded Firepower** (1,000 credits, 0 HP): +1 damage to one weapon system. Hard Mechanics check to install; risk of disqualification if weapons are too powerful.

**Sabotage (Table 1-3):**
- Easy check: failure = sabotage fails / success = adds a Setback die to target's Piloting checks.
- Average: failure = alarm raised / success = adds two Setback dice.
- Hard: failure = alarm raised / success = one vehicle characteristic decreased (PC's choice).
- Daunting: failure = alarm raised / success = two vehicle characteristics decreased.

#### 6. Welcome to the Winner's Circle & Interlude

**Situation:** After the race, the PCs are celebrated (or commiserated with). Media attention. Two hours until the gala begins at the Figg & Associates Art Museum.

**Aris contacts the PCs** via comlink, asking if they need anything else. She reminds them to arrive fashionably late.

**Episode I XP Rewards:**
- Exploring the museum and researching security: 5-10 XP
- Completing a computer spike, sampling encryption, preparing for the slice: 5-10 XP

### Episode II: The Greatest Caper

#### 1. Party on Cloud Nine (The Gala & Auction)

**Situation:** The gala is held at the Figg & Associates Art Museum, beginning in the early evening and continuing to an hour before midnight (just over five hours). Bidders view the gem, submit opening bids, then mingle. Droids relay the current high bid every half-hour; bidders may raise their offers.

**Maintaining Cover:** If the PCs did not win the race, the winning team (likely Stormhawks) is also present. Average Charm or Cool check to avoid drawing attention at the party.

**Running the Auction:**
- Without PC intervention, bids increase by 20,000 credits per round, with one bidder dropping out each round.
- Default drop-out order: Elaiza, Pos Podura, Shreya Ordassa, Mil Mikkir, Vorse Tabarith, Zekra Fol, then Kaltho the Hutt wins at 200,000 credits.
- **Bid Value** system: each bidder starts with a Bid Value equal to the round they would normally leave. Each uncancelled Success on a social check to persuade adds 1 to Bid Value; each Advantage adds 3.
- Bidders increase their own Bid Value each round (representing inherent drive). Kaltho adds 7 per round.
- The highest Bid Value each round sets the high bid. Anyone more than 10 points below the leader drops out.
- If PCs established a favourable impression beforehand, all persuade checks gain a Boost die; successfully increasing a bidder's Bid Value adds 10,000 credits to the high bid for that round.

**Bidder-Specific Approaches:**

**Kaltho the Hutt:** Hard opposed Charm vs. Discipline to persuade him to bid higher. Approaching requires impressing DZ-3PO (Average Charm or Deception). The Hutt is shrewd; GM may spend Despair to make him suspicious.

**Shreya Ordassa:** Hard Charm check to convince her to bid beyond her budget. Telling her Mil Mikkir is the high bidder (Easy Charm if true, Average Deception if false) reduces difficulty by one. Drawing the ex-lovers near each other grants a Boost die.

**Mil Mikkir:** Average Charm check to persuade. Telling him Shreya has taken the lead (Easy Charm if true, Average Deception if false) reduces difficulty by one.

**Pos Podura:** Hard checks to keep him bidding (may suffer additional Setback dice). Arranging a meeting with Shreya removes a Setback die or reduces difficulty.

**Zekra Fol (Tirsia Lek):** Average Charm check (reflects cover identity, not real interest). Once she is the high bidder, attempts to raise further suffer additional Setback dice.

**Elaiza:** Drops out after the first round. Daunting Charm or Coercion may keep her one more round, but her bid does not affect the high bid.

**Vorse Tabarith:** Hard Charm check. Can be plied with drink (200 credits/round). After 1 round of drinking, adds a Boost die to persuade checks. After 2 rounds, adds a second Boost. After 4 rounds, difficulty downgraded once. After 6+ rounds, he passes out.

**Life of the Party (Optional Events):**
- **A Bid for Affection** (Pos Podura & Shreya): Pos may confess his love. Likely results in rejection and Pos fleeing in tears, or Shreya being made jealous of Mil.
- **Blackmail!** (Vorse & Mil Mikkir): Vorse recalls holofootage that could embarrass Mil. If Vorse blackmails Mil, Mil offers to buy the gem for Vorse's silence; Vorse drops out, Mil's Bid Value increases by 2 per round, high bid increases by 5,000 per round.
- **Lover's Spat** (Mil & Shreya): If forced to interact, their conversation escalates from awkward to a heated argument. Hard Charm check to keep it civil; Daunting to defuse. If it escalates, both are removed from bidding. If managed, both increase Bid Value by 2 per subsequent round.
- **Seeing Spy to Spy** (Zekra Fol & Kaltho): Tirsia Lek attempts to pass an encrypted datapad to Kaltho (via the PCs if she has blackmail on them). Complex social manoeuvring required.

**Marus Grayson at the Gala:** Host of the event. Polite to alien bidders but shows clear preference for humans. If PCs mention the Shen family or Gantel Dro, Grayson puts guards on high alert (see Raising the Alarm).

#### 2. Banking on a Break (The Slicing Job)

**Situation:** The gala ends about an hour before midnight. The PCs must secure a remote banking droid to access the Cloud City Central Bank network and divert the winning bid before dawn.

**The Remote Banking Droid:**
- Three G-100 droids operate in different districts:
  1. **Industrial docks** — shipments handled until past midnight. Easy Skulduggery/Stealth to grab.
  2. **Port Town bar** — open until 2am. Average Skulduggery/Stealth.
  3. **Tourist District plaza** — active almost all night. Hard Skulduggery/Stealth.
- Upgrade difficulty if PCs do not silence or incapacitate the droid.

**Slicing Ahead:**
- Hard opposed Computers vs. Discipline check to plug in and disable the droid's motor functions and silent alarm.
- Alternatively: restrain with a bolt (Hard Athletics or Brawl check — success adds Boost dice to the opposed Computers check; failure activates alarm).
- The main slice is an **opposed Computers vs. Computers check** against Lobot's subroutine (effective Intelligence 5, Computers 2).
- All modifiers from preparation (spike, encryption sample, Lobot status, droid incapacitation) apply.
- Table 2-1 details spending Advantage, Triumph, Threat, and Despair during the slicing check.
- **The Press to Success:** If the initial check fails, the slicer may retry up to 3 more times (difficulty upgrades once per retry; half-hour per attempt; previously accumulated Successes may be banked but GM adds Threat for each banked Success).

**Leave No Trace:**
- The banking droid contains a "black box" recording all transactions.
- Dismantling: Daunting Mechanics check (2+ hours of work).
- Disposing of parts: Hard Streetwise check to fence, or dump into Bespin's depths.
- Leaving droid alive: Average Charm vs. Cool check to convince it to become a willing accomplice.

**Off the Record:** Aris Shen has bribed a Wing Guard controller. If PCs encounter the Wing Guard, Aris creates a distraction once (fake emergency on Level 224). This only works once.

#### 3. Double or Nothing (The Museum Heist)

**Situation:** Between midnight and dawn, the PCs must break into the museum and steal the Jewel of Yavin. Elaiza is also attempting to steal the gem, following the PCs at a distance (opposed Perception vs. Elaiza's Stealth).

**Breaking & Entering Options:**

| Method | Route | Key Checks | Advantages | Risks |
|--------|-------|------------|------------|-------|
| **Central Wind Corridor (from above)** | Rappel down from upper levels to garbage chute | Opposed Deception vs. Discipline (distract guards), opposed Stealth vs. Vigilance, Average Skulduggery (door without key) | Most sensible — no bystanders, no property damage | Upper entrances more heavily guarded; rawwk nests in corridor |
| **Central Wind Corridor (from Port Town)** | Climb up from Port Town hatches | Hard Streetwise (find hatch) or Ugnaught contacts (50 credits bribe); Average-Hard Athletics (depending on speed: 1 hour leisurely, 45 min Average, 30 min Hard) | Avoids Wing Guard on upper levels | Long, exhausting climb; rawwk nests; garbage dumps at intervals |
| **Adjacent Buildings (Hungry Beldon restaurant or Trin Bin casino)** | Break through shared wall | Average Charm/Deception (avoid suspicion inside), Average Perception (target entry point), Average Mechanics (breach wall) | Can choose entry room | Noisy — alerts security droids and guards; bouncers in both establishments |
| **Hiding Until Closing** | Stay in museum after hours | Opposed Stealth check vs. WA-7 droid | Already inside | Cannot assist with the slicing job at midnight; limited hiding spots |
| **Frontal Assault** | Break down front doors | Daunting Computers check (bypass security), or Hard Mechanics (breach) | Direct | Immediately alerts all security; Wing Guard response in 5 minutes |

**Hazards in the Garbage Chute:**
- Four loads of debris dumped per night from the cleaning room. Average Athletics or Coordination check to dodge (Boost die if on guard).
- **Rawwk Nest:** Hard Perception check to spot brooding rawwks. Average Athletics to divert course. If disturbed, one minion group of four rawwks per PC attacks.

**Arousing Suspicion (adds Setback dice to guard Vigilance/Perception checks):**
- Asking about garbage disposal without good excuse
- Asking the Exex for schematics
- Getting ejected from the museum
- Failing three times to find an arms dealer
- Resorting to threats/violence with Burr, Gantel, or Nallith
- Entering central wind corridor without a cover story

**Guards in the Museum:**
- Six guards at all times (one squad leader + five minions). Two in auction room, two on patrol (following security droid), two relaxing in tapcafe.
- Shift change at 3:00-3:30am (twelve guards present for ~10 minutes).
- Guards fight bravely; retreat to auction room if attacked, take cover (defense 1), shoot nearest target.
- If squad leaders become casualties, remaining guards fight until only two remain, then attempt to flee and alert Wing Guard.

**Security Measures:**
- **Cameras:** One per room (top right corner). Hard Perception to spot. Not monitored after hours but recording. Easy Computers to find feed; Average Computers to disable; Hard Computers to feed-loop.

- **Security Droid:** Patrols on a 60-minute circuit following the dotted route on the map. Spends 5 minutes per area. Does not check storeroom, tapcafe kitchen, refreshers, or cleaning room.
- **Cleaning Droids:** CLE-004 (window cleaner) and T3 (floor polisher). Follow security droid route. Make Perception check vs. target's Stealth if they encounter something suspicious.

### Episode III: Escape to the Clouds

#### 1. The Getaway (Arend's Betrayal)

**Situation:** After the heist, the PCs' easiest escape is to contact Arend Shen. He responds enthusiastically: *"Magnificent! I knew you could do it!... An automated speeder will arrive in a few minutes and transport you to a safe location so we can discuss how to handle the transfer of the gem. Well done!"*

**The Trap:** The automated droid-driven airspeeder is rigged with a powerful explosive set to detonate thirty seconds after the PCs board.

**Detonation Detection:**
- Average Mechanics or Computers check: alerts a character to significant alterations in the speeder's systems.
- Hard Perception check: notice something is wrong if no suitable technical skills.
- Force-sensitive character: GM may employ *deus ex machina* — ominous flash or gut instinct.

**Defusing the Bomb:**
- Hard Mechanics or Cool check to disengage. On failure, the PCs see the timer counting down ("three, two, one...").
- If defusing fails, forcing control of the airspeeder toward a control vane or landing platform: Average Piloting (Planetary) check.
- Jumping free: Easy Athletics or Coordination check (increase to Average if speeder is not near a platform).
- Whoever has the gem: Hard Coordination check to keep hold of it during the fall.

**If the Bomb Detonates:**
- Blast deals 10 damage (mitigated by soak) plus falling damage. Light side Destiny Points can introduce narrative elements (railings, platforms) to break the fall.

#### 2. Time for a New Plan (Multi-Faction Pursuit)

**Situation:** The PCs are immediately attacked by four agents of Arend Shen on an Arrow-7 airspeeder (starting at medium range). If the PCs did not report in to Arend, his goons track them down shortly after they leave the museum.

**Three Enemy Factions:**

**a) Shen's Network:**
- Disgraced former Cloud City civil servants. Little to lose. Four goons per Arrow-7 airspeeder; Aris Shen and two groups of four goons pursue into the holo-park.
- If PCs have not befriended Aris, she is Arend's point person for hunting them.

**b) Grayson's Security Forces:**
- Professional private security, vested interest in recovering the gem. Led by Gantel Dro (whose life depends on recovering it) and Burr Naxis.
- They ignore Cloud City rules if they believe they can recover the gem.

**c) Captain Selis and the Wing Guard:**
- As soon as the Jewel is noticed missing, Captain Bialar Selis is put on the case. Professional, tenacious, well-respected. Will not be bribed. His priority is to ensure the PCs cannot retreat into the city from the starport.

**Sweet Revenge (Optional — Hunting Arend Shen):**
- PCs can search for Arend via any computer terminal: Average Computers or Streetwise check reveals four holdings:
  1. Private residence in the plaza district (d100: found on 25 or lower at first location, 50 or lower at second, 75 or lower at third; automatic at fourth).
  2. Office near Figg Hall.
  3. Port Town merchant district building.
  4. Private yacht *Venture* docked at the plaza level.
- Each time PCs travel to a new location: Hard Deception, Stealth, or Streetwise check to avoid pursuers (Despair = one group finds them; Despair + Threat = two groups; Despair + Despair = all three converge).

**Arend Shen's Holdings:**
- **Residence:** Guarded by three K4 security droids. If Arend is present, he is in his private room; sitting room occupied by half a dozen goons.
- **Office:** Four of Grayson's security guards present (contracted by Arend). Emergency blast doors.
- **Port Town shops:** Ten goons along the boardwalk watching for the PCs.
- **Yacht (*Venture*):** Docked at Cloudscape Tours. If Arend is aboard, he reactivates stolen Wing Guard transponders, alerting them. Yacht crew (three groups of three) defend.

**With Friends Like These (Aris's Betrayal):**
- If PCs did not befriend Aris, she actively hunts them alongside two groups of four goons.
- She follows them into the holo-park, pursuing alongside Grayson's security and the Wing Guard.

#### 3. Flight over Fight (Starport Escape)

**Situation:** All three factions are monitoring the starport. The PCs must get to their ship (or steal one) and escape Bespin.

**The Grand Galaxy Holo-Park:**
- Only direct avenue between starport sectors passes through the holo-park.
- Holographic environments shift every two rounds (structured time) or every few minutes (narrative).
- Roll on **Table 3-1: Holo-Park Planets** (d100):
  - 1-23: **Alderaan** (rolling hills, pastoral beauty).
  - 24-47: **Christophsis** (crystal spires, ornamental fountains).
  - 47-71: **Coruscant** (cityscape, airspeeder traffic).
  - 72-95: **Kashyyyk** (wroshyr tree walkways, vertiginous drops).
  - 96-100: **Shut down** (Wing Guard cuts holograms, revealing bare room).

Each planet has unique Advantage, Triumph, Threat, and Despair effects for combat within the holo-park (detailed environmental interactions).

**The firefight** is a chase scene ending when: PCs reach long range from pursuers, they eliminate/scatter their enemies, or 10 rounds elapse.

**After the Battle:**
- All exits blocked by Wing Guard. PCs must push deeper into the starport.
- **Options:**
  - Find maintenance crawlways or sewers (uncomfortable but effective).
  - Push to their ship or steal one: Hard Computers check to access; all Piloting (Space) checks upgraded once due to lack of warm-up.
  - Blast out (adds 10 points of Obligation: Criminal).
- Once airborne, at least a dozen Wing Guard cloud cars pursue in squadrons of four.

**Going Respectable (Alternative):**
- PCs can turn themselves in to the Wing Guard or Baron Administrator Calrissian. If they help expose Arend and repair Cloud City's reputation, Calrissian may pardon their crimes (incurring 5 points of Obligation: Favour each, to be called in later).

**Episode III XP Rewards:**
- Successfully losing pursuers in the holo-park: 10 XP
- Allying with Aris Shen: 5 XP
- Tracking down and defeating Arend Shen: 15 XP
- Defeating or evading cloud car patrols: 5 XP
- Escaping Bespin with the Jewel of Yavin: 5 XP

## Solo Play Notes

### General Adaptation

The Jewel of Yavin is designed for a party of 3-5 PCs. For solo play, significant adjustments are needed:

1. **Action Economy:** The adventure requires simultaneous activity across multiple fronts (racing, slicing, bidding, heisting). A solo PC cannot do everything alone. Consider:
   - Aris Shen as an active companion NPC handling social/bidding tasks while the PC focuses on the heist or race.
   - Hired NPC slicers (R3-8C or Naboka) become essential rather than optional.
   - A hired pilot (Merl Terll or Thera Morrow) is practically mandatory for the Grand Prix.

2. **The Grand Prix:** Split the party normally allows pilot and gunner PCs plus spectators making Leadership/Mechanics/Perception checks from the grandstands. For solo play, assign an NPC co-pilot and have the non-participating tasks handled narratively or with a single check.

3. **The Auction (Bidding War):** A single PC cannot work all seven bidders simultaneously. Simplify:
   - Reduce the number of active bidders to 4-5.
   - Allow the PC to focus on 2-3 bidders per round, with Aris handling the others (at reduced effectiveness — GM rolls Average checks for Aris's efforts).
   - Consider reducing the number of bidding rounds from 10 to 5-7.

4. **The Heist:** This is the most solo-friendly section. A single capable PC (especially one with Stealth, Skulduggery, and Athletics) can navigate the museum alone. Key adjustments:
   - Reduce guard numbers: 4 guards instead of 6 (one squad leader + three minions).
   - Security droid patrol is unchanged — timing and stealth remain the core challenge.
   - The Elaiza confrontation should be a tense negotiation rather than a fight the solo PC cannot win.

5. **Episode III Escape:** Three factions pursuing a solo PC is overwhelming. Choose the single most dramatically appropriate faction (usually the Wing Guard under Captain Selis) and have the others arrive as complications rather than simultaneous combatants. The holo-park chase works well for solo play as a series of skill checks rather than a protracted firefight.

### Scaling Difficulty

- Remove minion groups or reduce their size (groups of 2-3 instead of 4-5).
- Decrease the defences and wound thresholds of rivals and nemeses.
- Subtract ranks from NPC characteristics and skills.
- Grant the solo PC an additional NPC companion (Aris Shen is the natural choice, with her full stat block available).

### Key Decision Points for Solo Play

| Decision | Recommended Solo Approach |
|----------|--------------------------|
| Who flies in the Grand Prix? | Hire Merl Terll or Thera Morrow; PC acts as gunner or spectator |
| Who handles the slicing? | Hire Naboka (Computers 5) or R3-8C (Computers 3); PC handles the banking droid acquisition |
| Who works the bidders? | PC focuses on 2-3 key bidders; simplify the rest |
| Museum entry method? | Garbage chute from Port Town (least combat, most skill-check focused) |
| Elaiza confrontation? | Negotiate — offer to help her find another corusca gem; avoid combat |
| Arend Shen pursuit? | Choose one or two of his holdings to investigate; skip the multi-location hunt |
| Starport escape? | Steal the *Venture* (Arend's yacht) for a dramatic and fitting conclusion |

### Obligation and Motivation Hooks (Solo)

The "Thick as Thieves" subplot works best for solo play — the PC has a personal history with one of Grayson's security team members or Vorse Tabarith's organisation, providing built-in social connections and dramatic tension throughout the adventure.

### Suggested Subplots for Solo PCs

| Subplot | Obligation | Motivation |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Fourth Wheel | Betrayal, Blackmail, Debt, Favour | Childhood Friends, Extended Family/Clan, Friendship, Love, Place of Origin |
| Proving Yourself | Addiction, Betrayal, Obsession | Expertise, Greed, Fame, Status, Wanderlust/Novelty |
| Mysterious Benefactor | Blackmail, Bounty, Criminal, Debt, Favour | Freedom, Greed, Local Politics, Power |
| Something Greater | Dutybound, Family, Favour, Oath, Responsibility | Charity, Love, Overthrow the Empire, Support the Empire |
| Thick as Thieves | Addiction, Criminal, Debt, Family | Comrades, Crime, Former Nemesis, Friendship |

### Extended Campaign Hooks

**Did Arend Shen Survive?**
- If alive: places bounties, blacklists the PCs' ship at reputable spaceports across the Outer Rim.
- If incarcerated: makes every sacrifice to reach beyond his imprisonment for vengeance.
- If killed: no post-mortem contingency plans — but Aris Shen may seek her own revenge or gratitude.

**Who Has the Gem?**
- If the PCs keep it: extremely valuable but very difficult to sell. Those wealthy enough to pay a suitable price (starting at 50,000 credits via Kaltho) will likely try to take it by force. The gem is worth at least 50 Obligation.
- If traded: can significantly reduce existing Obligation (50 points spread among the party).
- Pos Podura will pay 20,000 credits to give it to Shreya, no questions asked.

**Who Is Hunting Whom?**
- If the Wing Guard believes the PCs are solely responsible: 5+ points each of Bounty and Criminal Obligation. Bounty hunters will be dispatched.
- If Arend's involvement is exposed: the Wing Guard is mollified, though the PCs' crimes are not entirely forgiven.

**Working with the Windfall:**
- Pay off ship-related Obligation.
- Purchase a share in a "shipping enterprise" or smuggling ring.
- Secure a permanent docking bay for storing supplies and hiding from Imperials.
