# Long Arm of the Hutt — EotE Adventure

> Adventure module for the star-wars-eote skill. Follow-up to Escape from Mos Shuuta.

## Setting

The adventure spans multiple worlds across the Outer Rim:

- **Aboard the *Krayt Fang*** — the stolen YT-1300 freighter the PCs acquired during Escape from Mos Shuuta. The ship is in poor condition with several issues requiring attention.
- **Ryloth** — a harsh, arid world home to the Twi'lek species. The PCs visit the settlement of Nabat (an underground city) and the remote ryll mining town of New Meen in the wastelands.
- **Geonosis** — an insectile hive world close to Tatooine. The PCs attend a private party at Gogum Hive hosted by Duke Piddock, then travel to Trellik Hive to meet Duke Dimmock.
- **Tatooine / Mos Shuuta** — the PCs return to Teemo the Hutt's palace for the final confrontation.

The adventure takes place during the Imperial era, after the Battle of Yavin. The Empire maintains a presence on both Ryloth and Geonosis, and Imperial patrols are a background threat throughout.

## The Situation

The PCs have escaped Teemo the Hutt aboard the *Krayt Fang*, but the outstanding obligation they owe him is far from paid. Teemo is furious and has hired bounty hunters to track them down. His influence reaches across the Outer Rim — he is coercing a Twi'lek ryll mining community on Ryloth, dealing with Geonosian weapons manufacturers, and stockpiling military equipment to seize control of Tatooine's criminal underworld.

The PCs must:

1. Survive the journey to Ryloth and deal with the *Krayt Fang*'s many problems.
2. Liberate the Twi'lek mining settlement of New Meen from Teemo's thugs.
3. Travel to Geonosis to gather intelligence and turn Teemo's Geonosian allies against him.
4. Return to Mos Shuuta and infiltrate Teemo's palace to confront the Hutt — either killing him, blackmailing him, or passing evidence of his misdeeds to Jabba the Hutt.

## Encounter Flow

### Act 1 — Rugged Road to the Ryll Mines

#### 1.1 Exploring the *Krayt Fang*

**Situation:** The PCs have escaped Mos Shuuta but the ship has four urgent issues: alert icons on the nav computer (low fuel, crescent moon/fang icon, Trandoshan pop-up, cage icon), a terrible stench, a loud klaxon with Gamorrean Opera, and hidden cargo (Wookiee pelts and a Twi'lek prisoner).

**Mandatory tasks before reaching Ryloth:**
- Plot a course via the nav computer (Ryloth is the only viable destination)
- Find B'ura B'an in the number three cargo hold

**Optional tasks:**
- Find Geonosian chitin fragments (Hard Knowledge check to identify origin)
- Investigate the smell and find Wookiee pelts in hidden smuggling compartment (Average Perception check)
- Discover the hyperspace transponder (Hard Computers check to locate; Average Mechanics check to remove on ground; Easy Computers check to slow signal rate)
- Turn off the klaxon (Easy Computers check)
- Turn off the Gamorrean Opera music (Average Computers check)
- Read the crescent moon icon / Trandoshan text (Easy Computers check to translate)
- Read the cage icon — reveals B'ura B'an is a captive militant
- Read the intermittent pop-up — delivery notice for Wookiee skins destined for Teemo

**Skill checks:** Computers (Easy to Hard), Perception (Average), Knowledge (Hard), Mechanics (Average to Hard)

**NPCs present:** B'ura B'an (freed prisoner, provides backstory and asks to be taken to Nabat)

**Smell penalties:**
- Mathus, Oskara, Pash, Sasha: +1 Setback die to all activities
- Lowhhrick: +2 Setback dice to all activities; +2 Setback to others healing him
- 41-VEX: unaffected by smell but suffers 2 Setback dice if aiding Lowhhrick

**Noise penalty (until klaxon/music resolved):** Upgrade difficulty of concentration tasks by replacing one purple Difficulty die with a red Challenge die.

**PC options:**
- Jettison the Wookiee pelts to remove the smell (Easy Computers check for in-flight loading)
- Plot course to Ryloth (Easy Astrogation check; failure means longer route but still arrive)
- Slow the transponder signal before entering hyperspace to prevent bounty hunter interception

#### 1.2 The Hyperspace Route to Ryloth

**Situation:** The journey takes about six hours. The PCs can rest, recuperate, talk to B'ura B'an, and explore the ship.

**Ship areas to explore:**
- *Cockpit* — nav computer interface
- *Number Two Hold* — broken droid parts, oily tools (Hard Mechanics check reveals parts for an astromech worth 2,000 credits)
- *Main Cargo Hold* — empty crates, hidden smuggling compartment, holochess set
- *Galley* — raw meat, six ration packs
- *Crew Quarters* — three beds, wampa skin rug (250 credits), glow rod, two power pack reloads, heavy blaster pistol stashed under a bed
- *Engineering* — engines, fuel reserves, hand scanner, hypermatter reactor igniter
- *Number Three Hold* — B'ura B'an's cell, binders, chitin fragments

**NPCs present:** B'ura B'an

**B'ura B'an's story:** He organised resistance to Aqualish thugs extorting Twi'lek miners. Was attacked by Trex (who severed his left lekku) and imprisoned. He asks to be dropped at Nabat where he has friends. Promises the PCs lucrative ryll smuggling opportunities if they help save the mine at New Meen.

#### 1.3 A Violent Interception (skippable)

**Situation:** If the PCs did NOT slow the transponder signal, Thwheek the Kubaz spy intercepts them as they emerge from hyperspace above Ryloth in his custom Dunelizard starfighter. He attacks at Short range, attempting to cripple the *Krayt Fang* before it can land.

**If PCs slowed the transponder:** Skip this encounter entirely.

**Combat:** Space combat. Thwheek uses Stay on Target manoeuvre initially, switches to Evasive Manoeuvres after taking a hit. If his ship takes 5+ hull trauma he flees into Ryloth's atmosphere.

**Skill checks:** Cool (initiative), Gunnery, Pilot, Resilience (if crash-landing)

**If the *Krayt Fang* is shot down:** Hard Pilot check to crash-land near Nabat. Hard Resilience check for each crew member (failure = Critical Injury; each Threat = 1 wound). Pilot success upgrades Resilience once.

**NPCs present:** Thwheek (Kubaz Spy)

**Outcomes:**
- PCs victorious: Thwheek crash-lands, out of action for the foreseeable future
- *Krayt Fang* shot down: PCs crash near Nabat, strong incentive to help B'ura B'an get ship repaired

#### 1.4 Arriving at Nabat

**Situation:** B'ura B'an leads the PCs underground into Nabat's tunnel complex. He arranges docking, repairs, and removal of the transponder. They meet Nyn Kablo and her militant cell. All wounds and strain healed; a Twi'lek physician attempts to heal each Critical Injury (dice pool of 3 Ability dice).

**Nyn's request:** Return B'ura B'an to New Meen to boost morale. She provides a land speeder programmed to navigate to the mines. In exchange, she promises to help bring retribution to Teemo once they regain control of their mining operations.

**Negotiating for gear:**
- Average Charm check OR Hard Coerce check to get Nyn to open her armoury
- 1 Success: One frag or stun grenade per PC
- 2 Successes: Also worn but functional padded armour
- 3 Successes (Triumph): Also a single blaster rifle
- 4+ Successes (Despair): Equipment is defective, must be discarded after first encounter

**A message from the spaceport:** If PCs asked to be informed of arrivals from Tatooine, they learn a Human and a Gand arrived two hours ago — bounty hunters are on their trail.

**Skill checks:** Charm (Average), Coerce (Hard)

**NPCs present:** B'ura B'an, Nyn Kablo, unnamed militants (3 Twi'leks, 2 Humans)

**PC options:**
- Shop in Nabat (items up to 500 credits at listed price)
- Rest overnight before departing in the morning
- Ask for equipment from Nyn's cell

#### 1.5 Ambush at the Old Lylek Den

**Situation:** Travelling by speeder across Ryloth's badlands toward the ryll mines. The terrain becomes hazardous. The PCs are ambushed by bounty hunters (one per PC) at an old lylek den — a cave in a rocky bluff. One hunter is a Gand; another may be Trex (depending on events in Escape from Mos Shuuta). The rest are human.

**Approach:**
- If PCs slow down: Navigate safely
- If PCs do not slow down: Two successive Average Pilot checks required. Each failure = 1 strain to all on board from collision.
- Spotting the ambush: Hard Perception check to notice the cave entrance and an insect-like head ducking inside

**Speeder crash:** Average Resilience or Average Coordination check (player's choice). Failure = flung from craft, 6 wounds damage. Speeder can be repaired with Easy Mechanics check and 30 minutes' work.

**The bounty hunters' ruse:** They claim to be Imperial agents investigating ryll smuggling. This is a lie; they are not above brutality but would not shoot unidentified speeders without cause. PCs can recognise the Gand and possibly Trex. If Oskara is present, she recognises the humans as cheap bounty hunters from Mos Shuuta's cantinas.

**Combat options:**
- PCs did not slow down: bounty hunters each suffer a Setback die to Cool checks
- Rock piles provide cover; shooting at someone behind rocks adds 2 Setback dice to Ranged checks
- PCs can flank the hunters by scouting around the east side of the bluff (partially blocked entrance)
- Hunters prefer to capture alive (weapons set to stun initially); fight in earnest once one of their number falls
- If hunters retreat toward the southern cavern, they may disturb a bull lylek

**The Lylek:** If combat pushes southward or PCs roll an Advantage during a fight near the lylek's nest, the beast emerges. Use Captive Rancor stats (p.47 of Beginner Game Rulebook). The lylek attacks the nearest creature; B'ura B'an advises leaving it alone.

**After combat:** PCs can loot the bounty hunters' speeder — two stimpaks, a medpac, and an emergency repair kit.

**Skill checks:** Pilot (Average), Perception (Hard), Resilience (Average), Coordination (Average), Mechanics (Easy), Cool (initiative), Ranged, Brawl

**NPCs present:** Bounty Hunters (one per PC), possibly Trex, B'ura B'an

#### 1.6 Arriving at New Meen

**Situation:** New Meen is a shanty mining town under threat from Angu Drombb's "developers." The residents are initially suspicious but celebrate when they see B'ura B'an. The PCs learn the full story of Drombb's intimidation campaign.

**The story of Drombb:**
- Human contractor bought land nearby, claiming to build a leisure complex — actually muscling in on the ryll trade for Teemo
- His thugs have damaged power supplies, committed arson and larceny
- Twi'leks complained to feudal oligarchs but the land was clearly bought by whoever backs Drombb
- Twelve thugs total (mostly human, some Aqualish), operating in minion groups of four
- Scale thugs to party size: 4 PCs = 12 thugs (3 groups of 4); 3 PCs = 9 (3 groups of 3); 2 PCs = 6 (3 groups of 2)

**NPCs present:** B'ura B'an, residents of New Meen

**PC options:**
- Go directly to confront Drombb ("Taking the Fight to Drombb")
- Stay the night and potentially face the "Drunk and Disorderly" encounter
- Do nothing (Twi'leks eventually abandon the mine; B'ura B'an is very disappointed)
- Escort the Twi'leks back to Nabat instead

#### 1.7 Drunk and Disorderly (optional)

**Situation:** If the PCs stay the night at New Meen (and did not immediately go to Drombb), they are awoken by the sound of a revving vehicle and a crash. A group of Drombb's thugs (one minion group) have got drunk and crashed a tracked construction vehicle into a dwelling, destroying a vapour condenser. They demand the PCs' speeder.

**Combat:** The thugs are drunk and belligerent. They draw pistols and fight the moment anyone speaks out of turn. Setback die added to each action due to darkness.

**If Twi'leks were given weapons:** They join the fight using the Human and Aqualish Thugs minion profile.

**Skill checks:** Combat skills, Cool (initiative)

**NPCs present:** One minion group of Human and Aqualish Thugs

#### 1.8 Taking the Fight to Drombb

**Situation:** Drombb's settlement is about a mile south of New Meen. It consists of a vehicle parking bay, a prefabricated apartment, and a cantina. Nine humans and three Aqualish remain (some may have been killed in the earlier encounter). They operate in three minion groups of four.

**Approaching the settlement:**
- Without precautions: Spotted; a delegation of thugs meets the PCs aggressively
- With precautions: Drombb's men are distracted by their cantina; PCs can approach without skill checks
- If Twi'leks are armed, they form their own minion group (eager and rash — PCs should calm them)

**Non-violent options:**
- *Charming the thugs*: Does not work; they use any truce to lick wounds, reposition, and resume fighting
- *Intimidating after a show of force*: Coerce check after killing some thugs. Difficulty depends on how many slain: 1-4 thugs = Hard; 5-8 = Average; 9+ = Easy
- *Bribing the thugs*: Each thug expects ~500 credits. Offering 400-500 per thug = Negotiate check (opposed). Less than 400 = thugs refuse. Drombb cannot be bribed.

**Fighting in the cantina:** Drombb and remaining thugs make a last stand inside. They are drunk and expect no quarter. The cantina layout is similar to the one in Mos Shuuta.

**After victory:**
- Drombb's safe (around his neck or blasted open, or Hard Skulduggery check): 2,000 credits
- Drombb's office computer (Hard Computers check): Two messages to "Thwheek" discussing Sivor's death, Duke Piddock, and Duke Dimmock — clues for Act 2
- Cantina: plenty of drink and ration packets

**If PCs lose:** Bound and prepared for transport to Tatooine, but rescued by B'ura B'an and Ota, who lead them out and propose a new plan (proceed to Act 2).

**Skill checks:** Coerce (variable), Negotiate (opposed), Skulduggery (Hard), Computers (Hard), combat skills

**NPCs present:** Angu Drombb, Human and Aqualish Thugs (minion groups)

#### 1.9 Liberation of New Meen

**Situation:** With Drombb defeated, victorious Twi'leks ransack the settlement. PCs can loot and search for clues. B'ura B'an explains the PCs' heroism to the residents. The Twi'leks celebrate and invite the PCs to drink the cantina dry.

**Loot:** Drombb's safe (2,000 credits), messages on his computer, drinks and rations.

**NPCs present:** B'ura B'an, Twi'lek miners

### Act 2 — Geonosian Negotiations

#### 2.1 A New Day in New Meen / Return to Nabat

**Situation:** The Twi'leks celebrate for a week. They have little to offer as reward but provide free use of facilities, stimpaks, and rations. The journey back to Nabat is uneventful.

**At Nabat:** The spaceport is busy with traders. If PCs are alert, a Hard Perception check spots Thwheek (the Kubaz spy) surveying them with electrobinoculars. If PCs move toward him, he slips into the tunnels, leaving discarded insect parts on the ground (he was snacking).

**Kubaz knowledge:** Average Knowledge check reveals Kubaz are clannish mammals from Kubindi, eat insects, and have poor relations with insectile species like Geonosians.

**Skill checks:** Perception (Hard), Knowledge (Average)

**NPCs present:** Thwheek (observing), B'ura B'an

#### 2.2 Reuniting with Nyn / Meeting Ota

**Situation:** Nyn warmly welcomes the PCs back. She introduces Ota, a Bothan spy of great repute, who has been looking for the PCs.

**Ota's proposition:**
- Shows the PCs a holographic bounty notice: "Wanted: 50,000 credits reward" with their faces, broadcast on encrypted bounty hunter frequencies
- Offers to send them to a private party hosted by Duke Piddock (Geonosian) to make contact with those dealing with Teemo
- Their true goal: find out why Piddock stopped dealing with Teemo, then use that information to convince Duke Dimmock (Teemo's current Geonosian partner) to turn against the Hutt
- Ota promises to see that any bounty on their heads is removed and that they are rewarded with credits from Teemo's own coffers
- Ota provides a communicator with an encrypted signal to pass Geonosian security

**Cover story needed:** Ota poses as an arms dealer; the PCs are his representatives wanting to buy weapons from Piddock. They need a second cover story for other guests.

**Nyn's help:** She offers to supply barrels of rycrit stew as trade goods for credibility. She also offers 3,000 credits to buy Geonosian weapons for her militant cell.

**What PCs know to use against Teemo (five key pieces):**
1. Teemo keeps Thwheek, an insectivorous Kubaz spy, on his books (Geonosians hate Kubaz)
2. Geonosian chitin from Sivor was found aboard Trex's ship
3. Thwheek (working for Teemo) killed Sivor the Geonosian
4. Teemo collaborates with the Empire
5. Teemo plans to reverse-engineer a B1 Battle Droid to produce his own

**Skill checks:** None required at this stage

**NPCs present:** Nyn Kablo, Ota (Bothan spy), B'ura B'an

#### 2.3 Duke Piddock's Party

**Situation:** The PCs travel to Gogum Hive on Geonosis. Geonosians escort them to the venue. Offensive weapons must be left on the *Krayt Fang* (pistols allowed). The party is held in Duke Piddock's new cantina — a lavish underground chamber with a jatz band, a bar manned by Geonosian workers and a Roche J9 protocol droid (BG-222).

**Guests at the party (each with cover stories, real motives, and information to share):**

- **Duke Piddock** — Host. Grand old Geonosian. Wants to sell weapons. Will answer four questions before becoming impatient. Can reveal why he dropped Teemo (Kubaz spy was the last straw; suspects Sivor was killed). Hard Charm check reveals Teemo wanted battle droid schematics.
- **BG-222** — Roche J9 protocol droid. Cheerful, over-enthusiastic. Talks in insect metaphors. Can identify Sivor's shell. Knows all guests' cover stories.
- **Mu Nanb** — Shy young Sullustan. Cover: tourist. Reality: buying weapons for the Rebel Alliance. Knows Teemo collaborated with the Empire (his friends were caught smuggling and shipped to Kessel after Teemo informed on them). Requires Average Charm check (after expressing anti-Empire sentiment) to open up.
- **Vrixx'tt** — Large Gand in traditional garb. Cover: representative of insectile species organisation. Reality: buying weapons for Gand bounty hunters. After ~30 minutes, checks his wrist communicator and discovers the bounty on the PCs (Average Perception check to notice). Becomes very interested in the PCs. Will confront them later on Gantry AA7.
- **Maru Jakkar** — Elegant human woman. Cover: buying antiques. Reality: frontwoman for the Black Sun syndicate. Requires Average Charm check (discussing alien culture) to open up. Knows Teemo is making suspicious purchases that relate to B1 Battle Droid designs. Oskara or Lowhhrick get a Boost die on Charm checks (she finds aliens fascinating). Upgrade difficulty if 41-VEX is present (she despises droids).
- **Anatta** — Shifty light-blue-skinned Toydarian. Cover: "just loves a good party." Reality: information broker for Jabba the Hutt. Most gregarious guest. Sells gossip for 20-100 credits per item. Key information available (see The Information Broker below).
- **Orpa and Wex Vio** — Young human siblings from Corellia. Cover: none (they have nothing to hide). Reality: freelance smugglers. Fly the *Lucky Guess* (Nova Courier). Have a contract with Duke Dimmock to deliver cargo to Teemo tomorrow. Can be hired to smuggle PCs into Mos Shuuta (1,000 credits each, Negotiate check can reduce to 250 each). This allows PCs to skip the rest of Act 2.

**Piddock's weapons for sale:**
- Geonosian Heavy Blaster Pistol — 1,100 credits
- Geonosian Blaster Carbine — 1,300 credits (Critical 2)
- Geonosian Blaster Rifle — 1,500 credits
- Opposed Negotiate check for 10% discount

**Sivor's Shell:** If shown to Piddock or BG-222, they positively identify it. Piddock is curious where the PCs got it. Anatta can also identify it (charges 60 credits).

**Breaches of etiquette:**
- First offence: Average Charm check to apologise and remain
- Second offence: Hard Charm check
- Third offence: Forcibly escorted to sleeping quarters

**The Information Broker (Anatta):** Prefers to talk in private rooms. Sells information at fixed prices:
- Teemo has a Kubaz spy called Thwheek — 40 credits
- Teemo's relationship with Jabba (junior Hutt, too ambitious, Jabba would smack him down) — 60 credits
- Sivor's shell belonged to a Geonosian, one of Trex's last jobs — 60 credits
- Who is Sivor? Worker drone, used by Piddock as a technician — 20 credits
- What happened to Sivor / did Thwheek kill him? Sivor killed in a gladiatorial bout, Teemo let Thwheek deliver the killing blow — 100 credits
- Anatta works for Jabba — 40 credits
- Overheard Mu Nanb talking to Piddock about a huge weapons deal — 100 credits
- Corellian siblings are fast and reliable smugglers — 40 credits
- Vrixx'tt is a bounty hunter — 80 credits
- Maru Jakkar is a bigot who gives money to anti-droid organisations — 60 credits
- Duke Piddock and Dimmock both protest Geonosian subjugation under the Empire but might bond if not rivals — 80 credits

**Anatta's alternate solution:** If PCs mention a Teemo-Jabba conflict, Anatta offers to pass incriminating information to Jabba to "deal with" Teemo. Requires concrete proof found inside Teemo's palace (evidence of combat droid manufacturing, spying on Jabba, or ryll distribution without Jabba's cut). Anatta will pay PCs 500 credits each for sufficient evidence.

**Skill checks:** Charm (Average to Hard), Perception (Average), Negotiate (Average, opposed), Knowledge (various)

**NPCs present:** Duke Piddock, BG-222, Mu Nanb, Vrixx'tt, Maru Jakkar, Anatta, Orpa Vio, Wex Vio, Geonosian soldier drones (guards)

#### 2.4 An Audience with Duke Dimmock

**Situation:** The PCs travel to Trellik Hive. They are escorted before Duke Dimmock, who sits upon a throne in a passageway lined with a hundred Geonosian soldiers. He is impatient and supercilious but willing to hear them out as a favour to Ota.

**Points system — evidence against Teemo:**

*Points for:*
- PCs are polite and pass Average Charm check — +1
- PCs express enmity toward the Empire — +1
- PCs present any of the five key facts (1 point each):
  1. Teemo keeps a Kubaz spy
  2. Thwheek (Kubaz) killed Sivor
  3. Teemo collaborates with the Empire
  4. Teemo plans to reverse-engineer B1 Battle Droids
- PCs show Sivor's carapace and explain its provenance — +1
- PCs show Drombb's communications to Thwheek — +1

*Points against:*
- PCs are rude — -1
- PCs threaten and fail a Daunting Coerce check — -2
- PCs express loyalty to the Empire — -1

**Results:**
- **0-2 points:** Dimmock remains loyal to Teemo. Turns a blind eye but offers no help. PCs face 5 Geonosian soldiers on Gantry AA7.
- **3-5 points:** Dimmock is lukewarm. Offers limited help — tells PCs to wait 20 minutes before boarding the *Lucky Guess*, expects some resistance. PCs face 3 Geonosians on Gantry AA7.
- **6+ points:** Dimmock is fully convinced. Clears the way entirely. PCs face no Geonosians on Gantry AA7.

**Skill checks:** Charm (Average), Coerce (Daunting — risky)

**NPCs present:** Duke Dimmock, Geonosian soldiers

#### 2.5 Assault on Gantry AA7

**Situation:** The PCs must board the *Lucky Guess* on Gantry AA7. The number of Geonosian soldiers guarding the ship depends on how well they negotiated with Dimmock (0, 3, or 5). The Geonosians use stun settings and crates provide cover (Setback die to Ranged checks when shooting at someone behind a crate).

**Complication — Vrixx'tt:** The Gand bounty hunter from the party appears from the left side of the gantry to collect the bounty. Cannot be Charmed or Coerced. Can be bought off:
- 500+ credits: head start of a week
- 1,000+ credits: head start AND he snipes remaining Geonosians on the gantry

**The Vio siblings:** If Duke Dimmock warned them, Orpa and Wex direct the PCs to their seats and launch immediately. If not, they attempt to negotiate passage (1,000 credits each; Average Negotiate check reduces to 500; further to 250). If PCs are armed, the Vios offer no resistance.

**If PCs lose:** The Vio siblings rescue them, drag them aboard, and launch — cracking jokes about how much the PCs owe them.

**Skill checks:** Combat skills, Negotiate (Average)

**NPCs present:** Geonosian Soldiers (0-5), Vrixx'tt (Gand Findsman), Orpa Vio, Wex Vio

### Act 3 — Return to Mos Shuuta

#### 3.1 The Imperial Cordon / Coming in for a Landing

**Situation:** The journey from Geonosis to Tatooine takes several hours. If the PCs are aboard the *Lucky Guess*, there is no Imperial problem — the ship is expected. If they took the *Krayt Fang*, Imperials are on the lookout (handle as per Escape from Mos Shuuta TIE fighter patrol).

**Landing:** The Vio siblings handle spaceport control. PCs land at Landing Bay Aurek in Mos Shuuta. Thwheek's crashed Dunelizard occupies Teemo's palace landing pad.

**NPCs present:** Orpa Vio, Wex Vio

#### 3.2 Old Friends in Mos Shuuta (optional)

**Situation:** PCs may revisit characters from Escape from Mos Shuuta. These NPCs remember the PCs and their relationship depends on past interactions.

**Key contacts:**
- **Vorn Tel-Ovis** (droid expert): Can tell PCs where to find evidence of Teemo's B1 droid project inside the palace. Can confirm to Jabba/Anatta that Teemo is manufacturing battle droids.
- **Overseer Brynn** (Spaceport Control): Knows all comings and goings. Can reveal Thwheek uses Teemo's band to spy on Jabba. Has cargo manifests proving droid parts shipments. Can authorise palace access (door codes) but is loyal to Teemo and will not share incriminating information.
- **Vik** (cantina bartender): Not helpful. Will not rat out Teemo or his goons.

**Skill checks:** Varies by interaction

**NPCs present:** Vorn Tel-Ovis, Overseer Brynn, Vik

#### 3.3 Accessing Teemo's Palace

**Situation:** The palace is heavily guarded. A frontal assault would be suicidal. Several infiltration methods are available.

**Methods of entry:**
1. **Hide in cargo crates** (recommended): Empty crates from the *Lucky Guess*, hide inside, have the Vio siblings deliver them to the workshop. Safest approach.
2. **Bluff at the front gate:** Average Deceit check to claim they wish to "settle their debts" — leads to the waiting room and a large fight.
3. **Disguise:** Hard Deceit check to pose as someone else, then Average Charm check for an audience. Can then use Stealth to slip away.
4. **Fight through the front door:** Extremely dangerous. Not recommended.
5. **Sneak in the front:** Not possible — guards cannot miss anyone at the entrance.
6. **Brynn's door codes:** If Brynn has been persuaded, PCs can bypass locks and walk in.

**NPCs present:** Gamorrean Guards (at gates), Teemo's Henchmen

#### 3.4 Within the Palace

**Areas the PCs can explore:**

**Fortified Gates:** Two narrow rooms behind the armoured gate. Two Gamorrean guards and two henchmen normally present. Heavy pillars provide cover.

**Reception Room:** Comfortable chairs, tables, refreshers. Tables can be used as cover.

**Grand Hallway:** Lined with red velveteen, paintings in ostentatious golden frames. A large green holo-sculpture of Teemo with Twi'lek dancers. Intricate mosaic floor. Paintings are good imitations worth ~450 credits (bulky).

**Gladiator Cells:** Lower level. Small rooms with refreshers, common training area. Up to four gladiators present (use Thug stats). Can be turned with 1,000 credits bribe or successful Coerce check. Charm does not work on gladiators.

**Workshop & Cargo Receiving:** Vehicle maintenance area. If 41-VEX is in the party, this area is empty (Teemo has not replaced him). Contains: toolkit, two glow rods, two emergency repair kits, two stimpaks, three blaster carbines, three vibro-axes. Compartment at the back holds incomplete B1 Battle Droids (three, in various states of repair) and a datapad with a work log proving Teemo is reverse-engineering them.

**Kitchen & Storage Area:** Fine foodstuffs. Hard Perception check reveals two boxes labelled "Rycrit stew" actually contain ryll spice — evidence of Teemo's involvement in the ryll trade.

**Cantina Bar:** Bottles and barrels beneath a sturdy bar. Teemo's Industrial Automaton COO Cook Droid mans the bar (harmless, makes good cocktails). Bar provides cover but volatile containers nearby can explode (spend 2 Advantage on a Ranged check targeting someone behind the bar to cause a stun-grenade-equivalent explosion).

**Landing Pad:** Light laser cannon (vehicle-scale, Damage 4, Range Close, counts as Silhouette 3 vehicle). Manned by a henchman who uses Gunnery 1. Shooting at a PC is a Hard Gunnery check. Thwheek's crashed Dunelizard is also here.

**Communications Hub:** The nerve centre. Data terminals with chairs and droid ports. Security protocols add Setback die to all checks; if alarm raised, add a second Setback die.
- Access security cameras: Easy Computers check
- Disable alarms and cut outside communications: Average Computers check
- Access financial records (proof of ryll trade, not paying Jabba): Hard Computers check
- Find B1 Battle Droid schematics (proof of droid manufacturing): Hard Computers check
- Find recorded conversations between Teemo and his Bith spy proving espionage on Jabba: Hard Computers check
- Rolling Despair on any check locks the PCs out of all future checks

**Teemo's Throne Room:** Dominated by the gladiatorial arena. Tall dome-shaped ceiling, stadium seating. A massive chandelier hangs over Teemo's throne. The Bith house band ("Smooth" Banjaxx Wab and the Scundereds) plays dirty Tatooine blues.

Teemo reclines on his throne, flanked by two Gamorrean guards and a number of henchmen equal to the number of PCs. A C-3 protocol droid stands nervously nearby. Another group of henchmen sits at a table.

**Hidden alarm button** behind Teemo's throne summons Gamorreans from the Barracks (arrive at end of round, four rounds after button pressed).

**Eavesdropping opportunity:** If PCs sneak in, they can observe Teemo privately meeting with "Smooth" Banjaxx Wab (his Bith spy who performs at Jabba's palace). The conversation is in Bith and Huttese (Average Knowledge check to identify the languages). The Bith is not a fighter and will trade information (that he spies for Teemo) for his freedom.

**The spy twist:** PCs may assume Thwheek spies on Jabba. This is wrong — Thwheek knows the real spy is the Bith kloo horn player. If captured, Thwheek trades this information for his freedom.

**Skill checks:** Computers (Easy to Hard), Perception (Hard), Knowledge (Average), Stealth, combat skills

**NPCs present:** Teemo the Hutt, Gamorrean Guards (4 elite, 2 at gate, 2 at throne), Thwheek (wild card — appears anywhere), Henchmen (equal to number of PCs + table group), "Smooth" Banjaxx Wab (Bith spy), C-3 protocol droid, COO Cook Droid, Gladiators (up to 4)

#### 3.5 A Final Confrontation

**Situation:** If the PCs choose to kill Teemo, they must fight their way to the throne room. Teemo uses the Hutt Crime Lord stats (p.46 of the Beginner Game Rulebook) and is quite difficult to kill. Blaster carbines or rifles are recommended.

**Teemo's speech:** He monologues about ingratitude and punishment, then draws a massive laser pistol.

**Resolution options:**
- **Kill Teemo in combat:** Difficult straight fight. Teemo is flanked by Gamorrean guards and henchmen.
- **Drop the chandelier:** Average Perception check to notice it is spiky on the bottom and suspended by a single chain. Attack the chain (difficulty upgraded twice due to small size) with any blaster to drop it on Teemo, crushing him.
- **Blackmail Teemo:** If PCs outline what they know and demonstrate they can contact Jabba (e.g. via Anatta on comlink), Teemo furiously agrees to leave them alone. Any Charm, Coerce, or Negotiate attempt other than blackmail fails — Hutts are too strong-willed and cunning.
- **Pass evidence to Jabba via Anatta:** PCs contact Anatta with evidence (needs at least two of: combat droid plans, spying on Jabba, ryll without Jabba's cut). Anatta installs himself in the Mos Shuuta cantina. PCs give him the data via comlink; he pays 500 credits each. Then they can either blackmail Teemo or sneak out and wait. Shortly after, Jabba's bounty hunters arrive, escort Teemo onto his sail barge, and fly off into the sunset. Teemo is never heard from again.

**If Teemo dies:**
- His minions immediately desert and attempt to retreat
- Surviving minions begin looting the palace
- Beneath Teemo's throne: a strongbox containing 10,000 credits (Hutt cartel trade tokens). Not locked. Thwheek knows about it and will try to steal it.

**If PCs lose:** They wake in Teemo's gladiator cells, forced to fight for his amusement. Escaping becomes a new adventure.

**Skill checks:** Perception (Average), combat skills, Charm/Coerce/Negotiate (for blackmail only)

**NPCs present:** Teemo the Hutt (Nemesis), Gamorrean Guards, Henchmen, Thwheek, "Smooth" Banjaxx Wab

#### 3.6 Wrapping Up

**Outcomes:**
- If Teemo is dead or neutralised, the bounty is lifted. PCs are free.
- If they left the *Krayt Fang* on Geonosis, they can return to collect it.
- If Teemo was killed without informing Jabba, Jabba may be displeased — PCs killed a member of his clan. A bigger bounty could follow.
- If Anatta handled things, Teemo vanishes (probably imprisoned, not killed — Hutts dislike killing their own).
- Nyn and the Twi'leks remain friendly unless the PCs failed to protect New Meen and also failed to kill Teemo.
- Contacts made at Duke Piddock's party (Rebel Alliance via Mu Nanb, Black Sun via Maru Jakkar, smugglers via the Vios) may lead to future work.

## Solo Play Notes

### General Adaptations

- **Single PC with companion NPCs:** The adventure is designed for 4 PCs. For solo play, give the player one main character and have B'ura B'an, Nyn, and later Ota serve as active companions who can assist in combat and skill checks. The Vio siblings can also fill this role in Acts 2-3.
- **Scale all encounters down:** Where the module specifies "one bounty hunter per PC," use 2 bounty hunters total. Where thugs are scaled to party size, use the 2-PC scaling (6 thugs in 3 groups of 2).
- **Gamorrean guards at the palace:** Reduce from 4 elite guards to 2. Reduce henchmen groups proportionally.

### Act-by-Act Notes

**Act 1:**
- The *Krayt Fang* exploration works well for solo play — treat it as an investigation sequence with choices about which problems to tackle first.
- The Violent Interception (space combat) can be tense for a solo player. Consider having B'ura B'an assist with damage control or giving the PC a Boost die for the ship's automated systems.
- At the Lylek Den ambush, B'ura B'an should stay in the speeder. Limit bounty hunters to 2. The lylek encounter can serve as a dramatic escape sequence rather than a fight.
- The Drunk and Disorderly encounter can be simplified to a single thug confrontation or a Stealth-based evasion.

**Act 2:**
- Duke Piddock's party is the highlight of Act 2 and works exceptionally well for solo play — it is primarily social with multiple NPCs to interrogate. Present it as a series of conversations the player can choose to pursue.
- Track the four-question limit with Piddock carefully. Let the player choose which guests to approach and in what order.
- Vrixx'tt's discovery of the bounty creates excellent tension. For solo play, delay his realisation slightly to give the player time to gather information.
- The Dimmock audience and Gantry AA7 encounter should be simplified. If the player scored well with Dimmock, skip combat entirely.

**Act 3:**
- The palace infiltration is best handled as a stealth/social sequence for a solo player. The cargo crate method is strongly recommended.
- The Communications Hub is a critical location — present it as a series of slicing challenges with meaningful choices about which systems to access.
- For the final confrontation, the chandelier method is ideal for solo play — it turns a difficult combat into a single dramatic skill check sequence (Perception to notice, then an attack roll).
- The Anatta/Jabba alternate resolution is excellent for solo play as it avoids a large-scale combat and provides a satisfying narrative conclusion.

### Key Decision Points for the Text Adventure

1. **Transponder:** Slow it or leave it? (Determines whether Thwheek intercepts over Ryloth)
2. **New Meen:** Help the miners or leave? (Determines allies and information for Act 2)
3. **Drombb:** Fight, bribe, or intimidate? (Multiple resolution paths)
4. **Party guests:** Who to talk to and what to ask? (Information-gathering puzzle)
5. **Dimmock negotiation:** What evidence to present? (Points system determines Act 2 combat difficulty)
6. **Hiring the Vios:** Pay for passage or take the *Krayt Fang*? (Determines Imperial encounter in Act 3)
7. **Palace infiltration method:** Crates, bluff, disguise, or assault?
8. **Teemo resolution:** Kill, blackmail, or pass evidence to Jabba?
