## Chapter I: Criminal Empires — Key Facts

### The Hutt Species
- Hutts are large, long-lived gastropods capable of living over 1,000 years. They gain adult size slowly, growing larger and more corpulent with age.
- Newborn Hutts (Huttlets) remain in a parent's pouch for up to 50 years. Juveniles emerge at roughly 50 years of age.
- Hutts are hermaphroditic and can reproduce asexually, though they rarely discuss this.
- Their thick skin, massive bulk, and resistance to toxins make them physically formidable despite their sluggish appearance.
- Hutts possess remarkable mental acuity, with cunning intellects and powerful wills; they are natural schemers, negotiators, and crime lords.

### The Hutt Empire
- The Hutts originated on Varl, which was destroyed by the Cataclysms (possibly inter-clan wars using superweapons, though Hutts claim their star gods collided).
- They relocated to Evocar (renamed Nal Hutta), conquering the native Evocii through legal trickery and contracts roughly 15,000 years before the Battle of Yavin.
- The Evocii were displaced to Nar Shaddaa, enslaved, and eventually devolved into a sickly, pale species in the moon's toxic lower levels.
- Hutt civilisation is organised around kajidics — criminal syndicates that double as clan family structures. The five most powerful kajidics are Besadii, Desilijic, Gorensla, Anjiliac, and Qunaalac.
- The Ruling Council (Council of Elders) governs from the Bou'Halec palace in Bilbousa, though members rarely meet in person for fear of assassination.
- The Treaty of Vontor (c. 25,000 BBY) bound the Niktos, Klatooinians, and Vodrans to perpetual servitude under the Hutts after the Hutts defeated the warlord Xim the Despot at the Third Battle of Vontor.

### Kajidic Structure
- Each kajidic operates as both a family and a criminal enterprise, dealing in slavery, spice, weapons, and contraband.
- Loyalty within a kajidic is paramount; betrayal is punished severely, often fatally.
- Hutts employ majordomos (often Humans, Rodians, Twi'leks, or Weequay) to manage daily affairs.
- Inter-kajidic rivalry is constant, conducted through schemes, proxy wars, and assassinations rather than open warfare.
- The Code of Kajidic governs Hutt political manoeuvring; its first tenets are said to be recorded in an ancient tome hidden by the Ruling Council.

### Relations with the Empire
- Hutt Space is technically part of the Baxel sector of the Galactic Empire, governed by Moff Heedra, but Imperial authority is nominal.
- The Hutts maintain autonomy through bribery, political manipulation, and backdoor deals.
- Growing Imperial scrutiny and Rebel activity in the region have increased tensions.
- The Empire has established an orbital shipyard facility above Nar Shaddaa for TIE fighter construction.

## Chapter II: Hutt Space — Locations

### Nal Hutta
- **System:** Y'Toub system, Hutt Space, Mid Rim region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 413 days per year / 87 hours per day
- **Government:** Hutt Ruling Council
- **Population:** 7 billion (Hutt 43%, Evocii 28%, Vippit 15%, other 14%)
- **Languages:** Huttese
- **Terrain:** Forests, swamps
- **Major Cities:** Bilbousa (capital), Oedriga, Jiguuna
- **Areas of Interest:** Hutt palaces, Nar Shaddaa (moon)
- **Major Exports:** Tourism, contraband
- **Major Imports:** Consumables, luxury items, technology
- **Trade Routes:** Pabol Hutta, Ootmian Pabol, Shag Pabol
- **Special Conditions:** None

#### Description
Nal Hutta is a fetid, swampy wasteland that serves as the seat of Hutt power. Formerly Evocar, a lush jungle paradise, it was terraformed into a malodorous swampland through Hutt exploitation. A thick, clinging atmosphere covers the planet. Fetid swamps and bogs are divided amongst the great kajidics. The planet produces little of inherent value beyond contraband and limited tourism; most natural resources have been stripped to near-depletion.

#### Points of Interest

**Bilbousa (Capital):** Unlike typical urban sprawls, Bilbousa features giant hive-like complexes emerging from the ground that appear almost organic — another of the Hutts' illusions. The Bou'Halec ("Seat of Power") dome houses the Hutt Ruling Council. Formerly the property of Gardulla Besadii the Elder. Throughout the city, all manner of illegal goods and services are available.

**Guarja Shipyards:** Primary source for Hutt spacecraft. Located south of Bilbousa in sprawling facilities built over former swampland. Manufactures the rarely-seen *Dor'bulla*-class warship and smaller starfighters including the Z-95 Headhunter. The Hutts have been secretly selling ships to the Rebellion for profit.

**Oedriga:** A secondary city on Nal Hutta.

**Jiguuna:** A smaller settlement on the planet.

### Nar Shaddaa
- **System:** Y'Toub system, Hutt Space, Mid Rim region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 413 days per year / 87 hours per day
- **Government:** Hutt Ruling Council
- **Population:** 85 billion (Various 80%, human 20%)
- **Languages:** Huttese, Basic
- **Terrain:** Urban
- **Major Cities:** New Vertica, Old Duros Sector, Corellian Sector, Red Sector
- **Areas of Interest:** Drunken Drummer, Meltdown Cafe, New Vertica, Kuzbar's Cantina, Dark Melody, Headache Bar, Ko Hentota, Cluster Cantina, Orange Lady
- **Major Exports:** Contraband, technology, weapons, spice, slaves
- **Major Imports:** Contraband, foodstuffs, medicine, technology, slaves
- **Trade Routes:** Pabol Hutta, Shag Pabol, Ootmian Pabol
- **Special Conditions:** Lawless, unsafe conditions

#### Description
The Smuggler's Moon. If Coruscant had a dark twin, it would be Nar Shaddaa — a smog-filled, massive city-moon and the centre of commerce in Hutt Space. "All is found on Nar Shaddaa, though some things are better left lost." Kilometres-tall buildings stratify the world; the wealthy and powerful occupy the top levels whilst the Evocii remnants and the destitute occupy the toxic lower depths. The moon once rivalled Coruscant as a centre of commerce but devolved into a centre of slavery, weapon smuggling, spice trading, and other illicit pursuits after the Ootmian Pabol trade route collapsed.

#### Points of Interest

**Corellian Sector:** A bustling district popular with smugglers and freighter pilots. Home to the Orange Lady cantina.

**Old Duros Sector:** One of the oldest districts on Nar Shaddaa, originally settled by Duros traders.

**Red Sector (Red Light Sector):** The entertainment and vice district; notorious for its cantinas, spice dens, gambling halls, and other illicit establishments. Located above the Undercity.

**New Vertica:** A large section that seceded from Hutt control some three and a half centuries ago. After a bloody week of fighting, the Hutts allowed its independence in exchange for hefty monthly payments. New Vertica is now no longer responsible for maintaining its own security section.

**Dead Town:** Where the uncremated dead of Nar Shaddaa spend eternity in massive warehouse-mausoleums. Bodies are hermetically sealed in individual bays. Grave robbers are common; droids serve as security.

**The Darklands:** A region deep below the Red Light Sector without power for decades. Home to psychopathic gangs and degenerate cannibals using torches, bows, and spears in decaying luxury apartments. Sealed off from transit ways by Hutt enforcers.

**Imperial Shipyards:** A fledgling orbital shipyard focusing on TIE fighter construction, established to increase Imperial presence in Baxel sector. The Hutts protested but then turned the situation to their advantage by selling construction materials at double cost. The Rebel Alliance has attempted to sabotage shipments but without success.

### Varl
- **System:** Ardos system, Hutt Space, Mid Rim region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 378 days per year / 29 hours per day
- **Government:** Hutt Ruling Council
- **Population:** 176,000 (Klatooinian 39%, Nikto 21%, Twi'lek 17%, other 23%)
- **Languages:** Huttese
- **Major Terrain:** Airless craters (formerly polluted wastes)
- **Major Cities:** None
- **Areas of Interest:** Airdomes, rumoured mining centres
- **Special Conditions:** Heavily guarded, no breathable atmosphere

#### Description
The ancient Hutt homeworld, scoured over 20,000 years ago by the Cataclysms — possibly a supernova, possibly inter-clan wars using terrible weapons. The planet is a completely lifeless husk; its atmosphere was burned off, destroying all vegetation and life. Seen from orbit, Varl is a barren landscape pockmarked with craters. A few scattered domes on the southern continent house mining operations extracting aurodium from deep within the planet's core. Varl is completely off-limits to all outsiders; Hutt warships blockade all hyperspace routes.

#### Points of Interest

**The Godsheart:** A pulsar located to galactic east of Varl, held sacred by early Hutts who believed its pulses could divine the future. Said to have led the Hutts from Varl to Nal Hutta.

**Evona's Heart:** Legend holds that when the star goddess Evona was destroyed, she cast her heart into the void. Taking shape as a massive crystal, its impact destroyed most of the southern continent.

**The Yev'Anaha Belt:** A great debris-filled asteroid belt surrounding Varl, made up of remnants of moons, destroyed ships, and more. Hutt patrol ships use it to conceal their presence. Primary warship: *Potala Um Var* (Eternal Vigilance), captained by Ulal.

### Bootana Hutta
Known as the Garden of the Hutts, this subsector encompasses twenty-one worlds. Not directly affiliated with Hutt Space proper; worlds do not answer to the Ruling Council. The planets are or were throneworlds of many important (and formerly important) Hutt families. The Pabol Hutta transects the region as the only viable hyperspace route.

#### Key Features
- Access is through the Gos Hutta system (three verdant worlds and orbiting space stations) via Mulatan, the subsector gateway
- Open violence is very rare; kajidics suppress clan rivalries within boundaries
- Hutts enjoy gambling, gladiatorial combat on worlds such as Groth, and sadistic safaris on Langoona
- Notable worlds: Kor Besadii (lush swamps), Nar Chunna (trade outpost), Pybus and Cyax (taboo, avoided)

### Kintan
- **System:** Kintan system, Hutt Space (Si'klaata Cluster), Outer Rim Territories
- **Orbital Metrics:** 412 days per year / 32 hours per day
- **Government:** Hutt Ruling Council
- **Population:** 12 million (Nikto 99%, Hutt 1%)
- **Languages:** Huttese, Nikto
- **Terrain:** Rocky wastelands, archipelagos, swamps, forests, magma fields
- **Areas of Interest:** Canyon of Despair, Burning Moon Range, Fortress Kh'aris, Gluss'elta Archipelago, Endless Wastes

#### Description
A brutal world of continent-spanning wastelands, violent seas, and scattered archipelagos. Home to the Nikto, who were irradiated by the nearby M'dweshuu supernova, accelerating their evolution into five distinct subspecies. The ultra-violent Cult of M'dweshuu once seized power, plunging the world into civil wars, before the Niktos were permanently indentured to the Hutts via the Treaty of Vontor.

#### Points of Interest

**Endless Wastes:** Largest continent; barren, windswept plains roamed by Nikto tribes hunting elusive beasts. Small pockets form temporary communities during storm season.

**Canyon of Despair:** A ravine cut through the Endless Wastes by the River of Fire; perpetually changed by constant lava flow from the Burning Moon Range volcanoes. Crystalline structures along the walls can be harvested for industrial use.

**Burning Moon Range:** Seven active volcanoes on the western boundary of the Gluss'elta Sea. Venerated by Niktos as the hand of Ultan'eqiq, the god of fire.

**River of Fire:** Kilometre-wide lava flow fed by all seven Burning Moon Range volcanoes, travelling through the wastes and emptying into the Basin of Tortuous Flame.

**Fortress Kh'aris:** A massive natural rock arch deep within the wastes. Constructed by Kh'aris Fenn, a Twi'lek exiled from Ryloth. Said to have had ties to Count Dooku; most Niktos believe it is cursed.

**Gluss'elta Archipelago:** Home to the Pale Niktos. Twelve large islands and numerous smaller rocky inlets. Beneath Glumsa Island lies the home of the Numol'elrul — the lost sixth Nikto tribe, deep-green scaled aquatic Niktos who cut off contact five thousand years ago.

### Klatooine
- **System:** Klatooine System, Hutt Space (Si'klaata Cluster), Outer Rim Territories
- **Orbital Metrics:** 286 days per year / 26 hours per day
- **Government:** Aristocracy (Hutt ruled)
- **Population:** 9 million (Klatooinian 99%, other 1%)
- **Languages:** Huttese, formerly Klatooinian
- **Terrain:** Arid deserts, savannas
- **Major Cities:** Treema (capital)
- **Areas of Interest:** Derelkoos Desert, Fountain of Ancients
- **Special Conditions:** Intense temperatures

#### Description
An arid world of desert and savannah. Klatooinian society is based around tradition and the wisdom of elders. They revere the Hutts as near-gods, believing they were the same Ancients who created their species. The Council of Elders governs domestic matters whilst the Hutts control offworld affairs via the Trade Guild.

#### Points of Interest

**Treema:** The de facto capital, built atop a mesa in the northern Derelkoos Desert. A warren of tunnels and caves beneath the mesa's surface contains docking bays, a few trading company offices, and the central grotto marketplace. Native technology is primitive; high demand exists for mundane equipment like moisture vaporators.

**Derelkoos Desert:** Largest desert on the planet. Deep, rusty red shifting sands sparkling with crystals. Home to the Fountain of Ancients and Klatooinian desert tribes who maintain herds of voraxx and bracil.

**Fountain of Ancients:** Sacred structure in the Derelkoos Desert; a massive plume of cooled and hardened liquid wintrium that seeps from a fissure in the bedrock. Over a period of 10,000 years, the material cools and solidifies into the shapes seen in the fountain. The Council of Elders has decreed that everything around the fountain must remain as the Ancients set forth — no modern technologies within one kilometre.

**Wintrium:** A precious material found only on Klatooine; liquid when warm, harder than transparisteel when cooled. Ancient deposits have properties similar to cortosis. Mining is sacrosanct to Klatooinians and punishable by death. The Sith once came to Klatooine seeking wintrium to craft lightsabre-resistant armour.

**Ferelakk Savannah:** Temperate grasslands of the southern hemisphere with towns of up to 5,000 individuals. Houses are made of a mud-like substance that hardens in the sun.

### Saki
- **System:** Saki system, Hutt Space, Mid Rim region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 582 days per year / 20 hours per day
- **Government:** Clan meritocracy
- **Population:** 1.2 billion (Sakiyan 99%, other 1%)
- **Languages:** Sakiyan, Huttese, Basic
- **Terrain:** Forests, jungles, lakes, savannas
- **Major Cities:** Kaehaxa (capital, floating city), Ridgetown
- **Areas of Interest:** Rotoksa Ridge Grand Lodge, Hunter's Valley, Iepatap Harbour
- **Major Exports:** Technology, vehicles
- **Major Imports:** Industrial goods, raw materials, wildlife
- **Trade Routes:** Pabol Hutta, hyperspace routes to Sakidopa, Sakiduba, and Sakifwanna
- **Special Conditions:** High gravity

#### Description
A lush jungle world at the Spinward edge of the Bootana Hutta, orbiting the red giant Saki Prime. Despite Hutt Space location, Saki remains free from Hutt occupation thanks to Sakiyan adaptability, mobility, and guerrilla tactics. The Sakiyans' primary industry is repulsorlift technology — among the most advanced in the galaxy. Ubrikkian Industries acquires most of Saki's repulsor drives.

#### Points of Interest

**Kaehaxa:** A gargantuan durasteel floating city hovering above the jungle canopy. An eight-kilometre metropolitan platform topped with skyscrapers and dangling weather vanes. It meanders across the planet, stopping for remote villages and hunting parties. Massive scoops breathe chilled air to lower the city's heat signature; tractor beams pull cool ocean water to maintain fusion reactor temperatures.

**Ridgetown:** High atop the ringed Rotoksa Mountain Range. Dedicated to the Sakiyans' primal penchant for hunting. Home to the Rotoksa Ridge Grand Lodge, a stone and timber structure overlooking Hunter's Valley — a hundred-kilometre game preserve stocked with imported beasts from across the galaxy. A particle shield prevents creatures from escaping.

**Iepatap Harbour:** A sprawling shanty town on the Kaasban Sea's northern shore, hidden by dense jungle. Thousands of exiled and dishonoured Sakiyans struggle to survive. The Assassin's Guild has set up shop here, contracting harpoon vessels for sea creature hunts and running a gladiatorial arena funded by debts and favours.

**Sakiyan Colonies:** Sakidopa ("Second Saki") and Sakiduba ("Third Saki") serve as mining colonies. Sakifwanna ("Fourth Saki") is a nursery planet for breeding game stock. Sakiduba mines graxitium — an exotic element used in repulsorlift circuitry.

### Encounter 4: Rubbing Slimy Elbows
**Setting:** Nal Hutta (Wheeta Palace)

**Summary:** The PCs are invited to a Granee Noopa ("Grand Dinner") — a Hutt tradition in which invitees prove themselves against their peers in a series of increasingly dangerous events. The anonymous Hutt host observes from afar via intermediaries called bodddahs. Three groups of invitees compete (four were invited; one declined). The events are the Refreshments, the Dance, and the Dinner. Winners earn wealth, prestige, and reduced Obligations; losers are enslaved.

**Rules of the Granee Noopa:**
- Once entered, participants cannot leave. Attempting to leave before the Closing Ceremony means forfeiture (death or enslavement).
- Three groups of invitees compete. The PCs' boddah is Venik Rin, a scarred Zabrak.
- Participants may use whatever items and weapons they have, but killing other attendees outside specific events is generally "bad manners" (loosely interpreted).
- Venik is considered the host's proxy; losing him is bad form and could break ties against the PCs.

**Average Knowledge (Underworld) check** reveals:
- Success: Granee Noopa is a Hutt tradition for proving oneself; refusing is an insult
- Advantage: Multiple separate events, each more dangerous
- Advantage Advantage: Hutts never attend personally; they send boddahs
- Triumph: Survivors can expect profit and prestige; losers are enslaved

**Event 1: Refreshments**
- A lavishly appointed bar with cloudy green cocktails, each garnished differently. Half are poisoned.
- **Deception checks opposed by Discipline** to lie about which drink is poisoned
- **Hard Perception checks** to spot which drinks are being avoided
- **Skulduggery checks opposed by Vigilance** to switch drinks around
- If a character drinks a poisoned cocktail: **Hard Resilience check** (Threat = automatic Threat on checks for next hour; Threat Threat = additional hour of inebriation; Threat Threat Threat = additional auto-Threat; Despair = immediate Critical Injury)
- Boddahs know which third of drinks are poisoned and can be convinced to share with an **opposed social skill check**

**Event 2: The Dance**
- Combat-heavy event lasting six rounds in an open courtyard with high walls.
- Six automated stun blasters in wall alcoves fire on all participants (Ranged [Light]; Damage 6; Critical 3; Range [Medium]; Stun setting). Add Setback to combat checks targeting them; a successful combat check disables one.
- Each group enters from a different angle at long range.

**Event 3: The Dinner**
- Invitees are guided into a large, sealed hall. Hungry massiffs (one per two characters present) are unleashed and attack until killed.
- After the Dinner, survivors attend the Closing Ceremony.

**Closing Ceremony:**
- If one group remains, they are the victors.
- If two or three groups survive, victory is determined by kills and casualties. Boddahs break ties.
- If a tie cannot be broken, each group selects a champion for a fight to the death.

### Encounter 5: The Dead Road
**Setting:** Bootana Hutta (hyperspace route, asteroid belt, the Pearl station, Pybus)

**Summary:** A grizzled old spacer named Jacek tells a tale of Vortga the Hutt's lost treasure palace on the taboo world of Pybus deep in the Bootana Hutta. The PCs navigate the treacherous Dead Road hyperspace route, bypass ancient Hutt security measures, and discover that the treasures might have been better left as legends.

**Setup:** PCs are in the Orange Lady cantina (Corellian Sector, Nar Shaddaa). Jacek approaches with proof — the Erla Tomvaas, a curving deep red glass-like artifact that is warm to the touch and emanates a harmonious series of musical notes. Part of a larger set called the Siren's Soul.

**Key Skill Checks:**
- **Average Streetwise check** to find a shortcut to the hangars (if bounty hunters kidnap Jacek)
- **Hard Astrogation check with Setback Setback** at each leg of the Dead Road trip
- **Hard Computers check** to slice into Vortga's ancient computer systems (each trap has its own file and coding)
- **Hard Skulduggery check** to shut down each trap system
- **Average Vigilance checks** each round to detect pressure plates, trip wires, infrared beams
- **Hard Athletics check** to safely bypass a discovered trap (Threat can activate the trap, increasing difficulty for further attempts)
- Easy to increasing difficulty **Athletics or appropriate checks** to carry treasure out (difficulty increases with each item)

**Multiple Approaches:**
- Technicians can slice ancient Hutt systems to shut down defence grids
- Explorers can traverse trap-filled walkways and bridges over chasms
- Hired Guns and Smugglers can battle ancient security droids and unleashed predators
- The complex is set into a pyramid-like structure; Jacek's key is required to access the lock

**Locations Along the Way:**

**The Junkyard:** First stop — an asteroid belt littered with ruined ships and discarded garbage. PCs can recalculate hyperspace coordinates and investigate wreckage.

**The Pearl:** A nearly derelict space station at the intersection of the Dead Road and the Elgit system. Owned by Harke, an aloof Sakiyan trader. A relaxing stopover with accommodations (at a premium), supplies, and repair facilities. Harke can translate Jacek's map for a price.

**Pybus:** A lush jungle planet deep in the Bootana Hutta, considered taboo by the Hutts. Hundreds of ancient ruins dot the surface from an unknown, forgotten race. Vortga the Hutt travelled here in 684 BBY and built a sprawling complex in one of the ruins. A surviving worker, the Klatooinian Lalkan, escaped and passed on the knowledge through Jacek.

**Traps and Trials:** Pits, darts coated with poison, automated laser sentries, crushing walls. Each trap requires detection (Average Vigilance), then either deactivation (Hard Computers + Hard Skulduggery) or bypassing (Hard Athletics).

**Final Trap:** When the vault is accessed, a hidden countdown begins. After five minutes, the floor crumbles into a deep chasm. PCs have mere seconds to grab what they can and flee (escalating Athletics checks).
