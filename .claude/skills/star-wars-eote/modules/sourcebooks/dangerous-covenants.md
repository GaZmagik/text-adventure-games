## Chapter I: Armed and Dangerous

### The Hired Gun Career
- Hired Guns are soldiers of the Edge of the Empire. They fight for credits, not causes, though many have strong personal codes.
- They come from all backgrounds: ex-military, fallen heroes, vengeful warriors, criminals, athletes, and those turned to violence by circumstance.
- Common mission types include security, muscle work, bodyguard duty, recovery operations, mercenary contracts, and assassinations.
- Hired Guns are defined by flexibility and adaptability. The most sought-after are specialists in command, special operations, reconnaissance, logistics, demolitions, and heavy weapons.

### Hired Gun Backgrounds

**Yesterday's Hero:** Once famous, now fallen from grace. Scrounges for credits, haunted by former glory. May seek to reclaim fame or simply escape reminders of the past.

**Ex-Military:** Served in a planetary defence force, the Imperial Military, or similar. Combat training is the most marketable skill; mercenary work or criminal enterprise becomes a natural career choice.

**The Avenger:** Pursues righteous vengeance, leaving everything behind. Does whatever is necessary to survive until retribution is delivered. Wields a blaster for any employer that gets him closer to his goal.

**Scion of a Legacy:** Follows (or rebels against) a parent's or mentor's legacy. Driven by the desire to match or exceed the reputation of forebears.

**Peace Turned to War:** Began in an honest career or peaceful labour before a pivotal event set them on the path to becoming a hardened veteran. Personality is still defined by the work done beforehand.

## New Species

### Aqualish
- **Homeworld:** Ando (aquatic world in the Lambda sector along the Corellian Run)
- A triad of semi-aquatic sub-species evolved from aquatic mammals. Considered one of the most aggressive and contentious species in the galaxy.
- Three sub-species diverged thousands of years ago: all feature two enormous downward-thrusting tusks, skin colour ranging from green to blue, and fringes of wiry hair along their jawlines.
  - **Aquala ("finned Aqualish"):** Most numerous on Ando. Possess fins rather than hands. Least adapted to life offworld.
  - **Ualaq:** Five-fingered hands and a retained second set of eyes for seeing in darkness. Most have moved offworld to avoid persecution by the Aquala.
  - **Quara:** Most humanoid — five-fingered hands and only two eyes. Most common on the fringe; frequently encountered as bounty hunters or hired guns.
- All three varieties are violent and warlike, quick to take offence and slow to forgive.
- Aqualish vocal cords and overhanging tusks make it impossible to speak Basic without sounding abrasive and harsh.
- **Brawn 3, Agility 2, Intellect 1, Cunning 2, Willpower 2, Presence 2**
- **Wound Threshold:** 11 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 8 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 90 XP
- **Special Abilities:** All Aqualish are capable of breathing underwater. They begin the game with one rank in Brawl. Characters cannot train Brawl above rank 2 during character creation.
- **Sub-Species Options:** A player choosing to play an Aqualish must choose one of the following sub-species. No skills may be trained above rank 2 during character creation.
  - **Aquala:** Begin with one rank in Resilience. May remove Setback imposed due to cold or wet conditions.
  - **Ualaq:** Begin with one rank in Survival or one rank in Perception. May remove Setback imposed due to dark conditions, but suffer Setback to Perception checks in brightly lit environments.
  - **Quara:** Begin with one rank in Athletics or one rank in Coercion. May remove Setback when attempting to track anything through a natural environment.

### Klatooinian
- **Homeworld:** Klatooine (arid world of desert and grassland in the Si'klaata Cluster, galactic northern extreme of Hutt Space)
- Heavily built humanoids roughly the height of the average human, descended from canine ancestors. Renowned for loyalty and devotion.
- Lived under contracted servitude to the Hutts for more than 25,000 years since the Treaty of Vontor.
- Culture revolves around the concepts of time and age. Mythology centres on all-powerful patron-beings known as the Ancients. Tenacity and perseverance are the most prized cultural traits.
- Thick-set humanoid bodies with fierce, dog-like faces. Colouration ranges from brown to vaguely green. Heavy brows, blunt noses, thick prominent jowls. Strong, agile hands.
- Most Klatooinians encountered off their home planet began their offworld lives in service to the Hutts. Some have thrown off the yoke and escaped into adventure and exploration.
- **Brawn 2, Agility 3, Intellect 2, Cunning 2, Willpower 1, Presence 2**
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Klatooinians begin the game with one rank in either Brawl or Ranged (Heavy) or Ranged (Light). In addition, a Klatooinian starts the game with one additional rank of one non-career skill of his choice. He may not train any of these skills above rank 2 during character creation.

### Weequay
- **Homeworld:** Sriluur (inhospitable world of arid deserts, terrible volcanoes, and corrosive seas, just Core-ward of Hutt Space along the Sisar Run)
- Harsh creatures with thick, toughened skin and a dense, powerful bone structure. Often seen as ideal bodyguards.
- Cultural structure idealises violence and conflict, making them more than likely to follow the path of the Hired Gun.
- Most Weequays tend to be quiet, solitary beings, speaking very little to outsiders. Some have risen to prominence as pirates and brigands. There were even a few Weequay Jedi once.
- Can communicate non-verbally with other Weequays of the same clan using pheromones at short range. This is completely undetectable to other species.
- Society is complex and brutal, built around the clan unit. The two most important gods are Am-Shak (thunder god) and Quay (the brightest moon). Many Weequays consult a totem known as a Quay before any enterprise.
- Males keep their hair in a single topknot of multiple braids, traditionally one for each native year spent away from home.
- **Brawn 3, Agility 2, Intellect 1, Cunning 3, Willpower 2, Presence 1**
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 9 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 90 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Weequays begin the game with one rank in Resilience or one rank in Athletics. They may not train Resilience or Athletics above rank 2 during character creation.
- A Weequay can communicate with other Weequays using pheromones. This is completely non-verbal and undetectable to any other species nearby. Weequays can only communicate like this if they are within short range of each other.

## New Specialisations

### Enforcer
> Ruthless Thugs — Enforcers focus on social interactions as much as combat. They prefer to accomplish objectives through savvy, intimidation, blackmail, and straightforward threats.

- **Enforcer Bonus Career Skills:** Brawl, Coercion, Knowledge (Underworld), Streetwise
- Enforcers fill the dual role of hired muscle and the "face" of a criminal organisation. They bring a syndicate's message to the streets, then ensure that message is followed.
- In a group, Enforcers can take the lead in negotiations and social interactions. With Knowledge (Underworld) and Streetwise, they act as guides when the group delves into the criminal underworld.

### Demolitionist
> Force of Destruction — Masters of explosives and incendiary devices. They possess a deep understanding of the physics behind combustion and explosion on an intuitive level as well as an academic one.

- **Demolitionist Bonus Career Skills:** Computers, Cool, Mechanics, Skulduggery
- Demolitionists can be much more technically-minded than most Hired Guns. They excel at setting traps, from improvised trip-wires tied to grenades to sophisticated proximity and timed explosives.
- They favour flame projectors, grenade launchers, and missile tubes — anything that creates a large blast.

### Heavy
> Overwhelming Firepower — Size really does matter. These characters prefer to use the biggest guns possible, and usually have the physique to match.

- **Heavy Bonus Career Skills:** Gunnery, Perception, Ranged (Heavy), Resilience
- Heavies favour big, loud, and devastating weaponry. From heavy blasters to missile tubes, they wield the most punishing armaments available.
- In a Player Character group, a Heavy fulfils a role similar to a light battle tank in a military force. They can often deal out enough damage to wipe out entire groups of enemies single-handedly.

## New Talents

### Burly
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Heavy
- The character reduces any wielded or carried weapon's Cumbersome quality and Encumbrance rating by a number equal to ranks in Burly, to a minimum of 1.

### Fearsome
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Enforcer
- When an adversary becomes engaged with the character, the character may force the adversary to make a fear check, with the difficulty equal to the character's ranks in Fearsome.

### Heavy Hitter
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Heavy
- Once per session, the character may spend Triumph on a successful Ranged (Heavy) or Gunnery check to add the Breach 1 quality to the attack, or increase an existing Breach rating by 1.

### Heroic Resilience
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Heavy
- Immediately after being hit by a successful combat check but before damage is calculated, the character may spend one Destiny Point to increase his soak by a number equal to his ranks in Resilience.

### Improvised Detonation
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- Once per session, the character may make a Hard (3 Difficulty) Mechanics check to build an explosive device out of available materials. The device can be detonated via any logical means. When it explodes, it deals damage equal to the character's ranks in Intellect plus ranks in Mechanics plus Advantage on the initial check, and possesses the Blast quality at an equal value. The character can spend Triumph to increase damage by an additional 2. A Despair causes the device to detonate immediately in the owner's face.

### Improvised Detonation (Improved)
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- Reduce the difficulty of Improvised Detonation's check to Average (2 Difficulty) and increase the damage dealt by the explosive to ranks in Intellect plus twice ranks in Mechanics plus Advantage on the initial check.

### Loom
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Enforcer
- When an ally engaged with the character makes a successful Charm, Deception, or Negotiation check, the character adds Threat per rank in Coercion to the ally's check.

### Master Grenadier
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- The character decreases the Advantage cost to activate the Blast quality on any weapon he uses by 1, to a minimum of 1.

### Powerful Blast
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- The character increases the damage dealt by the Blast quality of explosives, explosive weapons, and grenades he uses by +1 per rank of Powerful Blast.

### Rain of Death
- **Activation:** Active (Manoeuvre) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Heavy
- The character may perform the Rain of Death manoeuvre. If he does so, when he makes an attack on the same turn he does not increase the difficulty of the attack due to the Auto-fire quality.

### Selective Detonation
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- When using any weapon with the Blast quality or explosive device, the character may spend Advantage or Triumph to exclude one target that would normally be affected by the explosion. Multiple targets may be excluded, but the total number may not exceed his ranks in Selective Detonation.

### Steady Nerves
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- The character removes Setback per rank of Steady Nerves from Cool or Skulduggery checks.

### Talk the Talk
- **Activation:** Active, Incidental | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Enforcer
- When making a Knowledge skill check, the character may spend one Destiny Point to substitute Knowledge (Underworld) or Streetwise for the required skill. Information gathered may be suitably embellished by the GM to reflect criminal and underworld origins.

### Time to Go
- **Activation:** Active, Incidental (Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- The character may spend one Destiny Point to perform a Move manoeuvre as an incidental. This manoeuvre may only be used to move into cover or out of the blast radius of an explosion. May be performed out of turn, after the character determines he would be hit by the blast but before damage is applied.

### Time to Go (Improved)
- **Activation:** Active, Incidental (Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Demolitionist
- When activating Time to Go, the character may allow one engaged ally to also perform an out-of-turn Move manoeuvre as an incidental to move into cover or out of the blast radius of an explosion.

### Walk the Walk
- **Activation:** Active, Incidental | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Enforcer
- The character may spend one Destiny Point to add damage equal to his ranks in Streetwise to one hit of a successful Brawl check.

## Hired Gun Signature Abilities

### Signature Ability: Last One Standing

**Base Ability (Cost 30):**
Once per game session during a combat encounter, the character may spend 2 Destiny Points to make a Hard (3 Difficulty) Resilience check. If he succeeds, he skips his next turn and eliminates all enemy minions in the encounter. The minions are all eliminated immediately and do not participate further in combat. The narrative means by which he accomplishes this is up to the player character, but should be suitably exciting and must be approved by the GM.

**Upgrades:**
- **Reduce Setback (Cost 10):** Remove Setback from skill check to activate Last One Standing.
- **Add Boost (Cost 10):** Add Boost to skill check to activate Last One Standing.
- **Reduce Difficulty (Cost 15):** Reduce the skill check to Average (2 Difficulty) instead of Hard.
- **Increase Effect (Cost 15):** When triggering, eliminate one rival per Increase Effect upgrade in addition to minions.
- **Destiny (Cost 15):** Last One Standing costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.

### Signature Ability: Unmatched Protection

**Base Ability (Cost 30):**
Once per game session as an incidental, the character may spend 2 Destiny Points to gain the following ability: once per round, after suffering a hit and determining damage, the character may halve the damage (rounded up) dealt before it is applied to his soak. This ability is active for the remainder of the current round and two additional rounds.

**Upgrades:**
- **Duration (Cost 10):** Unmatched Protection lasts for one additional round.
- **Soak (Cost 10):** Gain +1 soak whilst Unmatched Protection is active.
- **Protect Ally (Cost 10):** Once per session whilst active, when an engaged ally is hit by an attack, the character may choose to be hit by that attack instead.
- **Additional Reduction (Cost 15):** May reduce the damage of 1 additional hit suffered each round whilst active.
- **Destiny (Cost 15):** Unmatched Protection costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.

## Chapter II: Locked and Loaded

### Weapons Manufacturers
- **BlasTech Industries:** Produces the widest range of personal energy weapons of any manufacturer. Responsible for the DL-44 heavy blaster pistol and the stormtrooper's E-11 rifle. Refuses to sign an exclusive Imperial sales agreement.
- **Czerka Arms:** Third-largest weapons producer. Exclusive contract with the Empire has reduced public-market availability, but Czerka products still appear via black markets. Known for vibro weapons and flame projectors.
- **Merr-Sonn Munitions, Incorporated:** Second only to BlasTech in total sales. Particularly known for grenades, thermal detonators, missiles, and other explosives. Popular with Demolitionists.
- **SoroSuub Corporation:** Began as a mining corporation; mineral processing remains central. Produces weapons, body armour, defence equipment, and proton torpedoes. Primary offices remain on Sullust.

**SoroSuub VX "Sidewinder" Repeating Blaster:** Achieves its high rate of fire through five rotating barrels. Designed for use with its included bipod. Wielders with sufficient strength can carry and fire it on the move with reduced accuracy.

**SoroSuub HOB Heavy Repeating Blaster:** A powerful and reliable weapon. Rarely seen outside military forces, typically requires a crew of two to operate as an emplaced weapon. Mechanics checks to repair a HOB gain Boost.

## Chapter III: Action and Adventure

### Integrating Hired Gun Characters
- Hired Gun characters can be divided into those working for an organisation and those working independently.
- The Hired Gun needs a reason to be with the group, and that reason should involve payment and play to his mercenary nature.
- Key questions for the GM: Who is the employer? What are the specific duties? What happens if the Hired Gun is arrested, captured, or killed?

### Working for an Organisation
- The largest organisational employers are mercenary and paramilitary groups.
- Mercenary groups are normally freelance military units for hire. They range from a few individuals to forces rivalling minor planetary defence forces.
- Paramilitary groups are dedicated to a particular cause — armed organisations supporting a stated ideology, standing militias, corporate security agencies, etc.
- In the Outer Rim and less civilised regions, mercenaries operate more openly. Outside Imperial space, they operate under local laws, if they feel obligated to follow them at all.

### Working Independently
- Independent Hired Guns are freelancers willing to work for a much wider range of employers.
- They are much more open to roles of dubious legality. While not every independent Hired Gun is a criminal, the nature of his work leads to grey areas at the very least.
- Employers include nobles, politicians, criminal masterminds, and virtually anyone who can afford the fee.

### Inspiring Hired Guns — Story Hooks

**Abandoned:** The PCs discover a Hired Gun abandoned by his organisation — left behind, believed dead on a battlefield. Investigation leads to secret deals and rivalries.

**Guarding a Character:** The PCs must guard a specific NPC and hire a trained bodyguard. The interaction between the bodyguard and the hiring PC becomes a major plot point.

**Forced Enforcer:** The Hired Gun is ordered to accompany the party because one or more PCs owes the Hired Gun's employer.

**Hired Gun on the Run:** The Hired Gun is running from a bad situation — a deserter from Imperial forces, fleeing a powerful crime lord, or the survivor of a destroyed mercenary unit.

**The Protectors:** The PCs rent themselves out to defend others for a fee.

**Wanted: Hired Gun:** The PCs decide they need more firepower and hire it themselves. The first adventure might include competitive interviewing with rival Hired Guns.

### Hired Gun Missions

**Rescue and Retrieval:** The PCs are hired by a wealthy corporate executive to hunt down kidnappers who stole his personal attendant droid (containing encrypted corporate secrets) and the Surronian Conqueror-class Assault Ship it was being transported aboard. Three-part mission covering investigation, recovery, and a twist involving a rival crime lord.

**Anyyon, the Guardians of An'yettu:** A paramilitary group protecting the An'yettu Islands on Rodia. Locked in a never-ending feud with the Chattza clan. Three-part campaign involving recruitment, counter-attacks, and an air-to-air battle over the islands.

### Hired Gun Combat Campaigns

**Dirty Work:** The PCs are hired by a secret conclave of merchants (the Committee for Renewal, or CfR) to rid a trading colony of the Stalton Syndicate. Five episodes covering surveillance, ambushes, retaliation, betrayal within the CfR, and a final assault on the Stalton warehouse.
- Possible Location: A city of 50,000-150,000 on an Outer Rim world.
- Starting pay: 5,000 credits per person, negotiable up to 8,000 per person with 50 percent up front.
- Enemy Forces: The Stalton Syndicate, a growing criminal network. Led by Voran Stalton, a young and arrogant up-and-coming member of the crime family.

**Reog's Regiment for Hire:** The PCs join one of the largest independent mercenary units in the Outer Rim. The regiment operates from dreadnoughts escorted by frigates and gunships. Four episodes covering a mining station defence, a corporate research laboratory disaster, a fleet battle against a rival mercenary force, and a disastrous assault on a Gamorrean clan on behalf of the Gamorrean Shrook Clan.
- Starting pay: 3,000 credits per mission, increases with experience and rank.
- Employer: Reog, owner and commander of the regiment. After angering a powerful Hutt, Reog's opportunities diminish and the unit is targeted for attack.

### Hired Gun Combat Rewards

**XP in a Combat-Heavy Campaign:**
- The GM may reduce the amount or frequency of XP granted during combat-specific adventures.
- Restrict which skills the PC may increase by granting XP specifically for combat skills.
- PCs should receive XP comparable to regular adventures, awarded for their part in battles regardless of overall success or failure.

**Credits and Cash:**
- Where in the galaxy the work is carried out affects pricing.
- Illegal operations command higher payments.
- The employer's ability to pay, the risk level, and expected expenses all factor into pricing.
- Per-job costs may be divided into several payments over days to weeks.

**Material Rewards:** Equipment, weapons, armour, vehicles, and starships issued during missions are typically expected to be returned. Loaned objects should not count as rewards.

**Intangible Rewards:**
- **Opportunity:** Success in one mission may open up greater operations in the future.
- **Reputation:** Extremely important for a Hired Gun. Whether known as trustworthy, lethal, or incompetent, reputation precedes and influences all future work.

## Running Cinematic Combat

### Cinematic Inspirations
- **Swashbucklers and Serials:** Movement is the key element. Fights are filled with running, jumping, climbing, swinging, advancing, and retreating.
- **Westerns:** Cover and position are the central features. Showdowns favour a quick draw and a sure aim. Suspense, tension, and ever-present danger.

### Combat Situations in Edge of the Empire
- Two basic principles: the fight should have stakes, and the fight and its outcome should illuminate something about the characters.
- **Gunfights:** Cover and position are the most tactical considerations. How to leverage a strong position or minimise the disadvantages of a weak one.
- **Running Battles and Fighting Retreats:** Characters are often outnumbered and outgunned, retreating down a narrow corridor, laying down covering fire, or zigzagging through trees.
- **Ambushes:** Maximise advantages of cover and position. The PCs often confront ambushes set by crime lords, local authorities, Imperial agents, and untrustworthy former allies.
- **Showdowns:** One-on-one confrontations. Han and Greedo in the cantina is the most famous non-lightsabre example.
