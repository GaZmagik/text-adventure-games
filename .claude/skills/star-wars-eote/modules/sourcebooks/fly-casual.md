# Fly Casual — A Sourcebook for Smugglers
> Sourcebook module for the star-wars-eote skill. Smuggler career options, species, gear, weapons, vehicles, starships, and GM guidance for smuggling campaigns.

## Chapter I: Free Traders

### The Smuggler Career — Overview
- Smugglers are pilots, outlaws, and entrepreneurs who ferry contraband, evade Imperial patrols, and deal with the criminal underworld to make a living.
- The Smuggler career encompasses a wide range of character types: cunning Scoundrels, expert Pilots, quick-drawing Gunslingers, smooth-talking Charmers, risk-taking Gamblers, and light-fingered Thieves.
- All Smugglers share eight career skills: Coordination, Deception, Knowledge (Underworld), Perception, Piloting (Space), Skulduggery, Streetwise, and Vigilance. A Smuggler automatically gains one free rank in any four of these at character creation and receives a discount on purchasing ranks in them.
- A Smuggler's ship is both home and livelihood. Most Smugglers are passable Pilots at minimum, and all must be skilled at Deception and Negotiation.

### Smuggler Backgrounds
Characters may come from a variety of backgrounds that inform their approach to smuggling:

**Disadvantaged:** Tough upbringing, former slave, or otherwise rough around the edges. Pilots train through trial and error on barely functional ships. Scoundrels have experienced enough villainy to consider crime normal. Thieves steal out of necessity. Charmers learn to manipulate those who look down on them. Gamblers take life to extremes. Gunslingers from poverty rely on nothing but their weapons.

**Privileged:** Some Smugglers have known wealth, whether won, earned, or inherited, but lost it through bad luck, poor choices, or deliberate abandonment. Pilots may treat ships as expensive toys. Scoundrels enjoy the games of the powerful. Thieves from privilege may never have enough. Gamblers with means seem reckless. Gunslingers from rich families may have honed skills through hunting safaris or honour duels.

**Ex-Military:** Former Imperial Army, Navy, Customs, or Stormtrooper Corps personnel who left through expulsion or choice. Pilots are disciplined and highly skilled. Scoundrels chafed under regulations. Thieves found themselves interested in confiscated goods. Charmers held desk jobs but craved action. Gamblers may have enlisted on a bet. Gunslingers picked up their skills in the military.

**Respectable:** Former legitimate merchants or traders who turned to smuggling when laws changed or business dried up. Pilots flew by the book until encountering corruption. Scoundrels traded legally only because it suited them at the time. Thieves began as law-abiding citizens unfairly accused. Charmers erred once and ended up with a criminal record. Gamblers bent house rules one too many times. Gunslingers may have made a mistake, drawing a weapon on a client.

**Cynical:** A traumatic event changed this Smuggler's worldview — anything from the destruction of a homeworld to a personal betrayal. Carries a chip on the shoulder and has lost faith in law, order, or people. Pilots take meticulous care of their ships. Scoundrels shrug off deception. Thieves feel the galaxy owes them. Charmers are careful never to be deceived again. Gamblers know safe does not always work. Gunslingers are quick to become the aggressor after betrayal.

**Born and Bred:** Smuggling is all this character has ever known — raised in the life, possibly from a long line of Smugglers. Pilots know and love their ships intimately. Scoundrels thrive in the only life they have known. Thieves find stealing second nature. Charmers learned from a parent or mentor. Gamblers may have inherited the love of games of chance. Gunslingers have relied on blasters from childhood.

## New Species

### Falleen
Beneath the glamorous reptilian features and serene facade of the mysterious Falleen lies a shrewd mind and cold-blooded precision. Falleen use wiles and unique pheromones to manipulate other species with ease.

**Physiology:** Alluring reptilian humanoids with supple scales in tones from deep green to dull grey-green. Can temporarily change skin colour at will to shades of orange or red when exuding pheromones. Sharp ridges run down their backs. Males typically wear hair in a single long topknot or braid; females favour elaborate styles adorned with beads and decorative combs.

**Society:** The Falleen species shares its name with its homeworld. Several feudal kingdoms rule over lower classes of artisans, labourers, slaves, and technicians. The ruling noble houses conduct business and political negotiations peacefully whilst minor infighting between kingdoms occurs. Falleen value careful planning and reason, considering displays of emotion taboo. Through intense training and meditation, Falleen learn to control the colour of their skin and the use of their pheromones. They see species unable to control their emotions as inferior.

**Homeworld:** The planet Falleen rests in the Mid Rim off the Corellian Run in the Doldur sector. Expansive rain forests, sprawling plains, and snowy mountains remain unmolested thanks to modest urban sectors. The capital city of Throne has a population of over 520 million. Since the Empire's rise, Imperial R&D facilities monopolise Falleen's industrial sectors. Moff Eisen Kerioth distrusts the Falleen and maintains a blockade. Multiple shadowports operate around the planet, exporting slaves and smuggling offworld luxuries.

**Language:** Almost all Falleen learn Basic. They believe other languages are inferior. The Falleen language uses skin colouration and pheromones in place of vocal modulation, making it impossible for non-Falleen to master.

**Species Abilities:**
- Br 1, Ag 2, Int 2, Cun 2, Wil 2, Pr 3
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 12 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 90 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Falleen begin the game with one rank in Charm. They still may not train Charm above rank 2 during character creation.
- **Beguiling Pheromones:** By emitting pheromones and altering their skin colour, Falleen can affect emotional states of other sentients. Once per check as an incidental, a Falleen may suffer 2 strain to upgrade the ability of a Charm, Deception, or Negotiation check against a living sentient being within short range once. This ability has no effect on targets wearing breath masks or without respiratory systems.

### Gotal
The shaggy-haired Gotals are an empathic race of many talents and a benevolent nature. They possess the extraordinary ability to detect and interpret almost every manner of energy field, allowing them to sense the moods and wellbeing of nearby living beings.

**Physiology:** Tall, burly humanoids that evolved as hunter-gatherers on the harsh plains of Antar 4. A coarse, dense layer of fur covers these rugged bipeds in shades from sandy brown to smoky grey. Tiny vestigial nostrils, beady red eyes, and a wide grimace adorn their gristly, elongated faces. From their heavily knobbed brow sprouts a pair of conical bovid horns packed with millions of nerve clusters sensitive to electromagnetic fields, radio waves, and numerous other forms of energy.

**Society:** Antar 4's harsh, unpredictable environment forced the Gotals' primitive ancestors into a nomadic lifestyle. Gotals live free of any established government and maintain a peaceful anarchy. They are typically polite and discreet, avoiding expressing negative opinions. Gotal children are abrasive and temperamental, enduring bombardment of every conceivable form of energy emission until maturity around twelve years of age. Gotals struggle with droid technology; power sources in most modern droids emit an energy pattern causing particular discomfort, creating anti-droid sentiment.

**Homeworld:** Antar 4 is one of six moons orbiting a gas giant in the binary Antar system. The complex orbital pattern of moons, planet, and suns causes erratic weather and a constantly changing diurnal cycle. The land mass consists primarily of breezy grass plains and rocky steppes, with over sixty per cent of the surface covered in oceans, rivers, and streams.

**Language:** Antarian primarily relies on reading brain waves and electromagnetic auras. Gotals speak Basic but in neutral terms devoid of emotion or emphasis, leading to misconceptions that they can read minds.

**Species Abilities:**
- Br 2, Ag 2, Int 2, Cun 3, Wil 2, Pr 1
- **Wound Threshold:** 9 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 8 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Gotals begin the game with one rank in Perception. They still may not train Perception above rank 2 during character creation.
- **Energy Sensitivity:** The head cones of a Gotal are finely tuned sensory organs used to detect almost the entirety of the electromagnetic spectrum. Once per encounter as a manoeuvre, a Gotal may sense the presence and current emotional states of all living things within short range of himself.

### Quarren
Persistent and proud, the Quarren are deep-sea dwellers of the oceanic planet Mon Cala (or "Dac"). Their distrusting and often paranoid nature makes these stubborn aquatic humanoids successful in a wide range of business, political, and underworld professions.

**Physiology:** Sinewy, humanoid species descended from cephalopods, with triangular heads and four tentacles protruding from their faces. A trio of sharp fangs lines their tiny mouths. Above the cluster of tentacles is a pair of luminous blue or turquoise eyes. Leathery skin usually presents in subtle variations of pinkish-orange, though some are mottled brown or grey-green. Quarren with pink or purple skin are extremely rare and considered beautiful. The average Quarren has five sucker-tipped fingers on each hand. Quarren are amphibious, natural swimmers, and more comfortable breathing underwater.

**Society:** For untold ages, the Quarren maintained a peaceful existence at the bottom of the sea on their homeworld of Dac, alongside other sentient species such as the cetacean Whaladons and the idealistic Mon Calamari. Their tribal society upheld traditional ways, mining abundant mineral lodes from Dac's underwater mountains. The brutal conflict between the two civilisations ended in near-extermination of the Quarren. The Empire's arrival on Mon Cala has been yet another stumbling block for reconciliation. Now many Quarren live in slavery, mining valuable resources in labour camps to feed the Imperial military-industrial complex.

**Homeworld:** Mon Cala (Dac) in the Outer Rim, the last stop along the Overic Griplink beyond the Tion Cluster. A vast, planet-wide ocean with only a handful of islands and coral reefs. Immense floating cities and underwater population centres. Major metropolises include Foamwander, Heurkea, Hikahi, Kee Piru, Orotoru G'am, and Reef Home.

**Language:** Quarrenese is a unique collection of bellowed moans, reedy cries, and bubbly croaks capable of travelling great distances underwater. Most Quarren also speak Basic with a gravelly, sometimes gurgled tone.

**Species Abilities:**
- Br 2, Ag 2, Int 2, Cun 3, Wil 2, Pr 2 (Note: Intellect is 1 based on the stat block shown)
- Br 2, Ag 2, Int 1, Cun 2, Wil 3, Pr 2
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 8 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 95 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Quarren begin the game with one rank in Negotiation. They still may not train Negotiation above rank 2 during character creation.
- **Amphibious:** Quarren can breathe underwater without penalty and never suffer movement penalties for travelling through water.
- **Ink Spray:** Quarren can spit ink from a specialised sac within their gills that disperses in water, creating a brief murk used to confuse foes or escape predators. This ability can be used on land as well, though it is considerably less effective because it must be sprayed directly on the target. Once per encounter, as an out-of-turn incidental, a Quarren may suffer 2 strain to add Setback to a combat check made by a character within short range (under water, it adds Setback Setback Setback to the combat check instead).

### Charmer — The Smooth Operator
The Charmer combines the silver tongue of a Politico, the street smarts of a Scoundrel, and the keen mind of a Trader. Where the Politico might rally, chastise, or bribe, the Charmer showers his mark with flattery. Where the Trader haggles down a price, the Charmer convinces others that lowering the price is their idea.

## New Talents

### Call 'Em
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gunslinger
- This character does not add Setback to his combat checks due to the use of the Aim manoeuvre.

### Clever Solution
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gambler
- Once per game session, the character can choose to make one skill check using Cunning rather than the characteristic linked to that skill. He must explain how his wiliness helps overcome the challenge.

### Congenial
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Charmer
- When attempting a Charm or Negotiation check, the character may suffer a number of strain to downgrade the check a number of times equal to the strain suffered (cannot exceed ranks in Congenial). When the character is a target of a Charm or Negotiation check, he may suffer strain to upgrade the difficulty an equal number of times (cannot exceed ranks in Congenial).

### Disarming Smile
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Charmer
- The character can take the Disarming Smile action to make an opposed Charm check against one target within short range. If he succeeds, decrease the target's defence (melee and ranged) by a number equal to ranks in Disarming Smile (to a minimum of 0) until the end of the encounter.

### Don't Shoot!
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Charmer
- Once per session, the character can take the Don't Shoot! action to make a Hard Charm check. If he succeeds, he cannot be the target of a combat check until the end of the encounter or until he makes a combat check. The player must explain how he talks his way out of the fight, and the GM has final say on whether the ruse succeeds.

### Double or Nothing
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gambler
- Once per check, the character may suffer two strain to increase the difficulty of the check by one. Then, after cancelling opposing symbols, double the amount of remaining Advantage.

### Double or Nothing (Improved)
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gambler
- When performing the Double or Nothing incidental, after cancelling opposing symbols, the character doubles the number of remaining Triumph.

### Double or Nothing (Supreme)
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gambler
- When performing the Double or Nothing incidental, the character also doubles the number of Success and Despair.

### Fortune Favours the Bold
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gambler
- Once per game session, as an out-of-turn incidental, the character may suffer two strain to flip one dark side Destiny Point to a light side Destiny Point (this cannot interrupt the spending of a Destiny Point).

### Guns Blazing
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gunslinger
- When making a combined combat check with two Ranged (Light) weapons, the character may suffer two strain in order to refrain from increasing the difficulty of the check for attacking with two weapons.

### Just Kidding!
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Charmer
- Once per round as an incidental, when the character or any of his allies in short range generates Despair on a social skill check, the character may spend one Destiny Point to convert a single Despair generated on the check to Threat. He must explain what he says or does to smooth things over after the verbal misstep.

### Quick Draw (Improved)
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gunslinger
- The character may activate the Quick Draw talent twice per round (instead of just once per round).

### Second Chances
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Gambler
- Once per encounter, after the character has rolled a skill check (but before resolving the result), he chooses a number of positive dice from his dice pool up to his ranks in Second Chances and re-rolls them. The character must accept the second result. This talent cannot be used to re-roll Force dice.

### Sorry About the Mess
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gunslinger
- The character decreases the critical rating of any non-starship weapon he wields by 1 (to a minimum of 1) against targets that have not yet acted this encounter.

### Spitfire
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Gunslinger
- After the character makes a successful combined check with two Ranged (Light) weapons, each additional hit generated as part of the attack can be allocated to any other target within range of the weapon.

### Up the Ante
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Gambler
- When the character wins at gambling, he earns 10% more credits per rank of Up the Ante.

### Works Like a Charm
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Charmer
- Once per game session, the character can choose to make one skill check using Presence rather than the characteristic linked to that skill. He must explain how his social ability helps him overcome the challenge.

## Smuggler Signature Abilities

### Signature Ability: Narrow Escape
- **Cost:** 30 XP (base ability)
- **Base Ability:** Once per game session, the character may spend two Destiny Points to make a Hard Streetwise check. If he succeeds, the character immediately flees from the current personal-scale combat encounter unscathed. The challenge is not overcome or defeated, but the character evades the hazard or threat for the time being. The exact nature of the escape must be approved by the GM and should be suitably creative or daring.

**Upgrades:**
- **Reduce Setback (10 XP):** Remove Setback from the skill check to activate Narrow Escape.
- **Increase Effect (10 XP, x2):** Affect an additional number of allied characters equal to Cunning per Increase Effect upgrade purchased.
- **Add Boost (10 XP):** Add Boost to the skill check to activate Narrow Escape.
- **Change Scale (10 XP):** Narrow Escape can be activated in a vehicle with the Piloting (Planetary) or Piloting (Space) skill.
- **Reduce Difficulty (15 XP):** Reduce the skill check difficulty to Average.
- **Change Skill (15 XP):** Narrow Escape can be activated during social encounters with the Deception skill.
- **Destiny (15 XP):** Narrow Escape costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.

---

### Signature Ability: Unmatched Fortune
- **Cost:** 30 XP (base ability)
- **Base Ability:** Once per game session, as an incidental, the character may spend 2 Destiny Points to change the face of one positive die in the character's dice pool to another face adjacent to it. An "adjacent" face is any die face sharing an edge — not a point — with the rolled face. Unmatched Fortune cannot be used on Force dice.

**Upgrades:**
- **Frequency (10 XP, x2):** Unmatched Fortune can be used one additional time per game session per Frequency upgrade.
- **Increase Number (10 XP, x2):** Affects one additional die per Increase Number upgrade purchased.
- **Shared Luck (10 XP):** Unmatched Fortune can also be used on the dice pool of a willing ally within short range. All upgrades the acting character has apply.
- **Destiny (15 XP):** Unmatched Fortune costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.
- **Increase Effect (15 XP):** Unmatched Fortune can also be used on negative dice.
- **Increase Range (15 XP):** Increases the maximum range at which willing allies can be affected by 1 per Increase Range upgrade purchased.

### Running Smuggling Jobs

#### The Business of Smuggling
- A Smuggler needs two things: a ship and a cargo.
- Tramp freighters (cargo vessels with no standard port-of-call or shipping route) provide a cover of legitimacy and allow free movement between ports.
- Finding work comes through underworld contacts, stumbling upon profitable cargo, or Obligation-driven dangerous hauls.

#### Occupational Hazards
- **The Imperial Customs Office:** The Empire's agency for enforcing interstellar trade regulations and interdicting illegal cargo. Ships do not hesitate to fire upon suspected smuggling vessels. Imperial law authorises patrols to stop and board any starship.
- **The Bureau of Ships and Services (BoSS):** Certifies and records ships, captains, modifications, weapons, shields, and transponder codes. All ships have a BoSS datapad with relevant information. Falsifying records requires breaching the BoSS data net — slicing a BoSS datapad requires a Daunting Computers check; on a failed check, Despair fries the pad permanently.

#### Transponder Codes
- Built into a ship's sublight engines, broadcasting continually. Include a data signal and an underlying energy signature from the engine.
- Gaining physical access to the transponder system requires a Hard Mechanics check.
- Adding or replacing a transponder code "identity" requires a Daunting Computers check.
- Concealing a transponder code requires an Average Computers or Mechanics check.
- Masking a transponder is illegal and attracts attention.
- Mimicking another vessel of the same class is Daunting Mechanics; copying a much larger ship is Formidable; reverting to the ship's original profile is Hard.

### Con Jobs and Scams
A con unfolds in five stages:

1. **Finding a Mark:** Research or observe the potential target. Details to look for include the mark's finances, defences, security, acquaintances, business partners, temperament, and interests.
2. **Laying the Groundwork:** Formulate a plan, obtain necessary items, information, or resources. Gain the mark's trust through personal details that can be exploited.
3. **The Approach:** One or more con artists make first contact with the mark, arranged to look coincidental. Success hinges on the face's Deception or Charm.
4. **The Complication:** Something goes wrong — someone appears who could reveal the face's identity, the mark becomes suspicious, or another party lays claim to the prize. Most likely stage for combat.
5. **The Payout and Exit:** The party obtains the prize and makes their exit. Ideally the mark only discovers the deception later.

### Heists and Break-Ins

#### Security Measures

**Bypassing Security:** The skill required depends on the security measure. Entries describe the skill or skills used to disable or bypass the measure and the difficulty.

**Obstacles (Passive systems):**
- **Locks:** Require a Skulduggery check. Core World electronic locks might require a Daunting Skulduggery check; electronic lock breakers reduce difficulty by two.
- **Laser Gates and Energy Fields:** Impassable fields of energy. Making it through in time requires a Hard Athletics or Coordination check or 10 damage. Each Threat inflicts 1 additional damage; the GM may spend Despair to inflict a Critical Injury.
- **Pressure Plates:** Require a Hard Perception or Vigilance check to notice. Moving across without activating requires a Daunting Coordination check.

**Detection Devices and Triggers:**
- **Scanners:** Detect the presence of blaster energy cells, identify species and vital signs. GM calls for an appropriate skill check to avoid triggering.
- **Pressure Plates (contact triggers):** Small pads on door handles, container lids, etc. A Daunting Coordination or Skulduggery check can manipulate the device without activating the trigger.

**Active Systems and Traps:**
- **Laser Traps:** Automated blasters (Agility 2, Ranged [Heavy] 1). Silhouette 0; destroyed if a character performs a successful combat check against them.
- **Reversed Repulsorfields (Man Traps):** Create localised high-gravity fields to immobilise targets. Escaping requires a successful Daunting Athletics check. Deactivating without the control pad requires an Average Mechanics or Computers check.
- **Gas Dispensers:** Release airborne chemicals. Each round exposed, a character must make a Hard Resilience check. On failure: 3 strain plus 1 strain per Threat (knockout gas) or 3 wounds plus 1 wound per Threat (lethal gas, not reduced by soak). The GM may spend Despair to inflict a Critical Injury. Respirators provide immunity.
- **Detonators:** Self-destruct devices. All characters engaged suffer 15 damage and must make an Average Coordination or Perception check; each Triumph reduces damage by 1. The GM may spend Despair to inflict a Critical Injury. Disarming requires a Hard Skulduggery check or an Average Mechanics check.

### Showdowns and Shoot-Outs

#### The Quick-Draw Showdown
A quick-draw showdown unfolds in three steps:

**Step 1: Face Off.** The two characters face each other, attempting to intimidate or size the other up. Each chooses one option:
- **Size Up:** Makes an opposed Perception or Streetwise check against the opponent's Cool. If successful, upgrades his Cool check for initiative in Step 2 once, and once per additional Triumph. Adds Setback per additional Triumph to the check.
- **Intimidate:** Makes an opposed Deception or Coercion check against the opponent's Discipline. If successful, adds Setback to the opponent's Cool check for initiative in Step 2, and Setback per additional Triumph.

**Step 2: Draw.** Both characters make a competitive Simple Cool check (modified by Step 1). The character with the lower Initiative blinks first and tries to attack; the character with the higher result keeps his nerve, draws, and attacks in a seamless motion. The faster character must draw his weapon (if he wishes to use it) and attack. He can use remaining manoeuvres to Aim, take cover, or perform other relevant activities.

**Step 3: Combat Continues.** If both characters survive Step 2, combat proceeds as normal, with Cool checks for Initiative.

### Smuggler Nemeses

#### The Patron
A powerful figure directly connected to the business of smuggling — a current or former employer, crime lord, pirate captain, casino owner, or business executive. Motivated by power and control. The Patron uses his resources to make life difficult for the group if they break the relationship.

**Example: Noham the Hutt** — An upstart Hutt on the run from his own kajidic due to bad debts, currently inhabiting Squan Station, a minor trade stop near Arboo-ine. Desperate, canny, and avaricious. An excellent recurring business contact for the PCs, but if they grow too successful, Noham may have eyes for their profits.

#### The Law
A law enforcement nemesis who makes life difficult for PCs. Could be a local customs official, sector-level police officer, an agent of the Imperial Office of Criminal Investigations (IOCI), or a private investigator.

**Example: Graz the Hunter** — A Gank bounty hunter considered by many to be ideal: professional, efficient, and extremely adept at violence. Known to meticulously avoid collateral damage and harm to civilians. Skilled with a rifle, pistol, and a heavily customised Kihraxz assault fighter.

#### The Friendly Foe
An individual who shares a strong past with one or more PCs but whose trust is in limited supply. At one moment he might swoop in to save the group; the next, he grabs the loot and runs. The least predictable nemesis type.

**Example: Beladi Vosi** — A born swindler with an incredible debt hanging over her head. Ruthless in her own way — her brand of cutthroat business practices involves departing with the cash rather than violence. Extremely charming and convinces her partners they are her only friends (which does not stop her from robbing them).

#### The Elements
The countless hazards of the galaxy itself — navigating the Maw, dodging giant neebray mantas in the Kaliida Nebula, or steering through a Clone Wars orbital battlefield. Unlike other nemesis types, the Elements represent physical challenges that do not follow the PCs but exist as obstacles to certain goals.

### Smuggling Rings

#### Types of Smuggling Organisations

- **The Family (Hutt Kajidics):** Larger criminal organisations tied to family loyalties. Contracts require 50-65% of the cargo's profits going directly into Hutt coffers.
- **The Business (Tenloss Syndicate):** A legitimate business model with criminal enterprises behind the scenes. Operates in the Bajic sector; driven by profit; prefers negotiation over force.
- **The Entrepreneurs (Car'das):** Smuggling is the main business. Organised into a cell structure of semi-independent captains. Specialises in third-party goods and information brokering whilst avoiding the slave trade.
- **The Multi-Planetary Corporation (Black Sun):** A massive business conglomerate operating in the shadows. Many employees are oblivious to the underlying criminal presence. PCs working for Black Sun likely start with a smaller operation and discover the true affiliation later.

#### The Zann Consortium
A relative newcomer to the galactic underworld, younger than Car'das. Does not derive power from smuggling alone — focuses on racketeering, robbery, theft, and piracy. Its propensity for violence makes it a growing threat to trade routes.

#### Creating Your Own Smuggling Ring
GMs can create custom smuggling operations by addressing:
- **Where is the operation located?** A world, station, or particular hyperspace route.
- **What does it smuggle?** Spice, weapons, information, people, food, technology — anything and everything can be smuggled.
- **Why does it smuggle?** Profit, humanitarian reasons, or both.
- **How is it organised?** Small businesses, multi-tiered hierarchies, or decentralised captains sending a cut to leadership.
- **How does it smuggle goods?** Bribed officials, scan-resistant hidden panels, charming past customs inspections, speed, or stealth.
- **What is its cut?** Typically 30-45% of the profits from a given run (some as low as 20%, others above 50%). PCs can make an opposed Negotiation check to reduce the cut from a starting point of 45%.
- **Is it affiliated with a larger syndicate?** Creates tension between freedom and finances but provides access to wider markets.

### The Big Payoff

#### Ship-Based Rewards
- **An alternate identity for the ship:** An altered BoSS transponder broadcasting more than one identity helps the ship slip past authorities.
- **A safe berth at a shadowport:** Off-the-grid haven for captains and crews to move questionable wares, repair ships, and secure the next job.
- **A new crewmember:** NPCs or droids that fill specialised roles (medical technician, astrogation expert, dedicated cook) to lighten the crew's load.

#### The Debt
Obligation operates as a central dynamic of gameplay. Examples of debt-based adventure hooks:
- Rescuing a politician from pirates may leave him feeling indebted to the PCs, providing valuable contacts or resources later.
- A surplus datapad from an adventure provides an opportunity to trade valuable information.
- Winning a wildly successful game of sabacc creates a loan obligation that can be leveraged for access to off-limits areas or new contacts.
