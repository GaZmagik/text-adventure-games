# Suns of Fortune — The Corellian System
> Sourcebook module for the star-wars-eote skill. Corellian System locations and points of interest.

---

## System Overview

The Corellian System orbits the star Corell and is one of the most important political and economic entities in galactic history. Located to the galactic east of Coruscant, it is an economic and industrial powerhouse supplying ships, components, and high-tech goods to much of the Core. Corellians were among the first cultures to pioneer hyperspace travel, establishing the Corellian Run and the Corellian Trade Spine.

Five inhabited planets orbit Corell: **Corellia**, **Drall**, **Selonia**, **Talus**, and **Tralus**, along with two uninhabitable frozen worlds (Crollia and Soronia) on the system's fringes. The system's history is shrouded in mystery — it is widely believed that godlike beings called "Celestials" constructed the system, moving the worlds into their unusually regular orbits along with Centerpoint Station.

**The Corellian Run:** Plotted over 25,000 years ago by Corellian hyperspace explorers, the Corellian Run is the longest and most profitable hyperspace trade route in the galaxy. It stretches from Coruscant in the Core to Wrea in the far reaches of the Outer Rim.

### Description

Corellia is the largest and most populous planet in the system, a pleasant temperate world of rolling hills, soaring mountains, grassy plains, and broad tempestuous seas. It is known throughout the galaxy for its lovely beaches, handsome people, and prowess at shipbuilding. Considered the "capital" of the Corellian System, Corellia projects vast influence and provides most of the impetus behind the system's economy.

Corellia is currently governed by a corporate authority. Throughout its history it has been ruled by a galaxy-spanning empire, a hereditary monarchy, a constitutional monarchy, and a constitutional republic. It has given birth to the Corellian Engineering Corporation (CEC) and some of the most infamous pirates, confidence men, and smugglers ever to sail a starship. The current government is a corporatocracy fronted by the puppet Diktat (currently Daclif Gallamby) and run by the boards of directors of CEC, The Corellia Mining Corporation, Gowix Computers, and other powerful corporations.

Under Imperial influence, Corellia controls the sector under the rule of the Imperial Grand Moff — first Fliry Vorru, then Naomi Dargon. CorSec (Corellia's Security Force) now operates with Imperial "liaisons" and fleets of Star Destroyers are a common sight in orbit.

### History Highlights

- The Celestials are believed to have moved Corellia into Corell's orbit millennia ago.
- Earliest records speak of bondage under the Rakata of the Infinite Empire some 30,000 years ago.
- After throwing off the Rakata, Corellians reverse-engineered Rakatan technology and mastered hyperspace travel.
- Corellians mapped the Corellian Trade Run and established the Corellian Trade Spine, co-founding the Galactic Republic alongside the Duro and the humans of Coruscant.
- **The Great Re-Tooling:** Around 15,000 years ago, after ecological disasters from rampant pollution, the Corellians moved their entire industrial infrastructure into orbit over 50 years, allowing the planet's environment to heal completely.

### People and Culture

Corellians are renowned as hard-charging mavericks blessed with good fortune and endless self-confidence. They enjoy hard work, hard play, breaking rules, and subverting galactic norms. They are superstitious, putting great faith in charms and talismans (e.g. wearing green clothing before dangerous business). They are deeply sentimental about family and home.

**Corellian Soul Diamonds:** When a Corellian passes away, the body is cremated and the ashes turned into pure blue-white synthetic diamonds. These "soul gems" are said to retain a part of the departed's spirit and are commonly mounted in jewellery or displayed on small repulsors.

**Swoop Gangs:** A significant subculture of organised swoop-rider gangs exists, competing for turf and criminal profits. They are typically young, disaffected individuals from poor families.

### Points of Interest

#### Coronet City
The capital city, located on the southernmost continent on the shores of the great southern ocean. A huge, densely populated, thoroughly modern metropolis home to millions of sentients from across the galaxy. Renowned for its beautiful architecture, bustling night life, flourishing economy, and cultural institutions.

- **Central Business District:** Graceful soaring towers hundreds of stories high housing numerous corporations. The Coronet Times news organisation is headquartered here.
- **Coronet Heights:** The main entertainment district featuring theatres, dining, professional sports teams, museums, parks, and plazas. Home to the Coronet City Zoological Park, Botanical Gardens, and the Museum of Fine Art.
- **Governmental Sector:** Home to the Diktat, the planet's legislative branches, and the massive bureaucracy. Embassy Hill houses off-world ambassadors in tree-lined boulevards and multi-billion credit manors patrolled by armed security droids. The Coronet Arms Hotel is the most luxurious in the sector.
- **Blue Sector:** The seediest, shabbiest, most crime-ridden part of Coronet City. Dense residential and commercial zones with cantinas, tapcafes, tattoo parlours, run-down hotels, houses of ill-repute, crooked casinos, and disreputable enterprises. CorSec long ago ceded control to swoop gangs who act as the de facto government.
- **Treasure Ship Row:** A large open-air marketplace stretching for several miles within the Blue Sector. One part bazaar, one part black market, one part legitimate business. Packed day and night with throngs of sentients shopping, haggling for everything from counterfeit Coruscant timepieces to illegal military-grade starship components. The atmosphere is raucous — music, laughter, shouting, and the occasional shriek of a blaster.
- **The Pit:** Officially the Coronet City Waste Disposal Centre. An ancient landfill centred in a massive pre-historic meteor crater. A treasure trove for mechanics, hot-rodders, scavengers, archaeologists, and scientific explorers. Inhabited by desperately poor "scrappers" who jealously guard their claims.
- **Selonia Town:** A labyrinthine subterranean metropolis beneath Coronet's subway and sewer systems where the majority of Selonians on Corellia make their homes. Referred to as "Down Town." Hundreds of square kilometres of tunnels and vast terraced caverns home to hundreds of thousands of Selonians, Drall, and other sentients.
- **CEC Headquarters:** A grand sprawling campus overlooking the beach with administrative buildings and offices. Home to the Corellian Museum of Space Exploration, which tells the rich history of Corellian spacefaring with interactive exhibits, a planetarium, a theatre, and a huge hangar complex of preserved starships.
- **Corona House:** The ancient historical residence of the chief executive of Corellia (currently Diktat Daclif Gallamby). A sprawling, majestic four-storey mansion on a two-hundred-acre plot in the city centre with bedchambers, parlours, kitchens, sitting rooms, and two grand ballrooms. The Corona House Gardens are a popular public park.

##### Ixxis and Su's Floating Game
A semi-regular high-stakes sabacc game run by **Brail Ixxis** (a female Chandra-Fan) and **Prahman Su** (a dapper Corellian human), two of the smoothest and most disreputable scoundrels in the sector. The game floats from location to location over the course of a week, is by invitation only, and requires a minimum buy-in of five thousand credits. The only people they invite are those who have been in trouble with the law.

#### The Orbital Industrial Zone
Centuries ago, to avoid ecological disaster, Corellia moved its medium and heavy industry into orbit. Spread across half of Corellia's orbit like a huge metal net, factories, foundries, refineries, and shipyards can be seen from the planet's surface. Connected by flexible gantries tens or hundreds of kilometres long, the Orbital Zone rivals Kuat's mighty industrial ring.

Home to CEC's shipyards, the birthplace of some of the most famous ships in galactic history, and the headquarters of Corellia's fiercely independent navy and its academy. The entirety is overseen by the Corellian Port Authority, which also operates the Port of Corellia.

#### Kor Vella
A city located high atop the sheer Kor Vella mesa, one of Corellia's most popular vacation spots. The mesa is a tall, imposing, flat-topped rock thrust over a kilometre high from the Vella plains. Originally an austere religious settlement, it evolved into a respected commercial hub attracting both investors and tourists. The dramatic vistas and challenging sport of climbing and hunting are a huge draw.

**The Draknul Cafe:** Hidden within Kor Vella's Cliffside neighbourhood, a small cafe serving the best food and providing the juiciest rumours in town. Owner and head chef **Fianna** has strict control of the guest list, catering only to sentients she deems interesting. She is a discreet and canny information broker who will share knowledge for a price.

#### Tyrena
A large, bustling metropolis straddling the Auric River on Corellia's southern continent. The seat of culture and tourism on Corellia, home to the most luxurious hotels and resorts in the sector. Features a prestigious music conservatory, the Tyrena School of Art, a glamorous theatre district with some of the finest theatres and opera houses in the Core, and the Corellian Symphony Orchestra.

- **North-East:** The roughest district, a ragged scar of vice, crime, urban decay, and squalor rivalling Coronet's Blue Sector. Crime is rampant, streets are openly patrolled by gang enforcers, and all manner of legal and illegal goods are available.

**Contract Negotiations (Adventure Hook):** High-ranking CEC technicians, engineers, and naval architects have gone missing in North-East. Confidential corporate data (ship blueprints, inter-corporate memos, secret projects) has gone missing or turned up on Corellia's black market. Industrial espionage is suspected. Headhunters from Kuat Drive Yards, with aggressive recruiters and bottomless expense accounts, are rumoured to be operating in Tyrena.

#### Agrilat (The Crystal Swamps)
Located in the cooler regions of Corellia's northern continent, the Agrilat Region covers nearly 10,000 square kilometres of coastal marshes nestled between the northern sea and Orik's Spine mountains. The native plant life is a mix of organic matter and dense super-hard silica giving a crystalline appearance — hundreds of crystal plant species grow here including deadly razor grass, glowing singing ferns, and lovely dewdrop trees bearing fruit that look like fist-sized rubies and emeralds. The region is a protected nature preserve.

Hidden throughout the region are crude swoop racing tracks. The **Agrilat Swamp Circuit** is the most famous (or infamous) underground racing circuit, with legs named Kribben's Folly, the Oblivyn Gate, and the Sulphur Archway.

#### The Gold Beaches
The Corellian Sector's most popular tourist attraction. Stretching hundreds of kilometres along the western coastline of the southern continent. The sand is a combination of fossilised algae, metallic shells, and concentrated silica giving it a soft, powder-like consistency that glitters like gold. The more southerly beaches have purer gold sand ("24-karat beaches"), whilst the more northerly "Black Gold" beaches mix glittering sand with ancient black volcanic silica.

#### Other Geographic Regions
- **The High Deserts:** Mountain-locked desert regions on two of the larger continents. Cool, dry, rocky with few permanent inhabitants. Appealing to minor criminal operations.
- **Lowland Forests:** Temperate forests interspaced with grassland meadows. Small farming communities, hamlets, and outlying farms.
- **Plains:** Unspoiled wilderness at the base of mountain ranges, flattening into great grasslands. Home to great ranches, including nerf barons like **Torak "Baron" McCallok** and **Mastakatorus Darriovof III**.

### Description

The second of the five inhabitable planets orbiting Corell, Drall is a pleasant, temperate, largely peaceful world home to the learned Drall people. Slightly larger than Corellia, Drall's surface is composed of huge continent-sized landmasses punctuated by large lakes and small, brackish inland seas. The topography consists mostly of rolling ranges of low hills covered in dense forests or lush grasslands. No true oceans — lakes and inland seas are connected by a vast network of navigable rivers fed from countless springs and streams.

Drall is a warm planet with an atmosphere that makes everything appear in sharper focus with higher contrast and more brilliant colours. Modern Drall cities have domes of photo-sensitive transparisteel to protect light-sensitive Drall from the intense, focused sunlight.

### History Highlights

- The Drall have almost 35,000 years of continuous recorded history stored in their copious archives (though focused almost exclusively on Drall affairs).
- Like the other Corellian worlds, believed to have been relocated by the Celestials.
- Conquered by the Rakata approximately 30,000 years ago; the Drall worked in Rakatan factories and laboratories.
- After the Infinite Empire's collapse, the Drall turned inward to study and scholarship.
- Off-world visitors were drawn by Drall's beauty; the Drall built domed metropolises under photo-sensitive transparisteel.
- An ill-fated alliance with Selonia and Corellia during a failed conquest of Talus and Tralus some 500 years ago ended the Drall's dabbling in warfare.
- Today, Drall is a popular (if slightly faded) tourist destination, considered charming but a bit old-fashioned by more fashionable Core citizens.

### People and Culture

The Drall are diminutive, peaceful, and bookish. They rarely carry weapons and prefer to talk their way out of trouble. They are inveterate gossipers and the telling of tales and passing of secrets is a society-wide pastime, making them very effective information brokers.

Drallish culture is centred around the clan — powerful matrilineal family groups ruled by an elder female called a Duchess. Clans are hidebound, strictly hierarchical, and decidedly top-down, with rulings passing directly from the Duchess's mouth to her clanmates' ears.

### Points of Interest

#### Mastigophorous
The largest of Drall's domed cities, on the shores of the Boiling Sea. The political, social, and cultural centre of the entire planet. Described by visitors as "the largest small town in the Corellian System." Protected by an opalescent transparisteel dome dozens of kilometres across, rising almost 500 metres into the air and stretching over nearly a hundred square kilometres. Home to tens of thousands of native Drall and an equal number of off-world sentients.

- **The Great Archives:** A densely packed cluster of workmanlike buildings covering many square city blocks. Massive sentient computer mainframes, whole libraries of bound tomes, data hard-copied on thin sheets of plasfilm, and climate-controlled caverns deep below the surface housing fragile crumbling scrolls. The Archives hold galactic history as far back as the earliest days of the Republic. Scholars frequently hire freelance researchers, scientists, and explorers to collect information they cannot gather themselves.
- **Hall of the Clans:** A combination government services centre, courthouse, and assembly place for the planetary-ruling Council of Clans. A squat, slab-sided, flat-roofed stone building two stories high commanding an entire city block. Despite its ancient appearance, it is a thoroughly modern administration building within. Connected to surrounding government buildings by secure tunnels.
- **Old Town:** Mastigophorous's oldest area, home to many of the wealthiest and most influential citizens. Fine dining establishments, theatres, museums, and galleries. A huge park dominates the centre with hundreds of square hectares of walking paths, ancient towering groves, native trees, ponds, and streams. Features an art museum and a museum dedicated to Drall history.
- **The Mastigophorous Academy:** The premier research and education institution on Drall (if not the entire Corellian sector). The campus rivals the Great Archives in size, featuring old carved stone lecture halls, modern research laboratories, libraries, museums, and dormitories. Accepts individuals from any species. Many eminent scientists and engineers across the galaxy claim it as their alma mater.

**Expeditions to the Rim (Adventure Hook):** The Academy actively collects knowledge by running lucrative expeditions to the Outer Rim — zoological studies, exploring long-forgotten pre-Republic ruins, and more. The Academy prefers explorers who can take care of themselves and have some scholarly interests. A Drall scholar with an un-Drallish taste for excitement may even join the expedition.

#### The Luminous Gardens
A full-service resort and luxury spa perched in the picturesque hills above the Boiling Sea, catering to the rich and famous of the galaxy. Offers massage, hot spring, mineral and mud baths, wet and dry sauna, and many types of ancient, traditional, and holistic treatments.

Due to its popularity, the Gardens has gained a reputation as a place where galactic business is done — treaties drawn up on shaded verandas, business mergers hammered out in perfumed steam whilst wearing nothing but towels.

**Notable NPC — Drusa:** The proprietress, a savvy young female Drall. A discreet and canny information broker who knows the value of a rumour or a snippet of overheard conversation, willing to share her knowledge for a price.

#### The Boiling Sea
Located southeast of Mastigophorous, one of Drall's most famous natural wonders and the catalyst for Drall's tourism industry. A long, narrow, brackish body of water up to three hundred metres deep, home to a wide variety of aquatic creatures including solitary spikefish, slow-moving redjellies, and massive gentle semi-sentient aquatic mammals called Drallish Mermaids.

The sea takes its name from the quick-spreading algae called **kettleweed** — a thick, ropy, jet-black algae that covers large swaths of the sea. When the weather grows hot, the sensitive kettleweed dies off en masse and the rotting mats of dead algae release gas that causes the waters to steam and roil. The sea boils for no more than a few weeks at most, drawing tourists from hundreds of light years away.

#### Meccha
A middle-sized farming town near the equator, a complex of a dozen small domes connected by skywalks, tram lines, and underground tunnels. Surrounded by thousands of square kilometres of orchards, fields, ranches, and pasture land.

- **Meccha Wingrider Garrison:** An artificial plateau hundreds of metres high a few dozen kilometres north of Meccha, built by the ancient Rakata for unknown purposes. Home to a large garrison of Wingriders — the ibbot-mounted courier/peacekeepers who patrol Drall's skies. Rumours persist of ancient Rakatan artefacts being dug up during training exercises.

#### Other Geographic Regions
- **The Polar Ice-Swamps:** Constantly growing and melting vast ice swamps around Drall's shrunken polar caps. Rumoured to be infested with dianogas.
- **Barmooth Groves:** Huge groves of water-tolerant trees around the edges of Drall's larger seas with cathedral-like root systems. The groves become twisting mazes of narrow passages bounded by impenetrable clusters of roots and tree trunks, stretching for hundreds of kilometres and featuring heavily in local folklore and ghost stories.

### Description

The fifth planet of the Corellian system, Selonia is an ocean planet rich in natural resources and home to the fierce and insular Selonian people. The surface is mostly covered with deep, frigid, tempestuous seas with a handful of small islands, archipelagos, and atolls scattered here and there, mostly around the equator. Land is very scarce — many available landmasses are volcanic islands, often still active, with towering rocky edifices rising out of the ocean to incredibly impressive heights. On Selonia, only the simplest life has evolved to live on land; most advanced life forms live in the oceans or underground.

The Selonians themselves are a semi-aquatic humanoid musteline race of ancient lineage who live deep beneath the seas in a planet-wide network of tunnels and caverns called "the Dens," bored into the living stone over tens of thousands of years.

### History Highlights

- When the Rakata arrived, they found only an empty, wind-swept world of endless steel-coloured seas. The Selonians avoided subjugation by retreating into their tunnels and sealing them behind.
- After the Infinite Empire collapsed, Corellian surveyors mapped Selonia and Drallish scholars catalogued its flora and fauna — but the Selonians stayed in their dens.
- Nearly a thousand years after the Infinite Empire's collapse, Selonian scouting parties made first contact with their neighbours.
- After agonisingly slow diplomacy, the Selonians became part of Corellian society, proving adept at science, technology, and ship building.
- Several Selonian dens joined with CEC to build shipyards in orbit around Selonia.
- An ill-fated invasion of Talus and Tralus over 500 years ago made Selonia withdraw, severing nearly all ties for centuries.
- Only within the past few decades has a new generation of Selonian explorers emerged.

### People and Culture

The Selonian people are naturally suspicious of outsiders, rigidly honourable, and honest to a fault. Beneath their friendly demeanour lies an ancient, deeply ingrained distrust of outsiders, best expressed in the old Selonian saying: "Den and kin first, all others second."

Consensus is paramount — they debate courses of action for days or even weeks before acting. Their family structure is based around a fertile female known as a queen, the supreme ruler of a den. They have a strict caste system based on gender and fertility with little to no social mobility.

### Points of Interest

#### Cloudland Peaks
A volcanic mountain range dominating Selonia's northern hemisphere. Stretching over 2,000 kilometres, the range contains more than 100 mountains rising from 8,000 to 15,000 metres high. Some taller mountains are still active volcanoes. A major undersea fault line and volcanic vents make the seas bubble and steam, and the whole area is prone to groundquakes, tsunamis, and avalanches.

Despite the incredible danger, the Cloudland Peaks draw thousands of visitors yearly from Corellia, Talus, and Tralus to climb, hunt the massive predatory raptors nesting among the crags, and test themselves against the fury of Selonia.

#### Orbital Shipyards
Rivalling the CEC shipyards in orbit over Corellia in quality and production volume. A sprawling complex of hundreds of square kilometres of slips, hangars, gantries, orbital warehouses, habitation modules, and testing laboratories in low planetary orbit.

The Selonians have a reputation as competent technicians and shipwrights but are nearly incapable of designing starships — some quirk of their thought process makes the necessary mathematical and engineering skills completely incomprehensible. The few ships they have designed over history have been dismal failures. Their most infamous design is the **coneship** — crude, short-range, inter-system patrol craft driven by archaic chemical rocket engines, seating two Selonians in claustrophobic discomfort.

**The Deepwater Docks (Adventure Hook):** Smuggling into Selonia poses unique challenges since the majority of civilisation is underwater and underground. In the darkest oceanic trenches, minor Selonian dens construct water-locks large enough to hold light freighters. Smugglers must rig ships with underwater propulsion devices, drop to the surface on a ballistic course, then dive deep into the ocean to reach these hidden docks.

#### The Selonian Dens
Not a single location but a vast network of interconnected tunnels and caverns twisting for tens of thousands of kilometres beneath Selonia's oceans. Home to millions of Selonians. Seemingly primitive to an outsider but thoroughly modern within — state-of-the-art communications, transport systems, thousands of industrial and commercial zones, and city services that are the envy of many less fortunate cities. Sculpture parks, gardens of luminous fungus, arenas, stadiums, educational institutions, theatres, and tapcafes. It is the rare off-worlder who is granted permission to enter.

#### Other Geographic Regions
- **Island Surfaces:** Many island surfaces are completely undeveloped wildernesses, ranging from windswept barren rocks to lush grass-covered equatorial islands.
- **Den Entrances:** Some entrances to the dens have seen small surface communities spring up — a wary mix of outsiders and Selonians. Most structures are prefabricated and temporary.

### Description

Known as the Double Worlds, Talus and Tralus are small terrestrial worlds that orbit Centerpoint Station like oversized moons. Together, the two planets and the station inhabit the third orbit around Corell, lying between Drall and Selonia. Both planets are subject to tidal and tectonic forces well beyond that of other planets in the system, with fast-moving, capricious tides, savage continent-sized windstorms, and devastating groundquakes. Both worlds are quite similar in climate, terrain, ecology, and biodiversity.

### History Highlights

- No native sentient species existed on either world until the Rakata built massive agricultural and industrial complexes to strip the worlds of resources approximately 30,000 years ago.
- Populations of humans and Drall (criminals, political prisoners, rebels) were transplanted from other worlds to work in Rakatan mines, farms, and factories.
- After the Infinite Empire's fall, the peoples fought a long battle against entropy and the breakdown of society for nearly two centuries.
- Over the next 25,000 years, both planets gained a reputation as disreputable dumping grounds for the wretched and undesirable.
- Eventually, Talus and Tralus seceded from central authority, declaring themselves sovereign and forming the **Federation of the Double Worlds** (colloquially "Fed-Dub"). After a savage war against Corellian forces (who recruited Drall and Selonia), the Federation successfully repelled the invaders and established itself as a separate political entity.
- Today, the Fed-Dub maintains cordial if strained relations with its neighbours. Over the centuries it has evolved from a progressive republic to a corrupt plutocracy interested only in protecting the rights of the rich and powerful.

### People and Culture

The homogeneous culture of the Federation is a mixture of the best and worst of Corellia, Selonia, and Drall. Corellians from the Fed-Dub are wilder, more mercurial, and less trustworthy. Swoop racing culture is even stronger, with gangs on Talus and Tralus being positively insane, living by the maxim "Live fast, die young, and leave a beautiful corpse."

The Selonians on the Double Worlds are even more insular and xenophobic, considered heretics by the larger Selonian dens on Selonia. Their dens are crude and primitive. The Drall retain their native good nature and dignity but have developed their own particular eccentricities over the millennia, with a more obsessive and desperate edge to their scholarship.

### Points of Interest

#### Dearic (Talus)
The capital city of Talus and home to the planet's main spaceport. A large and bustling metropolis often compared favourably to Coronet City — soaring skyscrapers, broad lush parks, orderly neighbourhoods, and busy commercial districts. Beneath the glamorous surface lies a black heart of corruption, vice, and despair. The less fortunate are shuffled into segregated, desperate neighbourhoods full of addicts, the insane, the poor, and other undesirables.

**The Drall's Shop (Adventure Hook):** In the furthest outreaches of Dearic, in a slum called Shove-Off Point on the banks of the Icormek River, an elderly Drall (known colloquially as "The Drall") operates one of the best black-market shops in the sector. He has travelled far and wide, made many friends, and is reportedly able to find anything given enough time and money. He sets up decoys and tricks to test those who seek him out and to protect himself from authorities.

#### Kystes' Spine (Talus)
A long, nearly impassable mountain range bisecting Talus's great southern continent. Ragged, ancient mountains home to deadly native creatures, prone to violent storms and groundquakes. Sheltered alpine valleys with small human villages and Drall research stations. Vast forests stretch across the mountain flanks, broken by jagged rockslides and cloaked by mists.

Deep beneath the Spine lives the majority of Talus's Selonian population — a loosely allied confederacy of radical xenophobic dens who shun both aliens and their own kind on Selonia. Those outsiders foolish enough to intrude uninvited often meet a messy end.

#### Nashal (Talus)
Built along the banks of the scenic Mephyt River in Talus's far north. A sprawling, lovely riverside city of half a million citizens. Home to Talus's cultural elite, dedicated to cultivating native Talusian art, music, and writings. Also a neutral diplomatic ground where peace treaties and negotiations have been held for centuries. The most diverse city on Talus, with the languages of hundreds of species echoing in its streets and parks.

#### Sea of Jarad (Tralus)
A small, shallow, exceedingly stormy inland sea in Tralus's northern hemisphere. The small human and Selonian communities on its shores are a throwback to a simpler, slower age — sleepy fishing-and-aquaculture towns of typically fewer than 2,000 souls. Life is hard, subject to the whims of wind and tide. In what little spare time they can find, people make music or write verse, and the art of scrimshaw on styanax bones is quite common.

**The Jarad Run (Adventure Hook):** A deadly challenge race across the sea in high-performance low-altitude airspeeders. Split into three legs forming a massive triangle, the likelihood of encountering a storm increases each year. Many young Tralusians use this race as a rite of passage.

#### Rellidir (Tralus)
Tralus's largest city with over one hundred thousand sentients. The seat of the planet's local government. A sprawling, airy city nestled in a valley near the equatorial sea with broad, tree-lined streets and stout buildings of antique charm. Quiet and staid, with middle-class working inhabitants making their livings in mines, refineries, fields, pastures, and factory farms.

#### Qaestar Town (Talus)
The largest city on Talus and the Double Worlds — a quarter million people. Often described as what Dearic looks like under its veneer of gilt and propaganda. An ugly, dirty port town in the shadow of the massive Qaestar Ridge. The largest, most dangerous spaceport in the entire system. Frequented by pirates, smugglers, and assorted undesirables. Mean-spirited, suspicious citizens who look upon visitors as equal parts predator and prey.

**The Imperial Presence:** Qaestar Town hosts a small Imperial garrison — a few squads of stormtroopers, their officers, and a much-harried Imperial customs inspector. Ostensibly assigned to oversee customs, actually under orders to bring the Fed-Dub officials into the Imperial fold. Underfunded by Coruscant, ignored by Fed-Dub officials, and outgunned by the criminals.

**Port of Qaestar:** Sits high above the city on the Qaestar Ridge — an ugly, ramshackle collection of hangars, slips, warehouses, and dens of ill repute. Infamous for rival gangs of cutthroats and smugglers who charge the highest berthing fees but are just as likely to strip a ship to the hull or impound it indefinitely.

#### Other Geographic Regions
- **Mud Flats (Talus):** Extensive mud flats stretching for thousands of kilometres, formed by shallow seas and flat terrain. Home to the notorious sludge panther at the top of the food chain.
- **Steppes (Tralus):** Rolling expanses of cool grasslands, considered one of the more appealing places to visit. The local herding communities are free of much of the corruption plaguing the rest of the Fed-Dub.

### Description

Situated at the barycentre of Talus and Tralus, Centerpoint Station is home to over half a million citizens of the Federation of Double Worlds. The station is a 350-kilometre-long cylinder with a 100-kilometre sphere at its centre, rotating on its polar axis every twelve hours to generate artificial gravity via centrifugal force. All decks are concave and oriented so that "up" is towards the station's axis.

The interior is divided into four distinct regions: **Hollowpoint** (the open centre of the spherical section), **The Shells** (approximately 200 concentrically larger spheres around Hollowpoint with an estimated 2,000 surrounding decks), **TechSec** (the North Pole, pointing towards Corell), and the **South Pole** (the least explored area, home to a thriving criminal element).

The station is estimated to be over a million years old, making it one of the oldest known artificial constructions in the galaxy. No one truly knows who created it or why.

**Fluctuating Gravity:** Characters in Hollowtown or areas near the polar axis enjoy low gravity, whilst those in the outer Shells suffer high gravity penalties. Most inhabited polar areas have gravfield generators, but areas without them have either low gravity or none at all.

**The Centerpoint Spins:** The entire station spins to generate gravity via centrifugal force. Docking with a rotating station is challenging — most pilots rely on tractor beams. The spin-generated gravity causes nausea and claustrophobic reactions in newcomers.

### Regions

#### Hollowtown
The farms, ranches, estates, vacation villas, lakes, and parks occupying the 60-kilometre sphere at Centerpoint's middle. Suspended 30 kilometres overhead at the exact centre is the **Glowpoint** — a small artificial star providing light and warmth. The interior surface provides enough farmland to feed the entire station, and the lakes are large enough to host yachts and create rain.

The **Northern and Southern Conical Mountains** are centred on the polar axis, creating a near-zero gravity environment at the 7.5-kilometre central peak. Tourist attractions and resorts cluster near the Northern Conicals; the southern formation is surrounded by farms and ranches.

As many as 25 million tourists visit Hollowtown each year. Half of Centerpoint's population lives here — mostly farmers, nerf and nauga ranchers, or the very wealthy.

#### The Shells
Approximately 200 larger spheres nested inside Hollowtown, only partially explored. Over a dozen communities of around 10,000 inhabitants each ("shell-towns") exist between the North Docking Zone and Hollowtown, housing permanent support staff and families of those working in TechSec, the secretariat, or tourism.

#### Northern and Southern Docking Zones
Two major clusters of hangar bays where the central sphere joins to either pole. Each cluster has 30 bays arranged in a ring, each measuring two kilometres wide, one kilometre in height, and three kilometres deep. The Northern bays are controlled by the Executive Secretariat; the Southern bays are not in official use but have been appropriated by criminal elements.

Atmosphere corridors (functioning on similar principles to tractor beams) replace vacuum-sealed hangar doors, connecting ship hatches to station airlocks.

#### Technology Sector (TechSec)
The North Pole, occupied by various high-tech manufacturing concerns. Nearly two dozen major manufacturers (Corellian MasterNav, Corellstand, Corellidyne, CorelliSpace, and CEC) have robust workshops and leased hangar bays for prototypes. Security is exceptionally tight, shared between Centerpoint Security and private security firms. Also hosts the Admin Sector and most museum/university archaeological expeditions. Nearly a third of Centerpoint's population works here.

#### Null Town (Little Socorro)
The den of thieves, smugglers, and spice dealers near the Southern Docking Zone. Called Null Town because the polar region's gravity is only about five percent standard — residents need jetpacks, magnetised boots, jury-rigged electromagnets, or scrap-metal anchors. Controlled entirely by **Baron Kaldo**, whose syndicate takes a taste of all illicit sales or criminal acts. Daily operations are managed by **Gustip**, his Drallish majordomo.

### The People of Centerpoint

Centerpoint is governed by the **Executive Secretariat**, led by the Chief Executive appointed by the Federation of Double Worlds. The secretariat is divided between Administration (taxation, tariffs, leasing, permits) and Operations (security, construction, maintenance, transportation, orbital control, intelligence). Approximately 12,000 Fed-Dub personnel are on-station, half in Centerpoint Security.

Security is overstretched — there are not enough personnel to even map the entire station, let alone patrol it. The primary focus is protecting crops, tourism, and TechSec.

## Other Planets and Features

### Crollia
A rocky wasteland distantly orbiting Corell. Largely uninhabited due to a poisonous and nearly opaque atmosphere. Valuable to Corellia's industry — **crodium ore** was first discovered here (a very hard metal used in early hyperspace cannon spacecraft, rarely used in modern shipbuilding). Most mines were in Crollia's Maw, a massive canyon formed by diverging tectonic plates, but are now mostly played out.

The **Corellia Mining Corporation (CMC)** maintains a fleet of several hundred diggercrawlers, each crewed by a dozen sentients supervising mining droids on six-month tours. CMC favours all-Selonian crews. Transports arrive weekly.

### Soronia
The furthest planet from Corell. An uninhabited icy rock cloaked in mottled purple and blue atmosphere containing cyanogen and methane. Temperatures so cold that most life forms would freeze solid in moments.

At night, heavier gases in the atmosphere flash-freeze into delicate ice crystal "trees" forming haunting ethereal flash-forests — best known as part of a trendy fragrance advertising campaign on Corellia. The only visitors are scientists sending probes for cosmic radiation core samples and the occasional outlaw using natural caves to avoid CorSec.

### Kiris Asteroid Cluster
A dozen large tightly grouped asteroids far beyond Soronia's orbit. Unknown to even the most seasoned Corellian spacers. First discovered by CMC but faded into obscurity after surveys came back negative for precious metals.

### Pirate's Shadow Asteroid Field
Located in an asteroid-filled nebula just above the system's orbital plane. Long ago mined dry and abandoned by Corellian shipbuilders, leaving them riddled with old facilities and sealed mine shafts used as bolt holes by outlaws hiding from CorSec.

- **Rayless Lantern:** A sprawling warren of tunnels and chambers crawling with hundreds of Corellia's most ruthless criminals — the **Silestro Privateers**. Their beautiful and murderous leader **Captain Jostero**, supported by Black Sun, leads raids on merchant traffic throughout the system.
- **The Void:** A pocket of clear space within the Pirate's Shadow, fought over between the **Hidden Dagger pirates** and the **Hutt Cartel**. An excellent rally point for pirate raiders to strip or refit prize vessels.

### Gus Treta Inner System Market Station
Named for the small Corellian moon it orbits five times a day. A 300-metre station serving as a community centre for spacers — refuel, repair, resupply, look for work, and buy or sell cargo. Permanent population of four thousand, with another two thousand temporary residents. At peak time, as many as four thousand customers might be onboard. **Bell's Cantina** is the go-to spacer hangout for the cheapest spiced ale.

A few centuries ago, the pirate cruiser *Xim's Scion* was salvaged and its forward 250 metres converted into a refuelling depot. Eventually purchased by a Hutt named **Gormo Vosadii Grasso**, who worked with Hoersch-Kessel Drive to triple its size.

### Rubicund Eye (Adventure Hook)
An ageing extra-galactic observatory located below the Corellian System's orbital plane. Recently overrun by **Black Sun** operatives who have repelled numerous CorSec advances with advanced starfighters. No one knows why. Experts predict anything from looting to fortifying a permanent base to creating an exotic superweapon. CorSec is willing to pay good credits to anyone willing and crazy enough to take on Black Sun and kick them out.

## Adventure Hooks Summary

1. **Contract Negotiations (Corellia/Tyrena):** CEC employees disappearing in the North-East district. Confidential corporate data turning up on the black market. KDY headhunters suspected of industrial espionage.
2. **Agrilat Swoop Racing (Corellia):** The illegal underground swoop racing circuit in the Crystal Swamps — high stakes, high danger, and high reward.
3. **Ixxis and Su's Floating Game (Corellia):** A high-stakes, invitation-only sabacc game for those who have been in trouble with the law.
4. **Expeditions to the Rim (Drall):** The Mastigophorous Academy hires adventurers for lucrative research expeditions to the Outer Rim.
5. **The Deepwater Docks (Selonia):** Smuggling runs requiring ships modified for underwater travel, diving into the deepest oceanic trenches to reach hidden Selonian water-locks.
6. **The Drall's Shop (Talus/Dearic):** Finding the legendary elderly Drall black-marketeer hidden in the slums of Shove-Off Point, navigating his decoys and tricks.
7. **The Jarad Run (Tralus):** A deadly airspeeder race across the stormy Sea of Jarad — a rite of passage for young Tralusians.
8. **Centerpoint Mysteries (Centerpoint Station):** The ancient technology of the Glowpoint and the station's origins. Rorax Falken's journal hints at paradigm-shifting discoveries.
9. **Baron Kaldo's Empire (Centerpoint Station):** Sera Riggers hires independent contractors to root out organised crime on the station, putting them at odds with Baron Kaldo and his Selonian enforcers.
10. **Rubicund Eye (System Feature):** CorSec pays bounties for anyone brave enough to assault the observatory overrun by Black Sun.
11. **Pirate's Shadow (System Feature):** Captain Jostero and the Silestro Privateers operate from the Rayless Lantern; the Hidden Dagger pirates and Hutt Cartel fight over the Void.
