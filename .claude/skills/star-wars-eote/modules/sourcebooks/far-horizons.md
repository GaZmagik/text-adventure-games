## Chapter I: Building Better Worlds

### The Colonist Career
- Colonists are the galaxy's movers and shakers, from world-builders on the Outer Rim to social masters who use influence and personal charisma to advance their own agendas.
- They represent many fields of study or expertise, often prefer non-combat roles, and are typically highly charismatic.
- Isolation from the galaxy at large drives Colonists to be intelligent, focusing on leadership, healing, and legitimate commerce.
- Some seek personal goals such as celebrity; others strive to bring order and civil protection to far-flung settlements.
- Colonists account for the bulk of the galaxy's labour force — business owners, clerks, researchers, bookkeepers, accountants, public relations personnel, private security, entertainers, food producers, and healthcare workers.

### Colonist Backgrounds

**The Opportunist:** Arrives after the hard work of founding a colony is done, seeking fresh opportunity. Often looked down upon by established families. May be sent by a corporation or bureaucracy with minimal experience of local conditions.

**The Grizzled Settler:** Began as a young idealist, perhaps newly married, who chose the frontier for a fresh start. Life proved harder than expected — exotic diseases, hostile wildlife, and failed harvests can leave a settler with a pessimistic view of the galaxy.

**The Fugitive:** On the run from local governments, corporations, or the Empire. Colonies provide a safe haven where a person can hide, reinvent themselves, and start over.

**The Local Leader:** A prominent individual — businessperson, rancher, or professional — who steps in to fill the governance void in a small colony lacking formal government.

**The Idealist:** Leaves the Core Worlds with selfless intentions, seeking to aid the impoverished, oppressed, or endangered communities of the Outer Rim.

## New Species

### Arcona
- **Homeworld:** Cona (Inner Rim), a hot, dry desert planet with an atmosphere of nitrogen, hydrogen, and high ammonia
- **Physiology:** Slim-framed reptilian humanoids with clawed hands and feet, compound eyes (large, green, luminous), hairless scaleless skin of wood-like texture (green, brown, or near-black). Distinctive inverted-triangle head shape tapering into the neck. Infrared sensory organ between the eyes detects minute heat signature changes. Smell via tongues (flicking gesture). Poor eyesight; claws developed for digging.
- **Society:** Extremely community-oriented; think of themselves in the collective sense ("we" rather than "I"). Raising a family is both a duty and a privilege. Males are primary caregivers. Live in family-orientated nests clustered around a Grand Nest where the Nest Leader oversees communal decisions. Depend on ammonia supplements when offworld.
- **Salt Addiction:** Sodium chloride acts as a hallucinogen for Arcona and is ultimately fatal. Consuming 2-3 grams of pure salt disorients the Arcona for the remainder of the encounter; they must also make a Hard Discipline check or gain 2 Addiction Obligation. Addicted Arcona develop reflective golden eyes (natural colour is green).

**Arcona Species Profile:**
- Br 1, Ag 2, Int 2, Cun 2, Wil 3, Pr 2
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Begin with one rank in Vigilance (may not train above rank 2 during character creation). May remove Setback imposed due to arid or hot environmental conditions.
- **Mood Readers:** Add Advantage to any Charm or Negotiation checks.

### Chevin
- **Homeworld:** Vinsoth (Outer Rim), varied terrain from ice caps to deserts, mountains, and prairies
- **Physiology:** Solidly built, taller than humans and nearly twice as wide. Large, leathery faces with long, prominent snouts. Impervious grey skin over thick, muscular limbs. Keen sense of smell (can identify a case of coloured water in a shipment of booster blue). Double-lidded eyes for sandstorms/blizzards.
- **Society:** Trade is paramount; pride themselves on business acumen; consider wealth tantamount to prestige. Main exports are slaves and slave-related technology. Evolved alongside the Chevs on Vinsoth, whom they conquered and enslaved. Migratory species living in moving villages called "lodges." Chevin etiquette demands honesty, not mercy — when double-crossed, they prove violent and unrelenting in seeking revenge.
- **Slavery:** The Chevin enslaved the Chevs (the other sentient species on Vinsoth) using their hunting skills. Most Chevin consider this arrangement necessary and fair. The Empire is their main customer for slave-related technology.

**Chevin Species Profile:**
- Br 3, Ag 1, Int 2, Cun 3, Wil 2, Pr 1
- **Wound Threshold:** 11 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 11 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 80 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Begin with one rank in Negotiation (may not train above rank 2 during character creation).
- **Advanced Olfaction:** Add Setback to Perception checks involving the sense of smell.
- **Thick Hide:** Start the game with one rank in the Durable talent.

### Gran
- **Homeworld:** Kinyen (Expansion Region), on the Corellian Trade Spine. Lush forests and large plains of goatgrass. High-gravity planet.
- **Physiology:** Humanoid, three-eyed. Keen sense of sight beyond the spectrum of most sighted species, including infrared. Can detect minute physiological changes among their own kind that reveal emotions. Two stomachs; digest food slowly, allowing them to go without eating for days.
- **Society:** Socialists and staunch pacifists (though not all individual Gran follow this). Strong sense of community; raise children for specific careers to benefit the community as a whole. Depend on their kin emotionally and hate solitude — loneliness can drive them insane. The harshest punishment is exile, as leaving family and friends forever is a fate worse than death.
- **Gran of Malastare:** Many Gran now consider Malastare their homeworld. Their history there is more turbulent — they conquered the native Dugs with Republic support. Gran on Kinyen consider the Gran of Malastare to be almost a different species.

**Gran Species Profile:**
- Br 2, Ag 2, Int 1, Cun 2, Wil 2, Pr 3
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 9 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Begin with one rank in Charm or Negotiation (may not train the chosen skill above rank 2 during character creation).
- **Enhanced Vision:** When making ranged combat or Perception checks, Gran remove up to Setback Setback imposed due to environmental conditions or concealment (but not defence).

## New Specialisations

### Entrepreneur
- **Bonus Career Skills:** Discipline, Knowledge (Education), Knowledge (Underworld), Negotiation
- Motivated by profit and reliant on bribes and payouts. Lacks combat skills but makes up for it in contacts, knowledge, and the ability to buy himself out of trouble. Has a unique attitude towards Obligation with talents for dealing with it. Brings wealth to the group along with all the resources and shortcuts that credits can buy.

## New Talents

### Bad Cop
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Marshal
- May spend Advantage Advantage from a Deception or Coercion check to upgrade the ability of a single ally's subsequent Social Interaction skill check against the same target. Upgrade a number of times equal to ranks in Bad Cop. A single check may only benefit from one use of Bad Cop.

### Biggest Fan
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Performer
- Once per session, make a Hard Charm check. If successful, one NPC in the current encounter turns out to be the character's self-proclaimed "biggest fan." Effects vary: decreasing difficulty of Social Interaction checks targeting that NPC, the fan performing minor favours, or the NPC becoming a reoccurring ally.

### Bought Info
- **Activation:** Active (Action) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- Instead of making a Knowledge skill check, may spend credits equal to 50 times the difficulty of the check and count as succeeding with one uncancelled Success. GM may disallow if the information is particularly esoteric or the character has no access to the HoloNet.

### Congenial
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Performer
- When attempting a Charm or Negotiation check, may suffer strain (up to ranks in Congenial) to downgrade the difficulty an equal number of times. When targeted by a Charm or Negotiation check, may suffer strain (up to ranks in Congenial) to upgrade the difficulty an equal number of times.

### Coordination Dodge
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Performer
- When targeted by a combat check, may spend one Destiny Point to add Failure equal to ranks in Coordination to the check.

### Deceptive Taunt
- **Activation:** Active | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Performer
- Once per session, make an opposed Deception check targeting one NPC within medium range. If successful, the NPC must attack the character during its next turn. If the NPC cannot do so, it must spend all subsequent turns manoeuvring into position until it can make a melee or ranged attack against the character. Once the NPC has attacked, or the character is incapacitated or leaves the encounter, the effect ends.

### Distracting Behaviour (Improved)
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Performer
- When affected by Distracting Behaviour, NPCs suffer Threat Threat when making checks targeting the character's allies, instead of Threat.

### Good Cop
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Marshal
- May spend Advantage Advantage from a Charm or Negotiation check to upgrade the ability of a single ally's subsequent Social Interaction skill check against the same target. Upgrade a number of times equal to ranks in Good Cop. A single check may only benefit from one use of Good Cop.

### Greased Palms
- **Activation:** Active (Manoeuvre) | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- Before making a Social Interaction check, may spend up to 50 credits per rank of Greased Palms. For every 50 credits spent, upgrade the ability of the skill check once. Represents bribes, gifts, or purchasing advantageous information.

### Master Merchant
- **Activation:** Active | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- When buying or selling goods, paying off or taking Obligation, may suffer 2 strain to sell for 25% more, buy for 25% less, pay off 1 more Obligation, or take 1 less.

### Natural Athlete
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Performer
- Once per game session, may reroll any one Athletics or Coordination check.

### Natural Merchant
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- Once per session, may reroll any one Streetwise or Negotiation check.

### Sound Investments
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** Yes | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- At the beginning of each game session, gain credits equal to ranks in Sound Investments times 100. How this money is earned is up to the player and GM — actual investments, business dealings, or a small legal or illegal side business.

### Throwing Credits
- **Activation:** Active | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Entrepreneur
- At the beginning of a session (after the GM has rolled to trigger Obligation), may spend 100 credits to ignore any strain threshold penalties due to Obligation being triggered (if his own Obligation is triggered, it may still have plot complications, but he ignores the mechanical penalty).

### Unrelenting Skeptic
- **Activation:** Passive | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Marshal
- When targeted by a Deception check, automatically add Failure equal to ranks in Vigilance to the check.

### Unrelenting Skeptic (Improved)
- **Activation:** Active (Incidental, Out of Turn) | **Ranked:** No | **Trees:** Marshal
- When targeted by a Deception check that fails, may spend one Destiny Point to retroactively add Despair to the pool results.

## Colonist Signature Abilities

### Insightful Revelation
- **Base Ability (Cost 30):** Once per game session, spend two Destiny Points to perform the Insightful Revelation action: make a Hard Knowledge (Education) check. If successful, learn some valuable information not previously possessed, pertaining to the current situation. The information must be valuable to overcoming the immediate encounter or situation and cannot be obtainable by any other immediately available means.
- **Upgrades:**
  - **Reduce Setback (Cost 10, 15):** Remove Setback from the skill check to activate Insightful Revelation. May be taken twice.
  - **Destiny (Cost 10):** Costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.
  - **Reduce Difficulty (Cost 10):** Skill check becomes Average instead of Hard.
  - **Additional Skills (Cost 10):** May replace Knowledge (Education) with any other Knowledge skill.
  - **Add Boost (Cost 15):** Add Boost to the skill check.
  - **Increase Effect (Cost 15):** May spend Triumph to gain one additional piece of equally useful information.
  - **Duration (Cost 15):** May perform the action one additional time per game session.

### Unmatched Expertise
- **Base Ability (Cost 30):** Once per game session as an action, spend two Destiny Points to reduce the difficulty of all career skill checks by one (to a minimum of Easy) for the remainder of the encounter.
- **Upgrades:**
  - **Reduce Setback (Cost 10, 10):** Remove Setback from career skill checks while active. May be taken twice.
  - **Activation (Cost 10, 10):** Activate as a manoeuvre instead of an action. Second purchase makes it an incidental that may be triggered out of turn.
  - **Reduce Difficulty (Cost 15, 15):** Reduce difficulty by 2 instead of 1. Second purchase reduces minimum to Simple instead of Easy.
  - **Superior Reduction (Cost 15):** Once per session while active, may also reduce the difficulty of one non-career skill check.
  - **Destiny (Cost 15):** Costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.

## Chapter III: New Horizons Await

### Integrating Colonist Characters
- Colonists embody a variety of social and supporting roles. They tend to have higher social standing than other careers.
- Integrating Colonists into an adventuring group is simple when they fall into a natural role (healers, leaders). More difficult to explain can be the presence of a Scholar, Performer, or Marshal.
- Key background questions: Who is the character's most recent employer? What position does he hold? How much tolerance does the employer have for disruptions?

### Dealing with Specialised Roles

**Doctor — Applied Pressure:** GMs can increase pressure via dangerous locations (crossfire, fire, poison gas, rampaging creatures, collapsing structures), time pressure (adding Setback or requiring Cool/Discipline checks), and emergency triage (Daunting Medicine check to assess patients equal to Medicine rank in a single round).

**Entrepreneur — Cashing In:** Features toughness, strain recovery, access to funds, and unique ways to spend credits. Sound Investments provides a regular income. Encounters should highlight negotiation, selling goods legally, and paying his way to social encounter success.

**Marshal — Edge of the Law:** Combination of investigative talents and pure toughness. Good Cop and Bad Cop talents help allies question suspects. GM should prepare for the good cop/bad cop routine and consider how NPCs react to different questioning approaches. Investigation checklist: how the crime occurred, what clues were left, who has helpful information, cover-ups, and whether it is a one-time or recurring crime.

**Performer — Enter Stage Left:** Distracting Behaviour is one of the more disruptive combat-related talents. Smooth Talker provides better skill check results. Physical performers translate abilities into faster recovery via Second Wind and Natural Athlete.

**Politico — Talking Points:** Lives by his ability to talk his way through any situation. Inspiring Rhetoric and Improved Inspiring Rhetoric talents can be maximised by encounters that inflict strain on allies. Improved Scathing Tirade combined with high Coercion is devastating to nearby enemies.

**Scholar — Information from Everywhere:** Adept at revealing key information quickly. Respected Scholar talent reduces difficulty of interacting with institutes of learning. Speaks Binary talent grants Boost per rank on checks to direct droids (works on NPC droids only).

### Making Social Encounters Interesting
- Social encounters benefit from planning; major scenes should have key facts the GM wants NPC characters to know during the conversation.
- Talents can be used outside combat in social situations with appropriate adaptations.
- When a target exceeds their strain threshold in a social encounter: gives in to the opponent's demands, cannot perform actions (only basic manoeuvres/incidentals), cannot aid others, can talk but their words are ineffective.
- GMs should not be afraid of inflicting strain in social encounters — living up to a certain standard, acting a certain role, or negotiating for one's livelihood are stressful activities.
- **Information Types** to inject into social encounters: Anger Management, Blown Cover, Connection Revealed, Fraud Revealed, Love and Romance, Offer Terms, Technical Data, Unexpected/Unintended Insult.

### Colonist Contracts

**Picking at Scabs:** PCs hired by a Chevin mining tycoon to mediate between him and striking miners who have barricaded themselves in the mine. The mine may contain a unique material that the Empire or Rebellion also wants.

**Splitting Heirs:** PCs hired by House Demici on Serenno to locate a missing teenaged heir. Investigation leads to House Nalju territory, a kidnapping, and a conspiracy by both young heirs to kill their fathers and unite their houses.

**Political Party:** PCs hired to perform at a Fete Week celebration that doubles as a re-election campaign rally. A senior political aide is murdered, and the PCs must lead the investigation. The trail leads through affairs, gambling debts, and a local power broker called "Big Bantha."

**Frontier Claim:** A PC inherits a parcel of land with a cave system rich in minerals and gems. Upon arrival, a swoop gang has overrun the property. After dealing with the gang and exploring the dangerous caves, the swoop gang returns in force — and their leader may have a legitimate claim to the property.

### Colonist Campaign Ideas

**Neutral Practices:** PCs hired by the Refugee Relief Movement to reopen a hospital in an active war zone. Five-episode campaign involving clearing the hospital, staffing, supply management, dealing with Imperial demands, and discovering a Rebel recruiting operation disguised as a slave-rescue operation.

**Improving Station:** PCs travel to Gandanta Station to meet a Toydarian fence named Flummot. Ship is stolen; PCs are forced to work odd jobs, eventually being recruited by Black Sun to oppose Hutt Cartel control. Six-episode campaign culminating in the PCs choosing whether to stay with Black Sun, ally with the Hutts, go independent, or go legitimate.

**Going Native:** PCs sent by the Imperial Xenodetic Survey Department to study a recently discovered aboriginal species. Five-episode campaign involving first contact, learning native culture, discovering aurodium deposits, and trying to prevent the Imperial governor from strip-mining native land.

**How the Rest Was Won:** PCs hired by the Imperial Colonisation Board to evaluate a failing colony and get it back on track. Three-episode campaign involving curing a plague, solving a food shortage caused by ant-like insects, and identifying trade resources to attract merchant traffic.
