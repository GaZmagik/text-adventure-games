## Chapter III: Player Options

### New Species

#### Drall

Small, fur-covered, scholarly beings native to Drall, the fifth planet of the Corellian System. Drall prefer to read about and study the galaxy from afar rather than experience it directly. Those who do venture out tend towards the roles of Doctor or Scholar.

| Characteristic | Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Value | 1 | 1 | 4 | 2 | 2 | 2 |

- **Wound Threshold:** 8 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 12 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 90 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Drall begin the game with one rank in Knowledge (Education). They still may not train Knowledge (Education) above rank 2 during character creation. Drall have a mind for problems and their solutions. In addition to using his skill or characteristic rating, a Drall adds [Boost] to the dice pool when providing skilled assistance.

#### Selonian

Carnivorous, mammalian bipeds covered in short, sleek brown or black fur. Selonians have long, slender bodies, elongated heads with whiskers, and strong tails about half a metre long. Adult females average about two metres tall. Their society is organised around dens governed by a single fertile Queen. Honourable and forthright to a fault, Selonians are virtually incapable of outright deceit.

| Characteristic | Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Value | 2 | 3 | 2 | 1 | 3 | 1 |

- **Wound Threshold:** 11 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 80 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Selonians begin the game with one rank in Coordination. They still may not train Coordination above rank 2 during character creation. A Selonian's eyes allow her to see in near total darkness; she may remove all [Setback] added to checks due to darkness, though she still cannot see in absolute darkness.
- **Tail:** A Selonian's tail may be used as a Brawl weapon (uses Agility). It adds its weapon damage to the character's Brawn for total damage. It always counts as equipped. Profile: Brawl; Damage +1; Critical 5; Range [Engaged]; Disorient 2, Knockdown. The Selonian may trigger Knockdown with [Advantage], but still must spend additional [Advantage] to knock down targets with a higher silhouette as per the Knockdown quality rules.

#### Corellian Human

Renowned pilots and spacefarers, Corellians tend to have an independent, somewhat reckless streak coupled with an enterprising, can-do attitude. Corellians are naturally exceptional pilots, and nearly all are capable by maturity. They are also renowned explorers, traders, engineers, and tinkerers.

| Characteristic | Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Value | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 | 2 |

- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 110 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Corellians begin the game with one rank in Piloting (Planetary) or Piloting (Space). Because Corellians are naturally exceptional pilots and grow up handling swoops, airspeeders, and spacecraft from a young age, they may train Piloting up to rank 3 during character creation.

**CDEF Blaster Pistol:** A basic, cheap design by Corellian Arms, a subsidiary of CEC. Standard sidearm issued to CorSec officers. Small, reliable, and easy to maintain but lacks stopping power.

**Coronet Arms Duelling Pistol:** High-quality blasters almost invariably sold in pairs. Duelling has a long history on Corellia. The particle streams quickly dissipate, so they are meant for close range only. The power pack produces only one blast, but it is almost invariably lethal.

**Coronet Arms HL-27 Light Blaster Pistol:** A sterling example of superior craftsmanship. Favoured by the well-heeled of Corellia. An excellent choice for those who prefer discretion to bellicose valour. Popular with CorSec agents who can afford them.

**Coronet Arms H-7 "Equaliser" Blaster Pistol:** Considered one of the finest production model blasters in the galaxy. Produced in limited numbers with unparalleled quality and precision. Carried by high-ranking CorSec officers and successful free-traders as status symbols.

**Fed-Dub Protective Services Spukami Pocket Blaster Pistol:** Designed for close-quarters combat only. When firing at short range (or longer), gains the Inaccurate 1 quality (does not apply at engaged range). Add [Setback] to Perception checks when attempting to find a holdout pistol on a person's body.

**Corellian Arms CR-2 Heavy Blaster Pistol:** A somewhat outdated weapon no longer in production. Fires a focused beam of charged plasma. Reliable and easy to maintain, usually fitted with a second under-barrel forearm grip.

**SKZ Sporting Blaster:** A popular civilian weapon for hunting and self-defence. Compact, simple design with a rail system for attachments. Can be broken down into three parts for transport.

**Corellian Cutlass:** Traditionally granted to Corellian naval officers upon promotion to full lieutenant. A relatively short, broad sabre with a solid hand guard. The weight and craftsmanship makes it effective in close combat. Some free-traders who previously served in the navy retain and wear them.

**J-7b Beamdrill:** The standard beamdrill used by Corellia Mining Corporation. Uses intense pulses of plasma to strip away rock and can carve through virtually anything, including armour. Not intended as a weapon, but extremely lethal when used as one. Has even been used by pirates to breach hulls and bulkheads.

**Drall Flashstick:** Used almost exclusively by the Drall constabulary. A small metal baton that emits a brilliant, coruscating flash of light when striking, temporarily blinding and stunning the target. Particularly effective against Drall, Defel, Selonians, and Sullustans (light-sensitive species). Virtually no effect on species that do not rely on visual senses.

**Selonian Glaive:** Individually crafted by Selonian females as a rite of passage. A heavy wooden pole about two metres long with a large, razor-sharp obsidian blade and obsidian hooks and barbs. Selonian obsidian is unusually strong and tough with a distinctive iridescent quality; the blade edge is mere nanometres thick.

Used by the CDF and also popular with pirates, mercenaries, and bounty hunters. Provides protection from vacuum, concussive and energy attacks. Without an external oxygen source, a person can survive for up to 24 hours in hard vacuum.

**Cirenian Communications Pioneer Long-Range Transceiver (Hypertransceiver):** Subspace transceivers used for nearly instantaneous, faster-than-light communications between star systems. Powerful models can transmit up to 100 light years. Small, portable versions available with ranges up to about 25 light years.

**Carbanti United Electronics Whistler (Encryption Module):** Encryption modules attach to comlinks or hypertransceivers and use complex algorithms and ciphers to code transmissions. Without the decryption key, it is nearly impossible to break the code. Attempts to decode without a receiver with the decryption key are Formidable difficulty and require sophisticated equipment such as a ship's mainframe or a Class One droid.

**Bespin Motors Remote DVI Activator:** A remote activation controller (beckon call) is a small handheld device linked to a slave circuit that allows the user to remotely activate a coupled vehicle or starship. Inexpensive models have short ranges (no more than 2-5 kilometres). Sophisticated models can transmit through hyperspace up to a parsec away. Advanced models can remotely pilot via a slave circuit, though this requires a Daunting Piloting check.

**Corellian Bloodstripes:** The highest and most prestigious Corellian military honour. Yellow or red piping stitched down the trouser legs. Awarded for conspicuous gallantry and valour. A recipient may add [Boost] to any social skill checks when dealing with a Corellian. However, anyone caught wearing unearned Bloodstripes earns the enmity of all Corellians; future social skill checks with Corellians would suffer [Setback][Setback], and a hot-blooded Corellian might challenge the character to a showdown.

**Corellian Whiskey / Whyren's Reserve:** A popular alcoholic drink distilled and aged for three to twenty years in wooden casks. **Ji Rikknit:** A highly-addictive narcotic cultivated from the ovum sacks of rikknits on New Plympto. Provides only a mildly euphoric feeling. If someone tries the narcotic, they must make an Easy Resilience check or gain a 1 value Addiction Obligation.

**Nannarium Root:** Large blue flowers native to Drall with nootropic pharmacological properties. The extract increases cognition and mental acuity. A character under the effects may upgrade the ability of any Intelligence or Cunning-related check once, though doing so causes them to suffer 2 strain. Effects last about an hour.

**Ryshcate:** A sweet, cake-like pastry eaten by Corellians to mark holidays and special occasions. Made with Corellian whiskey and vweilu nuts. Due to the alcohol used in baking, ryshcate cakes can be preserved and remain edible for years or even decades.

### 1. Sabacc Game on the Row

**Location:** Treasure Ship Row, Blue Sector, Coronet City, Corellia
**Situation:** The PCs find themselves invited to a private sabacc game at Bovo's, a rundown cantina off the main pathways. The whole thing is a set-up by a con artist named Catrinna and a crime lord named Carn Truuvik to take advantage of newcomers.

**Involving the Players:** Catrinna -- a charming con artist -- approaches the PCs. She may strike up conversation and invite them to a friendly game, approach a PC by name claiming to have heard of his gambling skills, or stage a heated argument with the Rodian Grenzo to lure well-meaning PCs into offering help. If the PCs volunteer to help, she tells them about a sabacc game that night.

**The Game at Bovo's:** Catrinna leads the group to Bovo's back room -- a comfortable lounge with a bar, greel wood panels, nerf leather furniture, and a table for up to ten sabacc players. Four other players are present: Carn Truuvik, Kanz, Jalla, and Grenzo.

**Sabacc Rules:**
1. The table agrees on a wager and each player pays into the pot.
2. Players use Cool to establish a dice pool.
3. The GM determines difficulty based on the game and opponents' skill (opposed check if against a skilled NPC).
4. Each character rolls their check. Each also rolls a single [Force Die] to illustrate the shifting nature of the cards' signs.
5. To cheat, a character uses Deception, Computers, or Skulduggery instead of Cool. They must upgrade the difficulty once. If a [Despair] is generated, the character is caught cheating.
6. Success means the PC wins back his wager; failure means he loses. Each additional [Success] wins another wager's worth. A [Triumph] reveals a positive, negative, or natural 23 and wins the entire pot.

**Spending Advantage/Threat/Triumph/Despair:**
- [Advantage]: Recover one strain as cards unexpectedly flip usefully
- [Advantage][Advantage]: Add [Boost] to next check (successful bluff)
- [Advantage][Advantage][Advantage]: Figure out an opponent's tells; downgrade next check difficulty once
- [Triumph]: Reveal a winning 23 and win the entire pot (GM determined)
- [Threat]: Suffer one strain as cards flip detrimentally
- [Threat][Threat]: Add [Setback] to next check (fell for a bluff)
- [Threat][Threat][Threat]: Shattered concentration; upgrade next check difficulty once
- [Despair]: Caught cheating; character bombs out, runs out of chips, and can no longer play

**The Con:** In the early rounds, Carn lets the PCs win to keep them interested (GM sets Average difficulty, occasionally upgraded with Destiny Points). As things progress, Carn begins playing seriously (opposed Skulduggery check). He aims to force the PCs into debt. Add [Setback] for each gambler working against the PCs; increase gradually. Catrinna offers generous loans and eventually suggests using their ship as collateral.

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **PCs pull out early:** Carn grows cold and threatening, suggesting he does not appreciate them pulling out
- **PCs win significantly:** Carn congratulates them and allows them to keep their winnings (maintaining his con for future marks)
- **PCs catch Carn cheating:** They discover him if checks generate [Advantage][Advantage][Advantage][Advantage][Advantage] or [Triumph][Triumph]; he denies it and offers reimbursement
- **PCs lose or are caught cheating:** Carn establishes the PCs owe him, translating into a Favour Obligation of 5 for the entire group. They can only rid themselves of it by doing one or two jobs for Carn

### 2. Tunnel Delving

**Location:** Scasmirs Island, Selonia
**Situation:** The PCs have landed on Selonia and descended into a Selonian Den to explore. After visiting the underground settlement of Scasmirs Den, a cave-in cuts them off from the surface. They must press on through the tunnels, encountering obstacles, local wildlife, and xenophobic Selonians.

**Scasmirs Den:** A cargo loading bay the size of an arena with mobile pedways. A Selonian customs official provides a 24-hour visa. The cavern contains a prosperous main thoroughfare bordered by gardens of luminous fungus and pale ferns, with shops and businesses cut into the cavern walls.

**The Cave-in:** While wandering a deserted side passage, structural supports give way. PCs can make Average Perception checks; [Success] indicates low foot traffic, [Advantage] indicates unsound structural supports. PCs who failed the Perception check suffer one wound (ignoring soak) plus one additional wound per [Threat]. All PCs must make an Easy Resilience check or suffer one strain (plus additional strain per [Threat]) from choking dust.

**Two Choices:**
- **Wait for Rescue:** PCs will not be missed for 24 hours, then 6 more hours to search. Every six hours, make Resilience checks of increasing difficulty (Simple, then Easy, then progressively harder) as air grows stale. Failure causes strain equal to difficulty plus additional strain per [Threat].
- **Move On:** Navigate through a series of obstacles.

**Tunnel Obstacles:**

- **Light Sources:** Without glow rods, PCs suffer [Setback][Setback][Setback] to all checks. Bioluminescent fungus can be harvested with an Easy Survival check, reducing penalty to [Setback].
- **Climbing the Chimney:** Average Athletics check on a slick chute covered in dripping water. Failure with [Despair] or [Threat][Threat][Threat] means falling from short range and suffering falling damage. With rope, the first PC up can help others (reducing to Easy Athletics).
- **Narrow Passage:** A horizontal crack only 40 cm high. Easy Coordination check to wiggle through (GM may add [Setback] for bulky PCs or excessive equipment). Failure causes one strain; [Threat][Threat][Threat] means striking head and suffering one wound (ignoring soak).

### 3. Hard Bargain

**Location:** Gorn's Emporium, Seelom District of Jyvus City, Duro orbital city-station
**Situation:** Gorn Vorrox, a Duros fence and information broker, runs an unassuming shop. While the PCs are negotiating with Gorn, an unhappy customer named Rasz Kal arrives with thugs and a confrontation erupts.

**Gorn's Emporium:** Flanked by two battered OOM-series security droids (OB-1E and OB-2B) that bicker constantly. The shop is filled with junk, rusty surplus blasters, and spare droid parts.

**Hidden Storage Room:** Finding the concealed wall panel requires a Hard Perception check. The lock requires a Hard Skulduggery check. Contents: a CR-2 heavy blaster pistol, a duelling pistol, two thermal detonators, a damaged EVA powersuit, weapon and armour upgrades, about 2,000 credits worth of illegal spice, and an encrypted datapad with details about Gorn's business operations.

**An Unhappy Customer:** Rasz Kal enters with two Aqualish thugs and a Barabel, accuses Gorn of selling him a defective hyperdrive motivator, and demands 3,500 credits back. The situation quickly escalates to drawn blasters.

**Multiple Approaches:**
- **Diplomatic:** A charming, cool individual may attempt to calm Rasz and convince him the sale was an honest mistake, or suggest Gorn repair or replace the motivator. Good negotiation skills can facilitate an exchange.
- **Intimidation:** A physical character might try to intimidate either Rasz or Gorn into acquiescence.
- **Combat:** If no one intervenes, a firefight ensues between Rasz's thugs, Gorn and his droids, and the PCs caught in the crossfire. A Duros security detail led by Lieutenant-Constable Kreeg arrives 15-20 minutes after any firefight.

**Denouement:**
- If PCs help calm things at Gorn's expense, Gorn charges 10% more on future transactions
- If PCs convince Rasz to leave peacefully, Gorn offers a 10% discount on future transactions
- If Gorn is aided and Rasz dies or flees, Gorn offers a free item and 10% future discount (provided PCs help dispose of bodies)
- A security detail arrives after any firefight; 100 credits is enough to convince them to look the other way; if Gorn is dead, convincing Kreeg to let them go requires charm or a bribe of 1,000-2,000 credits

**Lasting Repercussions:** Both Gorn and Rasz have ties to various underworld organisations. Killing Gorn may earn the PCs Obligation to Black Sun. Rasz owes a debt to a Hutt crime lord. Harming Kreeg makes the PCs wanted on Duro.

**The Six Legs:**
