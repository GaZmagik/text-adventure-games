# Suns of Fortune — The Corellian Sector
> Sourcebook module for the star-wars-eote skill. Wider Corellian Sector locations.

## Sector Overview

The Corellian Sector is one of the most important regions in the galaxy, encompassing roughly thirty star systems. It has a long tradition of independence, codified in the ancient proviso known as Contemplanys Hermi ("meditative solitude"), which granted the sector distance from the Republic whilst remaining a member. The sector remained neutral throughout the Clone Wars, though the Empire has since revoked any semblance of special status, nationalising industries and reassigning the former Duro Sector under Corellian administration.

### Grand Moff Naomi Dargon

The sector is governed by Grand Moff Naomi Dargon, the second to hold the position after Fliry Vorru was framed and shipped off to Kessel by Prince Xizor of Black Sun. Dargon rose from a family of influential Coruscanti industrialists who backed Palpatine. She is a ruthless political manipulator who plays sector factions against one another rather than against the Empire. She ordered the devastating orbital bombardment of Froz, earning the title of war criminal from the Rebellion.

### Key Information Services

- **Corellia News Service (CNS)** — One of two major sector-wide information companies.
- **Corellia Sector Newsfeed (CSN)** — The other major information company; both are increasingly subject to Imperial oversight.

## Duro

### Description

Duro is a heavily polluted industrial wasteland in the Core region, its surface choked by centuries of unchecked industrialisation and rendered fully uninhabitable by General Grievous's orbital bombardment during the Clone Wars. The entire population of 18.5 billion (Duros 53%, Human 36%, other 11%) lives in orbiting city-sized space stations. The planet's surface is covered in barren wastes, massive mining caverns, greasy brown rivers, and a perpetual yellow-brown smog. Imperial permits are required to land on the surface.

- **System:** Duro System, Corellian Sector (formerly Duro Sector), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 420 days per year / 33 hours per day
- **Government:** Corporatocracy (Duros High House)
- **Language:** Durese, Basic
- **Terrain:** Polluted industrial wasteland
- **Major Cities:** Bburru Station (capital), Jivv Space City, Jyvus Space City, Ranadaast
- **Major Exports:** Starships, technology
- **Major Imports:** Foodstuffs, ore, labour
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Trade Spine, Duros Space Run

### Points of Interest

- **Duro Starshipwright Shipyards** — Massive, technologically advanced orbital shipyards operated by the Duro Shipwrights Guild. Corellian Engineering Corporation (CEC) is a major stockholder. The Empire has subsumed much of the capacity for Imperial Naval construction. Millions of Duros make a living by renting repair shop slots on the stations.
- **Valley of Royalty** — An ancient archaeological complex on the surface, once listed among the "Twenty Wonders of the Galaxy." Built to honour Duros rulers with enormous monuments, bas reliefs, and carvings detailing the planet's history. Abandoned nearly four thousand years ago due to acidic pollutants; now extremely dangerous to explore. Archaeological expeditions operate under severe restrictions.
- **Tayana Ruins** — A mighty mining and processing complex located within a giant meteor crater. Once a centre of ship-building resource extraction; now a smoking ruin on the toxic surface.
- **Tiercan Dam** — An extraordinary engineering feat from Duro's Industrial Age, designed to hold back acid-polluted waters from marginally habitable lands. A symbol of the planet's industrial peak.
- **Queen Rana's Vault** — A legendary hidden vault said to contain valuable Rakata technology collected by Queen Rana Mas Trehalt. Located somewhere in the ruins of Ranadaast, it is guarded by the inhospitable planet and vicious fefze beetles. No one knows precisely what it contains.

### Notable Features

- The Duros are credited with bringing intragalactic space travel to the Core Worlds, fundamentally shaping galactic civilisation. They evolved from ancient reptiles and were once enslaved by the Rakata to build the Star Forge.
- The Duros High House is a corporatocracy; the Chief Representative Officer is elected from representatives of major corporations.
- The Neimoidians are a distant offshoot of the Duros species, now slowly reintegrating following the fall of the Trade Federation.
- The atmosphere rates at minimum 5 on Table 6-8 (Fire, Acid, and Corrosive Atmospheres) in the EotE Core Rulebook, with some areas reaching 8. Rivers and streams are at least one level higher.
- **Fefze Beetles** — The dominant remaining life form on the surface. Originally imported from the planet Fef as workers, they mutated and now breed unchecked. They are landspeeder-sized, predatory, and spit digestive acid. They are the only creatures that can survive the toxic environment.

### Adventure Hooks

- Searching for Queen Rana's Vault amidst the toxic ruins of Ranadaast, evading fefze beetles and security droids.
- Smuggling goods to or from the orbital shipyards despite increased Imperial customs presence.
- Exploring the Valley of Royalty for historical artefacts before the corrosive atmosphere destroys them.
- Getting a ship repaired by a favourite Duros mechanic despite Imperial security patrols.
- Investigating the political machinations of Grand Moff Dargon as she plays Duros and Corellians against one another.

## Nubia

### Description

Nubia is a prosperous, well-balanced Core World known for ronto racing, starship design, and agricultural abundance. Terraformed roughly 22 millennia ago using massive subterranean aquifers, it has a temperate climate, gently rolling plains, mountain ranges rich in bronzium and precious metals, and two major landmasses separated by shallow mineral-rich oceans. The population of 2.6 billion (Human 92%, other 8%) enjoys one of the highest standards of living in the Core.

- **System:** Nubus System, Corellian Sector (Outlier Systems), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 289 days per year / 29 hours per day
- **Government:** Democracy (nominally; actually controlled by a corporate shadow government led by Prall the Hutt via PharmCorp)
- **Language:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Plains and mountains
- **Major Cities:** Nuba City (capital), Rordis City
- **Major Exports:** Starship parts, foodstuffs, ore, astromech droids
- **Major Imports:** Low-tech manufactured goods
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Run
- **Special Conditions:** Strong Imperial customs presence overseeing all major trade; major starship purchases interdicted for unauthorised clients.

### Points of Interest

- **Nubian Palace** — One of the most famous resorts on Nubia, featuring twelve casinos, numerous holovid theatres, live performance stages, a virtual reality installation, and the incredible Solar Gardens. Secretly serves as the base of operations for Prall the Hutt, PharmCorp's CEO.
- **Tallera Downs** — The most famous ronto racing track in the galaxy, located just south of Nuba City. Host of the annual Tallera Open, a glamorous event attracting the rich and famous from across the Core Worlds.
- **Nubian Design Collective Museum of Engineering and Design** — Celebrating the legacy of Nubia Star Drives, Inc. and the planet's engineering heritage.
- **The Shorn Stabiliser** — A neutral cantina considered safe ground by ruffians, smugglers, and criminals throughout the sector. Owned by an Ithorian named Aarrom, who maintains peace with four state-of-the-art security droids. Not a place for shady deals, but rather where enemies can meet to discuss differences without violence.

### Notable Features

- **Nubia Star Drives, Incorporated** — A prestigious starship manufacturer focused on unique, specialised designs rather than mass production. Best known for the J-type vessels (star yachts and transports designed for Naboo royalty) and the B4 Cloudbus. Most top engineering staff have quietly gone underground to keep their work from the Empire.
- **Grav-ball** — A popular Nubian sport played on a special court by two teams of six wearing hover boots, wielding repulsor batons to throw a ball at a goal.
- **Ronto Racing** — A passion for Nubians; rontos are large quadruped mammals native to the planet, bred and trained through exacting regimes for competitive racing.
- Nubia's ecological balance is maintained through strict environmental development principles established by the original terraformers, treating environmental preservation almost as a religion.
- The planet is secretly run by Prall the Hutt through a cabal of human executives, using PharmCorp's influence to control the democratic government.

### Adventure Hooks

- Infiltrating the Nubian Palace to uncover Prall the Hutt's corporate empire.
- Rigging or betting on a ronto race at the Tallera Downs during the annual Open.
- Making contact with underground Nubia Star Drives engineers who have hidden their best designs from the Empire.
- Negotiating a deal at the Shorn Stabiliser with rival factions.
- Smuggling restricted starship parts past Imperial customs patrols.

## Aurea

### Description

Aurea is a hidden jewel of the Corellian Sector — an artists' haven and resort planet famed for its extraordinary blown glass and crystalline artworks. Discovered accidentally when a passenger liner made an emergency landing near the Shakamm Crater, the planet has a diverse biosphere with tropical and temperate regions, golden coastal beaches, deep blue oceans, lush jungles, and vibrant desert interiors. The population of 240 million (Human 62%, other 38%) is governed by a meritocracy.

- **System:** Aurea System, Corellian Sector (Outlier Systems), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 266 days per year / 20 hours per day
- **Government:** Meritocracy (Soulworks Collective Advisory Council)
- **Language:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Temperate and tropical forests, desert plains, mountains, and oceans
- **Major Cities:** Crysallia (capital), Kammas, Ifeoma (Aurea's third moon)
- **Major Exports:** Glass, artisan crafts, spices, ore
- **Major Imports:** Foodstuffs, manufactured goods, technology (especially water collection and purification)
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Trade Spine

### Points of Interest

- **Shakamm Crater** — A gargantuan meteor crater over 10 kilometres across on the largest northern continent. The site where artist Coovo Mardri first discovered the burning crystals forged in the prehistoric impact. The Mardri arcology perches on the northwest lip. Extremely limited mining extracts crystals for use by the Soulworks Collective artisans.
- **Dome of Crystal Fire** — A gargantuan cave deep below the Torragan Mountains, over two thousand kilometres southeast of the crater. Its walls are covered in burning crystals that create a constantly shifting display of coloured light, believed to change based on the spirits of those within. Access is limited to trained guides and armed guards.
- **Eternal Furnace** — A giant lava pool in the Torragan Mountains used by the Aurean Glassworkers Guild for their most audacious projects. Apprentices spend many years developing tolerances to work near the fiery heat. Geologists speculate it may have formed from tectonic activity similar to the meteor impact.
- **Mardri Soulworks Collective** — A self-sustaining arcology housing over ten thousand artists, craftsmen, poets, and musicians, built on the site of Coovo Mardri's original home next to the Shakamm Crater. Features hydroponic gardens and water-reclamation technology.
- **Ifeoma (Moon)** — Aurea's third moon, tidally locked with a cool, humid, cloud-covered environment. The city of Ifeoma is a haven for wanderers, ne'er-do-wells, and those down on their luck.
- **Carmine Peaks** — Red-hued sandstone mountains on a smaller northern continent containing bronzium and other useful metals. Mining is heavily restricted by the Advisory Council.

### Notable Features

- Aurea's civilisation was founded by the artist Coovo Mardri, who discovered crystals forged from desert sands in the heat of the prehistoric asteroid impact. The planet's capital, Crysallia, was as much a massive art project as a settlement.
- The Aurean Glassworkers Guild guarantees that if any work by a Master is broken, the Master will re-craft the piece into something even more exquisite.
- There are persistent legends that some burning crystals are connected to the Force — tales insist the Soulworks Collective secretly provided the Healing Crystals of Fire to the Jedi Temple. The Advisory Council dismisses this as romantic nonsense.
- One third of the population works in the tourism industry.
- Trade in ghonna spice (a tropical seed used in cooking) forms a moderately healthy part of the economy.
- **Aurean Vultures** — Desert predators with wingspans over four metres; they follow prey for days before diving from the angle of the sun to blind targets.

### Adventure Hooks

- **Heist of the Century** — Notorious art thief Bryn Shale has publicly promised to steal Kasa Xanter's priceless sculpture, the Dawn of Light, from the supposedly impenetrable Vorheim Gallery. She is looking for a crew. Aurea's law enforcement is also offering a hefty reward for capturing her, "alive or identifiable."
- Smuggling stolen Aurean art off-world through Ifeoma's less-than-reputable moon city.
- Exploring the Dome of Crystal Fire for Force-connected crystals.
- Investigating illegal mining operations in the Carmine Peaks.
- Protecting a shipment of priceless glasswork from pirates along the Corellian Trade Spine.

## Sacorria

### Description

Sacorria is one of the "breadbaskets" of the Corellian Sector — a beautiful, hospitable agricultural world ruled by the mysterious and authoritarian Sacorrian Triad. Forged from a violent, bloody beginning, it is now a peaceful, law-abiding planet with a population of 600 million (Human 40%, Selonian 30%, Drall 30%) that values productivity and conformity above all else. It is highly insular, with very strict laws governing offworlders.

- **System:** Sacorrian System, Corellian Sector (Outlier Systems), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 343 days per year / 23 hours per day
- **Government:** Dictatorship (the Sacorrian Triad)
- **Language:** Basic, Drallish, Selonian
- **Terrain:** Forests, plains, mountains, small seas
- **Major Cities:** Dorthus Tal City (capital), Saccorata
- **Major Exports:** Wood, foodstuffs, alcohol (Sacorrian whiskey)
- **Major Imports:** Weapons and defence systems, electronics, luxury goods
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Trade Spine
- **Special Conditions:** Highly insular with strict laws governing non-natives. Visitors should keep to the Trade Zone of Dorthus Tal. Even minor infractions outside the Trade Zone can result in severe consequences.

### Points of Interest

- **Dorthus Tal City** — The capital, built on an island in a shallow sea. The main starport and trade hub; one of the few places non-Sacorrians may visit without extensive regulation. Gaining permission to travel beyond the Trade Zone is extraordinarily difficult.
- **Cobble Stone Square** — A tourist attraction on the south edge of the Trade Zone, portraying an idealised version of early Sacorrian life. Trained artisans and re-enactors work and live there, demonstrating ancient techniques. Discussion of Sacorria's violent early history is forbidden and punishable by imprisonment.
- **Dorthus Tal Prison** — A massive, imposing structure powered by geothermal energy from a nearby volcano. Claimed to be completely escape-proof. Visitors are never permitted inside, though tour guides point it out.
- **Watchtower Base** — A huge edifice housing an entire regiment of security police, overlooking the capital. Operates batteries of surveillance cameras, drones, listening devices, and police patrols blanketing the island.
- **Sarcophagus Moon** — Sacorria's sole moon, used exclusively for interring the dead. The Triad determined that using farmland for burial was wasteful. Telescopes for observing the moon are banned, as it is deemed "disrespectful of the deceased."

### Notable Features

- **The Sacorrian Triad** — A group of three individuals (one human, one Drall, one Selonian) who hold absolute authority. Their identities are utterly unknown to the general public; even the cabals who elect new members only know the identity of the member they chose. The Triad maintains peace through a combination of negotiation, coercion, and murder.
- **Sacorrian Meritorious Incentive Programme (SMIP)** — Every agricultural and logging operation is evaluated quarterly by inspectors. Meeting goals keeps the operation private; failing for two consecutive quarters results in nationalisation, with owners demoted to indentured servants.
- **Sacorrian Whiskey** — A sweet, potent liquor that mellows and grows more potent with ageing, growing in popularity throughout the sector.
- Strict social laws reinforce isolationism: Selonian males cannot leave their den without their queen's permission, Drall males cannot marry without their Duchess's permission, human females cannot marry without their father's permission, and all Sacorrians are forbidden from marrying offworlders.
- **Sacorrian Grey Bear** — Stands 2.5 to 3 metres tall with extremely long arms and blade-sharp claws. Found in the deep woods.
- **Korrak** — Long, sinuous reptiles with eight short legs, capable of climbing trees nearly as fast as they run. Their bite contains a potent paralysing neurotoxin.

### Adventure Hooks

- Attempting to discover the identities of the current Triad members.
- Smuggling contraband past Sacorrian security into or out of the Trade Zone.
- Rescuing someone imprisoned in the supposedly escape-proof Dorthus Tal Prison.
- Investigating the Sarcophagus Moon — what is the Triad really hiding there?
- Running afoul of SMIP inspectors whilst working undercover on a Sacorrian farm.

## Corfai

### Description

Corfai is a cold, rugged world with one of the oldest human civilisations in the Corellian Sector, originally colonised by pre-hyperdrive sleeper ships from Coruscant. Covered in evergreen forests, grand mountain ranges, huge oceans, and massive glacial lands in the polar regions, it demands a hardy, survivalist temperament from its 2.1 billion inhabitants (Human 79%, other 21%).

- **System:** Corfai System, Corellian Sector, Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 337 days per year / 28 hours per day
- **Government:** Democratic republic
- **Language:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Taiga, mountains, glacial plains
- **Major Cities:** Winterdome, Yorri, Knowles, Farrendal
- **Major Exports:** Lumber, manufactured goods, personal electronics, furniture, glacial ice
- **Major Imports:** Foodstuffs, high-end technology, luxury goods
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Run, Corellian Trade Spine

### Points of Interest

- **Mount Lorrist** — The highest mountain on Corfai, considered one of the great "danger sports" peaks of the Core Worlds. Popular with skiing and mountaineering enthusiasts.
- **Olvergast Glacier** — One of the single largest glaciers in the Core, protected by government decree. Located in the southern hemisphere.
- **Winterdome Museum** — A cultural institution in the planet's oldest settlement, celebrating Corfai's history and heritage.
- **Salvarrn Nature Reserve** — One of the largest natural reservations in the Core, dedicated to ensuring the survival of Corfai's native flora and fauna.
- **Hel's Outfitting** — A sporting goods shop in the Icecrest neighbourhood of Winterdome, run by Viktor Hel, a semi-retired bounty hunter. Despite appearing as a minor side-street enterprise, Hel provides pointers on bounty hunting, referrals to illegal weapons dealers, and advice on the best blaster for an individual's needs.

### Notable Features

- Corfai's cities are built in harmony with the natural landscape — into cliffs, hillsides, and fjords. Many buildings are still constructed from wood. Architecture blends with mountains, rivers, and trees.
- The planet is a major exporter of exceptionally high-quality lumber and fine wood furniture, prized across the Core.
- Glacial ice is a unique export: specialists carve gargantuan chunks from the polar regions, launch them into orbit via repulsorlifts, and haul them through space to their destinations for construction projects or water supply.
- Corfai was controversially not invited to the original Galactic Constitution Conference and is not listed as a Core Founder world. Corfinian historians maintain this was a deliberate snub that nearly caused a war with Coruscant, though the average Corfinian could not care less.
- Most Corfinians hunt for sport rather than necessity, and the mountainous regions are dotted with grand snow sport resorts.

### Adventure Hooks

- Seeking out Viktor Hel at Hel's Outfitting for bounty hunting connections and illegal weapons contacts.
- Skiing or climbing Mount Lorrist and encountering unexpected dangers.
- Investigating ancient pre-Republic ruins from the original sleeper ship colonists.
- Hauling a cargo of glacial ice across the sector — and dealing with those who would hijack it.
- Exploring the protected Salvarrn Nature Reserve for rare specimens.

## Froz

### Description

Froz is a devastated ruin — once a lush, pristine, low-gravity world covered in golden plains and emerald forests, it was subjected to a full orbital bombardment by Grand Moff Naomi Dargon's Star Destroyers as punishment for the Frozians' open support of the Alliance to Restore the Republic. Virtually all life on the planet has been extinguished. The sky is thick with smoke, ash, and soot, some ruins are radioactive or toxic, and breathing assistance is required.

- **System:** Froz System, Corellian Sector (Outlier Systems), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 187 days per year / 30 hours per day
- **Government:** N/A (formerly representative republic)
- **Population:** N/A (formerly 1.3 billion; Frozian 89%, other 11%)
- **Language:** Frozian
- **Terrain:** Barren wastes (formerly plains, forests)
- **Major Cities:** N/A (formerly Astromandicar [capital], Juleenasov, Halavortigas)
- **Areas of Interest:** N/A (formerly Fendwellifar Forest, Undescanatain Falls)
- **Major Exports:** N/A (formerly foodstuffs [especially produce], leather crafts)
- **Major Imports:** N/A (formerly technology, manufactured goods)
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Run

### Notable Features

- **The Frozians** — Thin, lanky bipedal mammals with multi-jointed arms and legs, evolved from plains-running vegetarian mammals. They were peaceful, introspective, giving, and tended to assume the worst about situations. They had an irrefutable reputation for integrity; a typical Frozian would rather die than break a promise. They were widely employed as civil servants, bureaucrats, and managers across the galaxy.
- Frozian science specialised in agriculture and biology, with plant breeding programmes as a particular strength. Heavy industry was notably lacking.
- When the Frozians openly rejected the Empire and supported the Alliance, Dargon used the planet as an example. The bombardment was so thorough that virtually all life was extinguished.
- Many Frozians were offworld when the bombardment occurred, but the species faces extinction: Frozians cannot successfully breed in normal gravity, nor without a particular plant once found only on Froz — a plant now gone with the rest of the planet.
- The destruction of Froz has had the opposite of its intended effect in the wider galaxy — sympathy for the Alliance has increased rather than waned.

### Adventure Hooks

- **Burned Treasures** — Searching the shattered surface for the Frozian planetary treasury and precious metal reserves that may have survived the bombardment.
- Locating an undamaged Frozian botanical hothouse or research laboratory to recover genetic information on thousands of now-extinct species — potentially including the plant Frozians need to breed, which could save the species from extinction.
- Documenting Imperial war crimes on the surface for the Rebel Alliance.
- Salvaging technology or supplies from the ruins whilst contending with radioactive hotspots and toxic conditions.

## Vagran

### Description

Vagran is a lush, ecologically diverse world whose population of 780 million (Human 84%, other 16%) lives in dense, green-focused urban centres surrounded by barrier zones that separate civilisation from one of the most varied biospheres in the sector. Plains, forests, jungles, rolling hills, and oceans cover the planet. Cities feature substantial parks, suspended skyways, and buildings covered in clinging mosses and vines.

- **System:** Vagran System, Corellian Sector, Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 329 days per year / 23 hours per day
- **Government:** Parliamentary democracy
- **Language:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Plains, forests, jungles, rolling hills, oceans
- **Major Cities:** Vagran City (capital), Abatore, Thaerne
- **Major Exports:** Foodstuffs (especially various meats and seafood), manufactured goods, lumber
- **Major Imports:** High-end technology, luxury goods
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Trade Spine
- **Special Conditions:** Some areas are utterly forbidden to fly over or set foot on due to ecological "purity" restrictions. Visitors must familiarise themselves with restricted areas before approaching.

### Points of Interest

- **Vagran Zoological Preservation Habitat** — A major centre for the study and preservation of the planet's extraordinary biodiversity.
- **Estainia Resort Islands** — Popular resort destination.
- **Estainia Hunting Grounds** — Government-sanctioned big game hunting area where licensed hunters from across the galaxy may hunt during specific seasons.
- **Barrier Zones** — Every community, from the largest city to the smallest outpost, is surrounded by acutal barriers combining durasteel walls topped with laser fences, energy screens to kill pollen, spores, and microbial life, and audio/olfactory deterrent mechanisms. These keep dangerous organisms out and invasive species in the wild.

### Notable Features

- Vagran was among the first planets colonised after Corellia adopted hyperdrive technology. Early colonists suffered dramatic casualties from the virulent flora and fauna until a collective of scientists established quarantine zones and adaptation protocols.
- The Vagran Colonisation Charter placed scientists in charge, creating biodomes, energy screens, and strict protocols that eventually allowed the population to adapt biologically to the local environment.
- Ironclad regulations ban construction outside approved zones. Clearing new zones takes years and requires costly barrier construction. Corporations are pressuring the government to relax these laws, with some turning to Imperial contacts.
- Imperial influence has thus far been limited to politely worded "suggestions" from the Grand Moff's office, but the unstated threat is pushing some elements towards the Rebellion.

### Adventure Hooks

- Big game hunting on the Estainia Hunting Grounds — or dealing with poachers operating outside sanctioned seasons.
- Investigating corporate attempts to bribe officials into relaxing environmental protections.
- Venturing beyond the barrier zones into the untamed wilderness for a mission.
- Smuggling exotic Vagran fauna offworld, evading both local authorities and the dangerous creatures themselves.
- Making contact with Rebel sympathisers within the government.

## Xyquine II

### Description

Xyquine II is a pleasant, stable world often considered the "safest place in the Sector," with a well-established civilisation, hereditary monarchy, effective parliament, and a strong tradition of civic involvement. Its population of 2.1 billion (Human 74%, other 26%) has long enjoyed peace and good governance, though recent events — particularly the destruction of Froz — have awakened a growing current of dissent against the Empire.

- **System:** Xyquine System, Corellian Sector (Outlier Systems), Core region
- **Orbital Metrics:** 348 days per year / 26 hours per day
- **Government:** Hereditary monarchy and parliament
- **Language:** Basic
- **Terrain:** Plains, forests, jungles, mountains, tundras, deserts, oceans
- **Major Cities:** New Coronet (capital), Agadore, Sirden, Valendia
- **Major Exports:** Foodstuffs, biotech, manufactured goods
- **Major Imports:** Metals and ore, high-end electronics, luxury goods
- **Trade Routes:** Corellian Trade Spine

### Points of Interest

- **Mount Vissa** — Towering slopes providing unparalleled views of the vast Umber Jungles in the equatorial regions.
- **The Strangling Sea** — Known for its fearsome whirlpools and ripcurrents, best viewed from afar.
- **Capitol Park** — A major green space in New Coronet.
- **The Millennial Forum** — A marble colossus spanning several square kilometres, with a stadium-sized amphitheatre in each corner. The largest forum hosts the planetary parliament; the others are open to the public for civic debate. A living monument to Xyquine II's democratic tradition.

### Notable Features

- "Welcome to Xy-2, where nothing ever happens" is a well-worn cultural phrase. The planet is popular as a retirement destination, with young people leaving in their twenties to explore the galaxy before returning decades later.
- Xyquine II's history is considered the model colonial success story — the "Xyquine II Story" was used as a blueprint for how colonisation should proceed throughout the sector.
- The destruction of Froz has caused a seismic shift in public opinion. The public outcry was deafening, and calls for secession became mainstream. The parliament passed laws limiting protest activities to maintain appearances.
- In private, the government has cautiously reached out to Rebel agents to support the Alliance whilst publicly condemning secession. "Nothing ever happens" is about to be over.

### Adventure Hooks

- Making secret contact with Xyquine II government officials sympathetic to the Rebellion.
- Attending a public debate at the Millennial Forum that turns violent when Imperial agents intervene.
- Investigating Imperial infiltrators within the planetary government and security services.
- Recruiting idealistic young Xyquinians ("wanderlusters") for a crew — they make enthusiastic if unskilled crewmembers.
- Using the planet's reputation for boredom as cover for covert Rebel operations.

## Other Corellian Sector Systems

### Altawar System

Home to the Corragut, a species discovered during Corellia's early expansion. The Corragut were welcomed into the sector but treated as a "client race," with trade, defence, and related matters controlled by Corellian bureaucrats. They were given only technology the Corellians deemed "safe" and have never possessed military assets. The Corragut are growing deeply resentful of their human overseers, and the pro-human Imperial agenda is stirring serious ire, though the fate of the Frozians has dampened any overt resistance.

### Craeen System

Colonised by Corellians for its mineral wealth. Numerous technology and manufacturing firms operate here thanks to extensive tax breaks. The environment has suffered from lax industrial regulations, though not as severely as Duro. Craeen has become a haven for scientific discovery and engineering innovation, with countless research labs on the planet, in orbit, and on its moons. Under Imperial guidance, this has shifted to outright encouragement of ethically questionable experiments. A small Imperial Navy detachment has recently arrived, suggesting military research projects are underway.

### Dorsis System

Originally colonised by Coruscanti sleeper ships, Dorsis is sometimes called "Mini-Coruscant" — heavily urbanised with sprawling cities, great skyscrapers, and manufacturing plants covering much of the habitable land. Even the oceans have floating habitats (mainly for the rich and powerful). The economy revolves around basic consumer goods produced as cheaply as possible. The seedier parts of the sprawls are a haven for criminals, fringers, cartels, and gangs. Some cartel leaders own private floating habitats.

### Forvand System

Contains only two uninhabitable planets. The system's sole value is a decent-sized space station that collects energy from the star. Located on the Duros Run trade route, the station provides repairs, refuelling, news gathering, and shipping brokerage. Popular with independent freighters and smaller shipping companies. A healthy black market operates despite increased Imperial customs presence (though extensive bribes are now necessary).

### Goorla System

An Outlier System with a rocky, mountainous planet, minimal water, light gravity, and a thin atmosphere requiring rebreathers outdoors. People live in environmentally sealed habitats. Mining colonies are profitable, and the low gravity aids manufacturing and chemical production. The most notable export is **Goorlish light source** — a highly malleable crystalline substance with a constant inner gentle light in pink and lavender spectra. A fist-sized chunk provides light for at least five years. Artists combine it with bioluminescent plant fibres (from Sacorria and Vagran) to create **Corellian flame miniatures** — delicate, lighter-than-air light sculptures resembling pink flames that float a few metres off the ground, each with a unique pattern. These are highly prized art objects.

### Govia System

An Outlier System discovered later in the sector's development, inhabited by the Govians — a near-human species (grey-white skin, more body hair, heavier and more portly than typical humans) who had developed near-orbit vehicular technology but not space travel. Archaeologists believe they were seeded by Coruscanti sleeper ships or the ancient Celestials. Govia's climate is slightly colder than Corellia's but otherwise temperate with four seasons. Exports are agricultural and manufacturing (middle-class consumer goods); they import considerable technology to catch up with the rest of the sector.

### Graland System

No habitable planets, but two worlds yield minerals and ore. A modest, mostly automated mining industry operates here, run by a dozen different corporations with Corellia Mining Corporation holding a stake. The small population and corporate complexity make it a popular hiding place for fugitives who can pose as employees of different companies.

### Jumus System

One of the most distant Outlier Systems. The sole inhabitable planet is a very wet, humid world covered in tropical rainforests and jungles with tens of thousands of rivers forming a dizzying web of waterways. The colonial administration welcomed all species, resulting in sizeable populations of Miralukas, Wookiees, Hutts, Twi'leks, and Bith. The extraordinarily diverse flora provides extensive natural resources for biomedical research and pharmaceuticals. **Corellian Chemical Corporation (CorChemCo)** essentially owns and runs the planet, with their lobbyists heavily influencing policy decisions.

### Keral System

Not far from Corellia but with no inhabitable planets or moons. Speculating miners eventually discovered **Keral firegems** on a moon orbiting the planet nearest the sun — gems glowing with near white-hot intensity, prized for their internal energies, warmth, and brightness. Corellian Engineering made a fortune in early sales. However, firegems' internal radiations can interact with hyperdrive reactors causing catastrophic explosions, so the Republic (and later the Empire) outlawed mining and sale. Passive security measures orbit the moon, but many smugglers risk it for the extremely high black market prices.

### Khomr System

A fairly productive agriworld with peaceful farmers and labourers who want little to do with galactic problems. Most notable for its burgeoning weaving industry — Khomrians take great pride in handwoven quilts and tapestries using traditional techniques. Khomri tapestries are highly prized works of art throughout the galaxy.

### New Plympto System

Located on the Corellian Trade Spine between Corellia and Duro. Home to the native Nosaurians, it was kept "off the books" for millennia due to its most unique export: **rikknit eggs** — a delicacy also valuable for producing the highly addictive narcotic **ji rikknit**. When the Nosaurians were officially brought into the sector, human-centric policies left them marginalised. Their resentment led them to join the Separatists during the Clone Wars. After Order 66, rebel Nosaurians fought clone troopers under Jedi leadership. They were ultimately crushed by the 501st Legion at the Battle of Half-Axe Pass, with survivors mass-executed or sold into slavery. New Plympto is now ruled by an Imperial Governor who spends most of his time synthesising ji rikknit on Karsten Moon. The Nosaurian population is effectively enslaved.

### Phemis System

A dry, rocky, habitable world with signs of regular off-world visitation but no current inhabitants. The Jedi secretly visited for millennia to harvest **rubat crystals** — Force-connected crystals used in lightsaber construction that create a slightly stronger, more intense blade. The Republic granted the Jedi exclusive rights to rubat whilst permitting sector firms to mine other resources (including bronzium). Since the rise of the Empire, Imperial forces enforce the rubat ban — officially to prevent smuggling, but actually to capture anyone who might use the crystals. Corellian Mining originally provided security for Jedi rubat sites.

### Plympto System

Located on the Corellian Trade Spine, this system is little more than a bright blue star with uninhabitable rocks. A couple of space stations serve as convenient stops for merchants. Minor mining yields marginal resources. Most notable for inspiring the name of the New Plympto System due to similar star characteristics.

### Polanis System

Primarily an agricultural world famous for its excellent alcoholic beverages. The entire culture revolves around breweries, distilleries, and wineries, with the government subsidising the industry heavily. **Polanis ale** (a smooth, golden beer) is the most commonly exported product under the Polanis Brewing label, which is partially nationalised. Polanis reds are considered among the finest wines in the galaxy. Tourism centred on beverage tours and resorts is also significant.

### Saberhing System

A system with a huge asteroid belt rich in ores and a nearly barren, habitable world. **Corellia Mining Corporation** and **Corellian Engineering Company** formed the Saberhing Development Compact — their largest-ever joint operation. Over a decade, virtually all the planet's usable surface was urbanised and dedicated to starship manufacturing. Saberhing is now an entirely industrialised planet with billions of inhabitants, supported by large-scale asteroid belt mining. A small fleet of Imperial Navy warships maintains security over this vital ship-building operation.

### Sileria System

Composed entirely of shattered planets orbiting the star in belts of debris — likely the result of a sentient-wrought cataclysm. Mining operations are scattered throughout, and the utter chaos of the system makes it a favourite hiding place for smugglers, pirates, and ne'er-do-wells. The **Silerian Run** is a specific course through the debris belts considered one of the ultimate tests of piloting skill. Officially banned by Corellian Sector law, dozens of pilots are killed attempting it every year — but Corellians have their pride.

### Talfaglio System

Volcanic, hot, and covered in a thin miasma of orange ash and mist. One of the most inhospitable Outlier Systems, of little importance. Corellian Mining runs the system using lava flows as natural smelting sources, with operations mostly automated. The sparse population and isolation make it a favourite destination for those seeking privacy.

### Tanthior System

A jungle world from pole to pole, populated by a dizzying array of lethally dangerous native animals and plants. The atmosphere is poisonous to most non-native species. Sentients live in biologically sealed habitats, seeking to unlock the planet's ecological treasures. It is also a favourite destination for hunters. Rumours suggest the Empire is building (or has already built) a maximum security prison on the surface.

### Truuzdann System

An economy based on hardscrabble agriculture and light industry, with two moons providing modest mining revenue. The spaceport city of Truuzdann (also the capital) is the centre of trade and has a reputation for being very "liberal" regarding regulations and law enforcement. Graft and corruption have plagued the system since its earliest days.

### Vasar System

Colonised early in Corellia's expansion, with most initial population coming from Talus. The biosphere and ecology are very similar to Talus. Vasar developed agriculture and industry, aligning itself primarily with the Double Worlds (Talus and Tralus). Best known for **Vasarian brandy** — discovered when a freighter's cargo of fruit burst, fermented in a reclamation system, and the crew found the result so delicious they drank themselves into a stupor before being rescued.

### Velx-Shel System

One of the few planets colonised exclusively by private interests, named after the two founding families who essentially own it. Velx-Shel became a planet of specialised universities, research facilities, design firms, and think tanks. The Core Worlds regard it as a destination for truly advanced and innovative thinking, with aristocratic families paying dearly to send their children to its schools. The founders ensured agricultural self-sufficiency and food storage as a model of planetary disaster preparedness.
