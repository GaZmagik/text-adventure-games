# Enter the Unknown — A Sourcebook for Explorers
> Sourcebook module for the star-wars-eote skill. Explorer career options, new species, specialisations, gear, vehicles, starships, and GM guidance for exploration-themed campaigns.

---

## Chapter I: Galactic Sojourners

### The Explorer Career
- The Explorer's eight career skills are **Astrogation**, **Cool**, **Knowledge (Lore)**, **Knowledge (Outer Rim)**, **Knowledge (Xenology)**, **Perception**, **Piloting (Space)**, and **Survival**.
- The Explorer automatically gains one rank in four of these skills (of his choosing) without spending experience, and receives a discount when purchasing ranks in any of these skills.
- The Edge of the Empire Core Rulebook introduced the Fringer, Scout, and Trader specialisations. Enter the Unknown adds three new specialisations: the Archaeologist, the Big-Game Hunter, and the Driver.

### Explorer Backgrounds
- **Academic:** Strong foundation in knowledge and research; appreciates the importance of first-hand experience over theoretical understanding alone.
- **Criminal:** Explorers may turn to crime through necessity or circumstance; the farther from Imperial power, the easier criminal activity becomes.
- **Entrepreneur:** Seeks opportunities in the outer reaches where societies and technology are still developing; requires adaptability and quick thinking.
- **Ex-Imperial Operator:** Former Imperial servants who departed service for various reasons — some fired, some disillusioned, some fleeing with dangerous knowledge.
- **Fallen Noble:** Aristocrats with poor reputations seeking redemption or a fresh start in the outer reaches; hobbies and intellectual pursuits become practical skills.
- **Wanderer:** Driven by curiosity, boredom, or tragic circumstance to roam the stars; the hardy soul who overcomes fear of the unknown.

### New Species

#### Chiss
- Near-humans with dark-blue skin and glowing red eyes from the Unknown Regions. Cautious, thoughtful, and highly cultured. Art, culture, intellectual advancement, and discipline are highly prized. Chiss live about 80 standard years and reach physical maturity in their early teens.
- The Chiss Ascendancy is a secretive civilisation; rare individuals venture into the galaxy at large for personal growth.
- Chiss have a cultural taboo against preemptive strikes; unprovoked advance during conflicts can result in exile from the Ascendancy.
- **Homeworld:** Csilla, an ice world in the Unknown Regions. Glaciers forced the population underground into thousands of warrens connected by extensive networks of passages.
- **Language:** Cheunh (native), Sy Bisti and Minnisiat (trade languages); also speak Basic.

**Brawn 2, Agility 2, Intellect 3, Cunning 2, Willpower 2, Presence 1**
- **Wound Threshold:** 10 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Begin the game with one rank in Cool (may not train Cool above rank 2 during character creation).
- **Infravision:** Chiss have adapted to see in both the infrared and normal visual spectra. This enables Chiss characters to remove up to Setback Setback added to checks by lighting conditions.

#### Duros
- Descended from reptiles; bipedal humanoids with large red eyes, smooth blue-green skin, long fingers, bald bulbous heads, and tiny slits below the eyes providing their olfactory sense. Not particularly brawny but possess sharp minds and keen intellects.
- Most scholars believe the Duros actually invented the first hyperdrive craft. It is considered polite to refer to any Duros by the honorific "Traveller."
- **Homeworld:** Duro, once a lush green world, now completely uninhabitable without extensive biohazard protections due to unchecked pollution and rampant industrialisation. The Duros live in massive orbital cities and space stations.
- **Language:** Durese (also called Durosian), widely spoken throughout the Corellian Sector and many Core Worlds.
- Duros and Corellians share a rivalry; some Duros use the term "mammals" in a derogatory fashion for nonreptilian species.

**Brawn 1, Agility 2, Intellect 3, Cunning 2, Willpower 2, Presence 2**
- **Wound Threshold:** 11 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 10 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 100 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Begin the game with one rank in Piloting (Space) (may not train Piloting (Space) above rank 2 during character creation).
- **Intuitive Navigation:** Duros may add Advantage to all Astrogation checks they make.

#### Toydarians
- Smallish, squat bodies that perpetually hover over the ground thanks to wings on their back and an abundance of natural gasses absorbed into their frames. Stubby protruding snouts, craggy faces, spindly arms and legs ending with three fingers and three toes respectively.
- Completely immune to any manipulations via the Force.
- Extremely active metabolisms; constant need for food, eating large quantities throughout the day. Natural body odour similar to sweetspice.
- Prideful, stubborn, and strong-willed merchants and opportunity seekers. Reputation for bad tempers and con jobs.
- **Homeworld:** Toydaria, a world of muck, mud, swamps, and massive quantities of algae. Monsoons, plagues of fungal origin, and huge carnivorous grabworms make it generally unpleasant.
- **Society:** Feudal vassal system ruled by a king. Members of Toydarian nobility constantly vie with one another for their lord's favour.
- **Language:** Toydarian (native); most also speak Huttese fluently.

**Brawn 1, Agility 1, Intellect 2, Cunning 2, Willpower 3, Presence 3**
- **Wound Threshold:** 9 + Brawn
- **Strain Threshold:** 12 + Willpower
- **Starting Experience:** 90 XP
- **Special Abilities:** Toydarians are smaller than average and count as silhouette 0.
- **Hoverer:** Toydarians have wings that allow them to hover slightly off of the ground. When hovering, Toydarians do not have to spend additional manoeuvres when navigating difficult terrain. Otherwise, their wings allow them no extra capabilities beyond those of a normal, walking character.

### Explorer Specialisations

#### The Archaeologist
- Archaeologists are firmly entrenched in the old. The galaxy is impossibly old, and nigh-infinite millennia have witnessed the rise and fall of countless civilisations. The Archaeologist endeavours to find lost cultures and unlock the secrets of their history, art, science, and everything else about them.
- **Bonus Career Skills:** Athletics, Discipline, Knowledge (Education), Knowledge (Lore)

**Archaeologist Talent Tree:**

| Row | Cost 5 | Cost 5 | Cost 5 | Cost 5 |
|-----|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Well Rounded | Hard Headed | Researcher | Grit |
| 2 (10) | Durable | Toughened | Resolve | Knowledge Specialisation |
| 3 (15) | Stunning Blow | Knockdown | Respected Scholar | Researcher |
| 4 (20) | Hard Headed | Enduring | Grit | Knowledge Specialisation |
| 5 (25) | Pin | Dedication | Respected Scholar | Museum Worthy |

- **Well Rounded:** Choose any 2 skills; they permanently become career skills.
- **Researcher (Ranked):** Remove Setback per rank from all Knowledge checks. Researching a subject takes half the time.
- **Resolve (Ranked):** When involuntarily suffering strain, suffer 1 less strain per rank of Resolve, to a minimum of 1.
- **Knowledge Specialisation (Ranked):** When acquired, choose 1 Knowledge skill. When making that skill check, may spend Triumph to gain additional successes equal to ranks in Knowledge Specialisation.
- **Stunning Blow:** When making Melee checks, may inflict damage as strain instead of wounds. This does not ignore soak.
- **Respected Scholar (Ranked):** May downgrade difficulty of checks to interact with institutes of learning by one level per rank.
- **Museum Worthy:** Once per session, take Museum Worthy action: make a Hard Knowledge (Education) check to ascertain information regarding a relic, ruin, or piece of history.
- **Pin:** Take Pin action; make an Opposed Athletics check to immobilise an engaged opponent until end of the character's next turn. Spend Advantage to extend duration one round.

#### The Big-Game Hunter
- With the skills of a survivalist, the instincts of a hunter, and the courage of a madman, the Big-Game Hunter actively tracks down, confronts, and does battle with the most terrifying creatures in the galaxy.
- **Bonus Career Skills:** Knowledge (Xenology), Ranged (Heavy), Stealth, Survival

**Big-Game Hunter Talent Tree:**

| Row | Cost 5 | Cost 5 | Cost 5 | Cost 5 |
|-----|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Forager | Grit | Stalker | Outdoorsman |
| 2 (10) | Toughened | Outdoorsman | Confidence | Swift |
| 3 (15) | Stalker | Natural Hunter | Expert Tracker | Heightened Awareness |
| 4 (20) | Grit | Hunter's Quarry | Quick Strike | Expert Tracker |
| 5 (25) | Bring It Down | Improved Hunter's Quarry | Dedication | Superior Reflexes |

- **Forager:** Remove up to Setback Setback from skill checks to find food, water, or shelter. Survival checks to forage take half the time.
- **Stalker (Ranked):** Add Boost per rank of Stalker to all Stealth and Coordination checks.
- **Outdoorsman (Ranked):** Remove Setback per rank from checks to move through terrain or manage environmental effects. Decrease overland travel times by half.
- **Natural Hunter:** Once per session, may reroll any 1 Perception or Vigilance check.
- **Heightened Awareness:** Allies within close range add Boost to Perception or Vigilance checks. Engaged allies add Boost Boost.
- **Hunter's Quarry:** Take Hunter's Quarry action against an opponent within long range, making a Hard Survival check. If successful, upgrade the ability of all attacks made against the target by one until end of the character's next turn.
- **Improved Hunter's Quarry:** Suffer 2 strain to perform Hunter's Quarry action as a manoeuvre instead.
- **Bring It Down:** Once per attack, spend 1 Destiny Point to add damage to a single hit equal to target's Brawn value.
- **Superior Reflexes:** Gain +1 melee defence.

#### The Driver
- Drivers understand the machines they operate and are exceptionally skilled with nearly anything that moves from one place on a planet to another, typically demonstrating some higher level of style or finesse.
- **Bonus Career Skills:** Cool, Gunnery, Mechanics, Piloting (Planetary)

**Driver Talent Tree:**

### New Talents

**All-Terrain Driver** — Activation: Passive; Ranked: No; Trees: Driver. When piloting a vehicle using Piloting (Planetary), the character does not suffer the penalties for driving through difficult terrain.

**Bring It Down** — Activation: Active (Incidental); Ranked: No; Trees: Big-Game Hunter. The character may spend 1 Destiny Point to add damage equal to his target's Brawn value to a single hit. May only be activated once per attack.

**Full Stop** — Activation: Active (Manoeuvre); Ranked: No; Trees: Driver. When piloting a ship or vehicle, the character may take the full-stop manoeuvre to immediately reduce speed to zero. The ship or vehicle then suffers one point of system strain for every point of speed reduced.

**Hunter's Quarry** — Activation: Active (Action); Ranked: No; Trees: Big-Game Hunter. Take the Hunter's Quarry action against an opponent within long range, making a Hard Survival check. If successful, upgrade the ability of all attacks made against the target by one until end of the character's next turn.

**Hunter's Quarry (Improved)** — Activation: Passive; Ranked: No; Trees: Big-Game Hunter. The character can choose to suffer 2 strain to perform the Hunter's Quarry action as a manoeuvre instead.

**Master Driver** — Activation: Active (Incidental); Ranked: No; Trees: Driver. Once per round when piloting a vehicle using Piloting (Planetary), the character may voluntarily suffer 2 strain to perform any action as a manoeuvre instead.

**Museum Worthy** — Activation: Active (Action); Ranked: No; Trees: Archaeologist. Once per game session, the character may take an action to make a Hard Knowledge (Education) check regarding a single relic, ruin, or piece of galactic history (subject to the GM's approval). If successful, the character knows an amount of information about the item based on the number of Success rolled.

**Natural Driver** — Activation: Active (Incidental); Ranked: No; Trees: Driver. Once per game session, the character may reroll any one Piloting (Planetary) or Gunnery check.

**Pin** — Activation: Active (Action); Ranked: No; Trees: Archaeologist. By successfully passing an Opposed Athletics check against an engaged opponent, the character immobilises that opponent until the end of the character's next turn. The character may spend Advantage to increase the duration by one round.

### Explorer Signature Abilities

Signature abilities are special, elite talents only experienced characters of the specified career may access. They are feats of strength only possible through skill and ability gained over a long and successful career. The Explorer career has access to two signature abilities: **Sudden Discovery** and **Unmatched Mobility**.

#### Sudden Discovery
- **Base Ability (Cost 30):** Once per game session, the character may spend 2 Destiny Points to make a Hard Knowledge (Outer Rim) or Knowledge (Core Worlds) check. If he succeeds, the character can pinpoint his exact location without a map or other guide, discover a lost or hidden item or location, or identify a safe and fast path through any terrain. The exact nature must be approved by the GM.

**Upgrades:**
- **Add Boost (Cost 10/15):** Add Boost to the skill check to activate Sudden Discovery. May be taken twice.
- **Reduce Setback (Cost 10):** Remove Setback from the skill check to activate Sudden Discovery.
- **Change Skill (Cost 10):** Sudden Discovery can be activated with the Astrogation skill to find locations in space or the Survival skill to find locations on the ground (instead of normal Knowledge skills).
- **Reduce Difficulty (Cost 15):** The skill check difficulty to activate Sudden Discovery is Average instead of Hard.
- **Frequency (Cost 15):** Sudden Discovery may be used twice per game session instead of once.
- **Destiny (Cost 15):** Sudden Discovery costs 1 Destiny Point instead of the normal 2.

#### Unmatched Mobility
- **Base Ability (Cost 30):** Once per game session as an incidental, the character may spend 2 Destiny Points to increase the number of manoeuvres he is allowed to perform in a turn to three for the next 2 rounds. This third manoeuvre may be gained through any of the means a second manoeuvre is normally gained.

**Upgrades:**
- **Duration (Cost 10/15/15):** Unmatched Mobility lasts for one additional round. May be taken up to three times.
- **Free Manoeuvre (Cost 10/10):** Gain one additional free manoeuvre while base ability is active. This does not increase per-turn manoeuvres. May be taken twice.
- **Melee Defence (Cost 10):** Gain +1 melee defence while Unmatched Mobility is active.
- **Destiny (Cost 15):** Unmatched Mobility costs 1 Destiny Point instead of 2.
- **Ranged Defence (Cost 15):** Gain +1 ranged defence while Unmatched Mobility is active.

## Chapter III: Grand Expeditions

### Explorer Nemeses

#### The Rival
A nemesis who works in the same field as the character and manages to compete at much the same level of competence. Need not seek to physically harm the PCs; may be one of good-natured competition. The rival has a good name for himself, and the PC may begin to get the feeling that every time he is on the verge of achieving a significant goal, the rival has managed to get there first or otherwise outshine him.

#### The Great Beast
A particularly monstrous example of a species already renowned for its dangerous nature — a krayt dragon of prodigious proportion, a lylek with a craving for human flesh, or a pale and ageing sando aqua monster ousted from its territory. Its real strength lies in its knowledge of its home turf, using ruses, hazards, and other dangerous animals to waylay and injure hunters.

#### The Missionary
An ideologue with a particular (and probably peculiar) perspective on religion, politics, or philosophy. Removed himself to an unexplored quarter of the galaxy where he has encountered a primitive society, spurring himself and his followers on with increasing confidence. Investigating may lead PCs into conflict with followers, loyalist tribes, and the missionary's well-equipped inner circle.

### Adventure Seeds

#### The Anaxian Farce
A Trader is sent to Anaxes to strike a deal with a snobbish mercantile patriarch. The Trader and companions must construct elaborate upper-class identities incorporating tales of dashing military exploits. A private point-scoring system tracks how convincingly they maintain the ruse; proximity to failure increases as the pretence grows more outrageous.

#### Mission to Mimban
The Circarpous system's planet Mimban has been largely unexplored, home to the primitive Mimbanites and the hostile Coway. The Empire has discovered dolovite ore and constructed five mining colonies, releasing radioactive particles into the atmosphere. A Scout PC is employed to map the underground tunnel complexes and log Coway settlement positions — but the mission reveals far more destruction than the Empire admits.

#### The Great Bespin Zoological Heist
A Big-Game Hunter is approached by someone who believes he has found a planet upon which beldons could be farmed for Tibanna gas. Collecting beldons from Bespin's atmosphere would be dangerous — avoiding local authorities, encounters with velkers and rawwks, and the beldons' own passive electrical field defences.

### Sponsors and Organisations

**The Hyperspace Navigators Guild:** Foremost amongst the guilds and corporations with a vested interest in space travel. Most interested in the blazing of new hyperroutes. Any find that contributes to the discipline of Astrogation would likely earn a cash reward from members of the brotherhood.

**Bearers of the Claatuvac Stacks:** Portable copies of the data once held by the Claatuvac guild on Kashyyyk. Entrusted to a small band of Wookiees who swore to keep the knowledge secret until Wookiee culture could be revived. The information combines to give the most comprehensive star charts and hyperroute maps in existence.

**The Fellowship of Kooroo:** Based around squat ziggurats (the Shrines of Kooroo) found dotted on worlds throughout the Outer Rim. Force-sensitive visitors sometimes experience odd visions. Some academics conjecture the shrines may be a telepathic communication or teleportation network.

### The Risks and Rewards of Exploration

- **Discovering New Worlds and Systems:** Corporate interests are always keen to learn of ways to outmanoeuvre their competitors and lay claim to sources of raw materials. The Empire, the Corporate Sector Authority, criminal organisations, the Rebel Alliance, and individual corporations all represent potential buyers.
- **The Discovery of Inhabited Planets:** Far more exciting but far riskier. The more civilised the life encountered, the more there is for an Explorer to boast about and possibly exploit.
- **Blazing New Hyperroutes:** A far more rewarding exercise than exploring realspace. Charting new hyperroutes is risky — objects in realspace can impact on the fabric of hyperspace, and a short unplanned journey between realspace and hyperspace is unlikely to result in anything good.
- **Paying Off Obligation:** Discoveries of new raw materials or habitable worlds are a good way to pay off Obligation. Conversely, selling information to the wrong party can be a source of new Obligation.

## Key Lore — A Brief History of Exploration

- Galactic exploration really began about 25,000 years before the Battle of Yavin, after the Rakatan Infinite Empire diminished through disease and slave revolts.
- Early hyperdrives were of limited use; the Devaronians and Gossams experimented with tumble hyperdrives before Rakatan technology was reverse-engineered.
- Between 25,000 and 20,000 BBY, early hyperdrive exploration saw the Republic push territory toward the galactic east, blazing the Perlemian Trade Route and Corellian Run.
- The Hyperspace Navigators Guild regulated space exploration, paying large credit rewards for new hyperspace routes and systems. It still exists but with much diminished influence.
- Under the Empire, exploration has changed from cooperation between peers into a complicated and paranoid enterprise. The Empire exploits new habitable worlds for resources, hidden facilities, and testing grounds.
- Regulatory bodies such as the Hyperspace Navigators Guild have been pushed aside as the Empire approves of speedier methods to locate and monitor possible sources of raw materials.
- The Mrisst Planetary University and other educational institutions also sponsor expeditions and may provide apolitical explorers the means to make a modest living from their discoveries.
- Newly discovered civilisations and primitive populations are now subject to Imperial exploitation rather than the Republic's previous policy of peaceful observation.
